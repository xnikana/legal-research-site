#### **Staff have drafted a DRAFT process timeline as follows**:

# **1) Preapplication Conference for Comprehensive Permit.**

Requirements: **PLEASE NOTE** - CANNOT require anything beyond this list for a pre-application conference (185-182 of the Zoning Ordinance effective January 1, 2024).

- Short written description of the project, including the number of units, type of housing, density analysis.
- Preliminary list of adjustments.
- Location map.
- A conceptual site plan.

According to 200-13 of the regulations (effective January 1, 2024), the preapplication shall be held with the Technical Review Committee.

Submission of these materials shall be subject to the 3-week-in-advance submittal deadline.

# **2) Preliminary Plan Submittal.**

#### **Step 1:**

![](PB Memo 01-09-2024_attachments/_page_1_Picture_19.jpeg)

Submission Requirements MUST include:

- A letter of eligibility from RIHousing or HUD.
- A letter setting forth the specific sections and provisions of the ordinance and regulations which the applicant is seeking relief/adjustments.
- Checklist items for preliminary plan major land development project in the Land Development/Subdivision Regulations.

The Administrative Officer will confirm the required documents have been provided prior to reviewing the content of the submission.

#### **Step 2:**

The Administrative Officer will review the materials AND refer the submission materials to the Technical Review Committee for their recommendation PRIOR TO certifying the application complete.

#### **Step 3:**

Certificate of completeness due within 25 days of submission.

# **3) Public Hearing**

To be noticed

- in newspaper Barrington Times
- on website barrington.ri.gov
- abutters within 200 feet by first-class mail(sent by the applicant with notarized affidavit of sending).

Public Hearing held as soon as practicable after certificate of completeness (i.e. next Planning Board meeting after certified complete).

- Planning Board may request additional, reasonable documentation THROUGHOUT THE PUBLIC HEARING including (not limited to):
  - o Opinion of experts.
  - o Credible evidence of application for necessary federal/state permits.
  - o Advice from other local boards and officials.

# **4) Planning Board Preliminary Plan Decision**

The Board shall render a decision within 90 days (approximately 3 months) of being certified complete, unless extension consented to by the applicant.

While making the Preliminary Plan decision, if approved, the Board may delegate final plan approval to staff, or require it be reviewed by the Board. The Board should be prepared to incorporate this choice as part of a motion.

#### **6. Board and Commission Liaisons**

#### **6.1 Discuss and Act: Board and Commission Liaison Assignments**

The purpose of the liaisons is for Board members to be in the know of Board, Commissions, and Committees working on initiatives directly connected to the Comprehensive Plan.

![](PB Memo 01-09-2024_attachments/_page_3_Picture_22.jpeg)

In preparation of the Comprehensive Plan rewrite through 2024 into 2025, staff were reviewing the Planning Board's liaison assignments to both best serve the rewrite process while being cognizant of Board members time serving these roles in addition to the Planning Board.

#### **6.1.1 Conservation Commission and Open Space Committee Assignments.**

It came to the staff's attention that there was not a liaison to the Conservation Commission. Staff believe it is critical to have a liaison to this Commission, as they oversee goals within the Natural & Cultural Resources chapter of the Comprehensive Plan. In addition, the Open Space Committee membership is compiled of members from other Board and Commissions, including Planning Board, Conservation Commission, and Resilience and Energy. Therefore, it is the staff's recommendation to remove the Open Space Committee liaison assignment and replace it with Conservation Commission.

The Conservation Commission meets on the second Tuesday of each month at 7 PM at the Barrington Public Library.

**MOTION:** To remove the Open Space Committee as part of the Planning Board's liaison assignments and replace with the Conservation Commission.

**MOTION:** To appoint [NAME] as the Planning Board liaison to the Conservation Commission.

## **6.1.2 Zoning Board Assignment.**

Due to the legislative updates effective January 1, 2024 that moves decisions that were once with the Zoning Board to the Planning Board and with the addition of a stenographer on staff for the Zoning Board that will provide a transcript of each month, staff does not believe it is necessary for a Planning Board member to attend Zoning Board meetings. The Zoning Board is more enforcement than policy making, however, any updates the Board may request of the Zoning Board can be communicated via the Planning/Zoning Assistant, who also serves as Clerk to the Zoning Board. Again, staff want to ensure that we are being cognizant of Planning Board members time, efforts, and resources.

**MOTION:** To remove the Zoning Board as part of the Planning Board's liaison assignments.
