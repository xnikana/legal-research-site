## **2023 Accomplishments: (Issues addressed, goals accomplished, projects)**

- Planning Board's Capital Improvement Program subcommittee recommended an approximately \$36,000 increase for the FY24.
- Approved a development plan review application for the proposed Chipotle at 223-227 County Road. The project is currently permitted and under construction.
- 25 Watson Avenue. Held 5 meetings (including regular and special meetings) to gather public comment and discuss, leading to recommendations to the Town Council and Financial Town Meeting. An RFP for the pocket neighborhood portion of the project is being drafted based on the Board's recommendation.

- Continued to received updates on the former Zion Bible Institute property at 33 Middle Highway (a standing item on the agenda).
  - o The Board had recommended an extension on the prior master plan, which the applicant withdrew in March 2023.
  - o The Board heard numerous updates from the property owner's representatives, including a preapplication meeting at the September 5, 2023 Regular Business Meeting.
- The Planning Board completed continuing education on the legislation passed by the General Assembly in June 2023 at their July Regular Business Meeting.
- Ensued in several discussions related to commercial signage, and recommended it be part of the Comprehensive Plan rewrite in 2024-2025.
- Zoning Ordinance and Land Development and Subdivision Regulations amendments (effective January 1, 2024). This included three (5) meetings, including a special meeting on November 27, 2023, where the Board completed the following:
  - o Review of all special uses in the use table.
  - o Set percentage for modifications (now required).
  - o Set threshold for inclusionary zoning.
  - o Review of use definitions, and specific and objective criteria for special uses remaining in the use table.
  - o Eliminated minimum lot sizes for existing lots.
  - o Reviewed consistency of the with the Comprehensive Plan.
  - o Amendments to the land development, subdivision, and development plan review checklists.
- Three (3) Comprehensive Permits:
  - o Finalized application for 17 Lorraine Street– 4 units, 1 affordable.
  - o Preliminary Plan approval for 173R Maple Ave 4 units 1 affordable.
  - o Pre-application meeting for 89 Maple Avenue 2 units, both affordable.
- Three (3) Administrative Subdivision Reviews:
  - o 359, 361/363 Maple Avenue.
  - o 2 Seabrook Court.
  - o 22 Briarfield Road.

## **2024 Goals:**

- Learn and adapt to the legislative updates, including unified development review, which will bring more applications before the Planning Board. Staff is working with RIHousing consultant Libra Planners to draft guidebooks for the various types of applications, and then work with the Solicitor's office to hold a training session.
- Anticipation of Comprehensive Permit Applications at 33 Middle Highway and 89 Maple Avenue.
- Comprehensive Plan rewrite process that will go into 2025.
