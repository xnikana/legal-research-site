# **4.1 Discuss and Act: Amendments to Chapter 200: Land Development and Subdivision Regulations to reflect amendments to Rhode Island General Law.**

*Present: Peter Friedrichs and Kim Salerno, Libra Planners*

Town Planner Crean announced that the Town Council passed the Zoning Ordinance amendments as presented and recommended by the Board at their December 4, 2023 Public Hearing. Crean then reviewed that the Land Development and Subdivision Regulations have less areas up for discussion and action, however the Planning Board has the authority to adopt these amendments.

Salerno reviewed several slides to review what contents of the regulations, and that the purpose is mainly to streamline processes, including unified development review, minor land development projects and subdivisions, public hearing notice requirements, application checklists, changes to plans, administrative waivers, plan review and approvals, and appeals.

Salerno then reviewed the flow charts provided by RIHousing for municipalities to help Boards understand the approval processes, including aspects such as:

# ➢ **Comprehensive Permit.**

- o Preapplication is optional, the review stages are now preliminary and final only.
- o Integrates administrative and board approvals, and a waiver process is codified. There are two tracks depending on the waivers.
- o Construction must start within 12 months and vested for 2 years.
- o Updated process for changes, including modification allowed at any stage, and applies to modification of an approved plan. Must be determined if a minor or major change, at the discretion of the Administrative Officer, major changes which go before the Planning Board.

# ➢ **Development Plan Review.**

- o Administrative or formal review (administrative review has one stage where formal has both preliminary and final stages).
- o The municipalities have the choice of permitting authority, which in Barrington's case is the Planning Board.
- o Change in use without construction is lower threshold.
- o Unified development review requires a public hearing.
- o Final plan review delegated to Administrative Officer and modification requests are submitted to the Zoning Officer.

Assistant Solicitor Goins noted the difference between development plan review and land development projects/subdivision review. Land development project review is two or more uses on one parcel, subdivision is dividing parcels, and development plan review is considered review of byright projects that are not categorized as a land development project or subdivision. Unlike land development projects and subdivisions, the State does not set forth exactly what the municipalities should review under development plan review and is an optional procedure. Assistant Solicitor Goins noted that she does not know of municipalities without it, however, it is not a requirement. Assistant Solicitor Goins clarified with Chair Phipps that the Board may apply reasonable conditions to development plan review decisions.

Salerno then review land development projects/subdivisions:

# ➢ **Minor Land Development Project and Subdivision.**

- o Preapplication option, review stages are preliminary and final.
- o Unified development review applies if variances or special use permits needed.
- o There is both an administrative and Planning Board review lane.
- o Modification requests submitted to the Zoning Officer.
- o Administrative Officer or Technical Review Committee may render approval.
- o Approval expires within one year and validity may be extended upon approval from the Planning Board.
- o Modification allowed at any stage.

Member Simms asked about the "failure to act" route. Assistant Solicitor Goins explained that route is when staff or the Board does not meet a required deadline that approves an application by default, however, Barrington has not run into this and is not likely to occur.

Member Godfrey asked about procedure for a complete zone change, noting the 33 Middle Highway property as an example. Assistant Solicitor Goins responded that the precedent of approvals is a section within the development review act was not revised, which outlines the process

- 1. Conditional approval from the Planning Board on master plan with recommendation to the Town Council.
- 2. The Town Council then reviews the proposed zone change.
- 3. If passed by the Council, the application then returns to the Planning Board for preliminary plan.

Assistant Solicitor Goins confirmed that with 33 Middle Highway, assuming an application will be submitted in 2024, it will likely be submitted as a major land development project, and thus would go through all three review stages of, and that the Town Council and Planning Board public hearings would be separate matters. Assistant Solicitor Goins she will provide the precedent of approvals section from the State regulations for the Boards reference.

# ➢ **Major Land Development Project and Subdivision.**

- o Three stages of approval (master plan, preliminary plan, and final plan) with public hearings required at master plan and preliminary plan.
- o Modification allowed at any stage submitted to the Zoning Officer.
- o Variances and special use permit requests trigger unified development review.
- o Approvals expire within a year and validity may be extended.
- o Changes to the plans are allowed at any stage, determined as either minor or major.

Vice Chair Rua asked about the difference between minor and major changes. Friedrichs noted that will be a policy choice to be discussed and acted on by the Board.

Friedrichs then discussed the changes within the Land Development and Subdivision Regulations that were made between the November 25 and 30 versions.

- ➢ **200-13:** Language change to allow the Technical Review Committee to review the preapplication meetings, along with the Planning Board. Friedrichs asked the Board if they would like to continue to hear pre-application meetings or rely on the Technical Review Committee. The Board then discussed commented on the following:

Friedrichs and Board Members then moved to overall changes:

- ➢ **200-4:** Update to the statement of purposes to conform to the Enabling Act.
- ➢ **200-7:** Member Bacher asked about the Technical Review Committee, noting that State law notes the Administrative Officer is to Chair the TRC. Town Planner Crean noted she will consult with counsel.

- ➢ **200-39(B):** Friedrichs reviewed the suggested changes and rationale. Assistant Solicitor Goins referred to it as a "zombie plan," i.e. a once approved but expired plan coming back. Assistant Solicitor Goins noted the three years is best practice suggested by Libra Planners. Member Bacher commented that he likes the one year. Member Simms concurred, commenting that a lot can change over the span of three years. Vice Chair Rua clarified with Assistant Solicitor Goins that it could be denied with zoning designation and/or Comprehensive Plan changes. Member J. Mueller noted the financial feasibility of an organization may be a factor for delay, and asked if that has been considered. - ➢ **200-41(D)(5):** Member Bacher asked about a revision to the language to make a complete sentence, and Friedrichs noted the revision was made.
- ➢ **200-51(C)(1):** Member Bacher asked about the wellhead/aquifer Town Planner Crean noted that will be removed/revisited in a later review with the Comprehensive Plan rewrite.

- ➢ **200-41:** Recommendation from Libra Planners to add subsection (C)(4) and (D)(6).

Member Godfrey asked about what happens if the property is flipped and if that would be considered a major change. Assistant Solicitor Goins responded that approvals run with the land. Assistant Solicitor Goins noted when an application is certified complete, they have the right to move forward with the zoning in effect at that time. - ➢ **200-91(A)(1) and (2):** Development Plan Review, administrative versus formal review, and some proposed projects for each. The Board had no comments and agreed to leave language as is.
