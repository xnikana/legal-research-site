## **Barrington Planning Board**

## **Notes for Minutes of Meeting – December 2023**

Hi Isabelle, Teresa and Roni:

Congratulations on a constructive meeting on Tuesday. We covered a lot of ground. I just want to confirm my comments.

- 1. Suggested Additions to Checklists Existing, Larry's and the MTAP
  - a. Building Types and Ownership Types –Number of buildings with each building identified on site plan, number of units in each building; the number of bedrooms and bathrooms in each unit, building heights, width and depths. For each unit, identify whether it will be a rental, condominium or single-family fee simple.
  - b. Affordable Housing Plan –For each unit, set forth the income affordability target, the number of bedrooms, and whether it is a rental, condominium or single-family fee simple.
  - c. Demonstrated Capacity of Development Team to Complete the Project Include ownership structure and principals, contractor, architect, financial advisor and project attorney and experience of each on projects of similar scale and complexity and expected sources of financing.

NOTE: One item that was mentioned in the discussions but not specifically indicated was a financial analysis of the proposal. This is normally required under a comp plan permit but would not be necessary if the proposal was an allowable use under the zoning ordinance. However, because the request to change the zoning seems purely motivated by the owner's desire to maximize a return on the property, it seems reasonable to request the financial documentation that would be required for a comp permit. Maximizing an owner's return is not a public purpose or mentioned in the comprehensive plan. Otherwise the town may be best served by maintaining the zoning as is. The owner may argue that the current zoning eliminates any productive use of the land. They should have to prove this.

- 2. Suggested Additional "Agency Comments" on Larry's checklist for 33 Middle Highway
  - a. Town Manager
  - b. Solicitor
- 3. Additional Consultants Recommended for 33 Middle Highway
  - a. Affordable Housing Consultant to analyze whether the proposal addresses the affordable housing needs of Barrington and the State of Rhode Island, not simply the statutory definition of affordable housing.
  - b. Project finance expert to analyze whether the numbers proposed by the owner/developer are feasible from a development cost and market perspective and likely not to generate excess profits to the detriment of the town. We may also need that expert to support the allowed zoning, whether it is R-10 or other use that complies with the comprehensive plan. In making a recommendation, it will require the Planning

Board to balance a reasonable return on the land versus the town's comprehensive plan. We may also need an appraiser to provide a market value on the land and, combined with the project finance expert's advice as to a reasonable rate of return, determine an appropriate development zone for the site.

- 4. 33 Middle Highway Process Memo. Please add the timelines for actions including:
  - a. 45 Days from submission for Planning Board to make a Recommendation to Town Council on the proposed Zone Change (if requested)
  - b. 65 Days from Submission for Town Council to have a public hearing on zoning change
  - c. 45 Days from Town Council Public Hearing to rule on Zone Change request
  - d. Timeline for Planning Board to render decision on Major Land Development as it relates to recommendation to Town Council and Zoning Decision separate from the recommendation on the zone change considering the Precedence of Approval Statute
- 5. Example of Density Bonus Calculation for 33 Middle Highway (which I stated verbally at the meeting)
  - a. Assumptions are that Planning Board Recommends and Town Council Approves Change of Zoning to R-10 (4 units per acre) and there are 30 developable acres
  - b. 30 x 4 =120 units
  - c. 25% of 120 = 30 affordable units
  - d. Density bonus of 2 for 1 (2 additional units for each affordable unit) = 60
  - e. Total units would be 120 + 60 = 180 (6 per acre)
