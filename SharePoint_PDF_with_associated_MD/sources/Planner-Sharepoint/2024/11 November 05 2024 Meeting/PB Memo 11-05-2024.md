# <span id="page-1-3"></span>**4.1 Discuss and Act: Amendments to Chapter 185: Zoning Ordinance (recommendation to Town Council)**

### <span id="page-1-4"></span>**Background & Process**

In June 2024, the General Assembly passed a package of land-use related amendments to State law, requiring an update to the Town's Zoning Ordinance to be consistent and compliant. Like the legislative updates of 2023, this legislation from the State is intended to reduce barriers to housing. The memorandum from the Town Solicitor's office was distributed earlier in the summer outlining these changes - the memo titled ["Recently Enacted Laws Affecting Land Use" dated June 24, 2024](https://twnbarrintonri.sharepoint.com/:b:/s/BoardandCommissionPacketLibrary/EXQUxiuFGMFDpgMvm5BqdgsBIknoMRrihdnMEHYYke-5TA?e=eLmNSb) is in the Board's packet materials for reference.

This is a two-step process:

- ➢ Planning Board to review and recommend amendments to the Town Council.
- ➢ Town Council to hold a public hearing and formally adopt.

The updates will also include amendments to Chapter 200: Land Development & Subdivision Regulations, however, this will be done following Zoning Ordinance amendments, and not part of this meeting's review/scope.

#### <span id="page-1-5"></span>**Amendments at a Glance**

- ➢ **[Accessory Dwelling Units \(ADUs\):](https://twnbarrintonri.sharepoint.com/:b:/s/BoardandCommissionPacketLibrary/EexjpfeLJ6RIv44Y8ivthUsBPqT7kXNFhZjfp9lfV0FX_g?e=e3BZrP)** to reflect new state law. Law is already in effect.
- ➢ **[Inclusionary Zoning:](https://twnbarrintonri.sharepoint.com/:b:/s/BoardandCommissionPacketLibrary/ERM-rHM5Wg5EkHpjzi7AMFIBzl_-hi8zMVO-70nagBa76A?e=uYN4Zr)** density bonus and inclusionary threshold rolled back from what was enacted January 2024. The law takes effect January 1, 2025.
- ➢ **[Substandard lots:](https://twnbarrintonri.sharepoint.com/:b:/s/BoardandCommissionPacketLibrary/EU0Ho8iXY7JGmJx-oe75GhsBsDw6mRv2ASRlnCXjdAMgXA?e=4gLSCW)** Clarifies how the building bonus is applied and revision to remove footnote 9 from the dimensional regulations table of zero minimum lot sizes. Law is already in effect.
- ➢ **[Development Plan Review:](https://twnbarrintonri.sharepoint.com/:b:/s/BoardandCommissionPacketLibrary/EVnHmjlBYsNBtaLbXvrf4ewB3R8n2vSW31Oh2njfi3xAvA?e=SvnLVG)** to reflect new law related to Development Plan Review (DPR). The regulations amendments (part of the next steps) will also address DPR. Law is already in effect.

### <span id="page-1-6"></span>**Consistency with the Comprehensive Plan (RIGL 45-24-52)**

- ➢ Consistent with the Comprehensive Plan because the Town ordinance needs to comply with the State law.
- ➢ Goal HN-1: Expand supply of senior housing to meet growing demand of retiring Baby Boomers and the needs of residents with disabilities.
  - o Action C notes Zoning Ordinance revisions for attached and detached "accessory apartments" on single-family lots. Terminology has evolved from accessory apartments to accessory dwelling units.

![](PB Memo 11-05-2024_attachments/_page_1_Picture_21.jpeg)

- ➢ Policy HN-2.1.1: Pursue Zoning Ordinance amendments and other strategies that encourage compatible scale and building massing.
  - o The substandard lot law from the State is an effort to afford more opportunities / reduce obstacles to development for being a substandard lot in a subject property's given zoning district.
- ➢ Goal HN-3: Continue to comply with the State's 10 percent affordable housing goal through provision of well-integrated and compatible affordable housing.
  - o Inclusionary Zoning continues to be part of the Town's Zoning Ordinance as a tool to encourage affordable housing in development. The amendments to the Inclusionary Zoning article reflect the new requirements at the State level for municipalities that have Inclusionary Zoning on the books.

<span id="page-2-2"></span>**4.2.1 Discuss and Act: Schedule a special meeting for Work Session re: Housing Chapter.**

Per discussion at the October 1, 2024 Planning Board meeting, staff are working to get a draft of the housing & neighborhoods chapter of the Comprehensive Plan for Planning Board review prior to sending to the Division of Statewide Planning for comment.

The Housing Board of Trustees reviewed the document at their October 16, 2024 meeting with the Director of Planning, Herb Durfee, in attendance. The Housing [Chapter Draft along with the Housing](https://twnbarrintonri.sharepoint.com/:b:/s/BoardandCommissionPacketLibrary/EUsLa1GOfCJChqSBzF7OZToBLSIjLwXhamjpdnmlY_umcw?e=ew4zHz) Board's comments [and recommendations](https://twnbarrintonri.sharepoint.com/:b:/s/BoardandCommissionPacketLibrary/EUsLa1GOfCJChqSBzF7OZToBLSIjLwXhamjpdnmlY_umcw?e=ew4zHz) are in the packet materials.

Given the agenda for the regular meeting and to allow the Board proper time to review, the request of staff is for the Planning Board to schedule a Work Session to gather group consensus and comments, with a focus on the goals and objectives, to send to Stantec for incorporation prior to sending the draft for review to the Division of Statewide Planning.

The Comprehensive Plan Steering Committee will also be extended the invitation to the Work Session when scheduled.
