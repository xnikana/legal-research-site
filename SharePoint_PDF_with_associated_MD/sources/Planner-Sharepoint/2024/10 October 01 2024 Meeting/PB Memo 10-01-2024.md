The following Committees and Departments have provided recommendations:

#### **1. Town Manager**

<span id="page-3-0"></span>The applicant team met several times with Planning Staff over 2024 to work to get their application certified complete, one of these meetings involving Town Manager / former Town Planner Phil Hervey.

The original concepts including parking in the front, which was not found to be ideal as it goes against the parking requirements in the Zoning Ordinance, and the second included removal of a Town light pole, which was found to be burdensome/costly for both the applicant and the Town. To find a solution that worked, the Town Manager suggested the current driveway configuration presented in the preliminary plat. Though it creates two curb cuts, contrary to the [Curb Cut Standards](https://ecode360.com/44745113#44745113) (200-51.2) in the Land Development & Subdivision Regulations, this solution of dual driveways avoids costs of having to relocate a townowned light post.

Under the [Waivers section](https://ecode360.com/44744918#44744918) (200-38(B)) of the Land Development & Subdivision Regulations, "the permitting authority may modify design standards as contained in Article IX (which includes the Curb Cut Standards) of these regulations, and required improvements… due to special conditions of the land or other features of the subdivision… and such modification is not contrary to the intent or purpose of these regulations." It is staff's opinion that it is not contrary to the intent/purpose of the regulations, as the same section notes to "utilize a shared drive with adjacent parcels when appropriate," which, given the unique circumstances of this lot with the light post, is an appropriate solution. The Department of Public Works did not address the proposed shared driveway as a concern in their review.

Given the fact that this component was vetted in depth by staff and the Design/Technical Review Committee, and the Bicycle-Pedestrian Advisory Committee's (BPAC) meeting schedule in the timeline, staff did not find it appropriate to refer this application to the BPAC.

#### **5. Planning, Building, and Resilience**

<span id="page-4-2"></span>Planning Staff recommend approval of the application, upon the following conditions / notes.

- ➢ For Requirement (i) of the survey plan requirements: Zoning Setback lines and analysis of the lot building coverage, surface parking areas, and other impervious surfaces).
  - o Per [185-79\(2\) of the Zoning Ordinance,](https://ecode360.com/7121372#7121372) because the property abuts a Residence District in the rear, a five-foot-wide landscaped strip containing an opaque fence of four to six feet or a double row compact evergreen screen of at least four feet shall be maintained between the Residence District and the parking facility proposed."
  - o A ten-foot-wide buffer is depicted on the "Preliminary Plat" by InSite Engineering (revised September 10, 2024) but **does not** indicate whether it is to be a fence or evergreen screen.

![](PB Memo 10-01-2024_attachments/_page_4_Figure_16.jpeg)

- o **STAFF RECOMMENDATION: Planning Board to indicate preference of fence or evergreen screen as condition of approval.** Design Review Committee did not indicate preference. At that meeting, applicant indicated there is an existing fence there, though not indicated on the plan.
- ➢ For requirement (n) of the survey plan requirements: Identification of areas of special concern Wellhead Protection Area.
  - o **STAFF RECOMMENDATION: The applicant does NOT need to bring this before the Conservation Commission**. Design Review Committee concurred.
  - o Staff have come to this opinion due to the following:
    - [Chapter 200-98\(B\)\(2\) of the Land Development and Subdivision Regulations](https://ecode360.com/44745362?highlight=wellhead&searchId=4552435734687322#44745361) requires written confirmation from the Barrington Conservation Commission that the plans of the proposed development provide sufficient measures to protect groundwater quality for a subject parcel that lies within the wellhead area, aquifer, or aquifer recharge. **HOWEVER:**
      - [Comprehensive Plan Goal NCR-1, Objective NCR-1.2, Action F](https://barrington.ri.gov/DocumentCenter/View/1371/5_NaturalCulturalResources_CompPlan_2015Update?bidId=) reads "eliminate the required advisory review of projects within the Groundwater Overlay District." Though the Land Development and Subdivision Regulations were never amended to comply with the reference above, the Comprehensive Plan is what ultimately oversees/guides both the Zoning Ordinance and Land Development and Subdivision Regulations.
      - In addition, in the spirit of state legislation in 2023 and 2024 with the goal to reduce barriers to housing, staff believe that an additional committee review is an additional barrier for a Comprehensive Permit application.
      - At their August 8, 2023 meeting, the Conservation Commission reviewed a proposal from 173R Maple Avenue, within 1,000 feet of 88 Maple Avenue, recommending approval to the plans as submitted / no additional concerns. This approval being within proximity to 88 Maple Avenue, staff believe the recommendation from 173R Maple can be applied here without the 88 Maple Avenue applicant team being required to attend another town meeting.
