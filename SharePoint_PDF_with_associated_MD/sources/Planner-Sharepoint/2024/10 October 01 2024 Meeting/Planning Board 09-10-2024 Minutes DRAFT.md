### **4. Continued Public Hearing**

**4.1 Discuss and Act: Major Change Request for an approved preliminary plan decision – Book 1401, Page 227 in the Town's Land Evidence Records – at 3 College Lane – Assessor's Plat 15, Lot 84, R-10 District. The major change requests to change item #5 in the "Findings of Fact" to allow the provision of affordable housing on the existing house instead of the newly created lot should the lot be further subdivided, per 200-41(D) in the Land Development and Subdivision Regulations (continued from August 6, 2024 Meeting).** 

*Present: Helen Judge, applicant*

*Frank Judge, authorized agent for the applicant*

*John Garrahy, attorney for the applicant*

*Melina Lodge, Housing Network of Rhode Island / Community Housing Land Trust of Rhode Island* 

*(serving as agent for the Town)*

Gillibrand provided an overview. Garrahy explained how he was contacted by the applicant following the August 6, 2024 meeting and provided additional information to the request, including how long the applicant has been in the home and their desire to stay in the home. Lodge provided details on the role of a monitoring agent, the obstacle of the 2014 decision's concept of "an affordable lot" and the challenge of getting that to an affordable unit, and the process of deed restriction. Sample income certification documentation was distributed to the Board.

Garrahy asked about income verification, and Chair Phipps noted that was not appropriate. Garrahy concurred. Lodge and Assistant Solicitor Goins confirmed that the Board's approval for making the existing dwelling affordable is dependent on income verification of the homeowner and deed restriction, which happens administratively. The Board confirmed why the applicant must qualify for low-to-moderate income (LMI) housing, and it was clarified that with the deed restriction the occupant is required to qualify. The Board asked if the sale of the market lot would affect eligibility, and Lodge confirmed a market valuation is needed and is accounted in the income verification at 1 percent. The Board and Lodge discussed assets valuation and income verification further. Assistant Solicitor Goins discussed LMI threshold and how it relates to the 2014 decision.

The Board asked more about the occupancy requirements should/when the current owner is no longer in the house, and Lodge explained the process further about how the clock resets on the deed restriction with change of ownership. The Board discussed how this type of application sets a precedent, and Assistant Solicitor Goins responded it does not set a precedent, how decisions run with the land, and this is an example of capturing existing houses as affordable. Assistant Solicitor Goins noted 30 years is typically the default. The Board discussed term further and the case for extending the term beyond 30 years. Garrahy and the Board asked about conditions of approval.

*The Chair opened the public hearing.* 

Shannon Kramarz, 1 Elizabeth Road, asked if this is a land hardship or financial hardship, if there a plan for frontage, and occupancy clarification, and expressed parking, traffic, and runoff concern. Garrahy responded that this request is not asking for any kind of relief and would otherwise be allowed if the 2014 decision was not in place.

*The Chair closed the public hearing.* 

The Board discussed the term of the deed restriction further. After further discussion, the Board concurred with 40 years. The Board clarified the action being taken, and Assistant Solicitor Goins confirmed they are approving a major change to an approved preliminary plan decision, assuming the 2014 subdivision satisfies findings of fact, and if any additional conditions on the term of the deed restriction. Garrahy noted the only thing being affected in the affordable housing provision and do not need to re-satisfy the 2014 findings of fact, and Assistant Solicitor Goins concurred.

**MOTION: Member Kilmartin seconded by Member \_Rua\_, moved to approved the proposed major change to item #5 of the Findings of Fact for the Preliminary Plan Decision Book 1401, Page 227 in the Town's Land Evidence Records – allowing the affordable** 

**housing unit to be on the lot containing the existing house rather than the new lot, with the discussed change of a 40-year term on a deed restriction, and conditioned on the approval of the subdivision application. Motion carried unanimously.**
