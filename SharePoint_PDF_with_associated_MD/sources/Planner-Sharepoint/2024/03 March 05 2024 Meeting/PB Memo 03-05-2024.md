## **7.2 Open Meetings Discussion, including Planning Board roles and responsibilities and communications.**

There have been miscellaneous questions from Board members related to Planning Board role/responsibilities, communications, and the like. Though staff anticipate some of these questions will be discussed and addressed as part of the by-laws item, a separate agenda item has been added to capture further discussion if needed/wanted by the Board.

The Planning Board is outlined in [Chapter 37 of the Town Code.](https://ecode360.com/7118788)

Summarized from the code (fully explained and outlined in § 37-7 **[Powers and duties.](https://ecode360.com/7118793#7118793)**):

- A. **Comprehensive Plan.** Prepare, adopt, and review, including holding public hearings on the Plan and/or elements (chapters) of the plan, and amending plan and chapters as deemed necessary.
- B. **Studies, plans, reports.** Review, recommend (example Complete Streets Plan, which was reviewed by the Planning Board prior to 2022 adoption by the Town Council).
- C. **Capital Improvement Plan.** Shall prepare an annual capital budget and six-year Capital Improvement Program.
- D. **Land Development and Subdivision Regulations.** Shall adopt, modify, and amend, in accordance with Chapter 200.
- E. **Advisory opinions and recommendations as referred by the Town Council.**
- F. **Authority to call upon other departments, boards, committees, and officials of the Town and upon regional, state, and federal agencies for information and assistance in the performance of duties and shall cooperate with such.**
- G. **Other duties as assigned.**

![](PB Memo 03-05-2024_attachments/_page_5_Picture_21.jpeg)

There have been several members that have asked questions related to ex-parte communications. Included in the packet materials is an [excerpt on ex-parte communications](https://twnbarrintonri-my.sharepoint.com/:b:/g/personal/igillibrand_barrington_ri_gov/EZlPLvaMKLdCrFPFXrBFQhQB2AhiWqIiw1hKJXHu1hGbNg?e=ZhpeRe) from a 2022 RI Municipal Board and Commissions Volunteers Workshop titled "Conducting Effective Public Meetings."

Assistant Solicitor Goins has provided the following explanation, and staff have added this as an agenda item should the Board wish to engage in further discussion:

"The rule is that Board members should consider the evidence at the hearing (application, testimony) and can consider their own personal knowledge of the area/neighborhood or anything else that is relevant. However, you should not solicit opinions from anyone on the application. And if someone offers an opinion to you (example: chance encounter at a coffee shop), you should encourage them to submit their comments in writing to the whole [Planning Board] and/or attend the meeting and testify. It is sometimes difficult to tell people you can't speak to them outside of the public hearing, but you can frame it as 'your comments are important and the whole [Planning Board] should have the opportunity to consider them.' All the decisions of the [Planning Board] are based on the evidence in the record as well as your personal knowledge of the area (for example, through site visits before the meeting, or your own experience in the neighborhood)."
