## **Ethical Behavior**

Ex Parte Communications - Ex parte communications are of particular concern when you are conducting a land use review. An ex parte communication happens when three things occur

1

A public official is contacted by an individual or group with an interest in the outcome of an application

2

A public official is outside of a public meeting

3

There is an attempt to influence or secure a public official's vote or opinion.

Ex parte communications can be difficult to identify – <u>be very wary</u> – avoid even the APPEARANCE of impropriety.

## **Ethical Behavior -** *Ex Parte* **Communications**

## **SCENARIO: You encounter a neighbor during site inspection**

You are a member of the zoning board, which is considering a controversial variance request. You stop by the property on Saturday morning to look at it. As you are standing by your car at the edge of the property, the next-door neighbor approaches you, says that he can't be at the hearing, and begins to give you his point of view about the project.

*RESPONSE - instruct the individual to submit their comments in writing to the planning office so that they can be included as part of the record.*

## **Ethical Behavior -** *Ex Parte* **Communications**

**SCENARIO:** You are a member of the planning board, which is scheduled to decide on proposed comprehensive plan amendment requested by a developer for a senior assisted living facility to be built. You have a chance meeting in a grocery store:

"*Susan, you're on the planning board. I've been reading in the paper about that proposed assisted living facility off Green Street. I think it sounds like something we really need here in town. What do you think*?"

![](Ex Parte Comms Excerpt - Resource_attachments/_page_2_Picture_3.jpeg)

*RESPONSE: Inform the individual that you cannot discuss the application outside of the meeting. Tell them to contact the planning office for the date, time and location of the hearing on the project.*
