# **4.2 Discuss and Act: Comprehensive Permit Process and Procedure Training (A. Goins).**

Assistant Solicitor Goins reviewed the purpose along with recent changes and amendments for Comprehensive Permits, noting the Low-to-Moderating (LMI) Housing Act was originally enacted to increase affordable housing stock in Rhode Island, and aims to do so by providing streamlining and "one-stop shopping" for requested relief, and provides one set of standards for all requested relief.

Assistant Solicitor Goins reviewed requirements of Comprehensive Permits, including that these applications must be reviewed by the Planning Board, and how a Comprehensive Permit is only allowed if 25% of the development is deed-restricted affordable. Assistant Solicitor Goins noted that inclusionary zoning also requires 25% affordable housing, however, each has their own process and are different tracks.

Assistant Solicitor Goins reviewed how Comprehensive Permits used to be a three-step process; however, as of January 1, 2024, the Master Plan step has been eliminated. This means there are only Preliminary and Final stages, there is one public hearing at the Preliminary stage, and the developer needs to have more details earlier to begin the process.

The single-most significant amendment, according to Assistant Solicitor Goins, is that there were previously no numbers laid out regarding the density bonus. Now, the State has set floors that the Planning Board must provide bonuses for Comprehensive Permit applications. Assistant Solicitor Goins noted that any questions and ambiguities are likely to be reviewed by the Court with the lens of "what was the purpose of the act?" For calculating the density bonus, Assistant Solicitor Goins reviewed that it is her interpretation the bonus is calculated on the existing base zoning (i.e. what can be built by right in the existing zone).

The Board discussed how the financials pan out for a developer, and confirmed that Comprehensive Permits are separate from inclusionary zoning. Assistant Solicitor Goins noted the bonus can be viewed as both a floor and a ceiling. Member Godfrey commented that with developers he has talked to, developers are unable to get the numbers to work due to rising costs.

Assistant Solicitor Goins noted how parking minimums, bedrooms, and floor-area were also amended in the act. The Board discussed the definition of "single-family," noting how there is room for interpretation.

Assistant Solicitor Goins noted that the general process for Comprehensive Permits is as follows:

- ➢ **Concept Review/Preapplication:** determined by the Administrative Officer (with the exception of the anticipated 33 Middle Highway project, which the Planning Board made a motion at their January 9 meeting to be the reviewer of that preapplication when submitted.
- ➢ **Preliminary Plan:** the stage where notice is given, and a public hearing is held. Decision must be made within 90 days from certification of completeness. Assistant Solicitor Goins then reviewed the standards for approval, including:
  - o Consistent with local needs, as identified in the Comp Plan, with emphasis on Affordable Housing Plan. Member Godfrey asked about the definition of "local needs."
  - o That adjustments granted do not outweigh the state and local need for LMI housing. The Board and Assistant Solicitor Goins discussed the definition of "adjustments" and the Board looking at applications with an affordable housing lens.
  - o Affordable units must be integrated throughout the development. Assistant Solicitor Goins noted that the "integrated" definition has been developed through limited case law).
  - o Health, safety, and compatibility with the Community.
  - o Physical access to a public street.
  - o The proposed development will not create lots with physical constraints that would make development impractical.

Assistant Solicitor Goins then reviewed required findings for denial, noting it is very difficult to deny applications. There was Board discussion on the "environmental concerns" item and state permits. The recommendation from Assistant Solicitor Goins was for the Board to issue a conditional approval upon receiving state approvals. Assistant Solicitor Goins confirmed that a denial must fit under one of the required findings for denial. The Board and Assistant Solicitor Goins reviewed vesting and extensions.

- ➢ **Final Plan:** stage is now to be done administratively. The Planning Board may only review the final plan if there are waivers from checklist items.
- ➢ **Appeal process with Superior Court.**

The Board and Assistant Solicitor Goins noted there can be some discretion over income ranges for the affordable housing units, however, must be tied to the Comprehensive Plan. The Board and Assistant Solicitor Goins then discussed pro forma requirements and financial feasibility.
