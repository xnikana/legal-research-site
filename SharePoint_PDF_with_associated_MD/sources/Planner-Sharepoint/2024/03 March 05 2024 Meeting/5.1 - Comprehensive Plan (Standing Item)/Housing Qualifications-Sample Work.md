### **CITY OF MENTOR HOUSING ANALYSIS**

![](Housing Qualifications-Sample Work_attachments/_page_2_Picture_3.jpeg)

### **MENTOR, OHIO**

Stantec is currently assisting the City of Mentor, OH, with a city-wide housing analysis to identify the gap between the existing demand and current supply of housing. The Stantec methodology incorporates multiple proprietary and public data sources (including ReplicaHQ, PUMS, Warren Group, scraping Padmapper and Apartments.com, CoStar, etc.) to capture the most current picture possible of the housing needs for a community. By comparing the demand demographics with the supply inventory of unit types and cost levels where shortages and surpluses are present, we are then able to identify the shortages—which are often the opportunities for new development, both market rate and affordable. This highly detailed level of analysis allows us to develop an action oriented housing strategy toolbox focused on resolving identified needs.

## **FLEMINGTON COMMUNITY ACTION PLAN**

![](Housing Qualifications-Sample Work_attachments/_page_4_Picture_13.jpeg)

### **FLEMINGTON, NEW JERSEY**

Working with a coalition of local and regional partners, we led a community visioning and consensus building process to establish a shared vision and set of priorities for Flemington Borough's downtown revitalization. Recommendations focused on actionable projects and policies that can be achieved in the following 1-3 years to build significant momentum in new housing development, placemaking, neighborhood walkability, and other concerns critical to positioning Flemington as a destination for today's and tomorrow's knowledge workers, creatives, and entrepreneurs. The project included a strong focus on understanding the housing market and how to channel significant potential for new development into the cornerstone of the revitalization action plan. This effort included building community support for higher density housing in downtown neighborhoods, identifying locations where development would be most appropriate and have the greatest economic impact, and incorporating meaningful affordability into the housing development program.

As a concluding task, we collaborated directly with a housing developer to translate the plan's recommendations into a mixed-income development plan for one of Flemington's most important sites. The development's first phase is currently navigating the approvals process and should begin construction in 2023.

## **LARIMER HOUSING PLAN**

![](Housing Qualifications-Sample Work_attachments/_page_6_Picture_3.jpeg)

#### **PITTSBURGH, PENNSYLVANIA**

Thae Larimer Consensus Group (LCG), a local community organization, looked to update their community plan and design guidelines in the face of increased development and fears of gentrification. In 2022, LCG wanted to prepare for increased development interest, but a prior community plan lacked specificity on the steps to take to secure publiclyowned housing lots (nearly 800 vacant, city-owned properties) and expedite their construction. LCG was also concerned that in their eagerness to uplift their community that they may lose their identity or displace existing long-term residents.

To counteract these concerns, we worked with local outreach and urban design consultant, MonWin Consulting, to execute a community-group led public process to identify what was worth keeping from previous plans and to understand where they were insufficient. We then helped the LCG board to craft an implementation plan that identified many different ways of developing vacant properties within their communities, focusing on the "who" first.

This plan recommendation categorized properties that were perfect for individual owners to build or renovate starter homes, ones that were well-suited for small local developers or Larimer entrepreneurs, and 2 zones that were opportune for the community group to partner with a larger builder. In this way, there was a path for many Larimer residents to share in the effort and the equity in their community.

At the same time, we created a set of design guidelines that were graphic and focused on the critical components of typical Larimer streets and houses. We created a scoring sheet to help the community members give constructive feedback to potential developers/designers. The guidelines focused more on building houses that "fit in" to the Larimer fabric, while allowing innovation in construction and material choices.

## **WATERTOWN COMPREHENSIVE PLAN**

**WATERTOWN, MA | 2022-2023**

### **PROJECT DESCRIPTION**

Watertown, a growing city of 35,000 people just outside Boston, had successfully completed many of the actions from its prior comprehensive plan. It needed a new plan built on robust community engagement to address emerging opportunities and challenges, and turned to Stantec's Urban Places to guide them.

The City is using this comprehensive planning process to revisit prior goals and identify any changes in direction. Watertown's recent growth has met with mixed reactions—some in support of new jobs and housing reactivating former industrial or retail sites, while others opposed to perceived changes in character or traffic. While the Town's traditional downtown, Watertown Square, is beloved by many, it has seen better days as small businesses struggle.

Stantec provided community outreach and engagement, baseline conditions assessment, strategy development, and implementation planning. Our in-person and online engagement encouraged people to participate in whatever ways were most convenient for them. Stantec's interdisciplinary team worked together to highlight the connections between land use, transportation, economic development, and community wellness so that people had a better sense of the big picture and how strategies complemented each other. Change is happening, but the comprehensive plan describes how to channel that growth to achieve shared community benefits. We also worked closely with City departments to create an implementation plan that they supported and felt is both ambitious and achievable.

Phil Schaeffing is Project Manager, Steve Kearney is PIC, and Alison LeFlore and Ralph DeNisco are supporting team members The plan was adopted in Fall 2023.

## **ATTLEBORO DOWNTOWN ACTION STRATEGY**

**ATTLEBORO, MA | 2021**

Stantec worked with the City of Attleboro and MassDevelopment to create an actionable economic development strategy that could build upon ongoing activity and provide immediate benefits while building to longer-term projects. The study is organized around several catalytic development sites and a list of the "top ten" implementation strategies. These strategies leverage existing partnerships and activities to provide a big picture strategy for revitalizing Downtown Attleboro.

The City of Attleboro is strategically located along a commuter rail line between Boston, MA and Providence, RI. It has a charming Downtown noted for its historic buildings, riverwalk, beautiful parks, and various cultural amenities. It is also one of the more affordable locations along a major transit line in the region with a stop conveniently located in Downtown. However, much of the City's industry has relocated outside of the Downtown, leaving significant land holdings ripe for redevelopment. This redevelopment has already started – there have been a host of new residential and mixed-use infill developments around the train station and more are planned. The Downtown Attleboro Action Strategy seeks to capitalize on these ongoing activities to further revitalize Downtown Attleboro and attract more businesses, residents, and visitors back to Downtown.

We conducted a market analysis that included local and regional market demand and customer characteristics. The recommendations identified by the Downtown Attleboro Action Strategy were informed by the market and demographic analysis as well as stakeholder engagement and public outreach.

## Mesquite Housing Data Assessment

City of Mesquite, TX | 2023

This housing data assessment examines current and future housing needs in Mesquite, Texas. It provides an analysis of employment and commuting patterns to understand workforce housing demand. The assessment analyzes Mesquite's housing supply, costs, vacancy rates, and other housing metrics. It compares Mesquite to neighboring communities and the broader Dallas-Fort Worth region. The report finds that Mesquite has a shortage of affordable housing for low-income residents and inadequate ownership options for middle-income households.
