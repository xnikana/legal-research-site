## Belton Court

Project Overview March 5, 2024

![](Belton Court - Project Overview - DRAFT  WITH PAGE NUMBERS_attachments/_page_0_Picture_4.jpeg)

![](Belton Court - Project Overview - DRAFT  WITH PAGE NUMBERS_attachments/_page_0_Picture_6.jpeg)

Create a high-quality neighborhood that fosters community and meets the diverse housing needs of current and future residents of Barrington

![](Belton Court - Project Overview - DRAFT  WITH PAGE NUMBERS_attachments/_page_1_Picture_3.jpeg)

## **Design Guidelines**

- Traditional neighborhood development that is built on the following principles:
  - > Meets the housing needs of residents with diverse demographic and economic profiles
  - Open space concentrated in public areas
  - Promotes walkability and social engagement
  - > High quality infrastructure designed to address stormwater and other environmental concerns
  - Traditional architecture that stands the test of time
  - Incorporate latest energy efficient technological advances

![](Belton Court - Project Overview - DRAFT  WITH PAGE NUMBERS_attachments/_page_2_Picture_9.jpeg)

#### **Development Plan**

- Mixed use development containing +/- 350 residential units and up to 20,000 square feet of ancillary retail/commercial space
- Development plan represents a density of approximately 11 du per upland acre
- Residential units shall consist of:

- > The town of Barrington (the "Town") has expressed interest in potentially securing a permanent easement on +/- 1.5 acres of land for the renovation of the existing gymnasium into a town recreation center and supporting parking.
- Developer has indicated a willingness to provide the permanent easement described above at no cost to the extent, for zoning purposes, the land conveyed under the easement is considered open/recreational space and does not negatively impact the developer's ability to develop a similar number and mix of units as if the land were open/recreational space.
- To the extent the Town decides not to pursue the option noted above by a TBD date, the land would be programmed as additional amenity/greenspace (land plan may be revised to re-distribute portions of the additional greenspace throughout the neighborhood).

## **Benefits of Development Plan**

- Mix of housing types will further diversify the housing options available for current and future residents of Barrington
- Provides high quality housing options in an aesthetically pleasing neighborhood for households including, but not limited to:
  - Move down buyers/empty nesters
  - Senior residents
  - Young couples seeking to establish a foothold in Barrington
  - Workforce housing
- Significant contribution of affordable housing to help Barrington lessen the gap between its current 3% level and the 10% goal mandated by the state
- Land plan and quality of infrastructure promotes walkability and social interaction
- > Improved stormwater management system will benefit surrounding neighborhoods
- Portions of Belton Court will be preserved/renovated and integrated into the community as commercial and civic spaces open to public
- Provides Barrington with the ability to acquire and renovate a town rec center at a fraction of the cost of developing a new one (potentially resulting in a \$5 million - \$8 million cost saving)

## Required modifications to current zoning

Following is our preliminary understanding of the key modifications to the current Senior Residential Village Zoning District that would be required to accommodate the development of the project, as described in the previous slides.

- Section 185-224 K: Replace with "Retain historical or architectural features that define the visual character of the community such as the Tower at Belton Court and monumental landscaping such as the cedar hedge and stone wall along Middle Hwy.
- Section 185-226 A (1) and (2): Change density requirement to equal the number of dwelling units in the Development Plan (347 units) divided by the developable acreage, which would be re-defined as total acres less state classified wetlands and land in the 100-year floodplain (31.2 acres for the subject property)
- Section 185-226 A (3): To the extent the above modifications are made to section A(1) and (2), section A(3) could be eliminated
- Section 185-226 D (8): Change limitation for on–street parking from 30 feet from a corner to 30 feet from an intersection, measured from the center line.
- ➤ Section 185-226 D (9) and (10): Delete these sections
- Section 185-227 E: Insert the words "across the entire site" after The maximum building lot coverage

Additionally, other modifications and/or variances may be required to the extent they contradict the Development Guidelines that have been applied in the design of the Development Plan as presented in **Attachment A** to this document
