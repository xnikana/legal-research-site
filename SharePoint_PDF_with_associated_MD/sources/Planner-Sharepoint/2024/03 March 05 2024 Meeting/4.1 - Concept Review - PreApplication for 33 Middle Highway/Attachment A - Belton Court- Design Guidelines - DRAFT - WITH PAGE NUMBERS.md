# Belton Court Design Guidelines

## Definitions

The following pages contain detailed site regulations for Belton Court site. The terms defined here include elements that are regulated by the urban and architectural controls established by the Design Guidelines.

#### Regulating Plan

A map precisely locating the various zoning categories. The Regulating Plan also shows the form and location of the following plan elements as listed below.

#### Frontages

All frontages that are along a street or open space are Primary frontages, unless otherwise noted in the Regulating Plan.

Primary Frontage [A-Street]: A pedestrian-friendly street with active uses at grade, held to the highest standard of urban performance. Entrances to parking garages and service bays are prohibited, unless specified on the regulating plan. Curb cuts for alley access may be permitted, according to the Regulating Plan. An A-Street network forms a continuous loop through the thoroughfare network.

#### Retail Frontage

Some Frontages require further specification. The Regulating Plan prescribes where retail is required and where it is allowed but not required.

#### Setbacks

All Frontages are assigned Setbacks. Setbacks specify the build-to line of the building façade.

#### Building Height

Building Height is assigned by Building Type and specified on the Regulating Plan.

#### Towers

Towers, permitted to exceed maximum building height are located on the Regulating Plan and further regulated in the Design Standards and Guidelines.

#### Vista Termination

Locations where a street aims prominently at a building facade – called a Vista Termination, are identified. Buildings located at Vista Terminations shall respond with a building element of appropriate size and impact to terminate the vista in a meaningful manner.

#### Open Space

Locations where specific open space types have been calibrated, are identified.

#### Civic Building

Locations for Civic Structures are suggested in the Regulating Plan and are further restricted in size according to the Open Space Types.

#### Curb Cuts

Curb cuts are indicated on the Regulating Plan, and are permitted to be within a 50 foot range from where it is indicated in the Regulating Plan.

#### Thoroughfares

Thoroughfare assemblies are assigned in the Regulating Plan.

#### 1. Purpose

- 1.a. The purpose of these Design Standards and Guidelines are to:
  - Create a predictable framework for development that is in keeping with the Belton Court Master Plan.
  - Coordinate the placement, orientation and design of buildings to ensure a coherent, walkable streetscape within a new urban neighborhood.
  - Encourage pedestrian-friendly development with a variety of building types that contribute to a robust economic base and support diverse locally oriented businesses and community amenities and is in keeping with the Town's Comprehensive Plan.

#### 2. Applicability

- 2.a. In addition to zoning requirements, these Design Standards and Guidelines shall further guide the development of the Belton Court site.
- 2.b. Where conflicts arise, the most restrictive standard shall prevail.

#### 4. Civic Structures

- 4.a. The location of Civic Structures shall be as identified on the regulating plan.
- 4.b. Civic Structures are limited to one story and shall reflect the nature of its role on all street facing frontages.

#### 5. Thoroughfare Design

- 5.a. Thoroughfares shall be according to the regulating plan and the "Thoroughfare Assembly" on page 84.
- 5.b. Thoroughfares shall be in compliance with site-line requirements in Code XX and shall provide a clear zone in accordance with Public Works. (to be coordinated with zoning)

#### 6. Parking

#### 6.a. On-street Parking for private streets

- On-street parking shall count towards minimum parking requirements according to the following:
  - When on-street parking is striped, each striped space located directly along the prop- erty shall count towards the minimum parking requirement of that lot.
  - When on-street parking is not striped, parking is calculated at one space for every 25 feet located directly along the property and shall count towards the minimum parking requirement of that lot.

#### 6.b. Off-street Parking

#### • Placement

- Surface parking lots are prohibited. Exceptions include:
  - Parking for short term leases while permanent parking facilities are being constructed, up to a maximum of 12 months.
  - A maximum 1 row deep head-in parking with a single drive aisle may be provided within 100 feet of Middle Hwy.
  - If the parking is fully screen by habitable using along all sides.
- Structured parking is prohibited. Exceptions include:
  - The parking shall be lined by habitable uses a min 24 feet deep.
  - If not lined, the parking may be slightly elevated, at a maximum 3 feet. The exterior wall shall be detailed as a building face, in keeping with the adjoining building.

#### • Design

- Lots used for off-street parking shall meet the minimum frontage requirements and shall be setback a minimum 10 feet from the property line.
- Any parking structures shall be wholly screened by a building or liner building.
- Underground parking, if wholly underground, may be placed up to the property line.
- Walls screening parking at grade shall have small windows that align or otherwise coordinate with windows in upper stories, creating the perception of an inhabited ground floor.
- Access shall be according to the regulating plan and limited as follows:
  - Curb cuts and parking access shall be according to the regulating plan or accessed from rear alleys, where available.
  - Parking access lanes shall be a maximum of 10 feet in width per lane.

#### 7. Block Standards

- 7.a. Pedestrian connections and breaks in blocks shall be according to the regulating plan.
- 7.b. Block length shall not exceed 410 feet, exceptions include the following:
  - Perimeter blocks may exceed the maximum length if there is no further development beyond.
  - Blocks may exceed the maximum length so long as a pedestrian passage is provided at a minimum 250 foot interval.

#### 8. Frontages

- 8.a. Primary frontages shall be as specified in the regulating plan.
- 8.b. Frontage Occupation shall be according to "Table 1 Form Standards" and the following:
  - Secondary frontages shall be a minimum 65%.
  - Any portion of the lot, in the Center Zone, facing a public frontage, that is not occupied by a building or driveway shall be occupied by a streetscreen.
- 8.c. Frontage Yard Types
  - Frontage Yard Types shall be assigned according to the regulating plan and as defined by Table XX. (to be coordinated with zoning)
  - Where Frontages Yard Types are not assigned in the regulating plan, Frontage Yard Types may be chosen for each lot frontage.
- 8.d. Facade Types
  - Facade Types shall be assigned according to the regulating plan and as defined by Table XX. (to be coordinated with zoning)
  - Where Facade Types are not assigned in the regulating plan, Facade Types may be chosen for each lot frontage.
- 8.e. Fences & Walls shall be according to the following:
  - Vinyl, PVC, Chain-link, exposed concrete block, barbed wire and razor wire are prohibited.
- 9. Building Massing & Articulation
  - 9.a. Pedestrian Access
    - General to all:
      - Location:
        - The main pedestrian access for residential uses shall be from the primary frontage.
        - Pedestrian entrances shall be separate for commercial and residential uses.

#### - Design

- Office and retail shall have doors which are principally of a translucent material.
- Residential entries shall be detailed as private and shall be principally solid with minimal glazing. Exceptions include entries to lobby's which are staffed between the hours of 7am and 9pm..
- Townhouses shall have a front door that receives either a stoop or front porch with stairs that reach down to sidewalk level.
- Townhouse end units may locate their main entry either at the front or the side.

#### 9.b. Height & Articulation

- Height is further restricted by building type, and is measured in stories.
- A maximum 50% of the roof or attic space may be habitable and shall not count towards the overall height.
- Windows shall be consistent across facades, establishing a symmetrical and/or balanced rhythm.
- Where provided, facade breaks should be broken up in such a way that each building facade has a clear and balanced composition that can read as a stand-alone building. Changes in material, color, vertical and horizontal articulation, shall not be arbitrary. Changes in material or color should correspond to variations in building mass, and should comply with the following:
  - Be convincing. The goal is to create a sense of authentic development of distinct buildings designed by different architects.
  - Make "buildings" that are themselves simple. Each building façade should be made of a limited amount of materials, four at most.
  - Demise lines should be accompanied by a change in building setback only when doing so makes sense as an urban design (space-making) strategy. Otherwise, unnecessarily wiggling undermines the street space.
  - Each "building" façade should look proper if isolated as a standalone structure, without neighbors. This means that the façade is a clear, balanced composition with a proper center and edges.
- Bay windows are an acceptable form of building articulation if the structure of bay window is carried to the ground or supported by brackets proportioned to visually carry the load above it.
- Townhouses shall have consistent materials and openings across a complete row.
- Each individual townhouse shall be separated from its neighbor by a downspout.
- 9.c. Setbacks shall be according to the regulating plan and "Table 1 Form Standards".

#### 9.d. Encroachments

- General to all:
  - Balconies are encouraged and shall establish a symmetrical rhythm.
  - Galleries are encouraged and may encroach into the setback according to Table 1. (to be coordinated with zoning)

- 9.e. Residential Windows shall be according to the following:
  - Windows in residential applications shall have muntins and shall be according to the following:
    - Muntin pattern shall be traditional
    - Muntin type may be either:
      - True divided-lite or
      - Simulated divided-lit
    - Window trim, sash, and muntins shall be of one consistent color.
  - Windows shall not be extruded and shall have a traditional profile.
  - Brick mould is required when windows are along a masonry facade. The application and profile shall follow American Vignola.
  - Window panes shall be vertical in proportion
  - Window material are limited to wood, aluminum clad, or vinyl clad.

#### 10. Building Material

- 10.a. Material and craftsmanship on the ground floor should be durable and of high quality to be able to withstand weather and wear.
- 10.b. Buildings shall have a maximum of 3 materials composing the building facade, excluding glazing, trim, and hardware.
- 10.c. Changes in material shall be along a horizontal plane and separate by trim.
- 10.d. When materials are stacked the heaviest looking material shall be below the lighter looking material.
- 10.e. Materials shall be limited to the following:
  - Wood
  - Hardie-plank
  - Brick, Stone, and other masonry
  - Stucco
  - Metal
- 10.f. Material shall be consistent along all sides that are on a public frontage. Along non-public frontages, the material shall wrap for a distance that is natural to the type of material, i.e brick should wrap 12 or 16 inches.
