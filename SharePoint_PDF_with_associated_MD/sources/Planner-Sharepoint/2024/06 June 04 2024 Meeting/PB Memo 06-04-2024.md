A webpage on the Town website is also available at [www.barrington.ri.gov/resilient114](http://www.barrington.ri.gov/resilient114)

#### **SS4A**

On Tuesday, May 21, staff met with consultants working with RIPTA on Barrington's Safety Plan as part of the Safe Streets for All (SS4A) program. As a reminder, the Town signed onto RIPTA's \$5 million application back in 2022, and are receiving funds to do action plan work. Where the Town already has an adopted Complete Streets Plan, the Bicycle-Pedestrian Advisory Committee recommended the Town request a Safety Plan as our primary deliverable, based on the options from RIPTA's scope of work.

The project will primarily go on through the summer with a fall-winter wrap up. The team has been informed and are confirming availability for the July 23 public engagement event.

The findings from this plan will feed directly into the Circulation Chapter of the Comprehensive Plan and add to the gap analysis/implementation work being done by Fuss & O'Neill.

#### **6. Comments – Board Member Reports, Council Liaison & Director of Planning**

*This agenda item is open to comments, however, staff wanted to be sure to address the guidebook item from the May meeting:*

#### **6.1 Discuss and Act: Comments on Draft Land Use Approvals Guidebook.**

From the comments received from Board members on the first draft of the Land Use Approvals Guidebook distributed as part of the May meeting materials, common threads of comments included:

- ➢ Removing the "Ex-Parte" Communications section (as recommended by Assistant Solicitor Goins at the May meeting) and changes to state law (as to not confuse with "what used to be").
- ➢ Include standards of approval for subdivisions and development plan review as well as variances and special use permits.
- ➢ Rather than quoting the ordinance/regulations, summarize information in more plain language for better flow and easier to understand at a high level.
- ➢ Reformatting text info flowcharts (where appropriate) to make it easier to understand process wise and help with organization/flow of the material.
- ➢ PLEASE NOTE: there were conflicting comments on references to sections of the ordinance some comments stating too many in one section that led to confusion, others requesting additional references be included.

Staff appreciate the Board's attention to this and feedback as we work to take the content provided by the consultant team and best suit it to the needs of being a usable, high-level guide for Board members and potential applicants.

This agenda item is if the Board wishes to discuss the matter further, and offer any additional comments and feedback not captured in the points above for consideration. Should the Board come to consensus on the revisions/comments provided, staff plan to use those to complete an extensive edit to address these with the aim of providing a revised version by the end of the summer.
