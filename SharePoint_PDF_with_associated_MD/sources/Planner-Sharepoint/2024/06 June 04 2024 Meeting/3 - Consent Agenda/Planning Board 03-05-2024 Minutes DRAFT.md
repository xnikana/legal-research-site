- **4. Public Hearing**
- **4.1 Discuss and Act: Concept Review / Preapplication for Comprehensive Permit at 33 Middle Highway, Assessor's Plat 14, Lot 2, Senior Residential Village District (One Hour TOTAL).**

*Present: Charlie Kalocsay, representative from Shineharmony Holdings Suann Santos, InSite Engineering Paul Bannon, traffic consultant*

The Chair introduced the agenda item. Assistant Solicitor Goins reviewed the concept review / preapplication procedure, emphasizing the Planning Board is not making motions at this stage. The Chair confirmed with Assistant Solicitor Goins the public comment requirements for concept review.

Kalocsay reviewed that the project plans to proceed with a Comprehensive Permit application and how review the application from scratch (noting former preapplication meetings). Kalocsay noted previous discussions regarding concerns on the number of school-aged children, and noted

supplemental materials they plan to bring to address some of the initial concerns. Town Planner Crean noted that this agenda item is being recorded.

Kalocsay provided a brief background and history on the property itself, including prior plan approvals that have since expired. They are now working with DPZ CoDesign, and Kalocsay outlined the firm's qualifications. Kalocsay then referred to the "Project Overview" document submitted as part of the preapplication, which outlined/reviewed:

- ➢ The vision statement for the project.
- ➢ Design guidelines and principles, including walkability, social engagement, green technologies, and architecture.
- ➢ Development plan, including estimated unit count (estimated 350 units, though noted that estimate may go down), density analysis, unit types, and plan to incorporate affordable units, as required with the Comprehensive Permit process.
- ➢ Kalocsay noted that the gymnasium and recreation center discussion with the Town is likely not moving forward due to it being cost prohibitive for the Town. The Shineharmony team plans to review and address this prior to submission of preliminary plan.
- ➢ Key benefits of the development plan, such as housing, including infrastructure improvements and affordable housing stock.
- ➢ Reviewed required modifications to the current zoning, as required for a preapplication meeting.
- ➢ Reviewed several maps, including a location map, existing conditions, and site plan.

The following discussion followed based on studies/information documents provided at the meeting:

- ➢ Kalocsay reiterated the initial estimated school-aged children and went into more depths of the data collected ("Estimated Population and School Aged Children – 3-5-2024" document introduced at meeting on monitor).
  - o "Application of Barrington Demographic Statistics to Barrington Housing Unit" ("Estimated Population and School Aged Children – 3-5-2024" document introduced at meeting on monitor).
  - o "Application of Barrington Demographic Statistics to Belton Court Housing Units" ("Estimated Population and School Aged Children – 3-5-2024" document introduced at meeting on monitor).
- ➢ Kalocsay then reviewed a document titled "Economic Fiscal Analysis 3-5-2024" (introduced at meeting on monitor).

Santos then reviewed a site plan (full-sized plan on easel, presented at the meeting) and drainage analysis, noting they are in the preliminary stages of the design. Santos reviewed that so far, the engineering team has completed the following:

- ➢ Delineation of the wetlands, complying with the new State buffer zones (shown in orange in a displayed site plan).
- ➢ Soil evaluation tests and groundwater elevation, which contribute to a stormwater system, noting how the applicant is aiming for a green, low-impact development and design. This includes bio-retention areas, rain gardens, and swales. This is in the process of design.

Chair Phipps asked about infrastructure capacity at the site, specifically noting sewer and water. Santos noted the site does have water and sewer, and confirmed there is enough capacity for sewer and they are analyzing the water. Chair Phipps noted the neighboring community's issues with water and flooding, and asked how the applicant plans to address this as to not negatively impact the abutting communities. Santos noted runoff is currently directed to the neighboring residential areas, and they will meet the requirement that there be no additional runoff. Assistant Solicitor Goins noted the legal standard is there be no adverse impact on neighboring properties, though cannot require them to fix a problem that already exists, only to not add to it as part of their proposed development. Assistant Solicitor Goins confirmed the Board and Rhode Island Department of Environmental Management (RIDEM) are to use existing conditions as the baseline.

Member Kilmartin noted preexisting structures on the property and historic standing and asked for the reasoning behind demolition. Kalocsay noted the other surrounding buildings other than Belton Court were condemned by the Town and have been demolished. As for Belton Court, the applicant found that due to the condition and configuration of the building, there was no scenario that was economically feasible to preserve the building, so instead the applicant worked to preserve certain aspects of architectural significance. Vice Chair Rua asked about distribution of the affordable units, which Kalocsay noted they are equally distributed, except for the single-family homes. Member Kilmartin asked more about the configuration of the single-family homes. Kalocsay commented on how the higher density will be toward the center of the site, and lower density going out, creating a village concept and dispersing varying unit types throughout the site.

#### Member Bacher commented on the following:

➢ **Density bonus calculation**. Noted the State-entitled density bonus calculation, and that several items are needed to address this. Member Bacher also commented on the importance of looking at the infrastructure and ensuring that the site can support the project. Member Bacher asked about wetland buffers and confirmed that they are needed to answer the density question. Member Bacher reviewed the history of the subject property, and reiterated this is the largest development the Town has seen.

### ➢ **Clarification on variances.**

- o Member Bacher commented that the applicant needs to indicate the proposed parking, as that may be a possible variance. Member Bacher also commented that easements and rights of way that need to be included as part of preliminary plan submittal, and commercial use with associated parking needs to be calculated in the residential site area, along with the area for Belton Court (which also goes to the density bonus calculation question).
- o Member Bacher noted the required zoning setbacks for the Senior Residential Village District, emphasizing the need to not disrupt the abutting neighborhoods. Member Bacher reviewed how the project overview notes uses proposed other than the residential, and asked for more specifics about those uses. Kalocsay reviewed this more in depth, including the idea to incorporate retail and minimal commercial uses scattered through the development. Member Bacher noted that because of the nature of the permit, these need to be more specified.

- ➢ **Infrastructure concerns.** Member Bacher reiterated the need for more information and studies on infrastructure and traffic, which he recognized the applicant has in progress, noting his concern for surrounding local streets. He commented on the street proposals, discussing more details such as widths of streets and details on sidewalks (noting the Town's Land Development and Subdivision Regulations and State requirements).
- ➢ **Natural resources and open space.** Member Bacher commented on natural resources and open space, including trees being removed and for the applicant to confirm they are replacing trees that are to be removed. He referred to the greenspace plan, and that there are not open active spaces proposed, however, he believes are a necessary amenity in a proposed development this dense. Member Bacher then commented that the proposed trails shall not go through the buffer areas, and to address prior to preliminary plan submittal.
- ➢ **Architecture.** Member Bacher commented on the applicant's proposed value to the Town, asking if the design presented in the "Project Overview" will be what moves forward. Kalocsay responded, reviewing the proposed architectural review board more in depth, and how the applicant is aiming for a New England village feel. Member Bacher asked who is to serve on the review Board. Kalocsay noted it would start as the property owners as the master developers, and then will move to members from general community, residents from the community, and will evolve overtime.
- ➢ **Green design.** Member Bacher asked about the green technology referenced in the presentation, and Kalocsay noted he did not go in depth in this presentation as the technologies are ever changing and must meet the needs of the residents who are to live there.

Member Mueller asked about existing infrastructure capacity, and Town Planner Crean noted that is covered in the preliminary plan submission, and that there is a sewer permit process through the Department of Public Works. Chair Phipps confirmed the applicant will review developable land as part of the preliminary plan submittal.

*The Chair opened the meeting to public comment.*

Neile Hartman, 18 Great Road, noted they are part of the Lions Head Association and thanked the Board for their questions. Hartman commented on their concerns regarding water runoff, and on the estimate of school-aged children, commenting how the middle school is at 95 percent capacity.

Gerry Gato, 5 Carriage Trail, commented on their concern about density, how he has been watching these presentations and updates from the property owner's representatives for years with no action.

Gerald Diebold, 118 Governor Bradford Road, asked about investors and if Shineharmony is involved with Chinese nationals, raising concern about the Town doing business with the Chinese government and China-based businesses, noting human rights issues. Diebold urged the Board to disallow the proposal and that the owners should sell the property to another.

Les Sheeley, 13 Great Road, asked if there is parking for all the vehicles, noting an estimated 200 vehicles (at least one car per unit). Sheeley commented on how this proposal is a city within a town, and asked if and how the Town can enforce movement on the project.

*The Chair closed the meeting to public comment.*

Vice Chair Rua and Chair Phipps noted it will be important for the parking to be flushed out in the preliminary plan submittal. Kalocsay responded that parking has been programmed into the design. Kalocsay confirmed he took notes of the public comments made to consider and address for the preliminary plan stage. Bannon provided some high-level details on the traffic study, including main roadways providing access, Rhode Island Department of Transportation (RIDOT) requirements, and data collection and analysis. Chair Phipps reiterated that the applicant is to get the developable acres to accurately calculate the density bonus. Chair Phipps also commented that the cottages proposed were two stories, however, for elderly residents they may prefer one-story.

# **7.2 Open Meetings Discussion, including Planning Board roles and responsibilities and communications.**

Assistant Solicitor Goins reviewed that there are two main considerations that govern Planning Board members: the Open Meetings Act and avoiding ex-parte communications. Assistant Solicitor Goins reviewed ex-parte more in-depth, noting the difference between legislative bodies (such as the Town Council) and quasi-judicial bodies (such as the Planning Board). Assistant Solicitor Goins reviewed to avoid making public statements on an application, avoid one-to-one communications with other members of the Board and instead wait to share with the group in an open meeting and/or submit questions to staff.

Chair Phipps asked about Board members working with staff. Assistant Solicitor Goins responded that the Board works by consensus, and staff shall follow consensus. Town Planner Crean commented if individual comments and questions are submitted by Board members the Friday prior to a meeting, those can be distributed in advance via a BCC email should other members have the same ones. Another option is direct questions to the Chair and work to put said questions as items on the agenda. Assistant Solicitor Goins reiterated how the burden of proof for evidence of an application, answering questions on it, etc., is on the applicant.
