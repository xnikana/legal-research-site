## <span id="page-1-4"></span>**4.1 Discuss and Act: Concept Review / Preapplication for 25 Watson Avenue Comprehensive Permit**

#### <span id="page-1-5"></span>**Background**

- ➢ At the May 22, 2024 Financial Town Meeting, the voters approved authorization to the Town to redevelop the property based upon substantial conformance with the site plan layout approved by the Planning Board on October 3, 2023 and Town Council on January 8, 2024.
- ➢ The plan involves demolishing the structures formerly known as the Carmelite Monastery, sell one lot for a "pocket neighborhood" consisting of twelve (12) cottages, all age-restricted, five (5) affordable units, sell six single-family lots, and create a two-acre public park.
- ➢ The Town, in partnership with FJS Associates, is presenting a Comprehensive Permit application requesting relief for the underlying zoning requirements for a total of eighteen (18) units (twelve (12) in the pocket neighborhood, six (6) single-family lots) and the public park.

<span id="page-5-3"></span>**5.1 Discuss and Act: Major Change Request for an approved preliminary plan decision – Book 1401, Page 227 in the Town's Land Evidence Records – at 3 College Lane – Assessor's Plat 15, Lot 84, R-10 District. The major change requests to change item #5 in the "Findings of Fact" to allow the provision of affordable housing on the existing house instead of the newly created lot should the lot be further subdivided, per 200-41(D) in the Land Development and Subdivision Regulations.**

#### <span id="page-5-4"></span>**Background**

- ➢ In 2014, there was an approved preliminary plan decision by the Planning Board for a minor subdivision to create three lots.
- ➢ In the Findings of Fact for that decision, it was stated that any future subdivision of Parcel 1 (i.e. 3 College Lane) would require the provisions of an affordable housing unit on the new lot.
  - o The proposal at the time was only proposing two (2) lots by subdividing again, would create three (3), triggering inclusionary zoning.
- ➢ Presently, the property owner of 3 College Lane is proposing a two (2) -lot minor subdivision at 3 College Lane, and wishes to make the existing dwelling the required affordable housing unit rather than the new lot.
- ➢ To do this, the applicant must request such a change from the Planning Board in the form of a public hearing, per [200-41\(D\)](https://ecode360.com/44744944#44744944) of the Land Development and Subdivision Regulations.
