### **4. New Business**

- **4.1 Discuss and Act: Comprehensive Plan 2024-2025 (Standing Agenda Item).**
- **4.1.1 Presentation and Discussion with Michael Baker International: Comprehensive Plan Overview, including debrief/recap of June 24 Steering Committee Kickoff, Survey, Comp-Plan Palooza, and schedule.**

*Present: Brittney Dremluk and Kirsten Compitello, Michael Baker International*

Town Planner Crean provided the overview role of Michael Baker International in the Comprehensive Plan, including the selection process for the consultant team and introductions of the project managers.

Compitello and Dremluk introduced themselves, reviewed the Michael Baker International team, the role of the Planning Board in the Comprehensive Plan process, the scope and schedule of the process, and engagement strategy. Dremluk emphasized the strategy is to approach the elements as questions and topics that will draw people in rather than just promoting "Comprehensive Plan update" (an example being "how do we transform downtown into a destination?", rather than more technical terms like "economic development").

The Board discussed best practices for public engagement. Member Kilmartin noted hardcopies of the surveys at Town buildings, and a flyer with some of the questions that has a QR code to all the surveys to post around Town. Chair Phipps reiterated the importance of hardcopy options and

noted locations for these should include Town buildings and the YMCA. Chair Phipps also noted the importance of distinguishing the flyer as to not have it lost in the clutter.

The Board then discussed focus groups. Chair Phipps suggested the Barrington Village and noted extracurricular organizations at the High School that are related to the elements of the Comprehensive Plan, and the importance of engaging student leadership to reach a younger demographic. The Board noted getting local businesses involved, including the Barrington Business and Community Association.

Member Bacher provided comments on the housing question posted on slide 7 of the presentation "What housing options will meet residents' needs and price points to keep people in Barrington?" and the reality that Barrington is mostly built out. Member Bacher then noted the land use question, reiterating that there's limited room for development. Member Bacher commented that it will be important to identify the sites the Town would like to see growth happen at that may not be growing now and focus on how that can occur. Member Bacher noted the Town has a lot of vacant retail space and are there ways to attract or repurpose those spaces. As for focus groups, Member Bacher noted the Senior Center has a small population and there are people there who are not residents and noted the importance of targeting groups such as residents at the Barrington Cove Apartments, Palmer Pointe, and Sweetbriar.

Compitello noted Michael Baker International will be preparing meeting toolkits as a public engagement tool. As for the land use comments, Michael Baker will plan to put together some materials for the Planning Board to provide comments in between now and the draft plan.

Chair Phipps asked how the Planning Board will check in on the visions and goals. Compitello noted those will be geared through the Steering Committee. The Board agreed that Michael Baker should have another check-in with the Planning Board on visions and goals. Town Planner Crean noted staff will work with Michael Baker on this request.

The Board discussed with staff more in depth the various surveys that are live, and distribution of such. The discussion then moved to promotion of the July 23 CompPlanPalooza.

No motion was made / no action taken.

**4.1.2 Presentation and Discussion with the Bicycle-Pedestrian Advisory Committee and Fuss & O'Neill on Complete Streets Implementation Plan, including existing conditions, desire lines and paths, level of user stress, and gap analysis.**

*Present: Arnold Robinson, Fuss & O'Neill Francisco Lovera and Maura McCrann, Bicycle-Pedestrian Advisory Committee*

Clerk Gillibrand introduced the Bicycle-Pedestrian Advisory Committee (BPAC) members and Arnold Robinson from Fuss & O'Neill, providing a brief overview of the gap analysis, and that the intent of the present is to seek feedback from the Planning Board as part of a summer-long public engagement effort.

BPAC Chair Lovera provided more detail onto the Bicycle-Pedestrian Advisory Committee's role in the Complete Streets Plan update project and introduced Arnold Robinson.

Robinson provided an overview of what a complete street is, the process of the Complete Streets Plan update so far, goals for the updated Complete Streets Plan, existing conditions and plans,

activity nodes, field assessment data collected, stress level ratings for both pedestrians and bicyclists, and a sneak preview at potential interventions for the gap analysis given the stress level ratings and existing conditions. Robinson noted the process is not at the stage of recommendation but are beginning to explore what the recommendations could be, with goals and priorities very moldable. Robinson noted the aim is to have a plan with a prioritized list drafted by the fall.

Member Bacher noted the struggle of the Hampden Meadows neighborhood in particular being connected to the rest of the Town by two State roads, and the importance of engaging the State. Robinson concurred and noted the amount of funding for multimodal transportation currently available, and the importance of having the political will and having a cost estimate to encourage the State to prioritize these projects.

Member Simms noted the signage discrepancy between different East Bay Bike Path crossings (some having stop signs for both vehicles and bike path traffic, others only for bike path traffic), and if there is any point to having a stop sign on a bike path. Robinson noted more modern standards that are required that he is seeing on other bike paths. BPAC Chair Lovera provided additional comments based on BPAC discussions and his experience in his professional career in transportation planning.

Member Kilmartin asked how e-bikes and e-scooters are being incorporated into this analysis and data, or if they are given the scope. BPAC Chair Lovera responded, drawing on BPAC discussion and his experience in his professional career in transportation planning.

Member Caito asked about traffic signals for bicyclists. BPAC Chair Lovera provided additional comments, and noted an example in Warren and another in Town by Veteran's Memorial Parkway for other measures that can be incorporated.

The Board thanked both the BPAC and Fuss & O'Neill on their work thus far and looked forward to seeing more developments as the plan moves along.

No motion was made / no action taken.

### **5. Public Hearing**

### **5.1 Discuss and Act: Discuss and Act: Amendments to Land Development & Subdivision Regulations – Technical Review Committee**

Town Planner Crean reviewed the process to date, and the Technical Review Committee's comments from their June meeting. Member Simms asked about the existing Technical Review Committee members, and Town Planner Crean noted that they have expressed interest in the Design Review Committee. The Board discussed the authority of the Technical Review Committee (TRC) versus Design Review Committee (DRC), and the change of reviews by the Planning Board given the 2023 and 2024 legislation.

Member Bacher noted a definition needed of "substantial work" and suggested "new construction or exterior alterations." Assistant Solicitor concurred that suggestion works as a new definition. Member Bacher noted some suggestions to the amendments, including that applications reviewed by TRC shall first be reviewed by the DRC, and additional items to be reviewed by the DRC.

Assistant Solicitor Goins noted she would want to review the draft language, however, the Planning Board wants to approve with revisions as discussed. Assistant Solicitor Goins commented that it is quite possible details may need to be refined later, and noted concern about the TRC and DRC having growing pains, though not to say it cannot be tried out. Member Bacher confirmed that sending his mark ups to Clerk Gillibrand as part of the record, and Clerk Gillibrand to forward to Assistant Solicitor Goins.

**MOTION: Vice Chair Rua, seconded by Member Costa, moved to accept the amendments to the Land Development and Subdivision Regulations with revisions as discussed, effective date August 1, 2024**. **These revisions to include:**

- **200-7(F) – a second paragraph to be added that notes that applications considered under section shall first be reviewed by the Design Review Committee.**
- **200-7.1(E)(e) - Substantial work to be defined as "new construction or any exterior alteration.**
- **200-7.1(E)(f) – items to be reviewed by the Design Review Committee shall include:**
  - o **185-151A: Exterior alteration of any non-residential structure.**
  - o **185-151B: Cellular towers and antenna arrays**
  - o **200-51.1: Building Standards, Commercial and Industrial Site Standards.**

**Motion carried unanimously (Member Kurtz not present for the vote).**
