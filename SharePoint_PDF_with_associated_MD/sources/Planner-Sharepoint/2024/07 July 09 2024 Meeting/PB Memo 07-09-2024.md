# **4.1.2 Presentation and Discussion with the Bicycle-Pedestrian Advisory Committee and Fuss & O'Neill on Complete Streets Implementation Plan, including existing conditions, desire lines and paths, level of user stress, and gap analysis.**

Please see [the Complete Streets Plan brief from Fuss & O'Neill,](https://twnbarrintonri.sharepoint.com/:b:/s/BoardandCommissionPacketLibrary/EWy4oOF1C89CmMgbKkOavegBYOHrXcHNSaoCT1UzH6gREw?e=km4Set) included in the Board's packet materials.

At the June 17 Bicycle-Pedestrian Advisory Committee (BPAC) meeting, Fuss & O'Neill presented the existing conditions and initial gap analysis to the Committee for the first round of feedback. This was based on data collected from field assessment, and existing plans and maps (both Town and State).

The intent of being before the Planning Board is for BPAC and Fuss & O'Neill to provide an overview of those findings and gather Planning Board feedback to incorporate.

Following the Planning Board presentation, the same overview will be given to department heads, the Town Council, and then be their focus at the July 23 Comp Plan Palooza. The findings from this plan will feed directly into the Circulation Chapter of the Comprehensive Plan and add to the gap analysis/implementation work being done by Fuss & O'Neill.

**5.1 Discuss and Act: Amendments to Land Development & Subdivision Regulations – Technical Review Committee**

At the April Planning Board meeting, the following motion was made (DRAFT from the May 7, 2024 meeting minutes):

**MOTION: Member Bacher, seconded by Member Simms, made a motion to endorse/recommend restructuring the Technical Review Committee with comments per Board consensus, and to direct staff to prepare amendments to the Land Development and Subdivision Regulations. Motion carried unanimously.** 

Areas of consensus included:

- ➢ A formal Design Review Committee as subordinate to both the Technical Review Committee and the Planning Board.
- ➢ Endorsing the framework restructuring the Technical Review Committee while beginning identifying how to establish a formal Design Review Committee.

Following further discussion at the June 4, 2024, the following second motion was made (DRAFT from the June 4, 2024 meeting minutes):

**MOTION: Member Bacher, seconded by Member Kilmartin, made a motion to continue the public hearing to the July 9, 2024 meeting, and by that meeting add Design Review Committee language to the proposed Land Development and Subdivision Regulations. Motion carried unanimously.**

At the June 13, 2024 Technical Review Committee meeting, the group provided comments and feedback on their thoughts of the Technical Review Committee format change, and on the proposed Design Review Committee (see excerpt motion from the DRAFT meeting minutes below):

**MOTION: Member Durham, seconded by Vice Chair Stabach, moved to recommend the following:**

- ➢ **The newly crafted Technical Review Committee does not include any public members as it will be difficult to attract resident working professionals during the daytime.**
- ➢ **The Town's on-call engineer and architecture firms shall be available as needed as nonvoting at the meetings at the discretion of the Administrative Officer.**
- ➢ **Establish a Design Review Committee made of public volunteer members to review design**
