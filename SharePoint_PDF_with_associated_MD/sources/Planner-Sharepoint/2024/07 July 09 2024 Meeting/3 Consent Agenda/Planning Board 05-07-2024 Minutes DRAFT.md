#### **4.2 Discuss and Act: Technical Review Committee Structure (T. Crean).**

Town Planner Crean reviewed the executive summary, noting how other municipalities model their Technical Review Committee and what the Town is proposing. Crean added that the Comprehensive Plan has the potential to result in a Design Review Committee, which, if formed, the current volunteers of the Technical Review Committee would be welcome to join. Assistant Solicitor Goins concurred and recommended that a motion be to direct staff to prepare amendments to the Land Development and Subdivision Regulations to reflect the changes to the

Technical Review Committee. Town Planner Crean emphasized how this model allows for Department reviews and reports to be incorporated into Technical Review Committee recommendations.

Member Simms asked how the procedure would change from what it is currently. Member Bacher commented on his concern that the Department Heads do not have architectural, design, and/or engineering backgrounds, as outlined in the current regulations. Assistant Solicitor Goins noted that for a design review board to function it would require more detailed design standards. Member Bacher noted that they do exist in the regulations, however, would like to see them improved. Chair Phipps asked about PARE's role, and Town Planner Crean confirmed, and elaborated further on the role of on-call consultants. Member Caito asked how many resident volunteers would serve, Town Planner Crean and Assistant Solicitor Goins noted that could be determined and amended in the Regulations. Member Costa added that the resident volunteers would be vetted by the Planning Board to review qualifications.

Member Bacher commented being amenable to the Technical Review Committee's role as a recommendation, however, if moving to new model, would want to remove administrative approval that does not require Planning Board review. Assistant Solicitor Goins noted a place to start would be to review the Regulations tasks into Technical Review Committee and Design Review Committee functions. Town Planner Crean noted that enhanced design guidelines is a potential practice as part of the Comprehensive Plan update.

The Board came to consensus of a formal Design Review Committee as subordinate to both the Technical Review Committee and the Planning Board. The Board came to consensus of endorsing the framework restructuring the Technical Review Committee while beginning identifying how to establish a formal Design Review Committee.

**MOTION: Member Bacher, seconded by Member Simms, made a motion to endorse/recommend restructuring the Technical Review Committee with comments per Board consensus, and to direct staff to prepare amendments to the Land Development and Subdivision Regulations. Motion carried unanimously.**

### **5.1.4 Circulation – Gap Analysis/Implementation Plan Update and Resilient Route 114**

Clerk Gillibrand reviewed the executive summary, commenting how Fuss & O'Neill and the Bicycle-Pedestrian Advisory Committee are in the field assessment stage, public engagement to come in the summer, with an implementation plan produced fall into winter.

Town Planner Crean then reviewed the Resilient Route 114 process to date, and who is involved. The study is intended to explore options and what may need to happen short and far term for the Department of Transportation (RIDOT) for the State's transportation improvement program (STIP). The study is being funded by Federal Highway Administration monies through the Department of Statewide Planning. The public event is scheduled for June 6, 2024 in the Town Council Chamber. The Board and Town Planner Crean noted similar examples from New Jersey. Vice Chair Rua asked what sway Department of Statewide Planning has over RIDOT, and Town Planner Crean confirmed that they are the stewards.
