## **4. Public Hearing**

# **4.1 Discuss and Act: Amendments to Land Development & Subdivision Regulations - Technical Review Committee Structure.**

Town Planner Crean reviewed the memorandum provided to the Board. Assistant Solicitor Goins reviewed the tracked changes to the amended regulations. Town Planner Crean noted how the role of the Design Review Committee can be flushed out as part of the Comprehensive Plan process.

Upon request from Member Kilmartin, Town Planner Crean reviewed the role of the Technical Review Committee (TRC) on a high level, how other municipalities model their Technical Review Committee, and clarified what the Town is proposing.

Chair Phipps clarified that the TRC in its new format would still be advisory to the Planning Board, recommendations submitted as part of Board packets and the meeting being the first Tuesday of every month. Town Planner Crean summarized the completeness review process and how the TRC will serve a role in that.

Member Bacher asked about specification of the meeting time. Assistant Solicitor Goins recommended language of "as specified/determined by the Chair." Member Bacher expressed concern about the proposed model of department heads not having the design review expertise, specifically noting components of signage. Town Planner Crean responded that the intent was to meet with the existing Technical Review Committee to discuss next steps. Assistant Solicitor Goins confirmed a Design Review Committee would need to be codified.

The Board discussed having the Design Review Committee as part of the regulations. Assistant Solicitor Goins responded that the Board could continue the public hearing, add Design Review Committee language to the Regulation. Continuance to the July meeting.

*The Chair opened the public hearing.*

Maria Burce, 31 Maple Avenue, wanted clarity on Board and Committee processes and which feeds into what in the decision-making process. Bruce asked about the Technical Review Committee's relationship to the Land Development and Subdivision Regulations. Assistant Solicitor responded that the Regulations guide the Planning Board and elaborated on the evolution of the Technical Review Committee with the 2023 state legislation. Town Planner Crean noted that the Land Development and Subdivision Regulations outline the process and procedures for subdivisions.

**MOTION: Member Bacher, seconded by Member Kilmartin, made a motion to continue the public hearing to the July 9, 2024 meeting, and by that meeting add Design Review Committee language to the proposed Land Development and Subdivision Regulations. Motion carried unanimously.**

#### **5.1.1 Housing – Stantec**

Town Planner Crean reviewed that Stantec and their subconsultant Community Scale presented their draft housing study to the Housing Board of Trustees on May 15. The draft of the initial analysis has been distributed to the Board, a survey was released on June 3, and more public engagement efforts will be conducted throughout July. Crean also reviewed that Stantec will meet with staff and Statewide Planning in late August.

Member Bacher summarized his comments, including concerns with "Barrington region" noted in the draft, and how that influences projections and predictions, including housing production, type of housing, and demographics (specifically noting senior housing). Town Planner Crean noted this input is what the consultants are looking for to drive the next step.

Member Kilmartin concurred, noting how senior housing was a significant driver of the 2015 Comprehensive Plan and that the study notes that is what is being used as the initial point. Town Planner Crean reiterated this is to start gathering comments about where the community wants the vision to go.

Member Bacher noted the 10-percent affordable mandate being a current obstacle and does not see that addressed in the draft. Town Planner Crean noted Stantec has been asked to coordinate with Rhode Island and the Department of Housing to better understand the 10 percent affordable housing expectation and any consequences.

Vice Chair Rua commented that he concurred with many of Member Bacher's comments.

Chair Phipps asked if the Town could request that Stantec revise its data, specifically where they are pulling and why they are presenting the data the way they are. Town Planner Crean responded Stantec has been asked to review the surrounding communities of Warren and Bristol to have an apples-to-apples comparison.

# **5.1.2 Circulation – Gap Analysis/Implementation Plan Update and Resilient Route 114**

Clerk Gillibrand reviewed the information in the memorandum for the Gap Analysis/Implementation Plan Update and the work being conducted for the Complete Streets Gap Analysis/Implementation Plan, Resilient Route 114 that would be having a public event that upcoming Thursday, June 6, and the municipal engagement plan draft for the SS4A funding from RIPTA, the findings from all of these efforts which are on track to be done toward the end of the year to feed into the Circulation Chapter of the Comprehensive Plan.

#### **5.1.3 Energy and Natural Hazards – Climate Action Plan**

Town Planner Crean noted the first advisory committee meeting to be held the following day, Wednesday, June 5, which will begin public engagement efforts for Ready & Resilient Barrington. The plan is anticipated to be done by the fall, with implementation to follow.

*The Chair opened the meeting to public comment.* 

Maria Bruce, 31 Maple Avenue, noted it is a shame how much work goes into the Planning Board meetings and there is hardly anyone attending. Bruce commented on the initial housing study and the survey lacks open-ended questions. Town Planner Crean noted the survey is only one part of the public engagement process, and more outreach, especially for the July 23 event, is underway. Bruce noted this is a critical time for decision making, and no one is happy from those she has talked to in the community. Bruce noted her concerns about the low density at 25 Watson Avenue and it's not fair that the housing is to be placed at places like 33 Middle Highway. Bruce asked how a resident could provide more input and be engaged rather than just a survey. Bruce asked about the ten percent affordable housing mandate and the consequence of not meeting it, and commented how the draft housing study should not be considering 33 Middle Highway, and it feels discriminatory to target that part of Town.

Town Planner Crean responded that the Town is getting clarification on the ten-percent mandate to better understand any consequences. Assistant Solicitor Goins added that at this time if the Town gets a Comprehensive Permit application and it is denied, the Superior Court will look at the application through a different set of standards because the Town has not met the 10 percent threshold for affordable housing.

Chair Phipps stated all members of the Board are Town residents who work their best to represent the best interests of the Town.
