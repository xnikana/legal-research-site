# **BARRINGTON TECHNICAL REVIEW COMMITTEE MEETING 7:30 p.m. Thursday, June 13, 2024**

Town Hall Council Chamber, 2nd Floor, Barrington Town Hall 283 County Road, Barrington, RI

## MEETING MINUTES

#### **1. Call to Order**

The meeting was called to order at 7:30 PM

#### **2. Roll Call and Determination of Quorum**

*Present:* Chair/Administrative Officer Teresa Crean, Vice Chair Jonathan Stabach, Ted Wetherill, and Jordan Durham

*Absent:* Richard Godfrey

#### **3. Approve Minutes – April 11, 2024**

**MOTION: Member Durham, seconded by Member Wetherhill, moved to approve the minutes from the April 11, 2024 Meeting. Motion carried unanimously.** 

**4. Discuss and Act: Amendments to Chapter 200: Land Development and Subdivision Regulations related to the Technical Review Committee, and next steps on establishing a Design Review Committee (draft minutes of discussion/any recommendations to be forwarded to the Planning Board for July 9, 2024 Meeting, where Public Hearing on this matter was continued from June 4).**

Chair Crean reviewed past meeting discussions and how the Town is seeking to change the format of the Technical Review Committee, noting examples of other municipalities around Rhode Island, and summarized the Planning Board's discussions, noting the Board's concern about losing the expertise on the Technical Review Committee currently. What the Town is asking the Committee is to provide comments on the proposed amendments to the Land Development and Subdivision Regulations and would like the Planning Board to know for the July 9, 2024 meeting.

Member Durham confirmed the Town's on-call engineering consult in the proposed format would be PARE Corp., and the Town is aware of these increased fees. Chair Crean confirmed that the funding is available. Member Durham commented that it is not necessary that an engineer be present at every meeting and only as needed, and the other Committee members concurred. The group agreed that PARE as an engineering consultant could serve as an advisor and attend remotely, and not necessarily be a voting member of the Technical Review Committee.

Member Wetherill asked if the new format would serve the same capacity as what is currently, or will it switch to be more code-oriented or the like. Chair Crean reiterated why the Town is seeking the change, which is to create better coordination between department heads, which is required for major projects. The Committee discussed further which applications require Technical Review Committee input and how it will be determined which applications will be administrative, require

Technical Review Committee, and/or proposed Design Review Committee.

The group agreed in disbanding the Technical Review Committee in its existing format, however, agreed to establish some threshold to review more substantial projects more in-depth from a design review standpoint though there are not currently the teeth to implement. Should there be an application requiring review in the meantime, the existing group can reconvene as a Design Review Committee in the meantime.

The group concurred that like the on-call engineer, the Town has the on-call architectural firm included in an advisory role and as needed.

Chair Crean noted as part of the Comprehensive Plan update process, an idea for consideration may be to designate certain zoning districts to design review requirements, using a pending Comprehensive Permit application in the Business District.

Chair Crean noted that she will check with the Solicitor's office to see the logistics of repurposing the existing Technical Review Committee. The Committee reviewed current design standards, noting how there is not much aside from the 1995 design guidelines, which are not codified. The Committee agreed that the Comprehensive Plan update and corresponding Zoning Ordinance updates should be highly graphic.

Chair Crean encouraged the members to attend the July 9 Planning Board meeting for the public hearing.

**MOTION: Member Durham, seconded by Vice Chair Stabach, moved to recommend the following:**

- ➢ **The newly crafted Technical Review Committee does not include any public members as it will be difficult to attract resident working professionals during the daytime.**
- ➢ **The Town's on-call engineer and architecture firms shall be available as needed as nonvoting at the meetings at the discretion of the Administrative Officer.**
- ➢ **Establish a Design Review Committee made of public volunteer members to review design for major projects, such as major land development/subdivisions and/or Comprehensive Permits, substantial work in the Business District, signage, at the discretion of the Administrative Officer.**
- ➢ **The Town seek more architects, landscape architects, engineering, historic preservation, or an allied field to serve on the Planning Board.**

## **5. Update – Site Readiness Grant from Rhode Island Commerce re: Comprehensive Signage.**

Chair Crean reviewed that Planning/Zoning Assistant Gillibrand secured a grant from Rhode Island Commerce to fund technical assistance for comprehensive signage into the Comprehensive Plan process and corresponding Zoning Ordinance amendments.

### **6. Adjourn**

By a unanimous vote of the Committee, the meeting adjourned at 8:15 PM.
