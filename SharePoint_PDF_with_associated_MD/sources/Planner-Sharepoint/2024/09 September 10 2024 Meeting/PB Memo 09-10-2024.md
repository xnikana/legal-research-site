#### <span id="page-3-1"></span>**5. Business**

<span id="page-3-2"></span>**5.1 Discuss and Act: Technical Review Committee – Next Steps on Amendments to Land Development and Subdivision Regulations approved at the July 9, 2024 Planning Board Meeting.** 

Beginning in May, staff proposed amendments to the Planning Board regarding the Technical Review Committee (TRC) section 200-7 of the Land Development and Subdivision Regulations to change the TRC to be an administrative body rather than volunteer run. This was in an effort to match the TRC format of other municipalities in Rhode Island and to assist with the expedited review process that came with the 2023 legislative changes

Throughout discussions at the May, June, and July Planning Board meetings, there was concern expressed with this approach of losing the value of having design professionals from the volunteer group by switching the TRC over to Town staff, leading to the implementation of a Design Review Committee in addition to the TRC to keep that perspective as part of the process, the Design Review Committee being advisory to the TRC, the TRC advisory to the Planning Board.

![](PB Memo 09-10-2024_attachments/_page_3_Picture_17.jpeg)

Staff moved forward with this, using a Comprehensive Permit Preliminary Plan as a proof of concept. Through this example, it's been found that this set up requires the applicant to attend an additional advisory meeting. Additionally, given the condensed timeframe and running into the risk of "failure to act," this extra DRC step contradicts the efforts from the State level to reduce barriers to housing, as the applicant and hired representation are expected to attend three meetings rather than two.

Recognizing these barriers and recognizing the Planning Board wishes to continue having the design review aspect that the volunteer Technical Review Committee format brings to the process, **staff are requesting to forgo the adopted amendments made at the July 9, 2024 meeting, and return to the prior format.**

The plan to move forward is that, with support from the Town Manager's office, Department Heads would convene to do a review internally , not as a Technical Review Committee. The Technical Review Committee would instead continue as a volunteer Town committee and review applications and how they meet the performance standards of the Land Development and Subdivision Regulations, signage regulations, etc.

When discussed with the volunteer Technical Review Committee (when they met on August 26 in their capacity of a Design Review Committee), the Committee concurred with the staff recommendation and noted that they would be open to more flexible scheduling/meeting "as needed" rather than a regular calendar to both provide their input as the TRC and meet the expedited schedule of applications.

Staff recognize the time and effort that went into reviewing the amendments, and hope that the proposed solution satisfies the Board's wishes and expectations while meeting the needs/changing demands given the land use processes over the past year. We continue to be grateful to the Board for patience and understanding as the Town adapts and learns these significant amendments to process and procedure.

**DRAFT MOTION: to forgo the amendments regarding the Technical Review Committee (200-7) in the Land Development and Subdivision Regulations on July 9, 2024.**

Former Member Richard Godfrey served that role, however, resigned from the Planning Board several months ago (though continues to serve on the Technical Review Committee).

A new Planning Board member shall be appointed to serve on the Technical Review Committee to serve this requirement.

The Technical Review Committee in the past has typically met on the second Thursday of each month, however, are exploring moving to an "as needed" basis. More information on the TRC can be found in 200-7 of the Land Development & Subdivision Regulations, or on their [webpage on the Town website.](https://barrington.ri.gov/185/Technical-Review-Committee-TRC)
