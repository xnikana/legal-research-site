### **4. Public Hearing**

# **4.1 Discuss and Act: Amendments to Chapter 200: Land Development and Subdivision Regulations to reflect amendments to Rhode Island General Law (Checklists).**

*Present: Peter Friedrich and Kim Salerno, Libra Planners*

Clerk Gillibrand reviewed the process to date, and the Board's task with adopting the checklists.

Member Bacher clarified with the Solicitor Goins that the application forms can be modified administratively, and the Board can focus on the checklists only, with staff editing the applications accordingly to reflect the regulations and checklists.

Friedrichs then reviewed each of the three checklists:

### **Attachment 1 - Preapplication Checklist:**

- ➢ Requirements for submission.
- ➢ Language from the enabling act.
- ➢ Requirements for conceptual site plan.

Chair Phipps asked about the number of bedrooms on requirement #2. Friedrichs noted some sort of table, and language open to interpretation.

Chair Phipps asked about letter (i) on the sketch submission requirements, and notification to abutters versus direct abutters on the plan. Friedrichs explained the difference between the adjacent abutters on the plan and notice abutters. Assistant Solicitor Goins noted this is a preexisting checklist item and no different from what was previously in place.

Chair Phipps asked about marking roads on the property. Friedrichs noted it is covered in requirement (f) under rights-of-way.

Vice Chair asked again to clarify the number of bedrooms on requirement #2 and specify the types of units. Assistant Solicitor Goins recommended adding to the motion to direct staff, legal, and consultant to tweak little details such as this. Member Godfrey also noted that this requirement should address aspects like ownership, rental, market versus affordable, types of units, bedrooms per unit, and income-level targeted information, and should be presented more as a matrix. The Board agreed with Member Godfrey's comments, noting how the preapplication is for the applicant's benefit, and it would only help them to get feedback.

Member Godfrey asked about requiring a topographical map. Town Planner Crean noted it was the staff recommendation of it not being included and not wanting to add burden. Member Godfrey clarified that it is readily available and not an additional cost. Friedrichs referred to letter (v). The Board discussed further about explicitly stating the requirement for a topographical map, and the justification and use for having it. Town Planner Crean noted it is relevant for the applicant to be aware of floodplain before spending significant funds on engineering. The Board agreed to add a requirement for topographical map and data, with note that the information is available through the Town's online mapper.

Town Planner Crean noted in 2024 will include both a rewrite of the Comprehensive Plan and a new Climate Mitigation Plan, which can address and embed the new data Member Godfrey commented on regarding floodplains, however, the focus at this meeting should be to meet the necessary requirements.

### **Attachment 2 - Subdivision Checklist:**

Friedrichs reviewed that this checklist covers all stages so that an applicant knows what is coming at each stage. The Board and Friedrichs discussed the differences between the subdivision and development checklists.

On requirement #4, Member Bacher recommended adding a clause for certification from a land surveyor or engineer that the items were submitted complete, in addition to being certified complete by the Administrative Officer, and that they have complied with the regulations. Assistant Solicitor Goins noted switching Member Bacher's suggestion to "applicant" rather than land surveyor or engineer. Friedrichs noted that his recommendation is to remove item four and move it to number eight. The Board concurred.

Member Bacher then asked about requirement #10 regarding modification request. Assistant Solicitor Goins responded there was a note in the templates, and how it may be a loophole that the checklist is looking to address. Member Bacher asked if this belongs in the regulations rather than checklists. Assistant Solicitor Goins noted adding the language to the "certification of completeness" section(s) in the regulations. Clerk Gillibrand commented that because she reviews both planning and zoning applications, she could assist with the crosschecking.

Friedrichs noted "consistency with the Comprehensive Plan in requirement #11. Member Bacher asked if the Board can still consider with the legislative changes. Assistant Solicitor Goins responded that it only applies to zoning, but it is still a consideration with subdivision and development projects.

Friedrichs reviewed some of the state language, commenting that it is not very direct. Friedrichs reviewed some of the asterisks, indicating minor and major projects.

Member Bacher referred to requirement #23, suggested adding BB and CC, which is the floodplain and coastal information. Similarly for requirement #25, suggested adding (aa), (bb), and (cc) as to flood information and definition of all the lot lines, noting how preliminary plan is the only time the Board sees the application. Friedrichs noted that (aa) may be a repeat of (j), and that it adds geographic coordinates for GIS.

Proceeding to page four, Friedrichs reviewed state-required language on what gets recorded, and the catch-all provision for additional items conditioned for approval by the permitting authority.

Proceeding to page five, the survey requirements are outlined, with the idea that there is more specificity as the applicant moves through the list. On subdivision survey requirement (d), Member Bacher suggested adding a legend as a requirement. On requirement (h), Bacher then recommended that the survey show the zoning district dimensional regulations of the land being subdivided, which is useful to the Board as a reference. For (p), Member Bacher noted the template from MTAP had more expansive language about different ways of access property, and good to add "rights of way," including trails, shoreline access, and the like, which Friedrichs noted and added to the checklists. On (t), Member Bacher suggested plan should show buffer areas for DEM and CRMC jurisdictions. The Board concurred with Member Bacher's comments.

Regarding (cc), Member Godfrey recommended adding language that reads "proposed hazard mitigation measures, if any" to show what will be put into place. Town Planner Crean noted that when an applicant goes for a CRMC permit, their application requires such, however, does not require implementing any hazard mitigation measures. If the Board wants to require, it would be a higher standard than CRMC. Member Godfrey noted these extend beyond CRMC jurisdictions, and the Board should be made aware of such measures. The Board concurred with the suggestion.

Friedrichs noted that requirement (cc) is also in the development checklist, which may be a more appropriate spot than a subdivision project. Member Godfrey agreed with Friedrichs comment, and that mitigation measures are important to see with projects. Town Planner Crean noted access, and is a point to revisit with the Climate Action Plan and Comprehensive Plan.

### **Attachment 3 - Development Checklist:**

Friedrichs reviewed the formatting of the development checklist, noting the differences between this one and the subdivision checklist. Friedrichs noted one of the differences is that there is no requirement for a deed restriction because not deeding land.

Member Bacher suggested making a similar change as the subdivision checklist, moving requirement #4 to requirement #6. On requirement #8, Member Bacher suggested language from the MTAP checklist, including density calculation, including:

- ➢ undevelopable land
- ➢ an estimate of the approximate population (if any) and an estimate of the number of schoolaged children to be housed in the development (if any). These two items feed into the potential fiscal impact, to be included.

Friedrichs noted the density calculation in the comprehensive permit requirements, and expressed concern that it has been historically used to deny projects. Town Planner Crean also noted that annually she reports to the School Department on an estimate. Assistant Solicitor Goins concurred with the recommendation to leave it out of the checklists. Goins commented that it is appropriate to include a fiscal impact study for a large project but wants to see the Board have flexibility when to require, as it is not always necessary for smaller projects. Member Bacher noted the anticipated comprehensive permit application at 33 Middle Highway, and he wants to see it required now. Assistant Solicitor Goins noted the difference between completeness versus evidence before the Board, and how without it the burden of proof cannot be satisfied.

Member A. Mueller asked for more information about how fiscal impact elements have been used to deny applications. Friedrichs provided more context, specifically noting the estimate on school-aged children. Member Godfrey concurred with Assistant Solicitor Goins recommendation, and how the Town would turn the application down if the applicant did not provide that information as evidence, and the developer will provide it because they know it will be brought up at the public hearing.

Member Kurtz noted that specifying every nut and bolt could lead to complications. Member Bacher noted that he believes the Board could regret this, but does not hear support from other members of the Board. Assistant Solicitor Goins noted that the checklist is universal for certification of completeness, which is distinguished from evidence to satisfy the Board that the application meets the findings, where there is discretion on a case-by-case basis.

Friedrichs noted requirement #17, the traffic study item and the threshold for such. Member Bacher asked for clarification on "100 vehicle trips per hour." Member Bacher noted the current standard of 20 parking spots is sufficient. Vice Chair Rua asked if that could be a reason for denial, and noted how that could be in conflict with other goals, such as circulation goals with complete streets and bike-pedestrian initiatives. The Board discussed and generally agreed that the 20 parking spots is

clearer than 100 vehicle trips per hour. Friedrichs referred to Assistant Solicitor's Goins earlier comment that if the Board wants additional information, they can request as such. Friedrichs noted each have pros and cons, and could become a battle of the experts, however, the Town has on-calls that could assist with the recommendation. Assistant Solicitor Goins reiterated the difference between the checklists and evidence for findings. Vice Chair Rua noted the language proposed allows for greater flexibility. Assistant Solicitor Goins concurred. Member Bacher noted that the Board may be left confused. The Board concurred that the item should be "if more than 20 spaces are proposed that a traffic study is required" keeping from the former checklists.

Member Bacher noted the locus map requirement only being required at Master Plan, which would be a problem for Comprehensive Permit applications. Friedrichs made a note to make that edit to make sure it is required for Comprehensive Permits.

Friedrichs then moved to drawing submission requirements. Member Bacher noted language from the MTAP template regarding surveys. Friedrichs noted he did not include a survey for a development project. Assistant Solicitor Goins reviewed when a survey is required and not, according to Rhode Island General Law. Clerk Gillibrand reviewed further the Zoning Board's process for surveys, and when they may or may not require, though noting it is not a condition for submittal. Vice Chair Rua noted that a survey is required as part of the building permit process, so if deficiencies are found then it gets sounds back to the Board. Clerk Gillibrand confirmed that the Building Official does require as-builts. Assistant Solicitor Goins noted how boundary disputes between abutters is a separate matter from the Board. Town Planner Crean briefly reviewed the permit process workflow.

Member Godfrey noted added a topographic plan as a requirement here as well, and, for future consideration, asking for a 100-year flood map as opposed to a 3-foot sea-level rise map under C(3). Town Planner Crean noted the Town cannot be more stringent than the building code, which currently follows FEMA floodplain maps. The Town can incentivize and educate on code plus activities on their properties but cannot be required. Member Godfrey asked why it is in the checklist. Town Planner Crean noted CRMC requirements and using the same tool to educate on sea-level rise risk, so the Town is staying on top of potential risks. Member Godfrey commented that the 3-foot sea-level rise may not be the best education tool, but instead the 1-foot or 50-year. Town Planner Crean asked Friedrichs if this item is in the MTAP templates and gave more context and information on sea-level rise data. The Board agreed to stick to the FEMA map in items B(11) and C(3) as that is in the building code, noting to circle back and adjust with findings from the Climate Action Plan.

*The Chair opened the public hearing.* 

Hugh Morgan, 6 Beaver Road, concurred with Member Bacher's comments on requiring the fiscal impact on the Town.

Vice Chair Rua noted the Bicycle-Pedestrian Advisory Committee's recommendation to add review by the Committee to the checklist, and Friedrichs confirmed that that was added to the checklist.

*The Chair closed the public hearing.* 

**MOTION: Vice Chair Rua seconded by Member Godfrey, made a motion to accept the revisions to the checklists of the Land Development and Subdivision Regulations as amended and revised during this meeting. Motion carried unanimously.**
