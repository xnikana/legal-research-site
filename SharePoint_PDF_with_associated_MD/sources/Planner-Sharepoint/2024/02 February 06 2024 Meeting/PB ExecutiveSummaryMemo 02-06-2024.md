# **4.1 Discuss and Act: Planning Board's Recommended Six-Year Capital Improvement Program, FY25**

The CIP Subcommittee meeting took place Monday, January 29, 2024. Capital Improvement Program inputs were reviewed with Town Departments and budget line-item numbers were adjusted as outlined in the provided summary table and separate memorandum.

![](PB ExecutiveSummaryMemo 02-06-2024_attachments/_page_0_Picture_21.jpeg)

The CIP Subcommittee recommendation for Capital Budget and Capital Improvement Program for the following Six-year period is reflected in the attached PDF/Excel Sheet entitled:

"[FY2025\\_CIP\\_SummaryTable\\_01-30-2024](https://twnbarrintonri-my.sharepoint.com/:f:/g/personal/igillibrand_barrington_ri_gov/Ep4q7y7jwiZAgExsriH30zoBtpK6iVXTIJlP95vnfqJ04Q?e=c1ZcgW)" with a recommended Capital Budget for FY2025 of

# **\$1,686,500.00, a \$29,500 increase compared to the \$1,657,000 approved in FY24, a 1.75% increase.**

In addition, the Board is asked to review the Harbormaster's request of \$28,000, which was missed at the January 29 meeting and therefore not reflected in the FY2025 CIP Summary Table. There is a [Department](https://twnbarrintonri-my.sharepoint.com/:b:/g/personal/igillibrand_barrington_ri_gov/EcN0x2yqFoJAt189vk_OTNwB9F_8D_Ox-ejUwPzvDfa5qw?e=hh4bA9)  [Input Form](https://twnbarrintonri-my.sharepoint.com/:b:/g/personal/igillibrand_barrington_ri_gov/EcN0x2yqFoJAt189vk_OTNwB9F_8D_Ox-ejUwPzvDfa5qw?e=hh4bA9) included with the packet materials outlining the request.

# **Harbormaster Requests:**

- ➢ **\$20,000 for Boat Replacement** The boats are aging out of usability. Edgewater is a 2005 (over 18+ years old) and will need significant funds to maintain at a minimum level of quality function. Replacement cost is over \$100,000.
- ➢ **\$8,000 for Navigational Aids/Boat Equipment** An average of 5-10 navigational aids/buoys are worn and unusable need replacing each year. Average cost per buoy is approximately \$1,800 each. The residual funds would be used for general maintenance of boat equipment and replacement engines as they age out.

Adding the \$28,000 brings the CIP total to \$1,714,500, which is a 3.35% increase from FY24 CIP, rather than the 1.75% increase at the end of the 29th meeting.

Staff are inquiring with Chief Correia to see if the current \$59K in the account for Harbormaster is encumbered between now and end of FY24, and when the boat is expected to be replaced that will require the \$100K expenditure.

As confirmed by Assistant Solicitor Goins, the full Planning Board is to address this and incorporate into final recommendation.
