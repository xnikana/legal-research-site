Waivers for buildings
1. Waivers in general – Barrington has regulations to create the type of town we want,
why would we wave any, never mind 15 of them
2. Waive 300 square feet open space/unit – Building mostly 2 & 3 bedroom units
means kids.
a. Most kids don’t care about pocket parks and walking trails
b. The parks looked nice in the presentation but the only kid in pictures was a

nobody on the “open space”. What good are open spaces that nobody wants
or use?
c. Mounded grass between the curb and sidewalk is not useful open space.
d. The planting blueprint and technical data in the plan make the green space
look nice but not very useable.
e. Not only are the proposed open spaces the wrong type, but they are also
too small. I would oppose waiving the minimum open space per unit.

a. Width of road?

4. Waive 35% of impervious surfaces per lot increased to 60% across the entire site.
a. The developer is asking for a waiver “per lot”. In response to the question on 3
March they responded, “per site”. I conclude they could barely get down to
60% by including the retention ponds, grass between the curb and sidewalk,
walking trails and wooded/unbuildable land never mind per lot.
b. The lots are going to look urban, which does not fit the community.
c. If they have excessive impervious surfaces, what do they do with the runoff?
d. Deceptive answers and unanswered questions brings up questions
about what they are hiding/planning. I would oppose waiving the
impervious surfaces minimum.
