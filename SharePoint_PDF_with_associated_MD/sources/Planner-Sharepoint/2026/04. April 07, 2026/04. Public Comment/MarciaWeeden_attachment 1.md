Re: School Resource Officers & Belton Court
Dear Above Committee Members:
My letter is in response to RI ACLU Executive Director Steven Brown’s January 27, 2026
letter to RI ACLU supporters regarding the School Committee’s consideration of adding more
resource officers at the Barrington Middle School and Barrington’s elementary schools.
As a quality assurance professional with expertise in root cause analysis and effective
corrective and preventive actions, and a Barrington resident who moved into town on
November 26, 1955, I would like to present my objections why such additions would be
ineffective and a waste of money. For starters, please read my attached October 8, 2024 letter
to your committee regarding an additional resource officer. My opinions have not changed.
Moving on, I ask that the committee consider alternatives to resource officers.
Specifically, currently debate is happening regarding Belton Court. This week, the Barrington
Times published an article that your committee has asked for a study regarding the impacts on
the Barrington school system should the proposed apartments be developed.
It is still within the town’s authority to decide what ultimately happens with Belton
Court. While currently I have no idea where the funding would come from, I do know that the
town has many residents with access to organizations and private citizens who could contribute
to the funding. Ambitious as it may be, money could be raised for a project that serves the
community.
Rather than permitting 344 housing units to be built, could the town purchase the
property from its current owners to develop it into a campus dedicated to providing education
opportunities for children and adults? Those education opportunities need not be restricted to
the Town of Barrington.
Perhaps Belton Court could become a satellite campus working in conjunction with URI

adults, teens, and children. There could be classes for legal professionals, community
