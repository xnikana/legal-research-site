Planning Board,
Sadly, I have an additional report of a very recent basement flooding disaster at my home to
report. I ask that it be added to your growing collection of neighborhood impact statements.
This is our THIRD flooding event since we purchased this home in 2018, eight years ago.
Date: March 14, 2026
Location: 18 Great Road
In short, due to the overwhelming amount of groundwater resulting from the melting of over
3 feet of snow that fell during the recent blizzard coupled with seasonal rainfall, our two
sump pumps could not keep up with the task of continually pumping out of water from
underneath our home. The constant running of our sumps and the pressure of the high
volume of water caused one of the pipe joints to loosen and eventually give way allowing
water to spray out all over our basement. This occurred undetected for hours.
Our teenage son, home alone at the time, went into our finished basement to watch TV only
to discover wet carpeting. Concerned, he ventured into our unfinished basement to check the
sump pumps and made the discovery. Thankfully, he immediately called 911 and the fire
department arrived. They quickly discovered that water was spraying directly onto our
electrical panel! The firemen were able to reconnect the pipe joint and turn off our
electricity. We returned home around this time and together worked until 3 am using two
shop vacuums to remove the 1-2" of water throughout our basement - unfinished and
finished - and also removed all the wet items bringing them up to the garage to dry. Sadly,
we lost some irreplaceable keepsakes from my husband's childhood.
The next day we cut and hauled out all the wall-to-wall carpeting and installed countless box
fans to mitigate any growth of mold. I do not yet have a total estimate of the cost of these
damages.
