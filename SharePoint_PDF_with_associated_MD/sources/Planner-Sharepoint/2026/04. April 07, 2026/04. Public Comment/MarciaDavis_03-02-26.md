To the Representatives of the Town of Barrington,
You have the power to insist on stabilization of the Belton Court property to stop further
deterioration at the owner's expense. This has gone on far too long.
Rhode Island's Property Maintenance Code 2019 requires that all vacant structures be
maintained in a safe and secure condition to prevent blight:
301.3 Vacant Structures and Land
"Vacant structures and premises thereof or vacant land shall be maintained in a clean,
safe, secure and sanitary condition as provided herein so as not to cause a blighting
problem or adversely affect the public health or safety."
It is time to make this owner abide by the law.
Developers who intentionally allow historic properties to deteriorate, a practice known as "
demolition by neglect", are generally considered untrustworthy by community members,
as this tactic is used to justify demolition. This passive destruction often involves ignoring
maintenance to force a redevelopment, which can result in the loss of irreplaceable,
landmarked, or community-significant structures. Property owners may use demolition by
neglect as a tactic and will often argue that the prohibitive cost of repairs and deferred
maintenance creates an economic hardship.
https://www.preserveri.org/demolition-by-neglect
Why haven't you been enforcing this law?
Belton Court is an asset to the Town of Barrington and it is your responsibility as Town
Manager, Town Selectmen and Selectwomen, Town Planner, and Planning Board
Members to know the law and protect the town's assets.
Please act now.
