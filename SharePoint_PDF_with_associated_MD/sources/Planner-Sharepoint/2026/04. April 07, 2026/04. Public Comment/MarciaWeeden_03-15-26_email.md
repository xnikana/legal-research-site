Hi, Herb,
Thank you for getting back to me so quickly.
I think my information should be presented to the Planning Board because I feel it would be
helpful for them to be aware of possible uses for the property and put the other proposals
into better perspectives. Depending on what they decide that information can be later
presented to the Town Manager.
To elaborate a little on my suggestions, I see Belton Court as a hub of information, programs,
and training on topics that are not traditionally covered at public schools (the three Rs) but are
now needed given how society has evolved.
Let the basics be created and offered at Belton Court, and they can be modified as
appropriate at RI's 39 municipalities as desired.
There is no need for each municipality to start from scratch to respond to its local needs. Let
Belton Court provide the foundation and expertise for RI's municipalities so each community
or organization can carry that foundation back and use as appropriate. The cost and time
savings to bypass development and assistance for implementation would be highly welcomed,
I'm sure.
There is also the benefit of expanding Rhode Island's sense of community if the municipalities
are willing to offer their experience, ideas, and/or volunteers to towns and cities in need of
them.
Let's not overlook how Rhode Island's size provides a unique laboratory to address many
different lifestyles and economic classes that would be difficult to implement and assess in
large states. That dynamic might possibly draw funding and support from higher education
schools, organizations, or institutions interested in research and outreach.
Finally, Barrington's public schools are considered among the best in New England. Adding a
hub such as I am suggesting would further enhance Barrington's reputation as being among
the best for education. There is no reason why we cannot add another (Eagle's) feather to
Barrington's cap.

Dear Ms. Elliot and Mr. Durfee:
My name is Marcia Weeden. I am a quality assurance professional who is also a long-term
resident of Barrington.
Attached are two letters that I have written to the Barrington School Committee regarding a
second student resource officer and possible ways to utilize Belton Court to benefit all
residents. I ask that you read them as I detail my positions in those letters.
I strongly urge the Town to consider utilizing Belton Court as a learning resource center not
just for Barrington, but for all the municipalities in RI. Organizations and higher-education
institutions could be involved as well.
Collaborative efforts lower costs, build communities, and help prepare children to understand
others and learn coping skills for dealing with today's society and its demands. Belton Court
could be fashioned into a beautiful campus that is open and welcoming to all.
A student resource officer is a band-aid. It is well documented how ineffective band-aids are
and the poor return that they give to investments. On the other hand, getting to the root
source of unacceptable behavior and taking actions to eliminate and mitigate those sources,
e.g., offering education that presents effective and ways to deal with an overwhelming and
confusing society and unacceptable behavior, provide high returns on investments.
One area of possible training might have to do with bullying. The interest is there. Bullying &
School Violence | RI Department of Education Rhode Island has 39 municipalities. Rather
than expecting 39 municipalities to respond to bullying, combine those resources to create
approaches that support and reinforce each other.
