Dear Planning Board Staff,
I am writing this note in an effort to expand the scope of questions that I feel need
to addressed by ShineHarmony in reference to the proposed Belton Court (Zion
property)project.
I know that Ms. Hartman and Dr. Art Solomon have brought attention to the issue
of long standing surface water mismanagement by the Belton Court owners. The
engineers from Insite Environmental Services have presented preliminary plans for
water management but have left some significant concerns out of the discussions.
One major concern is ground water management. Most homes surrounding the Zion
property have sump pumps that run almost year round. The planned excavation of
the Belton Court property will create approximately 7 to 10 acres of new basements
that will likely need continuous sump activity. Where will this water be directed and
how will the meager planned retention ponds handle this volume?
Addionally, the existing Zion property has a significant volume of it's surface water
drained through an easement that goes to the retention pond of Lion's Head
Homeowner's Association. This fact was illustrated by the Insite engineer at the
February Planning Board meeting. We need to know what the new plan is for the
existing easement. Will the new project continue to drain into Lion's Head retention
basin? Will this easement be filled in or left as an overflow feature?
Finally, I would like to know if the planned project will need to upgrade all the
sewer lines that run through the Lion's Head neighborhood? The Insite engineers
pointed out that they plan to use the sewer lines that are between 50 and 75 years
old to handle the sewerage volume for for 344 units. My suspicion is that these
existing lines cannot possibly manage the projected volume. New sewer lines would
create a very messy excavation of our neighborhood, especially if we get the long
awaited repaving of our roads as planned for 2026. It would be shameful and short
sighted to have to dig them up soon after repaving them.
Please add these concerns and questions to your list for upcoming Planning Board
meetings with Shine Harmony.
