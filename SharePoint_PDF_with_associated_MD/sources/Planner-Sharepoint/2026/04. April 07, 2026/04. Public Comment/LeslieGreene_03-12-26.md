Dear Members of the Barrington Planning Board,
My name is Leslie Greene, and I have lived in Barrington since 2005.
I would like to share a personal experience that illustrates the very real concerns many of us have regarding
stormwater management and drainage in our neighborhood, particularly as you consider the proposed
development at Belton Court.
In February of 2022, during a severe rainstorm, my daughter woke me at approximately 2:00 a.m. because water
was entering our basement. When I investigated, I discovered that the window wells near the basement bulkhead
were filled with rainwater. The pressure of the water overwhelmed the windows, and water began pouring directly
into the basement.
My daughter and I went outside in the middle of the night, in freezing temperatures and pouring rain on top of
snow-covered ground, attempting to bail water from the window wells. Despite our e(cid:431)orts for more than an hour,
we were unable to keep up with the rate of water accumulation. It was a frightening experience, and ultimately, we
had no choice but to stop.
By morning, the damage was extensive. My partially finished basement—approximately 1,400 square feet—was
flooded. Wall-to-wall carpeting, furniture, and personal belongings were destroyed. Because the incident was
classified by my insurer as an “act of God,” the damage was not covered by my homeowner’s insurance. I paid
more than $3,000 for cleanup and remediation. In addition, I hired a contractor, Dave Ramos of Bristol, to install a
French drain system at a cost of approximately $6,000 to prevent a similar event in the future.
I share this experience because it highlights how vulnerable existing homes in our area already are to heavy
rainfall and drainage challenges. Any large-scale development that significantly increases impervious surfaces
and residential density has the potential to further strain the local stormwater system. For those of us living
nearby, the concern is not theoretical—it is deeply personal.
I respectfully urge the Planning Board to carefully evaluate the stormwater management, drainage capacity, and
downstream impacts associated with the proposed Belton Court development to ensure that existing residents
and properties are not placed at greater risk of flooding.
