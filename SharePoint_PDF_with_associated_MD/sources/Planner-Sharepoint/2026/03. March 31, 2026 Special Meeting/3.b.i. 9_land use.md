Overview
Barrington is largely a mosaic of contiguous residential neighborhoods. In recent years, hous-
ing demand, both rental and ownership, has outstripped availability. Barrington must adopt
strategies to better meet demand while simultaneously preserving the distinct characteristics
that draw residents in the first place. In practice, this entails allowing for meaningful growth
that does not outpace the Town’s ability to maintain a high level of facilities and services.
Changes to the Zoning Ordinance and/or Land Development and Subdivision Regulations are
proposed to encourage increased density in local growth centers and neighborhood-
compatible development elsewhere, ensuring that future changes reflect and respect Barring-
ton’s historic pattern of development without compromising the provision of affordable hous-
ing in sync with the statewide goal for affordable units, unique natural setting, and geographic
constraints.
