                 c. Discuss and Act: Open Space designation for “Round Park”        
                                                                                    
          Planner Durfee presented an overview of the proposed open space designation for Round Park. He
          explained that federal conservation funds had previously been used at Veterans Park and that the
          later installation of a telecommunications tower conflicted with federal funding restrictions. The
          Town, working with RIDEM as its advocate, was pursuing an off-site mitigation strategy to avoid
          removal of the tower or repayment of federal funds.                       
          Planner Durfee stated that the Town proposed using Round Park, a Town-owned parcel near North
          Lake Drive, South Lake Drive, and the East Bay Bike Path, as mitigation. Proposed improvements
          included minimal recreational amenities such as benches, bike racks, and a bike path connection. He
          explained that the current issue was that Round Park was zoned Residential 25, whereas Veterans
          Park was designated as open space active recreation, and that the zoning and land use designation
          would need to be aligned for mitigation purposes.                         
                                                                                    
          Member Kilmartin supported the proposal, citing pedestrian and bicycle safety concerns at the
          nearby roadway curve and stating that formalizing Round Park as open space active recreation
          would improve connectivity and safety. Member Simms asked about the origin of the issue,
          consequences of not mitigating, and the likelihood that the proposed mitigation would be accepted.
          Planner Durfee responded that the issue likely arose through a funding review or audit and that
          failure to mitigate could result in repayment of federal funds or jeopardize future funding. He stated
          that preliminary feedback from state and federal agencies had been positive, provided the process
          was followed carefully.

                     i.  Land Use                                                   
          Chair Phipps opened discussion and action on the Comprehensive Plan. Planner Durfee noted that
          Board members had received draft Comprehensive Plan maps and related materials prepared by the
          Town Manager. He explained that, because some materials were distributed within 48 hours of the
          meeting, they were not formally part of the record and should not yet be transmitted to the State. He
          recommended that the Board discuss the maps but delay any formal action until they could be

          Chair Phipps stated that the Board would begin with land use and invited public comment from
          Walter Stitt, who had submitted a letter regarding housing needs for disabled adults.
                                                                                    
          Walter Stitt, 71 Vialls Street, spoke in support of explicitly addressing housing for disabled adults in
          the Comprehensive Plan, emphasizing the need for community-based, least-restrictive living options
          and citing examples of supported housing developments in Jamestown and Cape Cod.
          Chair Phipps and Member Kilmartin responded that housing for residents with physical, intellectual,
          and developmental disabilities was already included in the draft Housing Element. Member
          Kilmartin summarized Policy 1.3, which supported accessible and supportive housing, use of federal
          and state funding, and siting housing near services and transportation to encourage independent
          living. Mr. Stitt stated that the language addressed his concerns.        
                                                                                    
          Member Kilmartin further noted that the Board had discussed potential strategies, including
          encouraging accessible housing in walkable areas and near transit, while acknowledging the
          challenges of identifying specific sites. She referenced Courier Park as a possible opportunity site
          due to its proximity to transit and town ownership, while noting its physical constraints.
          Member Bacher cautioned against identifying specific sites in the Comprehensive Plan, noting that
          the Town would not act as a developer and that nonprofit developers typically approach the Town
          with proposed locations. He stated that the Plan should emphasize support and permissive zoning
          rather than site selection.                                               
                                                                                    
          Solicitor Goins advised that the Rhode Island Zoning Enabling Act already permitted community
          residences in all residential zoning districts and preempted local zoning restrictions for licensed
          group homes serving individuals with disabilities. She explained that such uses were protected under
          state law.

                     ii. Maps                                                       
          Land Use and Future Land Use Map:                                         
                                                                                    
          The Board reviewed the Land Use element of the Comprehensive Plan and the associated Future
          Land Use Map, emphasizing that the materials were still in draft form and not ready for formal
          action. Members stressed the importance of ensuring that prior Board discussions and guidance are
          accurately reflected in both the narrative and mapping, with Durfee explaining that meeting
          transcripts would be used to relay clear direction to the Town Manager.   
          The Board agreed that the Land Use chapter should provide long-term, town-wide planning
          guidance rather than reacting parcel by parcel to past comprehensive permits. Members noted that
          rezoning following comprehensive permit approvals should be strategic and infrequent, undertaken
          only when broader land-use patterns or future planning objectives clearly support a change.
                                                                                    
          A recurring theme was balancing state planning guidance—including expectations for higher
          residential densities within the urban services boundary—with local conditions, such as flood zones,
          wetlands, infrastructure capacity, and established neighborhood character. Members emphasized that
          high-density residential designations should be avoided in flood-prone or environmentally sensitive
          areas and that local context should be clearly explained through narrative where state and local
          approaches differ.

          Member Kilmartin added that several items had been relocated to the Facilities and Services section,
          including stormwater management systems and certain solar-related topics, in order to tighten and
          shorten the Climate Resilience section.                                   
                                                                                    
          Member Bacher stated that he had several comments and would submit them in writing, but raised
          one issue for discussion regarding the inclusion of a Solarize Barrington program. He explained that
          Barrington’s previous Solarize effort had been part of a statewide and national initiative administered
          by the Office of Energy Resources, which provided contracts, marketing, and administrative
          support. He stated that the town lacked the capacity, staffing, and resources to run such a program
          independently and that many residents had already installed solar panels, reducing the potential
          participation base. He expressed concern about including recommendations in the plan that the
          town could not realistically implement and recommended removing references to a Solarize
          program.                                                                  
          Member Kilmartin stated that this assessment made sense to her, noting that several incentive and
          matching programs at the federal level had been discontinued. Member Bacher added that the town
          had not conducted contractor vetting during the prior Solarize effort and lacked the staff and
          liability capacity to do so. He suggested that, instead, the town could direct residents to existing
          external programs that maintain vetted contractor lists.                  
                                                                                    
          Chair Phipps asked whether other members had comments regarding the solar program, maps, or
          related topics. Member Costa stated that he agreed with the direction of the discussion and
          supported limiting recommendations to feasible actions. He noted that solar installations on
          municipal buildings could be framed as recommendations but agreed that a large-scale Solarize
          program was not practical. Member Costa stated that he found the section well written and had no
          additional comments.                                                      
                                                                                    
          Member Kilmartin stated that she and Member Bacher would forward their written comments to
          the Town Manager. Member Kilmartin proposed returning to the prior position of forwarding the
          Climate Resilience and Mitigation chapter to the state for review with edits and revisions, noting that

          Planner Durfee stated that receiving state feedback did not preclude the Board from making further

          needed to be changed. Member Kilmartin responded that the title could be determined by the
          Board, provided that all required statutory elements were addressed, noting that the chapter
