Good evening Herb,

I understand that the deadline to submit public comments to the Planning Board to be considered at the February 3rd public meeting has passed. However, it appears that the public comment portion of the meeting will continue for at least another month. Nevertheless, I am planning on attending tomorrow's meeting as a nearby resident of this property. I also wanted to offer my initial comments to the proposed development, with specific emphasis on the traffic study:

- We should confirm that the Traffic Study was performed under the supervision of a registered Professional Engineer (P.E.). In Massachusetts, it is becoming customary that the study reports are submitted with a stamp and signed by a P.E.
- My opinion is that the study area considered is not consistent with a development of this size. At a minimum, I'd suggest that the intersection of Wampanoag Trail at Primrose Hill Road is included.
- The report identifies the deficient quality/absence of amenities for people walking and biking, but delegates the improvement of these amenities to others (State or town). Moreover, the amenities for people walking and biking are noted, but no analysis is provided for current volumes of people walking and biking nor level of stress for current or future conditions.
- The study was completed in September 2024, before the resurfacing and sidewalk work along County Street was performed. It would need to be updated with current conditions.
- The crash analysis notes a majority of crashes of angle and rear end collisions taking place at the County Road/Middle Highway signalized intersection. The appendices note that all the crashes were low severity. However, while the report notes that these types of crashes are typical, this statement generally applies to rear end crashes not right angle collisions. Right angle collisions appear more often at this intersection due to the imbalance of traffic patterns that take place at the signal, especially in the northbound direction. Most traffic makes a left turn to go west compared with the portion of traffic that goes through or turns right to go east. The additional traffic to be generated by the development will further exacerbate this conflict increasing the likelihood and occurrence of this type of crashes.
- The safety analysis is following old practice of reactive (looking at historic patterns) rather than a more modern approach of predictive crashes described in the Highway Safety Manual. Moreover, no analysis is offered for the safety of people using the roadways outside of a motor vehicle.
- The traffic study focuses on the traffic flow efficiency and safety, reflected on the motor vehicle focus of the analysis, but it lacks the projection and evaluation of future patterns for people

walking and biking.

- The traffic data was collected in two different periods, September 2024 and December 2023. The analysis collected people using the intersections, but it does not provide a summary of the people using the roadways outside of a motor vehicle. Moreover, the traffic data collection did not include vehicle classification. There is no percentage of heavy vehicles collected. Given some of the grades in the roadways part of the area of study, they are critical to better reflect motor vehicle operations.
- The traffic study states to distribute the generated traffic patterns based on census data for employment destinations. While it notes that half of the traffic is to leave towards and return from the north, the assumed traffic patterns missed a basic local network pattern. Most people looking to go north would use Primrose Hill Road to leave and return from places to the north given that Wampanoag Trails is a more efficient roadway. Under the current assumed trip distribution the amount of traffic on Primrose Hill Road would be expected to double. The impact on this roadway would only increase once the trip distribution is adjusted to reflect the lower friction routes.
- One big piece missing on the analysis is the analysis of no build conditions. Without it, it is hard to assess the true impact of the development as it misses the description of the conditions in the future without the development. While this step can be omitted on some cases, for a development of this size, it is necessary to have this analysis to determine the expected impacts of this development.
- While this report was completed before the Complete Streets Implementation Plan was complete and adopted, the previous version of the Complete Streets Plan was adopted in August 2022. It is sad to see that the traffic study does not include a single mention about it.
- As noted above, the report collected data in December 2023. Unfortunately, there is no information presented that accounts for people traveling on the roadways outside of a motor vehicle. Moreover, data collected in winter is very likely to miss the amount of people using the roadways walking and biking. As many of my neighbors can attest, most of the people using the roadways walking and biking are children on their way to and from school. Without proper amenities for them, the additional motor vehicle traffic to be generated by the proposed redevelopment would further increase the likelihood of a collision of one of them with a motor vehicle. As you may recall, not too long ago a resident of another neighborhood in town suffered the loss of a dog as a result of a car collision. That roadway (Ferry Road) has lower speeds, lower volumes and lower roadway classification than the roadways surrounding the Zion property. Without proper amenities and speed management mitigation measures provided by the proposed development.
- While I certainly welcome residential development for the area, i strongly urge that the Town requests the applicant to provide appropriate mitigation for the impacts that it would have to the neighborhood, enhancing the amenities for people walking and biking, speed management measures and provisions to extend transit service/facilitate access to transit to reduce the number of trip taking place in a car.
- Finally, I urge the town to request a formal peer review that can better analyze the traffic study beyond my 10,000 feet review of the document.

Thank you so much for sharing these comments with the Planning Board.
