To the Members of the Barrington Planning Board:

As a Barrington homeowner and long time resident, I am writing to urge you to reject the Belton Court Village proposal as it is currently presented. This project fails to meet the basic standards of responsible planning, and it does not reflect the needs, capacity, or character of our community. The proposal must not move forward in its present form.

One of the most important issues is the future of the Belton Court mansion. This historic structure is a defining part of Barrington's heritage, and its preservation must be a non negotiable requirement of any redevelopment. Allowing the mansion to deteriorate under absentee ownership has already been a serious failure of stewardship. Approving a plan that does not guarantee its restoration and long term protection would compound that failure. Retaining and restoring the mansion is essential to honoring the history of this property and the identity of our town.

It is also clear that the original intent for this site, a development focused on serving the elderly, is far more appropriate for the location, the surrounding neighborhoods, and the capacity of our municipal services. A senior oriented community would create far less strain on schools, traffic, and emergency services. It would also align with the demographic needs of Barrington, which continues to see growth in its older population. Returning this project to an elderly focused plan is not only reasonable but necessary if the town is to avoid the severe impacts associated with the current proposal.

The scale of the present plan, 344 units, far exceeds what our schools, roads, and public services can support. The School Committee has already raised significant concerns and requested a demographic impact study. Moving forward without that information would be irresponsible. School capacity is a measurable constraint, and if the town cannot absorb the projected enrollment, that alone constitutes a substantial and unmitigated impact that justifies denial.

Traffic and public safety concerns are equally serious. The proposed density would overload Middle Highway and surrounding corridors, increasing congestion and slowing emergency response times. These are core public safety issues. If a project compromises emergency access or creates unsafe traffic conditions, the Board is obligated to deny it.

Municipal services and utilities also face limits that this proposal does not meaningfully address. Water, sewer, stormwater management, and emergency services all require capacity that must be demonstrated, not assumed. When a development introduces more demand than the town can support, and when mitigation is insufficient or unproven, the Board has clear grounds to reject the application.

It is also important to acknowledge that this treasured property is being shaped by an out of state and foreign owned investment firm with no meaningful ties to Barrington. The Board represents the residents of this town, not the financial interests of ShineHarmony or the consultants they hire. Local heritage, community character, and responsible land use are legitimate planning considerations, and they matter here.

This proposal is not aligned with Barrington's capacity, its infrastructure, or its long term interests. The impacts are substantial, unmitigated, and well documented. Under the Comprehensive Permit framework, when the burdens of a project outweigh the benefits, the Board has both the authority and the responsibility to deny it.

For these reasons, I respectfully urge you to reject the Belton Court Village proposal in its current form. Any future plan must preserve the mansion and return the project to an elderly focused development that aligns with the needs and capacity of our community.

Thank you for your time and for your service to Barrington.
