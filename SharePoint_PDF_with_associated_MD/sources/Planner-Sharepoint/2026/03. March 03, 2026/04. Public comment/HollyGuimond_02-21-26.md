## Hello:

I would like to add my concerns to the list vocalized by my neighbors regarding the proposed Shineharmony Development.

My husband Jeff and I, along with our two children live at 4 Stanhope Dr. just below the proposed development site.

I say below because even though you don't think of our neighborhood as being situated on a hill we are in fact built at a lower elevation than the Mansion itself on land that was once part of the Peck Estate.

As the Barrington Flood Zone maps continue to expand with rising tides our lower neighborhood creeps closer and closer to being within the flood zone. As storms continue worsen and happen more frequently we are at the mercy of an older infrastructure and mature trees that were once part of the orchard and grounds of the historic estate.

In March of 2018, roughly 6 months after having my second baby we had yet another bad rain storm that took out power in our neighborhood. It took days not hours to restore the power. We had 2 sump pumps installed by the previous owners, with a battery back up so weren't concerned at first. But as the days went on the battery didn't last and our basement filled with 8000 gallons of water. Without a generator to continue to keep the pumps running the water continued to rise and rise. When the water level reached the bottom of our basement

staircase we had to call the fire department to come shut off our utilities so the our house would not be susceptible to fire or electrocution. The fire department was wonderful and friendly and actually set up a gravity operated pipe connected to one of their fire hoses to help pump the water once again after stopped working without a power source.

Next we had to pay a disaster relief company to come with a vacuum truck to get rid of the rest of the water and attend to any mold issues that may arise. They record the number of gallons that they pump out, which is how we learned just how much water came through our foundation flooding our heating system, washer and dryer and many prized possessions being stored down there. After this we invested quite a bit in a generator so that we would not find ourselves in this position again.

We have lost power again many times since that storm. But in the severe storm that happened this past July 4th, large trees on the Shineharmony property played a huge roll in taking out power in this area and blocking passage on Middle Highway for some residents.

If Shineharmony is allowed to proceed with frankly any large development whether senior housing or affordable units this will increase our risk of flooding in the future. We will be impacted by additional run off water run off from hundreds of new residents living in the area. As well as disrupt the water table from the removal of trees and excavation that will be required for new construction.

I respectfully request that Shineharmony not be granted permission to move forward with their development.
