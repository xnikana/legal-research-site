## Public Comments - Zion Bible College/Belton Court Property Development

From Cindy Elder

Date Thu 1/29/2026 3:02 PM

To Genevieve Elliot <GElliot@barrington.ri.gov>; Herbert Durfee III <hdurfee@barrington.ri.gov>

Cc Andrew Curtis

Dear Town of Barrington officials and the Barrington Planning Board,

I am writing in my capacity as Executive Director of the Barrington Land Conservation Trust to offer public comment on the proposal submitted by Shineharmony Holdings, Inc. for a 344-unit housing project on the former Zion Bible College property/Belton Court in Barrington. I am also a long-time resident of Barrington. I would appreciate the opportunity to speak at the public meeting on Feb. 3 and read this into the record.

The Land Trust Board is deeply concerned about the density of the proposal. We were dismayed by the extensive requests for waivers and allowances that run counter to the Town's commitment to open space and responsible development. We anticipate spillover consequences of this development on the Town's natural resources, which are shared by all.

We recognize the Town's responsibility to create affordable housing which will make Barrington more accessible and welcoming to all. However, this proposal's commitment to affordable housing is dubious and comes at a great public cost.

We cannot expect to build 344 residences on less than 40 acres without negatively impacting our already strained stormwater management systems. Even with parking and lane sizes that fall short of the Town's requirements, impervious surfaces would cover 60% of the property, not including building lots. At a time of rising sea levels, this seems an irresponsible and short-sighted plan. Many neighbors of the property already experience problems with runoff.

Shineharmony's requests to bypass open space requirements could have far-reaching negative impacts on our community, well beyond the bounds of this property. It would establish a pathway for development that wished to run roughshod over established regulations.

We recognize that the Town cannot purchase and preserve every piece of open space that comes on the market. Taxpayers ultimately fund these acquisitions. Likewise, the Land Trust does not have the resources to purchase land at market prices. We depend on land donations, philanthropists and support from local, state and federal sources to preserve open space. We also don't acquire land with existing buildings, as we don't have the ability to maintain them.

We anticipate this property will be developed in some fashion. As a community, we must let our voices be heard if we wish to limit the scope of this development and maintain some control over the scale of future development.

The open space in our town benefits everyone. Natural walking trails, open meadows, forests and salt marshes improve the quality of life for our community while serving as a buffer against the effects of climate change. The Shineharmony proposal chips away at the regulations which affect our ability to preserve open space throughout our town, not just at this site.

This property also includes a landmark listed on the National Register of Historic Places, Belton Court. Their proposal would demolish a portion of Belton Court.

The Barrington Land Conservation Trust was founded in 1979 by a group of volunteers who believed it was essential to protect and steward our local natural, scenic, and historic open spaces. Just as these community members did nearly 50 years ago, we will continue to advocate for the protection of these lands and educate the public about our natural resources.

Sincerely,

Cindy Elder, Executive Director, and the Board of Directors, Barrington Land Conservation Trust
