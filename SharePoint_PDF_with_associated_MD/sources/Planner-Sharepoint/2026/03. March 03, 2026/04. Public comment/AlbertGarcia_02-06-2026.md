**Planning Board of Review - Barrington Rhode Island PUBLIC HEARING COMMENTS Proposed Belton Court Village Comprehensive Permit Preliminary Plan Application February 3, 2026**

My name is **Albert Garcia**. I have lived across the street from Belton Court at 34 Middle Highway with my two daughters and my wife Abbe for the past 23 years.

I am an architect. I am the owner and Principal of KITE Architects in Providence. As a design professional, I fully support the growth of our community, the creation of affordable housing with safe, walkable neighborhoods.

I support the development of the Belton Court property, and I recognize that increased density is unavoidable and an essential component of sustainable development. However, I also recognize that there are a lot of other factors that need to be carefully considered to ensure a successful outcome.

Over the years, multiple iterations of master plans have been submitted by Shine Harmony Holdings for a variety of programs on this site. Unfortunately, each iteration has shared common shortcomings: a lack of sensitivity to **SCALE and DENSITY** in relation to the surrounding neighborhood, and **ENVIRONMENTAL IMPACT** in the form of stormwater runoff. These issues are again at the center of my objections to the current proposal.

The developer has worked with renowned city planner DPZ CoDesign and has shown us rendered plans, perspective views, and precedent images that are very compelling and seductive. They offer us a vision of a utopian world:

- A model walkable community that is less reliant on CARS.
- Compact arrangements of charming cottages and townhouses.
- Tree lined streets, gardens and pocket parks

Unfortunately, it is my opinion that in reality the proposal falls short of this utopian ideal:

The **DENSITY** of the proposed plan is predicated on WALKABILITY - both within the development and surrounding neighborhood. Unfortunately, the proposal does NOT PROVIDE the necessary access to amenities that enable LESS RELIANCE ON CARS.

The plan does not provide walkable connections to all the things that support daily life:

- o Connection to a true village center
- o Convenient access to a food market, pharmacy, hair salon, etc.
- o Gathering places for Community Events

In the absence of these adjacencies, the proposal grossly underestimates the number of cars that residents of the community will require to live their lives. This underestimate leads to inaccurate assumptions about the traffic impact both within and around the development.

**SCALE** The proposal asks for a dimensional variance to increase maximum building height from 50 feet to 75 feet. In other words, the developer is asking for permission to build structures well over 5-6 stories tall. This is far larger than anything that currently exists in town, and of a scale that is rarely seen outside of Providence and Boston.

By comparison, the recently built Christine Apartments along the Wampanoag Trail in East Providence provides a preview of the scale of some of the proposed structures at Belton Court. These condominiums are only 4 stories, less that the proposed 5+ story structures at Belton Court, and demonstrate how this build-out would dwarf the surrounding neighborhood. Also, the overall density of the Christine Apartments project is just over 8 units per acre. Far less than the proposed variance to allow density of 12 units per acre at Belton Court.

**ENVIRONMENTAL IMPACT** The proposed site plan will pose a significant threat to the surrounding neighborhood in the form of STORMWATER RUNOFF.

The current design requires extensive and complex storm water management systems to control VOLUME and WATER QUALITY coming from the site. Three large detention ponds are proposed along the west side of the site. Each of the ponds are approximately the size of a football field. These ponds and surrounding landscape will require PROACTIVE AND REGULAR MAINTENANCE

- Control overgrowth / incursion of invasive species
- Cleanout of silt and sediment
- Need for structural assessments

PERVIOUS PAVING is proposed to mitigate some of the runoff risk. Similarly, regular maintenance is required or the paving will quickly become impervious.

Without consistent and responsible stewardship, these systems will fail. Unfortunately, to date, the property owner has consistently demonstrated a wanton disregard for proactive maintenance of the property, so we are VERY CONCERNED that is is only a matter of time before these systems begin to degrade – and unleash further water runoff onto the surrounding neighborhoods.

## **IN CLOSING**

In 2023, after a series of workshops with the town, the planner DPZ CoDesign articulated the goal to create, "economically and environmentally resilient communities that foster physical and social well-being". To this end, they cited basic planning guidelines and principles used to inform discussions with the town to develop the 2023 Master Plan.

In 2023 the following positives / negatives were heard:

## 2023 negatives

- (Inadequate) School Capacity
- Additional Traffic
- Loss of parts of the (Historic) Mansion
- Encroachment into wetlands areas

## 2023 positives

- Inclusion of Multi-Generation Housing & Affordable Housing
- Compact Development
- Creation of a Village Center with Community Amenities

The proposed 2025 iteration of the 2023 plan drifts further from the originally stated goals. As it maintains the same 344 units on a more compressed (31.2 acre) developable area.

- None of the negatives cited in 2023 have been addressed
- The plan now calls for full (not partial) demolition of the (Historic) Mansion
- Former 2023 positives area now negatives
  - Further densified development
  - Further reduction of No Village Center
  - Further reduction of ancillary retail/commercial space
  - Fewer (if any) community amenities

To realize the goal of achieving a truly economically and environmentally resilient community at Belton Court, the developer should be required by the town to revisit the findings and discoveries of the earlier planning efforts, and to apply the planning guidelines and principles to a more acceptable proposal.

Further, a **STATEMENT OF DESIGN INTEGRITY** should be required as part of the permit application, from the design team DPZ CoDesign to serve as a formal assurance to the town that the currently proposed iteration of the project aligns with the designer's original design intent and professional standards as a DPZ CoDesign Project.
