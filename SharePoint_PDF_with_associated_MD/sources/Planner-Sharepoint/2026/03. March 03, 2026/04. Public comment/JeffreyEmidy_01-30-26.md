Dear Members of the Planning Board:

The Rhode Island Historical Preservation and Heritage Commission (RIHPHC) has periodically been contacted by members of the public regarding the plight of Belton Court, at 33 Middle Highway. Recently, the proposed demolition of the building as part of the Belton Court Village Comprehensive Permit Preliminary Plan Application review that is scheduled for public hearing at the 3 February 2026 meeting of the Barrington Planning Board has increased the volume and urgency of pleas for our assistance.

As you are no doubt aware, Belton Court is individually listed in the National Register of Historic Places. Belton Court was listed in the National Register in June 1976 under multiple areas of significance: architecture, commerce, communications, education, industry, politics/government, and other (collecting). The majority of these areas are associated with the Peck family, and specifically Frederick S. Peck, who was a member of the Barrington Town Council and later the state House of Representatives, during which time he served as the chairman of the finance committee, and also the only ever holder of the state position of Commissioner of Finance. In addition to his time in politics, he also held positions of prominence in many Rhode Island companies of consequence in the textile industry, public utility, and financial sectors, among others.

Articles, online videos, and public social media posts have for years discussed and shown the gradual decline of the condition of Belton Court. During the same time, this office has had conversations with potential developers about saving the building. Staff of the RIHPHC have not recently visited the site, nor have we recently reviewed a professional conditions or structural evaluation of the building, so it would be inappropriate for us to opine as to the current condition of the building or the feasibility of saving it.

To be clear, there is no project at Belton Court that is currently under formal review by the RIHPHC, and we have not been informed of any aspect of the project that is the subject of the upcoming Planning Board review that would cause the project to fall under RIHPHC regulatory jurisdiction. However, we urge the Town of Barrington and the Planning Board to consider and to encourage the owner of Belton Court to exhaust every option to ensure that this important historical building is rehabilitated and continues to be, as the National Register nomination states, "a good example of early twentieth century country house architecture and a symbol of the power and position of the Peck family in Rhode Island."

Thank you for your consideration of this matter. If you have any questions, please contact me at jeffrey.emidy@preservation.ri.gov.
