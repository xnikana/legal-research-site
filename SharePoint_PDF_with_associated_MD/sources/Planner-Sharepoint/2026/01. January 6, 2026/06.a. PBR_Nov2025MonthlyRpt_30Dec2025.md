- 13. Southeast New England Program Technical Assistance Program. Staff partnered with the technical assistance team from the University of Maine to agree on the scope of work that will be carried out. A contract is currently being prepared and will be available for signature in late December. Thereafter, a pre-qualified consultant will be selected, and a "kick-off" meeting will be scheduled. Barrington was selected to receive a technical assistance award to help with completing feasibility study including public engagement focused on the establishment of a stormwater utility in Town. Such a utility would help the Town address stormwater issues regarding requirements of US EPA but, importantly, would also help the Town to better plan for and carry out projects related to being more resilient to issues of climate change (e.g., sea level rise and storm surge, including related stormwater runoff issues).

The project sponsored entirely by RI Housing has Libra Planners conducting a build-out analysis, public engagement process, and preparation of recommended strategies to incorporate into the Town's various regulations that are intended to incent additional affordable housing in Town.
- 15. Collaboration with Town Energy Manager Energy Advocates, LLC (Kathy Stanley) serving as the Town's Energy Manager worked with the department to initiate "preplanning" for energy needs of "government center" (i.e., Town Hall and the Library/Peck Center) with the possibility of establishing a micro-grid project in the area. The project intends to carry out three general tasks: 1) improve and/or add HVAC systems and controllers to create energy savings/efficiencies as necessary in the two buildings, 2) carry out weatherization projects as necessary, also, to create energy savings/efficiencies, and 3) to replace the standby generator for Town Hall while working to install a similar generator at the Library / Peck Center that currently has NO backup power supply. The project likely will take 3-5 years at least and, again, the concept of a micro-grid implementation project is being woven into the project, including consideration of the EV chargers at Town Hall.

As update to the above from October, the Town Hall and Library/Peck Center underwent three stages of review and site visits by professionals involved with generator sizing, weatherization, and HVAC-related systems. Next steps currently include waiting for the individual consultants to prepare their report and recommendations that, then, will be woven into a plan for action. Any

implementation thereafter would be subject to review and recommendation by the Town Manager (in collaboration with the Town Energy Manager, other applicable department heads, to a limited degree Resiliency & Energy Committee) and review and approval by the Town Council, subject to funding, project extent and expense, feasibility, scheduling of projects in juxtaposition with other on-going capital projects, etc.
