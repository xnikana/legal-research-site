# **PURCHASE AND SALE AGREEMENT**

**1. PARTIES:** This Purchase and Sales Agreement (this "**Agreement**") is made by and between

**ALFONSO T. LUBRANO and JOAN LUBRANO** (hereinafter collectively referred to as, "**Seller**") having an address of 24 Briarfield Road, Barrington, Rhode Island 02806 and

**CHRISTOPHER JON TALBERT and LAUREN A. TALBERT** (hereinafter collectively referred to as, "**Buyer**"), having an address of 22 Briarfield Road, Barrington, Rhode Island 02806.

The Seller agrees to SELL and the Buyer agrees to BUY, upon the price, terms and conditions set forth below, the following property (the "**Property**"): the area constituting the northerly portion of Lot 323 on Barrington Tax Assessor's Plat 26, consisting of approximately 16,232 sq. ft. + acres of land, to be delineated on a survey or plan meeting the requirements of the Barrington Subdivision Regulations for an Administrative Subdivision (as defined in said Barrington Subdivision Regulations) to effectuate a lot line adjustment between Seller, the owner of Lot 323 on Barrington Tax Assessor's Plat 26 ("**Seller Property**") and Buyer, the owner of Lot 322 on Barrington Tax Assessor's Plat 26 ("**Buyer's Property**"), with the newly established Lot 323 boundary line to be mutually agreed upon by Seller and Buyer of the Property. The Property shall be annexed to or incorporated in Buyer's Property as more particularly described in the Administrative Subdivision Plan, as defined in Section 12(a) below A draft of the Administrative Subdivision Plan, subject to approval by the Town of Barrington, is attached hereto as **Exhibit A**. This Agreement contemplates a conveyance of vacant or unimproved land that is not intended to constitute a separate legal tract or parcel of land.

- **2. DATE OF THIS AGREEMENT:** The Date of this Agreement or "**Effective Date**" shall be the later of: (a) the date on which Buyer signs this Agreement, or (b) the date on which Seller signs this Agreement, as set forth by the Buyer's and Seller's signatures below.
- **3. PURCHASE PRICE:** The agreed Purchase Price for the Property is TWO HUNDRED THOUSAND DOLLARS (\$200,000) ("**Purchase Price**"), of which FIVE THOUSAND DOLLARS (\$5,000) will be paid within two (2) business days of the Effective Date (the "**Deposit**"), leaving the amount of ONE HUNDRED NINETY FIVE THOUSAND DOLLARS (\$195,000) as the BALANCE OF THE PURCHASE PRICE.
- **4. CLOSING DATE/PLACE:** Closing is to be held within ten (10) business days from the granting of approval of the Administrative Subdivision of the Property by the Administrative Officer of the Barrington Planning Board or the Barrington Planning Board, as applicable, with all appeal periods, if any, having expired with no appeals having been filed, at 10:00 A.M. at the office of the Land Evidence Records in the Town of Barrington or at such other time and place as may be agreed to by the parties. The balance of the Purchase Price shall be paid by Buyer by wire transfer. Payment of the balance of the Purchase Price by Buyer and delivery of the

Quitclaim Deed from Seller as grantor, shall occur at Closing, with the net Seller's proceeds to be paid to Seller upon recording of the Quitclaim Deed of the Property.

- **5. DEPOSITS:** The Deposit made hereunder shall be held in escrow by Hinckley Allen, attorneys for the Seller ("**Escrow Agent**"), and, except as otherwise provided herein, shall be duly accounted for at the time of delivery of the deed and applied as a credit against the Purchase Price. The refunding of the Deposit shall be upon execution of a written release by Buyer and Seller. In the event of a dispute between the Seller and the Buyer as to any provisions of this Agreement or the performance thereof, the Escrow Agent may retain the Deposit hereunder in said escrow account until the dispute is resolved by the parties as evidenced by joint written instructions from Seller and Buyer to Escrow Agent accompanied by an executed mutual release of all claims regarding the disposition of the Deposit, or by binding settlement or final court judgment, or Escrow Agent may place the Deposit with a Court of competent jurisdiction.
- **6. TITLE:** Seller covenants and warrants that Seller is the fee title owner of the Property and has the authority and capacity to enter into this Agreement and consummate the transaction contemplated herein upon issuance of approval by the Administrative Officer of the Barrington Planning Board of the Administrative Subdivision (lot line adjustment) memorialized in the Administrative Subdivision Plan (as defined in Section 12(a) below) of the Property. The Property is to be conveyed by a good and sufficient Quitclaim Deed from the Seller, conveying good and marketable title to the Property, free from all encumbrances except easements and restrictions of record to the extent approved by Buyer in writing within forty-five (45) days from the date of this Agreement and except municipal regulations. Buyer may at Buyer's own expense, conduct a title examination of the Property within forty-five (45) days from the Effective Date. Buyer shall notify Seller of any defects in title disclosed by such examination. If Seller is unable to remove such defects (and Seller assumes no obligation to undertake any efforts to remove such defects), Buyer shall have the option to: (a) accept such title as Seller is able to convey without abatement or reduction of the Purchase Price, or (b) cancel this Agreement and promptly receive a return of the Deposit. Seller may, at the time of the delivery of the Quitclaim Deed, use the purchase money or any portion thereof to clear the title of any or all encumbrances or interest to enable Seller to make conveyance as provided herein, provided that all instruments to be obtained are in the possession of Buyer's title insurer and recorded prior to recording the Quitclaim Deed, except for any discharge of mortgage from an institutional lender which maybe recorded subsequent to the Quitclaim Deed provided that Buyer's title insurer has obtained a pay-off letter from such institutional lender. To the extent there are any underground or above ground utilities located with the Property, the Quitclaim Deed from Seller shall contain a reservation that provides that Seller shall have the right to utilize and maintain any and all such utilities or utility lines, if any, located within the Property.
- **7.** (a) **TAXES**: Real estate taxes are to be prorated on a fiscal year basis, with the Seller paying for the period of Seller's ownership of the Property unpaid to the date of delivery of the deed and the Buyer paying the balance of taxes due allocable to the Property. All other taxes which are a lien upon the Property and on the remainder of Lot 323 owned by Seller shall be paid by the Seller at the time of the delivery of the deed to the extent required for the Administrative Officer of the Barrington Planning Board or the Barrington Planning Board, as applicable, to approve and sign the Administrative Subdivision Plan (as hereinafter defined in Section 12(a) below)

relating to the Property.

Seller and Buyer hereby agree to enter into a mutually agreeable tax proration agreement on or before Closing to ensure that prior to the recognition by the Barrington Tax Assessor of the Administrative Subdivision of the Property from the remainder of Lot 323 and annexation or incorporation of the Property to Buyer's Property, all as depicted on the Administrative Subdivision Plan, as separate tax parcels, a formula will be reasonably established to ensure that Seller will pay for the proportionate share of the real estate taxes for the remainder of Lot 323 on Plat 26 for the fiscal tax year in which the Closing occurs (and thereafter) from the Closing Date and the Buyer will pay (or reimburse Seller) for the proportionate share of the real estate taxes for the Property (annexed to Buyer's Property) from and after the Closing Date. Seller and Buyer hereby agree to reconcile any overpayments or underpayments by either party once the tax assessments by the Barrington Tax Assessor associated with the Administrative Subdivision of the Property are determined. The provisions of this Paragraph 7(a) shall survive the Closing and the delivery of the Quitclaim Deed.

 (b) **ASSESSMENTS**: All assessments constituting a lien on the Property which are payable over a period of more than one year shall be apportioned in such manner that Seller shall pay installments due during the municipal years prior to the year in which the Quitclaim Deed is delivered: the installment due in that year shall be apportioned in the same manner as above provided for taxes and Buyer shall pay or assume the balance, except where local ordinances require otherwise.

### **8. BUYER/SELLER INFORMATION:**

- (a) **ORDINANCES**: Buyers of real estate in the State of Rhode Island are legally obligated to comply with all local real estate ordinances.
- (b) **WETLANDS**: The location of coastal wetlands, bays, fresh water wetlands, ponds, marshes, riverbanks or swamps, and the associated buffer areas may impact future Property development. If known, Seller must disclose to the Buyer any such determination on all or part of the land made by the Department of Environmental Management.
- (c) **RESTRICTIONS OR LEGISLATIVE/GOVERNMENTAL ACTION**: Buyer is responsible for investigating whether there are any restrictions or Legislative/Governmental actions, present or proposed, which affect or would affect the use of the Property.

### **9. ADDITIONAL SELLER OBLIGATIONS:**

- (a) **NON-RESIDENT WITHHOLDING TAX**: If the Sellers are not residents of the State of Rhode Island or will not be residents at the time of the closing, the Seller shall present the Buyer with a 71.3 Certificate of Withholding Due from the Rhode Island Division of Taxation stating the amount to be withheld from Seller's proceeds or the Buyer must withhold six (6%) percent of the net proceeds to the Seller, in accordance with R.I.G.L. Section 44-30-71.3, and pay such amount to the Division of Taxation of the Seller's sale proceeds as a non-resident withholding tax. This tax liability of Seller shall be satisfied at or prior to closing.
  - (b) **FOREIGN INVESTMENT IN REAL PROPERTY TAX ACT ("FIRPTA")**: The Seller represents that they are not foreign persons as defined in FIRPTA and, accordingly, that the Buyer will not be required to comply with the withholding requirements of FIRPTA at the closing.

- **10. POSSESSION - NO SELLER WARRANTY OR REPRESENTATION WITH RESPECT TO BUYER'S PROPERTY:** The Property is being sold free and clear of all occupants and leasehold interests and otherwise in its "AS IS" condition and Buyer represents that he has not relied on the oral or other representations of the Seller as to the character or quality of the Property or the legal status of Buyer's Property, including, without limitation, no Seller warranty or representation is or has been given by Seller or Seller's representatives relating to the legal sufficiency of Buyer's Property for issuance of a dock permit under CRMC regulations after the recording of the Administrative Subdivision Plan and delivery and recording of the Quitclaim Deed.
- **11. WRITTEN NOTICE OF VIOLATIONS AND CHANGE IN OWNERSHIP:** If there is any notice of violation order outstanding against the Property, Seller shall immediately provide written notice to the Buyer of the Property with a true copy of any notice of violations or orders issued and notify the enforcing officer in writing of Seller's intent to transfer the property.
- **12. ADMINISTRATIVE SUBDIVISION PLAN AND CONDITIONS PRECEDENT TO CLOSING:** The transaction contemplated in this Agreement is subject to satisfaction of the following conditions precedent:
- 1. Buyer and Seller shall cause a land survey by a Rhode Island licensed surveyor to be completed that satisfies the requirements of the Subdivision Regulations for Town of Barrington to accomplish an Administrative Subdivision Plan for a lot line adjustment between the Seller Property and the Buyer Property – specifically, for the proposed annexation of a rectangular shaped portion of the Seller Property along its northern boundary to the east boundary of the Buyer's Property, a copy of which is attached hereto as **Exhibit A**, subject to approval by the Town of Barrington (the "**Administrative Subdivision Plan**").
- 2. Upon completion of the surveyor's field work and the generation of a draft Administrative Subdivision Plan to be approved by the Town of Barrington, Seller and Buyer shall work in good faith and best efforts to agree upon the establishment and location of the new north boundary that results in the designation of the precise area within Lot 323 that is to be conveyed to Buyer, currently estimated to be 16,232 sq. ft., subject to approval by the Town of Barrington.
- 3. The Buyer and Seller shall share equally in the costs and expenses of the surveyor to generate the Administrative Subdivision Plan as well as the expenses of the surveyor to generate and submit or file the Application for administration subdivision as well as the surveyor expenses associated with conferring, presenting and meeting with the Administrative Officer of the Barrington Planning Board and/or attend a presentation to the Barrington Plan Board to have the Administrative Subdivision Plan approved by said Administrative Officer and or the Barrington Planning Board.

In the event that despite the exercise of good faith and best efforts, Seller and Buyer are unable to agree on the Administrative Subdivision Plan or if the Administrative Officer of the Barrington Planning Board or the Planning Board of the Town of Barrington does not approve the Administrative Subdivision Plan or imposes conditions on the Administrative Subdivision Plan that are unacceptable to either Seller or Buyer, then either Seller or Buyer may elect to terminate this Agreement, in which case the Deposit shall be promptly returned to Buyer and the obligations of Seller and Buyer under this Agreement shall terminate and be of no force and effect.

Notwithstanding anything to the contrary in this Agreement, Buyer may, at the Buyer's sole cost and expense, choose to have any and all inspections of the Property that is to be annexed to Buyer's Property that Buyer may choose, e.g., title research and obtain a commitment for title insurance and determine whether a permit will be issued to Buyer for installation of a dock to be appurtenant to Buyer's Property. The Buyer shall have forty-five (45) calendar days from the date of this Agreement to conduct such inspections ("Inspection Period") or this contingency shall be deemed to be waived.

- **13. FURTHER ASSURANCES:** Seller and Buyer each agree to execute and deliver such other documents, instruments, and affidavits as may reasonably be required to effect the transaction contemplated herein, including without limitation, any affidavits and agreements which may be required by the Buyer's title insurance company.
- **14. NOTICES:** All notices as required in this Agreement shall be in writing. All notices are to be conveyed by mail, personal delivery, or fax. Notices shall be effective when postmarked, upon personal delivery, or upon fax transmittal date. Notices shall be sent or delivered to the respective addresses in Section 1 of this Agreement and, in the case of notice to Seller, with a copy to David I. Lough, Esquire, Hinckley Allen, 100 Westminister Street, Providence, Rhode Island 02903.
- **15. DEFAULT:** Upon default by the Buyer, the Seller shall have the right to retain the Deposit, such right to be without prejudice to the right of the Seller to require specific performance, which shall accrue by reason of such default. If the Seller shall default in the performance of this Agreement, the Deposit shall be promptly returned to Buyer or, at Buyer's election, Buyer may pursue specific performance as Buyer's sole and exclusive remedy.

In addition to the foregoing, the failure of either Seller or Buyer to pay its share of the surveyor expenses described in this Agreement shall constitute a default under the terms of this Agreement. The foregoing sentence is intended by Seller and Buyer to survive the closing and the delivery of the Quitclaim Deed.

- **16. ASSIGNMENT AND SURVIVORSHIP:** This Agreement may not be assigned by either party without the prior written consent of the other.
- **17. ACCURATE DISCLOSURE OF SELLING PRICE:** The Buyer and Seller certify that this Agreement accurately reflects the gross sales price as indicated in Section 3 of this Agreement. The Buyer and Seller understand and agree that this information shall be disclosed to the Internal Revenue Service as required by law.
- **18. BROKERAGE/AGENCY CONFIRMATION CLAUSE:** Each of the parties hereto

represent to the other that there was no broker involved in this transaction, and no commission is due to any party as a result hereof.

**19. ENTIRE AGREEMENT:** We, the parties hereto, each declare that this instrument contains the entire agreement between us, subject to no understandings, conditions, or representations other than those expressly stated herein. This Agreement may not be changed, modified, or amended in whole or in part except in writing, signed by Seller and Buyer.
