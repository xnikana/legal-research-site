## ➢ **Inclusionary zoning**

The Town currently has inclusionary zoning under Article XXVII of the Zoning Ordinance (still optional under RIGL). Because we have an existing inclusionary zoning ordinance, the Town is required to make the following amendments:

- o The minimum percentage of affordable housing must be no less than 25% of the total units in the development.
- o **The minimum number of dwelling units that triggers the inclusionary requirement shall not be higher than ten (10) dwelling units [this means that a lower threshold such as four or five units is ok, but a higher threshold such as 12 units is not].**
- o The density bonus shall be at least two (2) market rate units for each affordable unit, and minimum required lot area shall be reduced by the necessary amount.
  - o Setback, lot width, and frontage requirements still apply, and relief can be requested.
  - o Greater density bonus may be provided for an increased percentage of affordable housing.
- o List available zoning incentives and municipal government subsidies in the ordinance.

![](PB Memo 11-07-2023_attachments/_page_3_Picture_18.jpeg)

- o Fee in lieu: applications using this option shall not be eligible for the density bonus, and further, shall not be eligible for administrative review, i.e., must be reviewed and approved by the planning board.
- o Municipality shall allocate fees received within three (3) years of collection and the process for allocating these funds must be contained in the ordinance.

The minimum number of dwelling units that triggers the inclusionary requirement shall not be higher than ten (10) dwelling units – however, the threshold may be lower. The Town seeks the Board's input/recommendation on if there is an appetite to go lower than 10 or stick to the language of RIGL.

**MOTION: to recommend to the Town Council that the minimum number of dwelling units that triggers the inclusionary requirement shall be [XX] dwelling units.**
