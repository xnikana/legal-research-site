**5. Discuss and Act: Review A-Frame Signage Ordinance and Regulations (Town Council Referral)**

**See video from September 11, 2023 Town Council meeting for discussion/referral (begins at 03h30m30s): <https://vimeo.com/showcase/9672722/video/863562888#t=210s>**

Member Wetherill commented that the Committee made a statement to hire a professional to produce a comprehensive commercial sign ordinance – these two items are attaching it piecemeal, which could make the process more confusing without a comprehensive overview on signage. Member Wetherill reiterated the recommendation that a professional be hired to take on a comprehensive commercial sign ordinance.

Town Planner Crean reviewed the referral from the Town Council. Chair Stabach noted that the concern is with Bluewater's A-Frame and complaints it is a safety issue. Member Godfrey commented that the Committee should not deal with one-off issues but rather take a comprehensive approach, that A-Frame signs are typical though many towns ban them altogether, to consider allow plastic versus wood with chalk, but how ultimately this is a discussion for a comprehensive sign ordinance. The Committee commented that they can offer opinions, but there needs to be an expert to drive the overall discussion and manage opinions.

**6. Discuss and Act: Review Town's Lamppost Banner Program (Town Council Referral) See video from September 11, 2023 Town Council meeting for discussion/referral** 

### **(begins at 03h30m30s): <https://vimeo.com/showcase/9672722/video/863562888#t=210s>**

Member Wetherill commented that there are a lot of personal opinions by people who are not personally knowledgeable. Member Godfrey commented that this matter is not appropriate for the Technical Review Committee to solve. Godfrey then noted Trenton, NJ along the pedestrian mall as an example of varying public opinion, commented that he was surprised with the support of the High School Banners as there were children's names on them, and that, in his opinion, the banners tend to unify the business district. Member Wetherill concurred. There was further discussion about the varying opinions of the banners being up, and when and where they should be.

Chair Stabach acknowledged Member Godfrey's comment about the banners being a unifying piece, but commented that he sees them as white noise, and tends not to read them, but will read them when the student names are on them. Member Godfrey responded that he drives County Road twice each day and notices them regularly, though it's an aesthetics decision that is not the TRC's purview. Chair Stabach agreed, and agreed with Member Witherill that a signage consultant can advise the Town on this. The Committee discussed the need to get consensus from stakeholders and have the consultants complete renderings and visuals rather than just written word.

**MOTION: Member Wetherhill moved for the Town to engage a professional consultant to develop a comprehensive sign ordinance as opposed to dealing with issues on an item-byitem basis, and for this to include graphical examples. Motion carried unanimously.**

### **7. Adjourn**

By a unanimous vote of the Committee, the meeting adjourned at 7:57 PM.
