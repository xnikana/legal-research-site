"The Barrington Planning Board hereby grants approval for the Preliminary Plan/Comprehensive Permit Application for 173R Maple Avenue (AP 23, Lot 325 in the Residence-10 and Neighborhood Business Zone). Comprehensive Permit Application Package Binder, dated June 8, 2023; Existing Conditions: Boundary and Topographic Survey Plan, Waterman Engineering October 22, 2018, revised May 2, 2022; Site Plan, Construction Details, and Grading, Drainage, Utilities, Landscape & Erosion Plan, revised August 31, 2023."

Applicant proposes creation of four units on the site: Unit 1 is a first-floor apartment that would result from renovation of the existing garage onsite to an accessible LMI unit (1,440 square feet); Unit 2 is the existing market rate unit, which will remain unchanged (1,440 square feet); Units 3 & 4 will be new marketrate dwelling units attached to the existing two-car garage to the south (1,064 square feet each). Master Plan approval for this application was granted in June 2021.

This decision is based upon the following legal conclusions with supporting findings of fact and conditions of approval:

#### **Procedural History**

The Applicant filed a comprehensive permit application and Plans titled "Maple Grove Land Development," prepared by Waterman Engineering, 46 Sutton Ave., East Providence, RI, dated January 2020 and revised April 20, 2021. Applicant proposed to develop within the R10-zoned rear portion of the lot the following: convert existing single-family dwelling into a two-family structure with an affordable housing unit on the first floor and a market-rate unit on the second floor; and add a 2nd-floor addition to an existing garage and construct an apartment on the first and second levels, each approximately 832 square feet. On June 1, 2021, the Planning Board approved the master plan, subject to conditions. The 25 percent affordable housing percentage, based on one out of four units qualifying as affordable, meets the minimum 25% threshold required in a comprehensive permit application.

A Master Plan extension was granted by the Planning Board in May 2023, and the Preliminary Plan application package was submitted to the Town June 8, 2023, with the last revision of the package dated August 31, 2023. The Preliminary Plan was approved by the Planning Board at their October 3, 2023 meeting.

### **LEGAL CONCLUSIONS in accordance with R.I. Gen. Laws § 45-53-4(a)(4)(v) with supporting FINDINGS OF FACT**

The below conclusions and findings are specific to the evidence and testimony presented during the preliminary plan stage of review.

#### **Relief Approved:**

In accordance with Article XXVI Low- and Moderate-Income Housing, of the Barrington Zoning Ordinance, the Planning Board grants the following relief from the following provisions of the Zoning Ordinance:

> o Use variance for multiple principal and accessory dwelling units on one Neighborhood Business/Residence-10 zoned lot while retaining an existing home office. (Zoning Ordinance sections 185-69 and 185-70)

#### **Findings of Fact and Conditions of Approval**

Approval is based upon the following findings of fact and conditions of approval:

#### **Findings of Fact**

1. The proposed development is consistent with local needs as identified in the Town's comprehensive community plan with particular emphasis on the community's affordable housing plan, as the proposed. The plan, which proposes four residential units on a 1.03-acre lot, is consistent with Comprehensive Community Plan's Future Land Use Map, which designates the site as "Mixed-Use/Mixed Housing" (Neighborhood Business portion) and "Residential-Medium-High Density" (Residence 10 portion).

By providing one affordable unit, or 25 percent of the four total proposed units, the proposed development is consistent with local needs as identified in the local Comprehensive Community Plan, including:

- a. Continue to comply with the State's 10 percent affordable housing goal through provision of well-integrated and compatible affordable housing. (Housing & Neighborhoods Goal HN-3)
- b. Objective HN-3.2: Produce affordable housing units at a rate that keeps Barrington on the path toward achieving the 10 percent affordable housing goal by 2035. (Housing & Neighborhoods Objective HN-3.2)
- 2. The proposed development is in compliance with the standards and provisions of the Town's zoning ordinance and subdivision regulations, except where expressly varied or waived local concerns that have been affected by the relief granted do not outweigh the state and local need for low- and moderate-income housing.
- 3. The low- and moderate-income housing unit a handicap-accessible first-floor unit is integrated within the development (located within an existing structure along with a market-rate unit), compatible in scale and architectural style to the market rate units within the project; and will be built and occupied prior to, or simultaneous with the construction and occupancy of the market rate units.
- 4. The arrangement of buildings and vehicular access depicted on the Preliminary Plan drawings produce no negative significant environmental impacts, and adequately addresses stormwater management.
- 5. As the site is within a sidewalk priority area, the development shall improve the sidewalk along the Maple Avenue frontage to meet Town standards.
- 6. The property has access to a public street, Maple Avenue.
- 7. The proposal will not result in the creation of individual lots with such physical constraints to development that building on those lots according to pertinent regulations and building standards would be impracticable.

#### **Conditions of Approval:**

This application will be subject to the following conditions in submitting the Final Plan application:

- 1. The applicant shall receive the Town's final approval of the affordable housing unit deed restriction and provide a copy of an executed monitoring agreement.
- 2. The low-to-moderate income unit proposed for the first floor of the converted existing singlefamily dwelling into a two-family structure, shall be integrated in the development, and comparable in scale and architectural style to the market-rate unit in the same structure.
- **3.** The applicant will adhere to the Grading, Drainage, Utilities, Landscape & Erosion Plan, revised August 31, 2023.
- **4.** The applicant shall provide the Building Elevation Plans architectural drawings dated August 18, 2023 as part of the Final Plan submittal for recording in Land Evidence. These plans illustrate the architectural character of Buildings 1 and 2.

#### **Authorization of Final Plan Approval**

The Planning Board authorizes the Administrative Officer to grant Final Plan approval.
