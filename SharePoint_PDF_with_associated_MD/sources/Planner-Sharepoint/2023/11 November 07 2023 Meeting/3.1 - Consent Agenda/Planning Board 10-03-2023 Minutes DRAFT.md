**5.2 Discuss and Act: Provide recommendation on sketches by Union Studio of the development at 25 Watson Avenue illustrating 14 cottages and 5 single-family lots (referred by the Town Council). See page 3 of the renderings provided to the Town on August 24, 2023, available at barrington.ri.gov/25watson.**

*Present: Phil Hervey, Town Manager*

Town Manager Hervey summarized the process to this point, including the Planning Board's endorsement of a concept in April 2023. Since then, several images by Union Studio and an appraisal by Peter M. Scotti & Associates have been created to depict the concept and provide further information. Chair Phipps noted that she believes the depicted path in Development Scenario #1 (page 4 of the Scotti appraisal) was in a different location, and Town Manager Hervey explained the reason for changing it was access. The Board and Town Manager Hervey discussed the path further, noting the recommendation will be to eliminate the path. Town Manager Hervey noted that the Town Council has moved forward with the goal of age-restricted housing. Town Manager Hervey then presented a revised version of the Planning Board's endorsed concept (Development Scenario #1a, page 5 of the appraisal).

Chair Phipps noted that the former monastery still stands on the property, and that is a barrier to move forward with any development. Town Manager Hervey explained how the goal is to have a concept locked in prior to going into the Financial Town Meeting so the voters have a comfort level of what exactly is being proposed. Town Manager Hervey noted that new developer guidance and a brand-new zone would need to be created, which would be required with or without the existing building. Member Bacher noted the development could be done with R-25 zoning, and Town Manager Hervey responded that the cottage section would still require amendments as it is currently too restrictive, and some of the single-family lots may still be undersized. Town Manager Hervey noted the senior housing priority in the Comprehensive Plan, and that the intent of the monastery building when originally purchase was to be senior housing.

Town Manager Hervey then reviewed the revised plan in more depth, including increasing the cottage lot, reviewing the parking lot, and reiterated the recommendation on eliminating the thru path that goes between two house lots from Watson Avenue to Freemont Avenue in the original Planning Board plan. The revised plan would propose 19 units total, only one more unit than what the Planning Board originally recommended. Town Manager Hervey noted the sewer tie in on Freemont creating a significant expense. Town Manager Hervey explained how the current high interest rate and its effect on the proposed affordable units and the impact on this development. Town Manager Hervey then reviewed a revision to allow more buffer on the northside, noted as a consideration by the Town Council. Chair Phipps confirmed that in the revised plan the cottage area was made larger by eliminating one of the single-family lots.

The Board and Town Manager Hervey then discussed the proposed affordable units, along with requirements and feasibility. Member Kurtz asked about the projected price of the cottages, Town Manager Hervey responding they are about \$600,000 to \$700,000, but noted current high interest rates and construction costs. Town Manager Hervey noted the investment in the open space and age-restricted housing proposed with this development. Town Manager Hervey noted that the Planning Board plan was strong, and these revisions are to make it stronger.

Chair Phipps commented that she did not understand why the buffer needed to be increased, and Town Manager Hervey responded that it is due to the creation of a new zone, noted an encroachment found in the survey, and that it was a request by the Town Council.

Town Manager Hervey provided some context about affordable housing in Town throughout the past decade or so, noting Barrington Cove Apartments, and that since then have built over 120 family affordable units in Town, but zero senior units and zero senior affordable units. Town Manager Hervey commented that senior housing was the intent of the original purchase, and that this is a plan that has more buy-in from the surrounding neighborhood and is more appropriate for the neighborhood.

Member Bacher commented that he is not sure where there is a need for senior affordable housing, the documentation for that, and how the Town determines that it meets the need. Town Manager Hervey responded that it is the preference of the Town Council and meets current demographic trends. Member Bacher noted not having a market study, and Town Manager Hervey noted that 4Ward Planning provided one in 2022.

Solicitor Teitz reiterated to the Board the same message on the agenda regarding public comment, and that the referred item is to be focused on the specific changes, not redoing the concept. Member Bacher noted the Planning Board never recommended senior housing, but Solicitor Teitz and Town Manager Hervey noted that was determined by the Town Council prior to the Planning Board's recommendation in April and how that was always an underlying theme.

Member Simms reviewed the changes outlined by Town Manager Hervey, summarized his understanding of the conversation, then commented that it meets several goals and he finds the plan to be fine.

Member Kurtz commented on his concern of reducing number of the single-family lots, how that creates a barrier of selling the lots of individually, and that it is a bad trade because the single-family lots are worth more than the cottages. Town Manager Hervey noted that the single-family lot that is proposed to be removed for an additional cottage was to be an affordable lot and does not have as much financial impact. Member Bacher asked why the single-family lot needs to be affordable, and Town Manager Hervey noted that was part of the Planning Board's original motion. Member Kurtz expressed concern about being locked in, and the plan being more conceptual. Town Manager Hervey responded that the public expressed wanting a comfort level and a level of certainty. Member Kurtz reiterated getting more yield with a single-family lot than a cottage unit.

Member Bacher concurred with Member Kurtz and the concern that the Town would be giving up a single-family lot for cottages, commented that he does not believe the cottages will sell, and is not in favor of taking out the single-family lot.

### **6. Other Business**

# **6.1 Discuss and Act: Provide recommendation on use table of the zoning ordinance for introduction to the Town Council at their November 6, 2023 Meeting.**

Town Planner Crean briefly reviewed the new legislation that was introduced at the July 11, 2023 Meeting by Assistant Solicitor Goins, reiterated the January 1, 2024 deadline, and then reviewed the process with the zoning ordinance amendments to the Town Council.

Solicitor Teitz reviewed the special use table change specifically, and explained his opinion on special-use permits. Also noted that many special-use permits will now be before the Planning Board under unified development review. Town Planner Crean also noted the upcoming rewrite of the Comprehensive Community Plan, which will require updates to the Zoning Ordinance to comply.

Member Simms asked about how the standards will change for not allowed from special-use permit. Solicitor Teitz noted a "N" designation in the use table requires a use variance, which is a much higher burden of proof. Solicitor Teitz noted the other changes to housing and residential development, including accessory dwelling units (ADUs).

Member Bacher noted that the specific and objective criteria is not established in the memorandum and references the definitions and parking not being sufficient. Solicitor Teitz concurred with not referring to definition but adding criteria to the definitions or elsewhere in ordinance.

Vice Chair Rua concurred with most recommendations, only disagreeing with the changes to cultural activity. Town Planner Crean read the definition to the Board. Vice Chair Rua noted if a local church wanted to set up a museum or gallery. Vice Chair Rua recommended changing all the "S" to "P" rather than "N." Solicitor Teitz recommended obtaining as a special-use permit with criteria that it must be an existing building. Also, could be criteria about adequate parking and traffic circulation. Member Bacher disagreed with "P" in the Open Space Passive zone, and Town Planner Crean noted staff did not edit and focused on the "S."

Member Kurtz confirmed for Yacht Club and other uses that are currently there would be a legacy use. Solicitor Teitz confirmed this. Councilor Berard asked if businesses near the water would be affected by flood regulations and being a legacy use, and also asked for revision on the definition of "golf course."

Member Bacher asked the difference between two-family dwelling, accessory apartment interior, accessory living quarters, and the like. Solicitor Teitz noted the law is likely to change, and to focus on where the table says "S."

Member Bacher asked if "adaptive reuse" needs to be added to the table, and Solicitor Teitz clarified it is not a use but a process.

Member Bacher asked about accessory outdoor event use, and Solicitor Teitz clarified that was recently done for Air BnB events and the like rather than an event venue. Member Bacher expressed concern about a large event more than 50 people, and the need to look at that further in the use table.

Member Bacher recommended cemetery be "S" in the AR and R-25 Zones.

Member Bacher expressed concern about the Kent Street Plaza and making NB/RBF "N" for offices over 2,500 square feet. Solicitor Teitz noted that it applies to individual offices. Also noted the medical office building on Maple Avenue. The recommendation was to switch from "N" to "P."

Member Bacher than asked about "commercial off-street parking facility, lot or area" under Transportation and related uses, noting for special-use criteria that parking should be in the back. Also noted a criteria for a public park and ride only on County Road where there is public transit. There was further discussion on criteria for commercial off-street parking, including parking toward the back on County Road and Maple Avenue, and parking design standards in the subdivision regulations. The discussion then moved to public park and rides, including only with access to County Road. Councilor Berard asked about change in routes by RIPTA, and if it makes sense to restrict access to roads or routes. Member Bacher noted to make the changes when the time comes.

Member Bacher asked about "specialty business," and that there is no definition. The recommendation from staff and Town Planner Crean was to remove "specialty business" as a use.

For "wholesale business and open storage of new building materials and machinery," Member Bacher commented that he did not believe the Village Center was an appropriate location for the use, and recommended it be changed to "N." For "wholesale business and storage of nonflammable and nonexplosive materials in a structure or building," the recommendation was to switch both "S" to "P." For "storage of flammable materials," Member Bacher expressed concern about proximity to other buildings. For "above-ground storage of heating fuels for resale" there were similar concerns. Solicitor Teitz recommended "N" for that category. Solicitor Teitz recommended that for "Retail outlet accessory to a storage or manufacturing use" that the "S" be switched to "P." Member Bacher expressed concern over parking. Solicitor Teitz noted that there are other requirements for retail uses.

Clerk Gillibrand asked about changing single-family detached dwelling "N" in the RE Zone and if that would affect St. Andrew's School, and Solicitor Teitz confirmed it would not because it is not the primary use.

**MOTION: Vice Chair Rua seconded by Member Bacher, made a motion to recommend the changes that were submitted and as amended during discussion to the Town Council, and that the recommended changes are consistent with the Comprehensive Community Plan. Motion carried unanimously.**
