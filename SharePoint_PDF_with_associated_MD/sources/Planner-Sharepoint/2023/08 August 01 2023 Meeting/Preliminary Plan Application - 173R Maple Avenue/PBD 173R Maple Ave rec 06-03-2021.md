# <u>Decision:</u> Master Plan for Rogers Land Development Project ("Maple Grove"), 173R Maple Avenue

#### Relief Granted

- 1. The Planning Board, sitting as the Local Review Board for Comprehensive Permit applications, grants the following relief from provisions of the Zoning Ordinance, as requested by the applicant:
  - a. A use variance for multiple principal and accessory dwelling units on one NB/R10-zoned lot while retaining an existing home office.

### Findings of Fact

1. The proposed development is consistent with local needs as identified in the Town's comprehensive community plan with particular emphasis on the community's affordable housing plan, as the proposed. The plan, which proposes four residential units on a 1.03-acre lot, is consistent with Comprehensive Community Plan's Future Land Use Map, which designates the site as "Mixed-Use/Mixed Housing" (Neighborhood Business portion) and "Residential-Medium-High Density" (Residence 10 portion).

By providing one affordable units, or 25 percent of the four total proposed units, the proposed development is consistent with local needs as identified in the local Comprehensive Community Plan, including:

- a. Continue to comply with the State's 10 percent affordable housing goal through provision of well-integrated and compatible affordable housing. (Housing & Neighborhoods Goal HN-3)
- b. Objective HN-3.2: Produce affordable housing units at a rate that keeps Barrington on the path toward achieving the 10 percent affordable housing goal by 2035. (Housing & Neighborhoods Objective HN-3.2)
- 2. The proposed development is in compliance with the standards and provisions of the Town's zoning ordinance and subdivision regulations, except where expressly varied or waived local concerns that have been affected by the relief granted do not outweigh the state and local need for low- and moderate-income housing.
- 3. The low- and moderate-income housing unit a handicap-accessible first-floor unit is integrated within the development (located within an existing structure along with a market-rate unit), compatible in scale and architectural style to the market rate units within the project; and will be built and occupied prior to, or simultaneous with the construction and occupancy of the market rate units.
- 4. The arrangement of buildings and vehicular access depicted on the master plan should produce no negative significant environmental impacts, though the applicant will be required to provide more detailed engineering at the Preliminary Plan stage that includes measures to manage stormwater runoff.

- 5. As the site is within a sidewalk priority area, the development shall improve the sidewalk along the Maple Avenue frontage to meet Town standards.
- 6. The property has access to a public street, Maple Avenue.
- 7. The proposal will not result in the creation of individual lots with such physical constraints to development that building on those lots according to pertinent regulations and building standards would be impracticable.

## Conditions of Approval

This application will be subject to the following conditions:

- 1. The applicant shall provide, in addition to the applicable preliminary plan checklist items, the following:
  - a. Floor plans with details on the interior layout of all units, including a schedule for interior finishes for the proposed affordable unit.
  - b. An elevation sketch detailing work to be completed on the exterior of the existing structures.
  - c. A grading plan for the lot, including the contours and cross-section of the proposed driveway.
  - d. Drainage details including any dry wells design.
  - e. A detailed landscape plan to include a proposed buffer to include fencing and plantings.
- 2. Use of home office shall be restricted to residents living on site.
- 3. The Planning Board urges the applicant to incorporate the following plan revisions in the preliminary plan submission:
  - a. Shift the parking spaces to the south, away from the affordable unit, in order to preserve the pergola and patio area in front of the entrance, as a resident with mobility issues living in the unit needs accessible private outdoor space convenient to the unit. Move one of the spaces closer to the two units in the expanded garage in the northwest corner of the site, for guest parking.
  - b. Provide convenient, private outdoor space for each of the two market rate units in the expanded garage, near the structure.
  - c. Shift the proposed walkway to circumvent the tree to the west and avoid the drip line of the deciduous tree located near the center of the property.
  - d. Preserve the gazebo for use by residents within the site as common open space; consider moving the two proposed benches to this area.
  - e. Eliminate the proposed bike racks, as the number of units and unit types do not warrant provision of bike racks on site.

f. Relocate the proposed commercial Dumpster and enclosure to a location away from any of the residential units or eliminate the Dumpster altogether if it is not required per input from Public Works."

Pursuant to R.I. Gen. Laws § 45-53-5(a), the Applicant may appeal this decision to the State Housing Appeals Board within twenty (20) days from the date of the recording and posting of this decision.
