# **Legislation Enacted that Affects Local, Regional and Statewide Planning**

During the 2023 Rhode Island General Assembly session, numerous bills were enacted that will impact local, regional, and statewide planning. A total of 2,588 bills were filed in 2023. This year, 140 planning related bills were tracked and, of these tracked bills, more than 40 were enacted as laws. The Land Use Staff of the Division scans for a variety of topics related to centralized planning responsibilities for the Division, the State Guide Plan, and other requirements as the State's single Metropolitan Planning Organization. To supply a speedy summary this year, due to the volume of changes enacted, summaries are not provided to get the entire list of law amendments circulated as soon as possible. Below is a list of the enacted bills, accompanied by links to the actual legislation on the web. The bills that were signed into law and that affect the Division are shown first, then bills related to transportation, energy, housing, land use/zoning, other state government, solid waste, and water supply planning follow. The Summary concludes with a few selected "hot" topics that did not pass the session. A reminder: the effective dates vary for some of the enacted laws, municipal planners should consult with their municipal solicitors about amendments needed to bring zoning ordinances, subdivision and land development, and or other regulations into compliance with the newly enacted laws.
