**5.1 Discuss and Act: Administrative Subdivision for 2 Seabrook Court, AP 32, Lot 25 in the R-25 District.**

This application is to square two lots on Seabrook Court and eliminate a hook in the northeast corner of the lot (see included narrative). This application was referred to the Board on recommendation of Town Solicitor Teitz as there have been ongoing legal disputes regarding the development at 2 Seabrook Court, and due to a jog in the lot line (see § 200-47 of the Town's Land Development and Subdivision Regulations).

# § [200-47](https://ecode360.com/7123425#7123425) **Lots.**

**[E.](https://ecode360.com/7123433#7123433)** Lots in residential developments or subdivisions shall have the minimum area and dimensions as prescribed in the Barrington Zoning Ordinance**[\[1\]](https://ecode360.com/7123425#ft7123433-1)** for the applicable zoning district(s). In general, lots shall be rectangular in shape with a ratio of lot depth to lot width of no greater than 2 1/2:1, and no interior angle of a lot shall be less than 45º or greater than 200º. In no case shall side lot lines intersect.

At the April 26 Special Meeting, the Planning Board made the following recommendation:

**Member Rua, seconded by Vice Chair Lucini, made a motion to recommend Concept Plan 2 for redevelopment at 25 Watson Avenue, that includes the following characteristics:**

- **The three lots to the south remain open space and are not developed.**
- **The pocket neighborhood is individually owned, via a condo association or single-family lots.**
- **The thru-road becomes a path, not a cut thru.**
- **There be a maximum of 10-12 units in the pocket neighborhood.**
- **There be two separate RFPs – one for the individual lots, one for the pocket neighborhood.**
- **Maintain Town control, such as deed restrictions or phased development.**
- **One of the lots and 25 percent of the pocket neighborhood be dedicated to low-tomoderate income (LMI) housing.**
- **Revise the lots lines for the lots on the south side, where the connect road will not have vehicle access.**

#### **Motion carried unanimously.**

**6.1 Discuss and Act: Provide recommendation on use table of the zoning ordinance for introduction to the Town Council at their November 6, 2023 Meeting.**

As outlined and discussed at the July 11, 2023 meeting, there are a host of zoning ordinance amendments based on bills recently passed by the Rhode Island Legislature, effective January 1, 2024. Some to be made to the Town's Zoning Ordinance (requiring Town Council review and adoption) and some to be made the Town's Land Development and Subdivision Regulations (requiring Planning Board review and adoption).

# **This will be a two-phase process to meet the January 1, 2024 deadline:**

- 1. Updates to Chapter 185 Zoning Ordinance (focus of the Board's October 3 Meeting and into November).
- 2. Updates to Chapter 200 Land Development and Subdivision Regulations (focus of the Planning Board in November/December as the Planning Board has the ability to hold a public hearing, review, and adopt this chapter).
