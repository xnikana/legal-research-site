## **5. Continued Items**

# **5.1 Discuss and Act: 33 Middle Highway (former Zion Bible College) Process Discussion and Effects of New Legislation.**

This agenda is item was at the request of several members of the Board to discuss checklist items (specifically ones that are more supplementary) should the owners of 33 Middle Highway submit a development application soon. **Currently, there is no application for this property, and there will not be one submitted until 2024. Therefore, when an application is submitted, it will follow the legislative updates effective January 1, 2024.**

In the Board's packet materials is the following:

- ➢ Blank copy of the checklist reflecting the new changes from RIHousing (distributed in November packets as well).
- ➢ The marked up/redlined checklist distributed by Member Bacher at the November 7 Meeting.
- ➢ A marked up/redlined checklist attempting to reference the RIHousing checklist throughout Member Bacher's.

## **November 7, 2023 Meeting Minutes Excerpt:**

Town Planner Crean and Clerk Gillibrand reviewed the current master plan for major land development checklist, that there will be updates effective January 1, 2024 with the new legislation, and what the Board could consider in advance of a major land development application from 33 Middle Highway and the Town's expectations. They noted that there is no application submitted at this time, and no indication on when they may submit. The Board asked how the new legislation would affect an application submittal, and Assistant Solicitor Goins noted the new amendments would be required. Member Bacher distributed a marked up existing master plan checklist of optional items for consideration, and the Board should know upfront what to ask for in order for the application to be certified complete. Member Bacher specifically noted engineering/project reviews under 200-15.1 of the current Land Development and Subdivision Regulations, recommending queueing up those reviews and that the application will not be complete until those are submitted.

Town Planner Crean noted this can be discussed more in depth, and asked if some of the requested reviews would be at the preliminary plan stage. Assistant Solicitor Goins noted standards changing for master plan, but one item where there is leverage is a Zoning Ordinance amendment. The applicant would be encouraged to work with the Town on their application as the fate of the project rests with the Town Council. Member Godfrey concurred Member Bacher's comments and threshold questions.

Due to the library closing at 9:00 PM, the remainder of the agenda will be continued to the next regular business meeting.
