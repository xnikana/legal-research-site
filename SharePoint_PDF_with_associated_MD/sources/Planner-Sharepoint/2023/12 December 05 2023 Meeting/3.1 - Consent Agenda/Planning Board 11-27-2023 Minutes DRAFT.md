### **4. Legislative Updates**

# **4.1 Discuss and Act: Provide recommendation on the Zoning Ordinance Amendments for the December 4, 2023 Town Council public hearing.**

*Present: Peter Friedrichs and Kim Salerno, Libra Planners*

Town Planner Crean reviewed the meeting materials, including a summary of the amendments, consistency with the Comprehensive Plan, definitions for review, minimum lot sizes information, and drafted specific and objective criteria from Libra Planners. The objective of the meeting is to come to a motion that will provide recommendation to the Town Council for a public hearing on December 4. The Land Development and Subdivision Regulations will be covered at the December 5, 2023 Regular Meeting.

Friedrichs reviewed the work of Libra Planners, with a focus on development plan review, comprehensive permits, and special use permit specific and objective criteria.

Changes and discussions included the following:

## **Article I: General Provisions.**

- ➢ Absent land-use laws added.
- ➢ Deletion of "Community" from Comprehensive Plan for clarity, done throughout the document.
- ➢ Additional purpose included in response to the Americans with Disabilities Act that was absent from the ordinance.

# **Article II: Definitions.**

- ➢ **185-5.2.** New section for terms related to comprehensive permitting.
- ➢ Addition of terms from various enabling acts that are referenced in the Zoning Ordinance or to address uses already listed in the use table without definitions.
- ➢ **Cluster.** Member Bacher asked to strike "shall be specified in the ordinance" from the definition.
- ➢ **Development Plan Review**. Member Bacher requested amendment to Development Plan Review definition. Solicitor Goins noted to add "pursuant to 185-151" for reference.
- ➢ **Golf Course.** Discussion ensued on the definition of "golf course," reviewing the Town Manager's concern about the revised definition from the October 3 meeting. Member Bacher noted there is one golf course in Town, being the Rhode Island County Club, and it would be difficult to create another one, therefore was not overly concerned about the revised definition. The Board agreed to leave the language the way it was.
- ➢ **Fruit or Vegetable Stand.** Member Bacher expressed concern about the requirement to remove by sunset each day and overnight takedown, Chair Phipps noted the Farm School on Federal Road as an example. The Board discussed further, comparing it with chicken and hens, and noted that 50 square feet is not a substantial size. The Board agreed to strike language from the definition related to set up and breakdown.
- ➢ **Low-or-Moderate Incoming Housing**. Member Bacher recommended removing everything after the State law reference. Member Godfrey concurred to reference the definitions from State law. Assistant Solicitor Goins noted having the State definition in the ordinance is helpful and would recommend keeping the language. The Board agreed to keep as is, with reference to Rhode Island General Law.
- ➢ **Vested Rights**. Friedrichs then asked Assistant Solicitor Goins about "Vested Rights." In the absence of a state definition, Assistant Solicitor Goins believed it is fine to put the definition in and does not see it being harmful.
- ➢ **Modification**. Member Bacher noted under "modification" to change the percentage to 15 percent rather than the State's ceiling of 25 percent.
- ➢ **Recreation Boat Storage**. Member Bacher noted that under "Recreation Boat Storage" to add reference to 185-82.

### **Article IV: Use Regulations (Use Table)**.

➢ **B. Residential and Related Uses.** Separation of affordable ADUs and ADUs to reflect updates to the language in Article II: Definitions. Assistant Solicitor Goins noted that accessory dwelling units are likely going to be a topic of discussion and review in 2024.

- ➢ **C. Open Recreation Uses.** Member Bacher asked about the public or commercial swimming pool, and why that is allowed by special use permit in residential zones. The Board agreed to switch "S" to "N" in the R-25 and R-40 Districts.
- ➢ **E. Public and Semipublic Uses.** For "hospital or healthcare facility," Member Bacher commented that could also be a "clinic," suggesting the switch be to "S" in the Neighborhood Business District, only for the hospital or health care facility not for mental, alcoholic, or drug addiction treatment. The Board concurred.

## ➢ **I. Retail Business.**

- o For Community or regional shopping center, Member Bacher commented that by the Town's definition these exist in the Neighborhood Business District, and recommended allowing by special use permit. After further discussion, Town Planner Crean noted that the Board will have further discussion of individual uses during the Comprehensive Plan update process. The Board came to consensus to continue this item for a later discussion.
- o For Food Preparation, Assistant Solicitor Goins noted Member Godfrey's concern about food preparation and/or sales being not permitted in the Elderly Housing Zone and clarified that would mean that food preparation and/or sales is saying not permitted as a principal use, but allowed as an accessory one. Assistant Solicitor Goins reiterated that the use table generally refers to principal uses.
- ➢ **H. Transportation and related uses.** Vice Chair Rua asked about "Public Park and Ride," and Goins briefly reviewed the State's process for going through. The Board agreed to keep as "S".
- ➢ **185-8.2.** "Neighborhood Restaurants" was moved to 185-73.1 under "Restaurants."
- ➢ **185-12 Specific prohibited uses and uses not listed.**
  - o Addition of "driving range" and "miniature golf course" to specific prohibited uses.
  - o Addition of 185-12b with language from the new legislation, allowing an applicant to request a use not specifically listed. Member Bacher asked about process for those not listed, using an example of a residential windmill application when he was on the Zoning Board. Assistant Solicitor Goins noted at Zoning Board hearings there is counsel present who can assist.

### **Article VIII: Substandard Lots of Record.**

**185.28.** Friedrichs then moved to discussion on Article VIII: Substandard Lots of Record. Town Planner Crean referred to Libra's memorandum. Friedrichs walked through the memorandum, referring to the image on page two. By minimum lot sizes reduced to zero, the Town would not have to adjust setbacks for substandard lots. Member Bacher asked about how that would affect subdivisions. Assistant Solicitor Goins noted how Building Code would still need to be considered. The Board engaged in further discussion about options with Libra Planners. "Minimum Lot Area" for newly created lots. Assistant Solicitor Goins noted the State is not comfortable with Towns trying to get around the new legislation. The Board agreed to Option C in the memorandum from Libra, noting it would promote smaller, more affordable homes.

### **Article IX: Nonconforming Uses and Structures.**

**185-30.** Friedrichs then moved to discussion Article IX, 185-30 to review the required adaptive reuse language. Assistant Solicitor Goins noted it will be further reviewed and will be subject to Planning Board review depending on threshold. Member Bacher asked for clarified language on 185-30B3, and Member Godfrey asked to clarify 185-30B5. Assistant Solicitor Goins recommended redrafting based on State templates. The Board agreed.

Friedrichs then briefly reviewed required changes to the following:

- ➢ **Article X: Enforcement and Administration.** To reflect requirement to allow modification process, outlined in 185-40.
- ➢ **Article XI : Amendments.** Changes to public notice procedure and registry requirement.
- ➢ **Article XII: Zoning Board of Review.** Changes to reflect 2022 updates, unified development review language, and new notice requirement language.
- ➢ **Article XIII: Variances**. Required revised standards for dimensional variance and inclusion of the disabilities language.

## **Article XIV: Special Use Permits.**

185-73.1 – Specific and Objective Criteria drafted to address all categories of the Use Table with "S."

- ➢ **C. Senior residential community.** Typo in C1 to remove "4."
- ➢ **G. Accessory living quarters of guest house.** No special use, can be removed.
- ➢ **H. Hotel or motel**.
  - o **(1)** Member Bacher asked if a sprinkler system is required regardless, and if that needs to be a criterion. Friedrichs noted there is a State requirement that could be appealed or an applicant could request a variance, and adding that having a standard removes that option.
  - o **(2)** Member Bacher noted to add "vehicles for" after "sufficient to hold…" to clarify.
  - o **(5)** Member Bacher noted that this criterion is mentioned throughout, and confusion with tying the condition to the certificate of occupancy. Assistant Solicitor Goins explained how that has worked in other municipalities. Member Bacher noted it reads more as a requirement than criterion.
  - o **(6)** The Board agreed to add language of "for a new special use permit" after "submitted" and apply to all other uses that have this standard for clarification.
- ➢ **J. Golf Course**. Member Bacher asked the one standard listing chemicals is enough, however, from prior discussion noted there is rare likelihood for an application. The Board discussed the Fertilizer Ordinance, noting how it mainly addresses timing and amounts more than specifics. The Board agreed to revisit these criteria at a later date and to leave as is.
- ➢ **Q. Religious Building.**
  - o **(1B)** Member Bacher commented to strike "the main entrance."
  - o **(2)** Assistant Solicitor Goins and the Board agreed to strike the whole line related to religious motifs.
- ➢ **R. Hospitals or health care facilities.**

o (2) – add "and healthcare" after "hospital." The Board also agreed that rather than submitting detailed plans of the building the criterion be changed to provide a certification letter required by the Department of Health.

## ➢ **S. Congregate healthcare facility.**

- o (2) Architect certification letter required by the DOH. 5B "one vehicle for 20 residents" to replace "four vehicles." Town Planner Crean asked if threshold is needed – Friedrichs noted could be an issue. Friedrichs noted one vehicle for 20 residents is generally fine. Assistant Solicitor Goins noted that nursing homes are closing around the State, and recommended circling back at the beginning of the year.
- ➢ **T. Cemetery.** Member Bacher asked why there was so many criteria for cemeteries and asked about changing S to N. Town Planner Crean noted keeping certain areas as special use were a Town Manager note, noted the Town's Cemetery Commission, and how she would be reluctant to accept that without a larger conversation. The Board agreed to circle back on that item, so long as the standards are not affecting existing cemeteries to leave as is.
- ➢ **U. Day-care center.** Member Bacher suggested removing 3C. The Board agreed to strike 3C.
- ➢ **W. Cultural Activity.** The Board agreed to strike 1B.
- ➢ **AA. Utility Pole.** Regarding (1) requirement for landscape architect plan, after further discussion, the Board determined to keep the requirement as is.
- ➢ **AC. Commercial off-street parking facility, lot or area**. Clerk Gillibrand reviewed the Town Manager's recommendation to remove "Maple Avenue and County Road" as it would exclude other areas of the Business District. Member Bacher commented that Waseca Avenue is not an appropriate spot. Vice Chair Rua noted removing the street names applies the standards everywhere and not only to those two roads. The Board agreed to remove the "Maple and County" language to read "in the back of the building." Member Bacher noted a lot is not toward the back of a building as there is no building. The Board clarified that the commercial off-street parking would be a principal use and not an accessory to a building. Vice Chair Rua asked about changing the S to N and commented that is his recommendation. The Board agreed to that and struck the criteria.
- ➢ **AE to AG.** Added language to the traffic study criterion to read "for facilities that require land development project review."
- ➢ **AI. Dry-cleaning facility.** There was discussion on "no onsite laundering." The Board agreed to move "dry-cleaning facility" to the AF criteria instead.
- ➢ **AJ. Automatic car-washing facility.** Add language to 2B to read "into the sanitary sewer system" rather than "on site."
- ➢ **AM. Gasoline filling station and automotive repair garage.** Member Bacher noted that Department of Environmental Management (DEM) regulations are a given, and asked about striking criterion (1). Assistant Solicitor Goins noted those references are usually a comfort factor as they are required regardless. The Board then ensured in further discussion on criterion 3, but was left as is. The Board then agreed to add "into the sanitary sewer system" to replace "on site" in criterion 8.
- ➢ **AN. Restaurants.** The Board struck criteria 2 and 3.
- ➢ **AT**. **Drive-through.** The Boar agreed to add a traffic study requirement.

## **Article XV: Off-Street Parking Requirements.**

➢ **185-78 E (Shared Parking).** Friedrich revision to "permitting authority" for clarity.

## **Article XXIV: Development Plan Review.**

- ➢ **185-150 General Provisions.** Assistant Solicitor Goins recommended changing the language and clarified that Development Plan Review (DPR) does not require notice, and if the Board wants design standards to add that to the Land Development and Subdivision Regulations. Member Kurtz noted the Board should air on the side of more public hearings. Assistant Solicitor Goins and Friedrich noted these can be addressed with the Comprehensive Plan updates, and that design guidelines will require significant study and effort.
- ➢ **185-151 Uses requiring development plan review**. Assistant Solicitor Goins recommended to put back in the stricken language, the Board concurred.
- ➢ There was further discussion on the Planning Board being listed as the permitting authority, and that DPR is going to be further addressed in Comprehensive Plan update.

## **Article XXVI: Low- and Moderate- Income Housing.**

- ➢ **185-182 Application Procedure**. The Board agreed to strike (A) and to direct staff to reflect State requirements on fees.
- ➢ **185-185 Appeals.** To reflect required language and procedure.
