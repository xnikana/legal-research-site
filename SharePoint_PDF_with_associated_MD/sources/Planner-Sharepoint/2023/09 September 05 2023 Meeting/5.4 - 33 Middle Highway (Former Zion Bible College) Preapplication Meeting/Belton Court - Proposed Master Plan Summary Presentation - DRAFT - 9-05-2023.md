# Vision Statement \_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_

Create a high-quality neighborhood that fosters community and meets the diverse housing needs of current and future residents of Barrington

![](Belton Court - Proposed Master Plan Summary Presentation - DRAFT - 9-05-2023_attachments/_page_1_Picture_2.jpeg)

### Design Guidelines \_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_

- Traditional neighborhood development that is built on the following principles:
  - Meets the housing needs of residents with diverse demographic and economic profiles
  - Open space concentrated in public areas
  - Promotes walkability and social engagement
  - High quality infrastructure designed to address stormwater and other environmental concerns
  - Traditional architecture that stands the test of time
  - Incorporate latest energy efficient technological advances

### **Benefits of this Master Plan**

- Mix of housing types will further diversify the housing options available for current and future residents of Barrington
- Provides high quality housing options in an aesthetically pleasing neighborhood for households including, but not limited to:
  - Move down buyers/empty nesters
  - Senior residents
  - Young couples seeking to establish a foothold in Barrington
  - Workforce housing
- Land plan and quality of infrastructure promotes walkability and social interaction
- Minimum of 40% of property will be protected and or serve as useable greenspace/open-area spread throughout the community
- Portions of Belton Court will be preserved/renovated and integrated into the community as commercial and civic spaces open to public
- Significant contribution of affordable housing to help Barrington lessen the gap between it's current 3% level and the 10% goal mandated by the state

![](Belton Court - Proposed Master Plan Summary Presentation - DRAFT - 9-05-2023_attachments/_page_12_Picture_11.jpeg)

# **Benefits** of this Master Plan (cont.)

- Minimal impact on school capacity as project is estimated to only add approximately 77 additional school aged children upon full buildout (spread between grades K-12)
- Provides Barrington with the ability to acquire and renovate a town rec center at a fraction of the cost of developing a new one (potentially resulting in a \$5 million - \$8 million cost saving)
- Provides significant positive fiscal impact to Town:
  - Approximately \$3,7 million gross tax revenue upon buildout (in 2023 \$'s) resulting in a revenue surplus of \$2.1 million after allocated municipal costs and school expenses
  - Secondary positive fiscal impact from new resident spending at local retail and restaurant establishments
- Improved stormwater management system will benefit surrounding neighborhoods
- Increased energy efficiency of homes by incorporating latest economically viable green technologies
