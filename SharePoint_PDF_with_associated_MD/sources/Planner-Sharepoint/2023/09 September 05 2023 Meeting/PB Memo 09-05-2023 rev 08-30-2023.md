**5.2 Discuss and Act: Minor Subdivision, Concept Review for 3 College Lane, AP 15, Lot 84, Residence-10 Zone. Applicant proposes to create one new residential lot for affordable housing**.

Please see the included application, site plan, and correspondence with the Housing Network of RI regarding the application. At this stage, the applicant is seeking feedback and comments from the Board prior to the Preliminary Plan submission. The Board is not charged with making any decisions or motions on this matter at the concept review stage.

There are the following conditions on the subject property, from a 2014 Decision of the Planning Board (see item 5):

*Minor Subdivision – 39 Primrose Hill Avenue. Proposed subdivision of Assessor's Plat 15, Lot 84, to create two new house lots with frontage on Primrose Hill Avenue, College Lane and Elizabeth Road. Existing house at 39 Primrose Hill Avenue to remain.*

Motion: On a motion by Ms. Robertson seconded by Mr. Adams, the Board approved (5-0) the following:

"The Barrington Planning Board hereby grants Preliminary Plan approval for the "39 Primrose Subdivision," as depicted on plans for property located on Primrose Hill Road, College Lane and Elizabeth Road in Barrington, Rhode Island, Assessor's Plat 15, Lot 84, Residence 10 Zoning District. Plans by: Scituate Surveys, Inc, 410 Tiogue Avenue, Coventry, RI, dated February 25, 2014, with revisions through April 16, 2014. Engineering plans by SFM Engineering Associates, 510 Tiogue Avenue, Coventry, RI 02816. Approval is based upon the following findings of fact and conditions of approval:

#### **Findings of Fact:**

- 1. The proposed subdivision is consistent with the Comprehensive Community Plan in that the site is zoned for residential development with a minimum lot size of 10,000 square feet each (Residence 10 zoning);
- 2. The proposed lots are in compliance with the standards and provisions of the Barrington Zoning Ordinance, with the exception of the existing house at 39 Primrose Hill Road, which encroaches in the minimum front-yard setback. The encroachment is pre-existing, and the non-conformity will not increase with the proposed subdivision; therefore, no relief from the Zoning Board is required.
- 3. There will be no significant negative environmental impacts from the proposed subdivision, which is located on an infill site within an established neighborhood and requires minimal grading and vegetation removal for construction of the new house. Further, the property is in the "X" flood zone, with 0.2% annual chance of flooding, and the site contains no wetlands. Finally, the engineer's drainage study concludes the development will reduce the overall impervious surface coverage, with the new houses and driveways replacing the existing greenhouse and parking areas.

- 4. All proposed lots would have adequate and permanent physical access to public roadways Primrose Hill Road, Elizabeth Road and College Lane.
- 5. By adding two buildable lots, with the third lot containing an existing dwelling, the proposal does not require the provision of an affordable housing unit under mandatory inclusionary zoning. However, any future subdivision of Parcel 1 would require the provision of an affordable housing unit on the new lot.
