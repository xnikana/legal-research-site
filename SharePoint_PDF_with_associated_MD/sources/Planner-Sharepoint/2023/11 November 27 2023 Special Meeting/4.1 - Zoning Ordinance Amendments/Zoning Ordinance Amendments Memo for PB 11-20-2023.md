SUBJECT: Zoning Ordinance Amendments (to reflect State Legislative Updates effective January 1,

2024)

There are a host of ordinance amendments to be made based on bills recently passed by the Rhode Island Legislature, effective January 1, 2024. Some to be made to the Town's Zoning Ordinance (requiring Town Council review and adoption) and some to be made the Town's Land Development and Subdivision Regulations (requiring Planning Board review and adoption). The majority of amendments are dictated by Rhode Island General Law, and the Planning Board is working with Town staff and the municipal technical assistant program (MTAP) consultant on areas where there are increased options for local control.

#### **This will be a two-phase process to meet the January 1, 2024 deadline:**

- **1. Updates to Chapter 185 – Zoning Ordinance**
- November 6 Town Council: Introduce Amendments.
- **November 27 Planning Board: Recommendation on Amendments.**
  - o Please read through the provided draft of the Zoning Ordinance (provided both as simple markup and clean copy).
  - o **SPECIFIC ITEMS FOR REVIEW**:
    - **Specific and objective criteria for special use table** (use table 185-8, criteria 185-73.1).
    - **Minimum lot size**s (185-28).
    - **Definitions** (185-5).
- December 4 Town Council: Public Hearing and Adoption.
- 2. Updates to Chapter 200 Land Development and Subdivision Regulations
- November 7 Planning Board: Introduce Amendments.

- December 5 Planning Board: Public Hearing and Adoption. [Per state law, the Planning Board has the authority to adopt/amend the Regulations without Council approval.]

### **C.FOR REVIEW:**

**(1) Definitions that were added to Article II, 185-5 to reflect the use table. Please review for any recommendations on revisions:**

#### **ANIMAL HOSPITAL**

Provides the services of a veterinary clinic with more specialized care and emergency facilities.

#### **AUTOMOTIVE REPAIR GARAGE**

A structure sheltering the type of automotive maintenance and repair work customarily conducted in gas station garages.

#### **BATHING BEACH (PUBLIC OR PRIVATE)**

Specific area of land and waters designed and intended for the recreational use or enjoyment of users.

#### **DRY CLEANING FACILITY**

A building or facility used primarily for dry cleaning clothing and garments. This is a separate use from a dry-cleaning drop off service (see Personal convenience services).

#### **FRUIT OR VEGETABLE STAND**

A temporary seasonal structure used for the sale of fruits, vegetables, or agricultural products that is sited on the lot on which the products being offered for sale have been grown or reared. The square footage of the stand shall not exceed 50 square-feet in footprint. The stand must be removed by sunset each day, with overnight setup prohibited.

#### **FUNERAL HOME**

A place, establishment, or premises, licensed by the State of Rhode Island, devoted to the activities that are incident, convenient, or related to the care and preparation, arrangement, financial and otherwise, for the funeral, transportation, burial, or other disposition of human dead bodies and including, but not limited to, a suitable room with all instruments and supplies used for the storage or preparation of dead human bodies for burial or other disposition. Also referred to as a mortuary in this chapter.

#### **GOLF COURSE**

A parcel used for golf purposes, including fairways, greens, tennis, squash and related sports facilities, pool, restaurants and banquet facilities, and/or maintenance facilities.

![](Zoning Ordinance Amendments Memo for PB 11-20-2023_attachments/_page_22_Picture_17.jpeg)

- **Though this definition was the initial recommendation from the Planning Board discussion on October 3, internal Town staff discussion elicited the concern that this would make the Rhode Island Country Club nonconforming. Staff asks the Board to revisit this definition and consider expanding the definition to accommodate the Country Club uses. Golf Courses are only permitted via special use permit in the Recreation and Education Zone, prohibited in all other zones.**
- **Suggestion from Solicitor: "accessory uses, including but not limited to…"**

#### **MORTUARY**

See "funeral home."

#### **PARK (PUBLIC OR PRIVATE)**

Specific area of land designed and intended for the recreational use or enjoyment of users.

#### **PARK AND RIDE FACILITY**

Parking lots that are free of charge to anyone to encourage public transit or carpool. All vehicles are parked at the owner's risk.

#### **RECREATION BOAT STORAGE**

Dry-storage of boats intended for recreational use.

#### **RECYCLING CENTER**

A building or facility for the purpose of collecting and sorting recyclable materials.

#### **SANITARY LANDFILL CENTER**

A licensed land disposal site employing an engineered method of disposal of solid waste in a manner that absolutely minimizes environmental hazards, including: spreading the solid waste in thin layers, compacting the solid waste to the smallest practical volume; and applying cover material at the end of each operating day, or at such more frequent intervals as may be necessary. A sanitary landfill shall also mean a solid waste landfill.

#### **VETERINARY OFFICE**

A building or facility providing veterinarian services, including medical treatment, examination, surgery, diagnosis, grooming, and general health care of domestic animals and birds.

#### **WATER STORAGE TANK**

An elevated structure containing potable water.
