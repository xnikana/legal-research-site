## Roni,

At the Planning Board meeting last week, the Solicitor advised that we needed to develop more detailed Special Use conditions than we currently have. In view of that, and recognizing that staff has an awful lot on their plate, I sat down over the past week to draft this suggestion as to how these conditions might be approached. This is a draft, for discussion, and of course there will be other views and thoughts to be incorporated. Given our tight deadline, some version of this could be adopted now, and then modified in the spring when we've had more time to think it through. The Solicitor also advised that these conditions should properly be incorporated into Article XIV of the Zoning Ordinance. Accordingly I have formatted these in a way that I think would easily side into those provisions. These conditions would replace ALL the footnotes we've been looking at in the Use Tables.

Underneath the Special Use permit conditions, I have noted suggested modifications or additions to the Definitions section of the ordinance, which either support the conditions suggested or reflect other comments from last week's meeting. Then I have included a suggested way of including the new Adaptive Reuse provisions, since that needs to get into the Use Tables in some fashion.

I hope you find this helpful in advancing the discussion.

Best regards,

Larry

# §185-5 **Definitions**

### **FARMING**

*Per suggestion of the Solicitor, the setback requirement for accessory structures housing livestock should be removed to a footnote in the Dimensions Regulation Table.*

# **FRUIT OR VEGETABLE STAND**

*Suggest removing the the last sentence, requiring removal each evening. I believe this would be an onerous requirement for the Farm School, and at least one woman on Sowams runs a small stand selling vegetables from her garden - not sure she could manage this. If the intent is to prevent the establishment of permanent commercial stands, the 50 square feet limitation restricts most productive commercial use.*

### **GOLF COURSE**

A parcel used for golf purposes, including fairways, greens ands related sports facilities.

*Following the discussion about this use at the Planning Board meeting October 3rd, suggest removing the language addressing accessory uses. The Special Permit conditions above are intended to address the accessory uses.*

#### **HOTEL**

*Per suggestion of the Solicitor, the minimum lot size requirement for hotels should be removed to a footnote in the Dimensions Regulation Table.*

#### **INCIDENTAL USE OR STORAGE**

Use or storage as a subordinate activity to that which establishes the occupancy or area classification.

### **MATERIAL RECOVERY FACILITY**

A facility which receives recyclable materials and sorts them by type or grade to meet commonly accepted standards needed for further processing or manufacturing.

# **MATERIAL TRANSFER STATION**

A facility which receives, temporarily stores and ships unprocessed waste and recyclables.

### **MODIFICATION**

*Suggest the 25% restriction, which is legacy language from the current ordinance that is at the same disabled in that definition, should be modified to 15% in agreement with H6059 45-24-46. Believe it would also be good form to remove this procedural statement from the Definitions to Article VIII.*

#### **ORGANIC WASTE MATERIAL**

Materials that are the organic portion of the solid waste stream, contain carbon-to-carbon bonds and are biodegradable, such as paper, wood, food scraps, food processing residue leaves, and yard waste.

### **RECYCLED CONTENT MANUFACTURING**

Processing to turn recyclable materials into either consumer products or products used in further manufacturing.

## **SWIMMING POOL**

*Suggest deleting "in connection with a residential use". The Use Tables address a commercial swimming pool that is otherwise left undefined in §185-5.*

#### **WATER STORAGE TANK**

*Suggest deleting the word "potable". Water storage tanks are commonly used for industrial, fire protection and farm uses that would not require a potable source. As written, the term also seems to prohibit grey water systems, which I do not believe is the intent*

# §185-8.3 **Adaptive Reuse**

Adaptive reuse is permitted as set forth in §185-73.1 providing it includes the development of at least fifty percent (50%) of the existing gross floor area into residential units except where such is prohibited by environmental land use restrictions recorded on the property by the state of Rhode Island department of environmental management or the United States Environmental Protection Agency preventing the conversion to residential use.

#### A . Density.

- (1) For projects that meet the following criteria, the development may include up to fifteen (15) dwelling units per acre:
  - (a) Where the project is limited to the existing footprint, except that the footprint is allowed to be expanded to accommodate upgrades related to the building and fire codes and utilities; and
  - (b) The development includes at least twenty percent (20%) low- and moderate-income housing; and
  - (c) The development has access to public sewer and water service or has access to adequate private water, such as a well and and/or wastewater treatment system(s) approved by the relevant state agency for the entire development as applicable.
- (2) For all other adaptive reuse projects, the residential density permitted in the converted structure shall be the maximum allowed that otherwise meets all standards of minimum housing and has access to public sewer and water service or has access to adequate private water, such as a well, and wastewater treatment system(s) approved by the relevant state agency for the entire development, as applicable, The density proposed shall be determined to meet all public health and safety standards.
- B. Dimensional Non-conformance.
- (1) For adaptive reuse projects, existing building setbacks shall remain and shall be considered legal nonconforming, but no additional encroachments shall be permitted into any nonconforming setback, unless otherwise allowed by zoning ordinance or relief is granted by the applicable authority.
- (2) For adaptive reuse projects, the height of the existing structure, if it exceeds the maximum height of the zoning district, may remain and shall be considered legal nonconforming, and any rooftop construction shall be included within the height exemption

*The above suggested addition to §185-8.3 follows very closely the language of H6090 Substitute A 45- 24-37(h)(1), and I have referred to it in editing the special use requirements. Counsel may have a better way to insert this and better wording to deploy.*
