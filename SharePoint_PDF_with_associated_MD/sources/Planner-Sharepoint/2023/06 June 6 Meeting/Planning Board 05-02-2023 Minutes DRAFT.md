# **4.1 Discuss and Act: Master Plan Decision, Comprehensive Permit, 17 Lorraine Street**

Robinson Property, 17 Lorraine Street. Proposal would subdivide a 15,687 square foot lot (Assessor's Plat 10, Lot 41) into three separate lots. The existing multi-family building will be demolished. The existing "old windmill" will remain; it will make up the single-family residence on Lot #1. Lot #2 will have a single-family dwelling. Lot #3 will contain a two-family dwelling. The site is zoned Residence-10. One of the units, proposed as part of Lot #3, will qualify as Low-Moderate Income Housing. Relief from provisions of the Zoning Ordinance and Subdivision Regulations is being sought through the Comprehensive Permit process.

Town Planner Crean provided an overview of the application proposal, and then reviewed the Master Plan draft, including the motion, conditions, compliance with the Comprehensive Plan and Zoning Ordinance, the relief being sought, and notes from the prior meetings regarding parking, the units (affordable versus market-rate), request for an arborist report, and requirements for Preliminary Plan submission.

Chair Phipps clarified how the conditions are enforced and followed through, and clarified it is to be two spaces for each unit.

Assistant Solicitor Goins addressed Member Bacher's question about the required findings, reviewing state law and how for Comprehensive Permits the Planning Board acts as the Zoning Board for what relief will be granted from the ordinance.

Member Rua said that parking was not determined for the proposed single-family home, and Member Bacher responded how the Zoning Ordinance outlines parking requirements. Assistant Solicitor Goins, Town Planner Crean, and the Board noted the decision will be revised to reference the appropriate plan set from the March 7, 2023 Meeting, and to include the following:

- Page 6, 2A1 on page 6, fourth line correction "to develop."
- Page 7, 3C correction should be 3A, and strike the word "less."

Chair Phipps asked about the arborist report. Town Planner Crean noted that the Preliminary Plan application will require a public hearing, which will happen at the June meeting, and that report is part of the application package, and the Board will be able to discuss the report at that time. Town Planner Crean and Vice Chair Lucini noted that the Technical Review Committee has already reviewed and approved the application. Town Planner Crean noted the newspaper and abutter notice requirement for the public hearing. Town Planner Crean confirmed that she will make the revisions, have Chair Phipps sign the decision, and then it will be recorded with the Clerk's Office as part of Land Evidence.

**MOTION: Member Bacher, seconded by Member Rua, made a motion to approve the Master Plan decision with the corrections noted at this meeting. Motion carried unanimously, with Member Simms abstaining due to being absent for the February and March hearings.** 

### **5. New Business**

# **5.1 Discuss and Act: Training topics and schedule for summer Planning Board meetings.**

RI House Bill H7203 passed in June 2022, and requires initial, biennial, and continuing education in land use law and planning for local planning and zoning board members and members of historic district commissions. RI House Bill H5042 enacted in 2017 requires in (j) Each member of a planning board or commission shall participate in training and education classes concerning the effects of development in a flood plain and the effects of sea- level rise once every two (2) years. Each member shall complete two (2) hours of training in order to be certified for the two (2) years required by this subsection. Upon completion of the training, the planning board or commission member shall file with the municipal clerk a statement asserting that the training course has been completed.

Town Planner Crean reviewed the training requirements enacted in 2022. Assistant Solicitor Goins noted that herself and Solicitor Teitz are two of the people offering training, and in addition there are opportunities from Statewide Planning. Assistant Solicitor Goins noted that the training requirement will not impact decisions of the Board, but with a dedicated group the Board should aim for 100 percent completion. Assistant Solicitor Goins clarified that in-house training should be held in person rather than virtual due to Open Meetings Act requirements. Member Simms noted one of the Statewide Planning trainings Solicitor Teitz offered, and the benefits he gained from the training, including networking with other communities. Assistant Solicitor Goins recommended

waiting until the legislative session adjourns as there could be changes to the new requirement. Member Simms reiterated the value of the former instructional sessions he attended.

Town Planner Crean noted PREP-RI, which reviews topics about resilience, climate change, and mapping tools, and noted there is a module from Solicitor Teitz on resilience and legal issues, which is 18-minutes long that could be viewed and discussed as a Board. Town Planner Crean also noted the Roger Williams Marine Affairs and Law pilot program to look at regulations and legal issues related to coastal resilience, which the Town Council approved participation in at their May 1, 2023 meeting. Town Planner Crean provided some examples of what the clinic could research. Assistant Goins reviewed the coastal resilience overlay district, using South Kingstown as an example, intended to put property owners on notice and have such recorded in Land Evidence. Member Rua asked if the Board could participate in both in-house training and Statewide Planning's training.

Town Planner Crean noted the charge is to get a sense from the Board of what topics they would like to cover to meet the requirements. Chair Phipps asked about the July meeting being an option for training. Member Bacher asked about Shineharmony, and if the former Zion Bible College property at 33 Middle Highway will be on for the June meeting. Town Planner Crean responded that has yet to be determined, and then said she would send the PREP-RI training link to the Board.
