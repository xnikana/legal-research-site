## No minutes to approve – the minutes will be available at the June TRC Meeting.

4. Discuss and Act: Development of Design Guidelines for Commercial Signage (referred by the Town Council at May 1, 2023 Meeting).

Chair Stabach introduced the item, summarizing that Town Council referred review of commercial sign guidelines to the TRC, in context of how supportive they are of businesses, ease of understanding and implementation. Planner Crean provided a one-page summary of the Town Council discussion from the video of the May 1, 2023 meeting.

Chair Stabach expressed it's important to consider what Barrington wants to be. It's not a historic town similar to Newport; Barrington is a suburban community. He asked if there is guidance that indicates the actual look of signage – contemporary, or in line with Town Hall. There are no images in the existing ordinance, and images can say a thousand words to show range of dimensions, visibility, setbacks, obstruction of visibility to drivers, and other features. Examples and images showing details and dimensions to provide clarity for this.

The TRC was provided with the signage sections from the Zoning Ordinance, Chapter 185, Article XVII, and Section 185-76 pertaining to Special Use Permits. Planner Crean shared that the due date to Town Council could be for the July 24th meeting, so TRC could use the June meeting to continue discussion on this.

Member Szosz noted Barrington does not have actual design guidelines and there are currently no images included in the existing ordinance and agreed that visuals/images can be helpful. Ground mounted and others – how do we consider design character and safety considerations? Member Wetherill added there are "look and feel" concepts in the ordinances, but the ordinance is vague and there is a lot open for interpretation. He added there should be guidance but also flexibility.

Member Wetherill asked who would write the update and suggested bringing in a technical writer to compile all ordinances in one place. Discussion by members noted that the resulting ordinance should be user friendly, easier to follow, organized by use, and overall, a simpler synopsis of what's permitted or prohibited.

Member Wetherill shared a concern that recent applicants either haven't read the existing guidelines or haven't interpreted them accurately. Member Lucini added that in recent reviews it was addressed that "internally illuminated" is a vague notion now and isn't the same meaning as it used to be.

Member Wetherill asked what the Town Council is looking for from the referral. Planner Crean shared that the Town Council is interested in knowing if the Town needs an update to the sign ordinances and how the Boards/Commissions would like to see this effort advance.

Chair Stabach shared that consultants hired to modify sign ordinance can meet with the Economic Development Committee, the TRC and Planning Board to ensure each group has a chance to review a draft of the ordinance and emphasize need to make ordinances clearer and more accessible.

Planner Crean shared that the Town's Solicitor can do the writing for a fee, but we will have to decide if we assign this to staff for research or contract the work out to a separate paid consultant. Chair Stabach said it would be helpful to have someone who does this every day and has signage expertise, and a consultant outside of Town Hall could be more discerning.

Member Szosz suggested that the Town shortens the ordinance and make it streamlined, and he noted that the Town-owned signs should also be included in the regulations. The recent addition of the landmark/wayfinding signs and also the many signs at the library campus should be carefully reviewed and subject to approvals. Member Wetherill asked if the TRC needs to recommend restrictions.

Member Lucini asked if a related light ordinance process could be combined with this discussion. Planner Crean shared that the Solicitor is currently working on an updated light ordinance in coordination with the Town's Building Official to address lighting disputes and complaints that have been presented to the Town. As included in the Comprehensive Plan, "Dark Sky" principles are being considered for inclusion in the ordinance, as well as what is required for safety purposes. Members recommended we combine this signage task with the lighting ordinance update.

Member Wetherill directed that Planner Crean write up the minutes, then they can determine how they want to make a recommendation at the June meeting.

Chair Stabach noted there have been many changes to signage in recent years – materials, life cycle, lighting, new technologies, timing. Member Szosz added that many items in the ordinance are mixed up and out of order, some repetition, and the Town needs someone to spend some time working on it to make it look better.

No motion was made, but the TRC will review the minutes from this meeting and continue the discussion in June 2023.

5. Discuss and Act: Establish a cutoff date for submission of materials to the Planning, Building, and Resilience office for TRC packets/handouts.

Planner Crean shared that the Planning Board passed a three-week deadline for applications to the Planning Office to ensure the Planning staff and Planning Board have ample time to review and be prepared for Planning Board meetings.

Planner Crean researched that Westerly and South Kingstown have an 8-week lead time for applications. To ensure staff has enough time to review and issue Certificate of Completeness, request Department reviews, and have a TRC meeting in advance of Planning Board, an 8-week lead time is being recommended for major subdivisions only, to become effective July 1, 2023 if supported by the TRC and Planning Board.

Chair Stabach asked what happens if the Planning Board has major revisions, noting that it's not fair to the applicant if Planning Board then requires significant changes that late in the process. Members noted that a pre-application meeting and TRC review should be able to address major issues to prevent applicants from having to experience unnecessary delays when the application goes in front of the Planning Board.

The TRC agreed that they only need one week to review application materials in advance of their meeting, and all members agreed that they prefer to receive electronic copies, rather than hard copies.

MOTION – Member Wetherill made a motion to require that the Planner provide application materials to the TRC members one week in advance of their meetings, seconded by Chair Stabach. Motion passed unanimously.

6. Adjourn

MOTION: Chair Stabach made a motion to adjourn, seconded by Member Wetherill. Meeting adjourned at 8:18 PM.
