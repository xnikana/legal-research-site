#### **DECISION**

The following Planning Board Decision, which provides procedural history and establishes legal conclusions, is based on the Planning Board's motion granting Master Plan approval.

The Barrington Planning Board hereby grants Master Plan approval for the Master Plan/Comprehensive Permit Application – Robinson Property, 17 Lorraine Street. Proposal would subdivide a 15,687 square foot lot (Assessor's Plat 10, Lot 41) into three separate lots. The existing multi-family building will be demolished. The existing "old windmill"/tower will remain; it will make up the single-family residence on Lot #1. Lot #2 will have a single-family dwelling. Lot #3 will contain a two-family dwelling. The site is zoned Residence-10. One of the units, proposed as part of Lot #3, will qualify as Low-Moderate Income Housing. Relief from provisions of the Zoning Ordinance and Subdivision Regulations is being sought through the Comprehensive Permit process.

This decision is based upon the following legal conclusions with supporting findings of fact and conditions of approval:

#### **Procedural History**

The Applicant filed a comprehensive permit application in September 2022, consisting of a proposal to subdivide a 15,687 square foot lot (Assessor's Plat 10, Lot 41) into three separate lots. The existing multi-family building will be demolished. The existing tower will remain; it will make up the single-family residence on Lot #1. Lot #2 will have a single-family dwelling. Lot #3 will contain a two-family dwelling. The site is zoned Residence-10.

One of the units is to be deed restricted as affordable housing; the remaining two units are market rate, the "tower" will remain in its existing condition with no proposal for additional structures built on Lot 1. The 25 percent affordable housing percentage, based on one out of three units qualifying as affordable, meets the minimum 25% threshold required in a comprehensive permit application.

# LEGAL CONCLUSIONS in accordance with R.I. Gen. Laws § 45-53-4(a)(4)(v) with supporting FINDINGS OF FACT

The below conclusions and findings are specific to the evidence and testimony presented during the master plan stage of review.

#### **Relief Approved:**

In accordance with Article XXVI Low- and Moderate-Income Housing, of the Barrington Zoning Ordinance, the Planning Board grants the following relief from the following provisions of the Zoning Ordinance:

- Density requirement: The proposed density for the site is 0.12 acre lots, 4 dwelling units per acre. The allowed density is 0.25 acre lots.
- Dimensional relief: Existing non-conforming "old windmill" on the property (Lot 1) will be in violation of side yard and front yard setbacks when new Lot 1 is created.
- The applicant is requesting relief from provisions of the Zoning Ordinance Sections:

- 185-8(B), requirement that two-family dwellings in an R-10 Zone obtain special use permit.
- 185-17 & 185-96, minimum lot size requirements and minimum lot width/frontage requirement.

### Findings of Fact and Conditions of Approval

Approval is based upon the following findings of fact and conditions of approval:

#### Findings of Fact:

- 1. By providing one affordable unit, or 25 percent of the four total proposed units, the proposed development is consistent with local needs as identified in the local Comprehensive Community Plan, including:
  - a. Continue to comply with the State's 10 percent affordable housing goal through provision of well-integrated and compatible affordable housing. (Housing & Neighborhoods Goal HN-3)
  - b. Objective HN-3.2: Produce affordable housing units at a rate that keeps Barrington on the path toward achieving the 10 percent affordable housing goal by 2035. (Housing & Neighborhoods Objective HN-3.2)
- 2. The proposed development is in compliance with the standards and provisions of Barrington's Zoning Ordinance and Subdivision Regulations, and where expressly varied or waived, local concerns that have been affected by the granting of a use variance and special use permit do not outweigh the state and local need for low- and moderate-income housing because of the following:
  - a. The variance is required as the proposal calls for:
    - i. Creation of three 0.12 acre lots, or four dwelling units per acre. The permitted density in the R-10 is 0.25 acre (10,000 square foot) lots. The proposal will remove a former multifamily nonconforming structure to develop residential-scale lots that are more in keeping with the character of the surrounding neighborhood.
    - ii. The existing non-conforming "old windmill"/tower on the property (Lot One) will be in violation of side yard and front yard setbacks when new Lot One is created.
  - b. The applicant is requesting relief from provisions of the Zoning Ordinance Sections:
    - i. 185-8(B), requirement that two-family dwellings in an R-10 Zone obtain special use permit.
    - ii. 185-17 & 185-96, minimum lot size requirements and minimum lot width/frontage requirement.

- 3. The proposed low- and moderate-income housing units are integrated within the development and are compatible in scale and architectural style to the market rate units within the project, as the affordable unit is on the second floor of the proposed two-story house on Lot Three. The affordable units will be built and occupied prior to, or simultaneous with, the construction and occupancy of the market rate units.
- 4. The Planning Board finds that there will be no significant negative environmental impacts from the proposed development, with all required conditions for approval. The applicant will be required to demonstrate that there will be no net increase or runoff of stormwater from the site compared to existing conditions.
- 5. There will be no significant negative impacts on the health and safety of current or future residents of the community in areas including, but not limited to, safe circulation of both pedestrian and vehicular traffic, with compliance with the conditions of approval of this decision. Detailed plans for utility connections, including water and sewer, site lighting and stormwater, will be required at the preliminary plan stage.
- 6. The proposed development will have adequate and permanent physical access to a public street, Lorraine Street.
- 7. The proposed development will not result in the creation of individual lots with any physical constraints to development that building on those lots according to pertinent regulations and building standards would be impracticable.
- 8. Consistent with the policies articulated in the Town of Barrington's Comprehensive Plan, the Board supports higher density than single-family housing at this location, which is in a Residence-10 zone.

## **Conditions of Approval:**

This application will be subject to the following conditions:

- 1. At the preliminary plan stage, the applicant shall provide:
  - a. An arborist report of existing trees on site.
  - b. Landscaping plan to show all removal of existing vegetation, revegetation and tree planting and landscaping on street rights-of-way and individual building lots.
  - c. Grading plan at one-foot contour intervals to show all proposed grading for drainage facilities and individual building sites or house lots; plan shall indicate limits of disturbance.
  - d. Plans for the control of soil erosion and sedimentation.

- e. Proposed drainage plan, including the addition of on-site drainage facilities.
- f. Proposed utilities plan, including the location and dimensions of all gas, electric, water and sewer lines, or other proposed above or underground utilities as applicable.
- g. Drainage calculations supplementing the proposed drainage plan prepared by a registered professional engineer.
- h. Written confirmation from the Department of Public Works that a designee has reviewed the preliminary plans for proposed sewer connections and stormwater control.
- i. Written confirmation from the Bristol County Water Authority that it is able to provide water service connection to the subject parcel.
- j. Draft copies of all legal documents describing the property, including proposed easements and rights-of-way, dedications, restrictions, or other required legal documents.
