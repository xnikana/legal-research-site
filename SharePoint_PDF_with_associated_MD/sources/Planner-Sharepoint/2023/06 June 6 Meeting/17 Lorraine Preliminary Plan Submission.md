17 Lorraine Preliminary Plan - Sanitary Sewer Review, Stormwater Control Review

Barrington Department of Public Works ("Barrington DPW"/"DPW") staff has reviewed the preliminary plan for a land development project at 17 Lorraine Street in the Town of Barrington, Rhode Island ("Town"). Barrington DPW's review of this plan was at the request of InSite Engineering Services LLC ("Applicant"), part of their due diligence for their preliminary plan checklist for the Town. DPW staff has prepared the following statement regarding the availability of sanitary sewer and preliminary stormwater control plan:

There exists a sanitary sewer force main on Lorraine Street potentially available for connection by the applicant. In the event of such a connection, the applicant must adhere to all Town ordinances, regulations, and permitting requirements relating to sewer connections. These stipulations include but are not limited to: Applicant's sanitary sewer service connection permit application being approved by the Public Works Director or designee, all laterals must have backflow preventers and cleanouts, all fernco saddles for connections must be encased in cement concrete, DPW Sewer staff must be present during all work to inspect the sewer connection, etc. It is the sole responsibility of the applicant to familiarize themselves with the regulations, ordinances, and permitting requirements, as well as all Federal and State regulations relating to sewer connections before commencing the work.

Barrington DPW Staff has also reviewed the proposed stormwater control plan for the land development project at 17 Lorraine Street. Applicant must adhere to the Stormwater Design and Installation Standards Manual set forth by the State of Rhode Island, as well as all stormwater drainage regulations from Design Standards for Developments and Subdivisions found in Barrington's Planning Board Regulations. The proposed drainage system must be designed such that post-construction conditions do not result in peak run-off increases in rate from pre-development conditions. Peak run-off rates should be calculated for two-year, ten-year, twenty-five-year, and one-hundred-year frequency rainfall events. Erosion and sediment control BMPs should be designed, installed, and maintained in accordance with the Rhode Island Soil Erosion and Sediment Control Handbook. These erosion and sediment control measures must be installed prior to any land-disturbing activities, post-construction BMPs will be installed as necessary. All stormwater BMPs must be inspected after every precipitation event during construction.
