4. Discuss and Act: Comprehensive Permit Review, Preliminary Plan Submittal for 17 Lorraine Street (A.P. 10, Lot 041). Applicant seeks to subdivide the parcel into three separate lots, with one unit deed restricted as low-mod income (LMI) housing. The existing multi-family building will be demolished. The "tower" on the property would be preserved as a housing unit, it will make up the single-family residence on Lot 1. Lot 2 will have a single-family dwelling. Lot #3 will contain a twofamily dwelling.

The Planning Board directed the Planner to draft a Master Plan Decision at their March 7, 2023, Planning Board meeting, which is still being drafted and will be reviewed for approval at the May 2, 2023, Planning Board Meeting.

Member Lucini commented that the Preliminary Plan package look the same as the Master Plan submission. The new package includes the sewer and water confirmation, planting plans, and arborist report as required.

Attorney Hartman provided an update. Mr. Lewis Gentilly, engineer from InSite Engineers, noted that the only substantive change to note is the rain garden on Lot 1. Sheet number 3 illustrates that there's a 40x1 foot rain garden (40 square feet), and that the correct configuration is 40x2 feet, or 80 square feet. The civil and landscape plans don't match, but the landscape plan is the correct plan, and it will be reconciled to show the larger 80 square foot rain garden on Lot 1 in the engineering plan.

Member Wetherill asked that in the minutes from the planning board meeting, they requested

that the market rate unit be same footprint as the affordable unit. Scott Weymouth's plans were to be modified to ensure both units are the same size. Mr. Zeilstra noted that the footprint didn't change but the architectural details were modified to increase the interior dimensions. Previously the layout of the first-floor unit had a 2-story, 1st floor living/dining area. The revised plans removed the two-story living/dining area and added a full layout to the 2nd floor to create a similar footprint on the 2nd floor unit.

Chair Stabach asked for a summary of the stormwater analysis and impacts from the project, and the management design features on the site. Mr. Gentilly noted that the runoff from this development will be reduced from current conditions. Applicants used the State of Rhode Island Stormwater Management Guidance for Residential Lot Development (15–20-page document) to include a series of rain gardens, sized to address runoff from the impervious area proposed for the lots. Flow offsite will be reduced to all adjacent properties and the road right of way.

Chair Stabach asked for a description of the topography on site to ensure that runoff will be reduced to adjacent sites. Mr. Gentilly shared that the northern part of site is flat, but then slopes toward the southwest.

Mr. Gentilly noted they added the utility sheet to separate the site plan from the utility plan. Chair Stabach asked the applicant to confirm that water and sanitary sewer services are addressed, which was confirmed by the applicants. Member Wetherill asked if there is a force main in the street, and Chair Stabach referred to the plans and noted that DPW says 8" sanitary sewer force main is available for connections. Mr. Gentilly shared that a sewer connection plan would be required. A separate sanitary sewer connection permit will be filed with the Town.

Member Wetherill asked about the existing building and when it was last occupied. Mr. Meyerson noted that there were 10 units with occupancy 15 years ago. Member Wetherill noted that the letters from neighbor's address traffic and concerns with density. Fifteen years ago, there would have been many more cars. Mr. Meyerson shared that in the past there could be more than a dozen cars on the property, and Member Wetherill confirmed that with this proposal, there would be eight cars.

Member Wetherill asked if the deed would restrict future development on Lot 1 and noted that the plans show an area that could accommodate a building adjacent to the tower. Mr. Meyerson said he wants the option to construct an additional 900 square foot addition to the Tower. He is not necessarily going to do it but wants to retain the option to expand the footprint of the structure on Lot 1 by putting an addition on the Tower.

Member Wetherill asked what approval would be needed to build addition to the Tower, and that the Planning Board had a concern with lot coverage on Lot 1 and asked to see clarification on plans. Attorney Hartman explained that any development on Lot 1 would need to go to the zoning board if an addition is proposed to the Tower. Mr. Meyerson shared that they have been sensitive to the overall density of the plan.

Member Wetherill asked about DPW weighing in on the intersection. Planner Crean shared that DPW did not comment, and for this application there is no direct relationship to this intersection configuration. Planner Crean and Town Manager Hervey visited the intersection and noted that there are two stone structures at each corner of this intersection, which act as a

traffic calming feature for cars turning into the property. This applicant is not required to address the design of the intersection for this application.

The TRC expressed interest in knowing when Lorraine was a 40-foot right of way. Then added that the roadway itself was likely never wider than 40-feet. DPW asked for granite bounds on the 17 Lorraine property as part of this application. If a survey is completed for the ROW, the lots across the street could see that part of their yards are within the 40-foot ROW.

MOTION – Member Wetherill made a motion to recommend the Preliminary Plan for 17 Lorraine to the Planning Board, seconded by Member Lucini. Motion passed unanimously.
