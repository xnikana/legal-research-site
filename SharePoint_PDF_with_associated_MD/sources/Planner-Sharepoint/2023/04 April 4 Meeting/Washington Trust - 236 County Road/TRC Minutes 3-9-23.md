4. Discuss and Act: Development Plan Review signage application for 236 County Road (A.P. 23, Lot 250, Business Zone). The Washington Trust Company seeks approval of its signage application for the branch office, per 185-151A of the Zoning Ordinance. Proposal includes a directory sign installed along County Road on the existing posts on site and a halo-lit wall sign on the west elevation of the building.

Ms. Regan, representing Washington Trust, clarified that the ground sign was already approved and is being permitted now, so that is not part of the application for approval at this meeting as noted on the agenda.

Ms. Regan explained that the building sign is made up of individual letters, proposed with halo lighting. The proposed sign meets the restriction on size limits.

Member Stabach clarified that the sign is proposed for the west elevation only, not north elevation, and that the sign is consistent with signage ordinance. Chair Stabach went on to note that this sign will be viewable for southbound traffic along County Road.

Ms. Regan explained that Washington Trust considered signage on the north side of the building, but decided on the west elevation only. She went on to explain how the halo-lit signs work, and are different from a back-lit sign. The thickness of the letters with lighting behind result in the halo effect.

Chair Stabach noted that the former Rite Aid had signs on both sides, as one can see the outline of the old signs on the building. Ms. Regan added that it looks like Rite Aid signage was internally lit.

Member Lucini talked about the ordinance being unclear and Chair Stabach noted that it looks like

Barrington has many non-compliant back-lit signs.

Ms. Regan shared that she sits on the planning board for North Providence, and that it's very hard to tell the first applicant after a code is changed to comply, when there are others who don't comply.

Ms. Regan confirmed that this proposal for Washington Trust is compliant: 32 square feet is the ceiling, the proposed sign is at 31.89 square feet, and no signage is proposed on the ATM being constructed in the parking lot.

Chair Stabach mentioned that if any changes are made after tonight, Washington Trust will have to seek zoning relief or come back in front of the TRC.

MOTION – Member Szosz made a recommendation to the Planning Board to approve the sign package as submitted, seconded by Member Lucini.

Motion carried unanimously.
