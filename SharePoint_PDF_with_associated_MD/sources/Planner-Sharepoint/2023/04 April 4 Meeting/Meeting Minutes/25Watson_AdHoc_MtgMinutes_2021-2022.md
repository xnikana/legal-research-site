**3. Overview of 25 Watson Re-Use Study Process,** Barrington's Director of Planning, Building and Resilience, Teresa Crean, will offer a summary of the planning process to date

Chair Crean provided an overview of the process to date, including the purchase of the property through a vote at the 2021 Financial Town Meeting, the visioning process and assessment, the design funnel process, the role and goals of the Committee, the 2022 Financial Town Meeting

with the vote to not allow the option of demolition, and the consultant's strategy phase. Chair Crean then introduced the consultant team.

4. **Presentation: 25 Watson Avenue Site Development Strategy,** Lead Consultant Todd Poole, 4ward Planning and Eric Army of SignalWorks Architecture will offer an overview of the report, available for download and review here:

https://www.barrington.ri.gov/361/Watson-Ad-Hoc-Committee *Once on the site, click link to the "Site Summary Report, 09-06-2022"*

Poole reviewed the process of the strategy phrase and its three components, including:

- utilize build-out program
- perform research
- conduct analysis.

Poole reviewed six build-out scenarios, including reuse of the building only, reuse of the building with an addition (one that amounted to 12,000 square feet and one 21,000 square feet) and three cottage court options. The research components was then reviewed, including cost estimator's input, review of third-party real estate data, and interviews with local active developers and town staff, including Jordan Durham and Carla DiStefano from the Committee.

Poole then reviewed the analysis, including rental rates (market and affordable), cottage court sales prices (market and affordable), development costs per square foot and per unit (including factors like asbestos abatement and renovations), internal rate of return, maximum loan based on loan-to- value ratio, maximum loan based on debt service coverage ratio, and capitalization rate, explaining each of the metric's definitions and meanings. Poole then reviewed the major influential factors, including historically high cost of construction, specifically the price per square foot, rising rent and sales prices (though at lesser rates than construction costs), and the project's relatively low-density that further challenges financial viability.

Poole then reviewed the findings and takeaways from the report, including:

- The 24 and 32 unit scenarios result in negative internal rates of return
- The 44 unit scenario had a positive 1.45% rate of return, but explained how the percentage is likely not high enough for a developer to take interest.
- With the three cottage court scenarios, only option 3 had a positive return, however the return is less than 5 percent and unlikely to move a developer.

Poole concluded by reviewing some highlighted takeaways and quotes from the market and real estate analysis.

#### **c. Initial Market Analysis Findings**
