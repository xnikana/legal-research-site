**6.1 Discuss and Act, Development Plan Review: 227 County Road (A.P. 23, Lot 332; Business zone). Applicant proposes to demolish the existing gas station and redevelop the property for a free-standing 2,985 sf Chipotle Mexican Grill restaurant. Improvements include 30 parking spaces, a single curb cut, an outdoor seating area at the front of the building, landscaping and signage, all as shown on the submitted site plans. There will be 30 outdoor seats and 42 indoor seats. The submission includes a traffic impact assessment report, existing site photographs, signage exhibits and project plans (engineering, landscape, and architectural).**

*Present: Ken Caputo, Caputo Project Delivery Systems Peter Bassett, Bristol Property, owner of the subject property Brett Neilan, Civil Engineer Dan Radman, architect for Chipotle Brad Leach, traffic engineer*

Caputo reviewed the application via a PowerPoint presentation, including:

- ➢ An aerial view of the subject property in current conditions, noting that it is a small-sized property. Caputo noted that the canopy is still there, but the underground storage tanks were removed in 2022, and the last use for area in the back was for storage.
- ➢ Existing conditions plan, where Caputo noted that most of the frontage is driveway and pointed out the residential abutters to the right and rear of the property. Caputo reviewed existing noncompliance with the property today, and how the redevelopment will bring much of it into compliance.
- ➢ Reviewed aspects of the project that align with the Town's Comprehensive Community Plan.
- ➢ Civil Site Plan, including setback requirements, green space between the building and residential line, parking, and how the redevelopment will be shrinking the site of impervious coverage. Caputo noted that from a dimensional perspective the project is in full compliance with the Town's zoning regulations.
- ➢ Civil grading and utilities, such as how the building will rise in elevation and grade down toward the back, adding a buffer that is not there today. With the redevelopment will also come new utilities, including underground electric. Capulo also noted there will be permeable pavers.
- ➢ Seating plan, which will include 42 indoor seats and 30 outdoor seats for a total of 72 seats with 5 accessible seats, along with a walk-up window. No drive-thru is being proposed. Caputo noted Chipotle's popularity with online take out and services such as Uber Eats.
- ➢ Parking plan. The ordinance allows 27 parking spaces, and the redevelopment is seeking approval for 3 more for a total of 30 spots. Caputo noted that the application team is going before the Zoning Board on March 16 for a special-use permit. Caputo then reviewed the entrances and exits for the building in relationship to the parking lot. Noted the change from perpendicular placement Chipotle typically does to comply with intentions of the Town's Comprehensive Plan. He then pointed to the six bicycle spaces proposed at the bottom end of the patio area. Caputo noted that the Technical Review Committee (TRC) asked about installing more, but that the project is constrained by lot coverage, so no more have been added and plan to stay with the six spaces. He then noted the enclosed dumper in the back lot, as far from residential abutters as possible.
- ➢ Traffic study key statistics and conclusions.
- ➢ Landscape design, including border landscaping on the property lines and plantings between the patio, sidewalk and more. Caputo then showed the landscaping palette, including plantings

- that are tall growing to fill in gaps along the fence line. Caputo noted previous variance for the eight-foot fence along the side granted in the 1990s.
- ➢ Lighting plan, indicating existing lighting versus proposed lighting aimed to reduce and eliminate light spillage into the abutting residential properties. Caputo then reviewed the elevations.

Radman then reviewed Chipotle's prototype, including daylight, strategic lighting utilizing glass, subdued lighting around the patio area, and building colors.
