### Property Description

The Property is located on the west side of Middle Highway, across the street from its intersection with Primrose Hill Road. It is a site with substantial historical and recent disturbances. There are many buildings on-site, the oldest of which being a large building referred to as "Belton Court." The rest are buildings associated with 'Barrington College" which moved onto the site around 1950. According to our aerial photo interpretation, the buildings and fill that was deposited for the buildings appeared on-site between 1951 and 1972. This means that all fill on-site is historic (pre-Act and Rules) and therefore non-jurisdictional. The Property is bordered to the south, and west by Red maple swamp. Great Road to the south, Beverly Road to the west, Beaver Road to the north, and Middle Highway to the east are all residential roads with densely established single family homes near the Property.

*Wetland Permitting Definitions State‐RIDEM & CRMC*

As mentioned above, work and activities in and around wetland resource areas (freshwater wetlands and their buffers and buffer zones) are regulated by RIDEM *at this site*. RIDEM regulates most work and activities in upland-adjacent areas, and are defined as follows:

- Jurisdictional Area: Includes any Freshwater Wetlands, Buffers, Floodplains, Areas subject to Storm Flowage, Areas Subject to Flooding, and a variable width contiguous area extending outward from a River, Stream, Drinking Water Supply Reservoir, or any other Freshwater Wetland Type. The Jurisdictional Area includes all lands and waters that are subject to regulation under the Rules.
- Buffer Zone: A varying width area contiguous to, and extending outward from a Freshwater Wetland in which work is regulated by the RIDEM or CRMC (RIDEM for this Property). Any work proposed within the Buffer Zone of a Freshwater Wetland may require the creation of additional Buffer (see "Buffer" definition below), if the lot is developed (i.e. an area of land that consists of buildings, impervious surfaces, bare gravel, lawn, or landscaped areas). While RIDEM has influence on work inside of Jurisdictional Areas, most of their rules and regulations are implemented on work within the Buffer Zone. All "Buffer" (as explained below) exists strictly within the Buffer Zone of the particular Freshwater Wetland.
- Buffer: An area of undeveloped vegetated land adjacent to (within the Buffer Zone of) a Freshwater Wetland that is to be *retained in its natural undisturbed condition* or is to be *created to resemble a naturally occurring vegetated area*. All work proposed within the Buffer of a Freshwater Wetland must meet the performance standards set forth in the Rules through a variance. This includes, but is not limited to meeting the impact avoidance and minimization requirements as well as mitigating for any work within the Buffer Zone. In section 3.7.1(B)(4) of the Rules, there is no standard in the Rules for "minimum target for creation of new Buffer" for work within the Buffer Zone on a developed single-family house lot. Therefore, avoidance of work in the Buffer Zone is required when possible for the Property, however if work within the Buffer Zone is unavoidable there is no designated "minimum target for creation of new Buffer" to mitigate for said work. This is not a guarantee that the RIDEM will *not* request creation of Buffer.
- Setback: The minimum distance from the edge of a freshwater wetland at which an approved activity or alteration may take place. The Setback standards for primary structures (e.g. residential dwellings, commercial. industrial structures, schools, churches, etc.) is that they must be located to meet a setback distance of no less than the Buffer width plus twenty feet (20'). Accessory structures must be located to meet a setback distance of no less than the Buffer plus five feet (5'). Setbacks for Onsite Wastewater Treatment Systems (OWTS) are such that the leach field and the required ten foot (10') cleared zone around it shall be located outside the Buffer.

### Regulated Wetland Areas and Jurisdiction

Wetlands occur in the western and southern portions of the site *(This site is located within 'River Protection Region 2')*:

- Deciduous Swamp: The B-Series and D-series wetlands are best classified as a deciduous swamp **greater than one (1) acre but less than ten (10) acres**. This swamp is dominated by red maple (*Acer rubrum*), red oak (*Quercus rubra*), highbush blueberry (*Vaccinium corymbosum*), sweet pepperbush (*Clethra alnifolia*), and cinnamon fern (*Osmunda cinnamomea*) with silt loam soils on very low slopes.
- Deciduous Swamp: The A, E, and F-series wetlands are best classified as deciduous swamp **less than one (1) acres in size**. As with the B and D-series wetlands, these wetlands are also dominated by red maple (*Acer rubrum*), red oak (*Quercus rubra*), highbush blueberry (*Vaccinium corymbosum*), sweet pepperbush (*Clethra alnifolia*), and cinnamon fern (*Osmunda cinnamomea*) with silt loam soils on very low slopes.

### *Wetland Permitting Jurisdiction*

As mentioned above, work and activities in and around wetland resource areas (wetlands, ponds, streams, and their buffer zones) are regulated by RIDEM at this site. If additional impervious surfaces are added to the site that drain to areas within the Buffer Zones, even if they are outside of those areas, require permitting. RIDEM regulates most work and activities in upland-adjacent areas, and are defined as follows:

- Performance Standards: The buffer within a buffer zone shall consist of undeveloped vegetated land. When a project is proposed within a pre-disturbed buffer zone, new buffer may be required to be created within a portion of the buffer zone to resemble a natural area. At this site, we expect the performance standards for work to adhere to the following:
  - o For proposed projects or activities on a Property that is greater than or equal to three (3) acres, that is not a single-family residential lot of record, that contains one (1) or more existing structures, and where the proposed land disturbance total is greater than or equal to 10,000 square feet, the minimum target for total buffer width is 25 feet in the non-urban River Protection Region 2.
  - o Primary structures can be built no closer than 20 feet, from the outer Buffer boundary accessory structures can be built no closer than 5 feet from the outer Buffer boundary, and septic systems (including the 10' cleared area surrounding) must be built outside of the buffer.

Applicants may request variances to the performance standards if they cannot be met. This means that any work proposed within undeveloped, vegetated Buffer Zone, within 25' of the wetland boundary within disturbed Buffer Zone, or cannot meet the 20' construction setback from a Buffer will likely require a variance under Rules §3.7.
