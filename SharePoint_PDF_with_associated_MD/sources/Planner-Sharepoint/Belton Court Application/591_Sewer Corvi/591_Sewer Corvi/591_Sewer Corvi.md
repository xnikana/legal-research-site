## **MEMORANDUM**

DATE:

May 16, 2025

TO:

Barrington Planning Board/Town Planner Herbert Durfee III

FROM:

Alan M. Corvi – Barrington DPW

RE:

Proposed Belton Court Village - Off-Site Sanitary Sewer Capacity Analysis

In September 2024, InSite Engineering Services, LLC submitted designs plans (on behalf of ShineHarmony Holdings, Inc.) for the development of the former Zion Bible School at 33 Middle Highway. InSite had designed a residential development with 344 units with a proposed average day sewer flow of nearly 88,000 GPD and were requesting a letter of eligibility to connect to the Town's sanitary sewer system. The Department of Public Works had concerns with the proposed development and it's potential impact to the Town's sanitary sewer system including the Bay Spring Pump Station. ShineHarmony Holdings brought in Pare Corporation to conduct a sanitary sewer capacity analysis of the existing municipal sanitary sewer collection system located immediately downstream of the former Zion Bible College campus inclusive of the receiving Bay Spring Pump Station.

Based on the analysis, Pare Corporation has recommended that the three sections of 8-inch sewer pipe in County Road (SMH 7-8, SMH 8-9 and SMH 9-10) have their capacity increased by replacing them with larger pipe (12 inch or 15 inch diameter) to accommodate the increased sanitary sewer flow from the proposed development. The approximate lengths of these three sections of pipes is as follows: SMH 7-8 = 122 feet, SMH 8-9 = 120 feet, and SMH 9-10 = 409 feet for a combined total of 651' feet of pipe replacement. (see Pare Technical Memorandum revised dated: May 6, 2025 by Brian Wrigley and Brandon Blanchard of Pare) The 651 feet of 8-inch sanitary sewer main will need to be upgraded to the larger pipe (inclusive of all manhole work/replacement as required, design plans(approved by the Town)/utility permits, police details, construction inspection, excavation, dewatering, by-passing of the sewer flow to keep constant operation without disruption, all materials, pipe and manholes, all roadway restoration to the satisfaction of RIDOT inclusive of all hot mix asphalt paving (temporary & permanent), traffic line markings replacement, loam and seed, sidewalk restoration/curb reset as needed, as-built plan of all work and any incidentals need to compete the work to the satisfaction of the Town. This work should be competed by ShineHarmony at their expense prior to or at the same time that the new development's connection is made into the Town's sanitary sewer system.

Pare Corporation has also noted that due to the significant increase in sanitary sewer flow (from this proposed development) to the Bay Spring Pump Station, ShineHarmony Holdings will assist the Town with identifying the modifications to pump operations that are necessary to accommodate this increase in flow. Therefore, Pare Corporation (working through/for ShineHarmony) should direct/guide the Town with the necessary modifications to the Bay Spring Pump Station operations with all expenses covered by ShineHarmony.

InSite Engineering is proposing that the sanitary sewer from the new development exit the site through the easement to Great Road. ShineHarmony should have their engineer ensure that this pipe system can accommodate the flow of this development and also ensure that this existing pipe and manhole system is in good operating condition and does not need upgrading. Any needed improvements to this sewer main will be at the expense of ShineHarmony.

Therefore, the Department of Public Works is requesting that ShineHarmony Holdings address the abovenoted items as conditions of approval for connection of the proposed development to the Town's off-site sanitary sewer system. This approval memorandum is for only the sanitary sewer connection to the Town system and that all other review/comment and approval for the on-site work (roadway design, on-site sanitary sewer design/details, grading, drainage, etc.) will be at later submissions.
