## **BRISTOL COUNTY WATER AUTHORITY**

July 31, 2025

Suanne Santos InSite Engineering Services, LLC 1539 Fall River Ave, Suite 1 Seekonk, MA 02771

RE: Water Main Extension- Comprehensive Permit "Belton Court Village"-33 Middle Highway Barrington

Dear Ms. Santos,

Thank you for the opportunity to review the preliminary design documents relating to the proposed development at 33 Middle Highway in Barrington, Rhode Island (also referred to as "Belton Court Village").

Bristol County Water Authority (BCWA) met with representatives of InSite Engineering Services, LLC (InSite), Shine Harmony Holdings (Owner/SHH), Pare Corporation (Pare), and the Town of Barrington on July 10, 2025. The intent of the meeting was to discuss the water availability request for Belton Court Village, the acceptance of which being necessary for preliminary approval of Belton Court Village by the Town of Barrington. BCWA has not yet approved water availability for Belton Court Village due to the contents of Pare's hydraulic analysis of the development, dated April 7, 2025, and the lack of supplemental information from InSite/SHH requested by BCWA. In the summary of the analysis, Pare states the following:

"It is also estimated that the system can provide an available fire flow of approximately 1,000 gpm to the proposed development without causing the pressure in other parts of the system to fall below 20 psi. SHH's fire protection engineer should review this information to determine if the estimated available fire flows are sufficient to meet their fire protection needs."

As of the date of writing this letter, BCWA has not received any verification that 1,000 gpm is sufficient to provide fire protection service to Belton Court Village. As discussed in the meeting, in order to fully approve the project, the owner must fully demonstrate that the available supply from the water system can support the project without detrimentally impacting the surrounding customers or the performance of the water system as a whole.

However, the report does demonstrate that water is available for Belton Court Village for domestic use alone. As reported by Pare, supply is available at anticipated pressures ranging

between 48 psi to 54 psi for domestic supply alone. No information was provided for irrigation supply and its associated water supply demands.

It should be noted that the hydraulic analysis performed by Pare represents a very unique operational scenario/configuration of the BCWA water system. This scenario is based upon the current BCWA system configuration and may be subject to change. If for some reason the system configuration changes, e.g., the Fountain Ave. Tank is put back into service, then a full analysis of the project's water system impact for both domestic and fire flow demands will be required.

Moving forward, BCWA will require all associated water supply demands for the project in order to fully evaluate the project's impact on the water system. BCWA understands that this information will be provided as the project's design advances and we look forward to continuing conversations with all parties involved. BCWA trusts that this letter satisfies the request from the Town and SHH during July 10<sup>th</sup> meeting.

Please advise if you would like to meet to discuss this matter further. And as always, please feel free to contact me directly with any questions or concerns.

Thank you,
