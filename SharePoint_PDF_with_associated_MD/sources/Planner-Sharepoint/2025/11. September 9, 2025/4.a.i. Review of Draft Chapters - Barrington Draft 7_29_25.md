Our office greatly appreciates the opportunity to work with our municipal partners in the drafting of community comprehensive plans. We have completed our review of the Barrington *Comprehensive Community Plan Update* ("the Plan") draft chapters for Housing, Historic and Cultural Resources, and Natural Resources. The following is our evaluation of the draft material provided.

Please be advised that this is a preliminary evaluation and does not constitute official findings.

What follows is divided into two sections: Section I sets forth concerns with the draft material that relate to approvability issues, while Section II presents other suggestions that, although not directly related to approvability standards, could improve the plan's overall accuracy, readability, and usability, once addressed.

### **I. STATE APPROVAL CONCERNS**

The following approval concerns were identified during the review.

### **General Approvability**

**Comment 1:** With the submission of these chapters for review the Town of Barrington mostly chose not to include any of the maps that are required under the *Rhode Island Comprehensive Planning and Land Use Regulation Act,* specifically those required in RIGL§ 45-22.2-6 and the *Rhode Island Comprehensive Planning Standards Manual* Planning Standards 2.1(a)-(h). While we do not require these maps to be submitted during our review of municipal draft materials, this does significantly limit the breadth of approvability comments we can provide to the Town as part of this review, specifically as it pertains to approvability issues for the required content of the Natural Resources element of the Plan which includes required maps for surface water, wetlands, floodplains, forested areas, wildlife habitat, valuable agricultural soils, and permanently protected

conservation areas. We cannot provide any substantive approvability comments on these required Planning Standards with this review letter. As the Town prepares drafts for these required maps, staff should familiarize themselves with the map requirements found in the *Rhode Island Comprehensive Planning Standards Manual* in Planning Standards 12.1 – 12.5.

**Suggestion –** The Town needs to provide us with the required municipal Comprehensive Plan maps in future submissions of draft chapter materials for review if it wants approvability comments from the Division of Statewide Planning ("the Division") prior to the Town completing its Plan update and submitting it for final state approval review.

**Comment 2:** With the submission of these Draft chapters the Town has not yet assigned responsible municipal parties to the action items included in the reviewed materials nor timetables for implementation. Planning Standard 11.2 (a) – (c) requires that a municipality:

"(a) Include sufficient, specific implementation actions to which the municipality will commit in order to achieve each of the comprehensive plan's goals; b. Provide a timeframe for each implementation action; c. (Recommendation) Assign a responsible party to each implementation action, including the city or town council for actions that will require the amendment of codes and ordinances by the municipal legislative body".

**Suggestion –** Begin the creation of a draft Implementation Table for all goals, policies, and actions and ensure responsible parties are listed for each action. This will aid us in providing more thorough and detailed approvability comment for future submissions.

### **Historic and Cultural Resources**

**Comment 3:** Planning Standard 4.1a(i) of the *Rhode Island Comprehensive Planning Standards Manual* requires that a municipality provide "one or more maps showing properties or districts listed on the State or National Register of Historic Places". Barrington has not provided this required map with the current Historic and Cultural Resources chapter.

**Suggestion –** In future draft Plan provided to the Division for review, please provide the required map for "Historic & Cultural Resources" so we can provide approvability comments for Planning Standard requirement 4.1a(i).

**Comment 4**: Planning Standard 4.3 requires that municipalities must "Include goals that embody the State's goals for historic and cultural resources and policies to support each goal". The "Historic and Cultural Resources" chapter as currently drafted does not have a goal which embodies the goal of the State Historic Preservation Plan, "*Protecting Our Legacy of Buildings, Places, and Culture: An Historic Preservation Plan for Rhode Island"* (State Guide Plan Element #210) to "Ensure equity and representation in historic preservation and cultural heritage." This goal is central to the State Guide Plan element and the Town should address this topic area in the goals and policies of the draft chapter.

**Suggestion –** Add additional goal(s) and policies that would help to fully embody and address the state's goals for consistency. These additional goal(s) and policies should address how the Town

will ensure equity and representation in preserving and accessing historical resources within the community and how the Town will provide information to its residents to make the public more knowledgeable about historic buildings and cultural heritage. Please consult the *Comprehensive Planning Guidance Handbook* (2021) for additional guidance on goals and policies for state guide plan consistency for historic and cultural resources.

### **Housing**

**Comment 5**: The draft chapter outlines actions and policies under Goal HN-2 that are related to increasing low-to-moderate income ("LMI") housing. However, there is no Implementation Program that includes time-based benchmarks, level of priority, and ways in which Barrington can measure their progress toward the goal and, if they fall short of their goal, how they can change course to meet the goals. The Housing element of the Plan should also note how many LMI units each relevant action or strategy is proposed to create within twenty years or at build out (recommend whichever comes first).

The Rhode Island Comprehensive Planning and Land Use Act 45-22.2-6 (12)(v) requires that "[t]he implementation program shall contain a concise strategic plan that details actions to be taken annually to achieve the goals and policies of the plan." The draft plan does not contain this information, especially as it relates to achieving the ten percent LMI threshold during the twentyyear planning horizon of the Plan or at build out. Planning Standard 5.5. requires that a municipality "demonstrate how one of the mandated low- and moderate-income thresholds will be met" with several recommended ways to achieve this. We advise the Town to review the content of Planning Standard 5.5(b)(i. – xiv) for ways to achieve this.

**Suggestion –** The Town must provide an affordable housing strategy within the Housing chapter to show how the mandated ten percent LMI housing threshold as required will be achieved as part of a comprehensive plan update. There are several ways this can be done, but the Housing element must show how the town will attain the number of deed-restricted affordable housing units to meet the ten percent threshold either during the twenty-year planning horizon of this Plan or the expected date of community build out. The Plan will not meet state approval without this required strategy (or strategies) to fulfill Planning Standard 5.5

### **Natural Resources**

**Comment 6**: Planning Standard 2.4 requires that a municipality "[i]dentify and discuss the municipality's priorities for land conservation."

The section on Pages 11 - 12 of the Natural Resources chapter discusses four areas of Barrington that could be acquisition priorities for future conservation. This list was developed from a 2011 Open Space Plan, as well as input from Town officials and the Barrington Land Trust. However, this section only lists these four properties for open space acquisition with no other sites mentioned. The conservation area that the Town owns and manages is quite small at only sixty-four acres, according to information provided on Page 6 of the chapter. It seems from this data that there could potentially be more land in Town that should be assessed as part of Barrington's priorities for future land conservation, especially as it relates to coastal retreat and impacts of sea level rise as discussed in other sections of the chapter.

**Suggestion –** Please provide a complete and detailed list (preferably in a table format) for all future land conservation properties for the Town to achieve its conservation priorities during the twentyyear planning horizon.

**Comment 7**: The draft chapter for Natural Resources does not have any mention of the applicable State Guide Plan ("SGP") elements that impact natural resource planning, or the applicable goals and policies for these elements; there is no way to discern if the chapter would be consistent with the SGP. The Town must include mention of the applicable SGP elements and how the Plan embodies the goals and policies of the SGP through the data, goals, policies, and actions provide in the municipal plan.

**Suggestion –** The current Draft chapter is inconsistent with the applicable SGP elements by omission. Please cite the following applicable SGP elements within the Plan and how the Town's efforts embody the goals and polices of the state as described in the following SGP reports:

- SGP Element 121 *Land Use 2025: State Land Use Policies and Plan*
- SGP Element 156 *Urban and Community Forestry Plan*
- SGP Element 161 *Forest Resources Management Plan*
- SGP Element 731 *Water Quality 2035*

# **II. COMMENTS AND SUGGESTIONS TO ENHANCE ACCURACY, READABILITY, AND USABILITY**

Comments and suggestions common to multiple chapters are presented first, followed by a chapterby-chapter assessment.

### **General Comments**

**Comment A:** Although these are early draft chapters for the Town's Comprehensive Plan update, it would be helpful if the Town provided an Implementation Table for all goals, policies, and actions and ensure responsible parties are listed for each action.

**Suggestion –** Considering producing an Implementation Table to be included in future draft Plan materials sent to the Division for review and comments.

### **Historic and Cultural Resources**

**Comment B:** Reviewers from the Rhode Island Historical Preservation and Heritage Commission ("RIHPHC") have two minor corrections that they noted as the following:

• There are two references to the Aborn Jackson House, which should be called the Benjamin Aborn Jackson House, for consistency with the National Register nomination for the

property.

• The General Thomas Allin House was listed in the National Register in 2025, not 2024.

**Suggestion** – Please make the corrections as noted.

**Comment C:** Planning Standard 4.2(c) recommends that a municipality **"**[a]ssess issues related to historic and cultural resources by including the following: Identification of any existing local programs related to the management of historic and cultural resources, such as a local historic district ordinance"*.* Page 9 of the chapter includes text which indicates the Town wants to explore the creation of local Historic Districts but does not indicate whether the Town will have voluntary historic districts or historic district zoning, and a local Historic District Commission as required under RIGL § 45-24.1.

Action Item G under Policy HCR-1.2 seems to indicate that the Town only wants to establish a committee to study the establishment of a voluntary historic district. If this is the case, then the Town should clarify the language on Page 9, as it only creates confusion as to the direction the Town wants to go with regards to future local historic preservation efforts. The text and correlated polices and actions could be construed as creating an internal inconsistency in the Plan as written.

**Suggestion –** Please clarify the text on Page 9 to indicate if the Town would like either establish of a voluntary local historic district or a historic district zoning regime, as enumerated under RIGL § 45-24.1, and add policies and actions that are consistent with this text.

## **Natural Resources**

**Comment D: W**ith regards to Action Item NR-2-2B, reviewers from the Rhode Island Department of Environmental Management Division of Planning and Development suggest that the Town include an action item to review land evidence records and ensure that all relevant easements – and grant requirements – are noted in the land evidence records for all town owned open space properties.

**Suggestion** – Please make the addition, as noted.

**Comment E:** On Pages 3 and 9 of the chapter, there is information about the roles that the Barrington Conservation Commission and Barrington Land Conservation Trust play in certain protected parcels in Town, but no real explanation as to the different roles and responsibilities these entities have and what the Town wants to assign to each for future conservation efforts. More detail should be included as to how these two entities will work together to achieve the natural resource and conservation goals of the Town during the twenty-year planning horizon of this Plan.

**Suggestion –** Within the text of the Natural Resources chapter, please provide clear and concise information describing the roles and responsibilities for the Barrington Conservation Commission and Barrington Land Conservation Trust. In several Rhode Island communities, private non-profit community land trusts play crucial roles in establishing new conservation land, maintaining trails

and access points for public conservation land, and partnering with towns in planning and financing of conservation projects.

### **Housing**

**Comment F:** Planning Standard 5.1(a) recommends that a municipality "[p]rovide an overview of the existing housing context by including the following data points; Existing housing units, both the number and the relative trend in housing development over the past 10 or more years". Pages 9–12 of the Housing chapter provides information on housing types by tenure and vacancy, but there is no chart or table showing the total housing unit types over a ten-year trendline.

**Suggestion -** We recommend that the Town provide ten years' worth of data that is readily available via the Census American Community Survey ("ACS") and the series of HousingWorks RI *Housing Fact Book* series going back to 2015.

**Comment G:** Planning Standard 5.1(g) recommends that a municipality "[p]rovide an overview of the existing housing context by including the following data points; the current median home sale price, and the general trend in home sales prices over the past 10 or more years".

On Page 15, the Town has provided the HousingWorks RI 2023 median home sale price in Barrington, which was \$680,000, However, the HousingWorks RI 2024 Housing Fact Book shows that a median home sale price of \$710,000 for a single-family home. This is a thirty-six percent increase in the median sales price since 2018. The Town should use the more recent housing sales price data.

**Suggestion –** Please use the most recently available HousingWorks RI 2024 Housing Fact Book data for median home sales price on Page 15.

**Comment H:** There is a paragraph on Page 24 after the header "Low-and Moderate-Income Housing Goals" with information concerning the RI LIFE INDEX and how housing affordability impacts health which is actually a duplicate paragraph. This same exact paragraph is included on page 20 of the Draft chapter.

**Suggestion –** We suggest deleting this duplicate paragraph and leaving in the original paragraph which is on page 20 of the Draft chapter.

**Comment I:** Table 8 on Page 13 is completely blank; the cited ACS 5-year data should be included here. Additionally, it appears that the numbering of the tables in the document is out of order. On Page 23, the Town has included Table #12 but the next table included on Page 25 is shown as Table #11.

**Suggestion –** Please resolve the missing data in Table #8 and correct the numbering of the tables.
