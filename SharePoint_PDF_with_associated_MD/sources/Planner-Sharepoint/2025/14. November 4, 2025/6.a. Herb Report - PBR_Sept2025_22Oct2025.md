- 1. Comprehensive Update to Sign Regulations. Finalized contractual obligations necessary to retain the services of the UMass-Amherst Center for Resilient Metro-Regions to help with carrying out the comprehensive update to the Town's various sign regulations but especially in areas zoning for business uses. Also, in collaboration with the Center, the Department began discussing and preparing for public workshops to be held in October and November along with a corresponding sign survey. That public engagement, including direct contact with various designated stakeholders (e.g., Barrington Economic Development Committee, Barrington Planning Board, Barrington Zoning Board of Review, et al), will help with the Center's ultimate effort to recommend amendment to the Town's sign regulations for formal consideration by the Solicitor's Office, the Planning Board, and the Town Council. Lastly, as part of the preparatory work, the Department of Center discussed the forthcoming efforts to prepare a visual library of existing signs throughout Town along with some alternative style signs not necessarily present in Town. That "library" of visual signs will be used during the public engagement process to help participants understand and explain what type of signage should and shouldn't be enabled in Town, especially in areas zoned for business.
- 2. FEMA-BRIC Grant Mussachuck Creek Corridor Flood Risk Reduction Project Scoping Study. Completed request for proposals process, established a review team, reviewed and scored proposals, interviewed two finalists (Fuss & O'Neill and GZA Environmental), conducted final evaluations and selected a preferred consultant (Fuss & O'Neill), prepared a recommendation for consideration by the Town Council during its October 2025 meeting essentially to negotiate a contract with the preferred consultant.

During the week of September 15, the curbside pick-up program started with an initial (and still growing number of resident participants) 250+ signups.

In addition to working with the Town to secure the next electricity contract, importantly, residents need to know that winter rates will be in effect beginning in November (i.e., rates slightly higher than the summer rates.
