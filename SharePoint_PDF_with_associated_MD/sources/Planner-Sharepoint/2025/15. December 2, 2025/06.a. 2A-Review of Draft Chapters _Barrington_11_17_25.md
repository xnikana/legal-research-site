## **I. STATE APPROVAL CONCERNS**

The following approval concerns were identified during the review.

## **General Approvability**

**Comment 1:** As with the previous Draft chapter submissions, the Town of Barrington chose not to include a map that is required under the *Rhode Island Comprehensive Planning and Land Use Regulation Act,* specifically the required map is a transportation system map which is required in RIGL§ 45-22.2-6 and the *[Rhode Island Comprehensive Planning Standards Manual](https://planning.ri.gov/sites/g/files/xkgbur826/files/documents/comp/2021/Comprehensive-Planning-Standards-Manual-Approved-8-12-21.pdf)* ("the Manual") Planning Standard 8.1(a)-(g). While we do not require these maps to be submitted during our review of municipal draft materials, this does limit the breadth of approvability comments we can provide to the Town as part of this review. Specifically, it pertains to approvability issues for the required map for the Circulation/Transportation chapter showing the "Existing transportation network on a map, inclusive of major streets, highways, and interstates, bus routes and major bus hubs, separated bicycle paths, rail stations and railway lines, and ports, harbors, and airports (as applicable)."

**Suggestion –** As the Town prepares drafts for this mapping requirement, staff should familiarize themselves with the map requirements found in the Manual in Planning Standard 8.1(a)-(g). The Town will need to provide us with the required municipal Comprehensive Plan map(s) in future submissions of draft chapter materials for review if it wants approvability comments from the Division of Statewide Planning ("the Division") prior to the Town completing its Plan and submitting it for final state approval review.

## **Circulation/Transportation**

**Comment 2:** Within the Draft chapter there are no references to the applicable State Guide Plan element for Transportation, SGP Element 611 - *[Moving Forward RI 2040, the State of Rhode Island Long-Range](https://planning.ri.gov/sites/g/files/xkgbur826/files/documents/trans/2020/Final-LRTP-December-2020.pdf)  [Transportation Plan](https://planning.ri.gov/sites/g/files/xkgbur826/files/documents/trans/2020/Final-LRTP-December-2020.pdf)* ("LRTP)"*.* This is an approvability issue as the four goals identified in the Draft chapter should detail how the Town's Transportation element embodies the State goals for transportation identified in the LRTP, *[Moving Forward RI 2040](https://planning.ri.gov/sites/g/files/xkgbur826/files/documents/trans/2020/Final-LRTP-December-2020.pdf)*.

Although the goals, policies, and actions do appear to be generally consistent with *[Moving Forward RI](https://planning.ri.gov/sites/g/files/xkgbur826/files/documents/trans/2020/Final-LRTP-December-2020.pdf)  [2040](https://planning.ri.gov/sites/g/files/xkgbur826/files/documents/trans/2020/Final-LRTP-December-2020.pdf)*, the Town must add additional language citing the State Guide Plan and give a short explanation of how the Town's goals align with the goals of *[Moving Forward RI 2040.](https://planning.ri.gov/sites/g/files/xkgbur826/files/documents/trans/2020/Final-LRTP-December-2020.pdf)*

**Suggestion –** In future iterations of the Circulation/Transportation element of the Plan, the municipality must make specific reference to SGP Element 611 - *[Moving Forward RI 2040, the State of Rhode Island](https://planning.ri.gov/sites/g/files/xkgbur826/files/documents/trans/2020/Final-LRTP-December-2020.pdf)  [Long-Range Transportation Plan](https://planning.ri.gov/sites/g/files/xkgbur826/files/documents/trans/2020/Final-LRTP-December-2020.pdf)* and give an explanation of how the Town's goals embody those of the applicable State Guide Plan element. Omission of reference to the State Guide Plan is considered an approvability issue. In addition, as of November 13, 2025, the State Planning Council has adopted an updated SGP Element 611, now [Moving Forward RI 2050, the State of Rhode Island Long-Range](https://planning.ri.gov/sites/g/files/xkgbur826/files/2025-10/LRTP_FullPlan%20%281%29.pdf)  [Transportation Plan \(LRTP\)](https://planning.ri.gov/sites/g/files/xkgbur826/files/2025-10/LRTP_FullPlan%20%281%29.pdf) - it may be worth considering ways to ensure that this element conforms with this most recent iteration of the LRTP.

# **II. COMMENTS AND SUGGESTIONS TO ENHANCE ACCURACY, READABILITY, AND USABILITY**

Comments and suggestions common to multiple chapters are presented first, followed by a chapter-bychapter assessment.

## **General Comments**

**Comment A:** There are still a few small formatting errors included in this latest draft chapter submission, but noticeably fewer than in prior submissions. One notable formatting error that should be resolved within this draft chapter occurs on Pages 23 – 24 where it appears the Town may have copy and pasted information directly from the *Barrington SS4A Safety Action Plan* strategies relating to school zone speed enforcement cameras and related traffic calming measures. This entire section begins with yellow highlighted text and then includes underlined text throughout. This section needs to be cleaned up and better integrated into the Draft chapter.

Also, on Page 28, Goal C-2 includes action items that are not numbered consistently, with action items 1-4 and the action item 7 included sequentially. This will need to be revised for clarity.

**Suggestion –** Please review the information from the *SS4A Safety Action Plan* included on Pages 23 – 24 and clean up the formatting of these pages prior to final submission of the Plan. Also review the numbering and sequencing for the action items included under Goal C-2 on Page 28.

## **Circulation/Transportation**

**Comment B:** Planning Standard 8.2 (j)(i-iii) recommends that a municipality include within the Circulation/Transportation element of their comprehensive plan "the percentage of the population that lives in a household without a private vehicle, the percentage of the working population that uses public transit for commuting purposes, and the percentage of the working population that bike or walk for commuting purposes." While the Town has provided this information within a table on Page 3 of the Draft chapter, there is a discrepancy between the text description of this data and the ACS data provided within the table. The "Worked at Home" data within the table states a total of 6.8% of the Barrington workforce is working from home while the paragraph above the date table states that 19.4% of Barrington residents work from home. The Town needs to resolve this discrepancy between the text and the ACS data table.

**Suggestion** – Please revise the data table or the text description to provide the accurate number and percentage of residents working from home in Barrington according to the latest U.S. Census American Community Survey data.

**Comment C:** On Page 26 of the chapter, prior to the Town's Goals, Policies, and Actions section, Barrington has provided a picture of flooding at the RIPTA and Congregational Church "Park N Ride" facility along County Road/Route 114. While the caption states that further information regarding flood risk for critical infrastructure facilities will be included in the Natural Hazards and Climate element of the Plan, it is also appropriate to include that information here as well since this facility is in a coastal flood zone and has high demand for services along the 60 Bus Route.

**Suggestion –** Please provide more information regarding flood risk at the Park N Ride facility along County Road/Route 114 and if the State or the Town has any plans for backup Park N Ride locations in Barrington if the parking lots are rendered unusable from a coastal storm or flooding event.

**Comment D:** Reviewers from RIPTA noted that although the Town has an action item listed under Policy C-3.1 to encourage RIPTA to expand the Riverside bus route into Barrington if the Zion Bible Institute campus is redeveloped, RIPTA noted that the *[Transit Master Plan](https://www.ripta.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/07/TFRI-Recs-Briefing-Book-Final-201230.pdf)* (which is part of the *[Long](https://planning.ri.gov/sites/g/files/xkgbur826/files/documents/trans/2020/Final-LRTP-December-2020.pdf)  [Range Transportation Plan](https://planning.ri.gov/sites/g/files/xkgbur826/files/documents/trans/2020/Final-LRTP-December-2020.pdf)*) does not recommend extending the 33 Route. Barrington should have follow-up conversations with RIPTA staff on this action item before it is included in the final Plan as it could end up being considered an inconsistency with the [State Guide Plan.](https://planning.ri.gov/state-guide-plan)

**Suggestion** – Please follow up with RIPTA staff and discuss if Action Item 1 under Policy C-3.1is feasible and should stay in the Plan, or if RIPTA is opposed to it and would consider it inconsistent with the *[Transit Master Plan](https://www.ripta.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/07/TFRI-Recs-Briefing-Book-Final-201230.pdf)*.

**Comment E:** The Town of Barrington has done an excellent job discussing the Town's Issues and Opportunities on Pages 16-26 of the chapter. To strengthen this section of the even more, the recommends that Barrington identify the specific statuses of projects contained in the *State* 

*Transportation Improvement Program* ("STIP") FFY 2018-2027 and/or what new projects were submitted for the [FFY STIP 2026-2035 rewrite.](FFY%202026-2035%20rewrite) (This would include a wide range of projects such as – bridge preservation, bridges needing major rehabilitation work or total bridge replacement, drainage improvements, roadway projects, and intersection safety improvements).

**Suggestion –** Consider reviewing the FFY 2026-2035 STIP rewrite and incorporating a list of STIP projects for Barrington along with status updates. The Town can find this information within the document itself at the [FFY STIP 2026-2035](https://planning.ri.gov/sites/g/files/xkgbur826/files/2025-09/STIP_26-35_DRAFT-FINAL_9-11-25.pdf) rewrite or the [STIP Mapping Dashboard.](https://ristate.maps.arcgis.com/apps/dashboards/7c9e74d483ae45d8abb561e163f49f2c)
