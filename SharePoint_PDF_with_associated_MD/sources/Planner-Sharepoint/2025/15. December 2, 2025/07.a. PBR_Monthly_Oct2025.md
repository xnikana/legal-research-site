- 1. Comprehensive Update to Sign Regulations. Planned for and held the first of two public engagement workshops to solicit insight on what the public at-large and business-related stakeholders believe the Town's sign regulations should allow (or not). As announced during the workshop and via the Town's various forms of community notification, a sign survey was "opened" to gain additional insight into how the Town sign regulations should be comprehensively updated. The survey remained open throughout the month and into November. The results of the survey are targeted for use during the second public workshop scheduled for November.
- 2. **Curbside Composting Pilot Program**. Through October there have nearly been 300 sign-ups to the pilot program along with a continuance of collaboration with ERICD to encourage participation by the public, either through the discounted paid for curbside program or by participating in the free drop-off locations for the Barrington Farm School.
- 3. **Notable Development Applications**. The Planning Board's November agenda did not include any notable applications for review. As an FYI, the Planning Board's December agenda includes review and public hearing of the Town's Preliminary Plan Application for the **25 Watson Ave Comprehensive Permit** proposal. Also, to date (Nov 26, 2025), the **Belton Court Village** Comprehensive Permit Preliminary Plan Application remains "incomplete". This means the earliest the Planning Board could hear the application no earlier than February 2026.
- 4. **Comprehensive Plan Update**. The Planning Board continues to prepare the statutorily required elements of the plan. As of the end of October, the draft elements' status is indicated parenthetically:
  - a. Housing (sent to the State for preliminary comments; State comments received)
  - b. Cultural & Historic Resources (sent to the State for preliminary comments; State received)
  - c. Natural Resources (& Conservation) (sent to the State for preliminary comments; State comments received)
  - d. Outdoor Recreation (& Open Space) (sent to the State for preliminary comments; State comments received)
  - e. Economic Development (sent to the State for preliminary comments; State comments received)
  - f. Circulation (& Transportation) (sent to the State for preliminary comments; State comments just received)

- g. Community Facilities (Services & Facilities) (amid being prepared for PB review likely in January)
- h. Natural Hazards & Climate Change (updated draft up for review by PB probably in January)
- Land Use (draft being reviewed and updated by PB in November and December)
- j. Implementation (amid being prepared)
- k. Maps (28 draft maps up for review by the Planning Board in December)
- 5. FYE 2027-2032 Capital Improvement Program (CIP). As is customary for the department, updated forms for CIP were prepared and sent to department heads, et al to solicit capital projects for the coming fiscal year and through fiscal year ending June 20, 2032. Information from department heads is due in November. Thereafter the department will work with the Town Manager and Finance Office to pull the requests into an updated worksheet. The worksheet will be reviewed by the Town Manager and the CIP Sub-Committee of the Planning Board. That working group will make recommendations to the Planning Board overall. Thereafter, the Planning Board's recommendation will be further considered by the Town Manager, the Town Council, and the Committee on Appropriations as part of the overall process for preparing and adopting the FYE 2027 Town Budget.
- 6. Foote St / Maple Ave Tree Planting Project. Through early November outreach to the neighborhood continued. For the fall planting effort, 76 trees were planted in the designated "heat island" area of Foote St / Maple Ave. 50 of the trees were planted via volunteers, including but not limited to department staff, Town Councilors, area Boy Scouts, Energy & Resilience Committee members, residents receiving the trees, and others wanting to lend a hand. The remaining 26 trees planted were carried out by the Town's contractor and tree supplier North-Eastern Tree Service, Inc. Thank you to all the volunteers for donating your personal time.

Lastly, for now, the project is on hold due to winter conditions. The project will resume in the spring when the department plans to plant an additional 41 trees to complete the project that is in large part funded with a grant from the RI Infrastructure Bank. If you are a resident that Bel Johnson or other Town officials have been in contact with or you otherwise would like to participate in this free tree project, please let the department know. We can get you signed up for the spring 2026 planting in that neighborhood. Additional thanks to everyone that's helping to minimize the impact of the "heat island" this project is tackling.

7. American Planning Association – Rhode Island Chapter. Continued to serve on the chapter's Executive Committee.

- 8. State Planning Council's Technical Committee. Continued to serve on the committee. The committee is a permanent committee that serves as a forum for RI municipal planners to share challenges and ideas and hear about state and local initiatives that are of importance to the planning community. Membership of the committee consists of municipal planners from Rhode Island's urban, suburban, and rural communities, ensuring representation of diverse interests and views. The Assistant Chief of Statewide Planning's Community Coordination and Outreach team serves as the Committee's Secretary.
- 9. **School Committee Building Steering Committee**. Continued to participate in scheduled meetings of the Building Steering Committee. Also, the Department has been working with the Town Manager's Office to review and prepare comments on the construction plans and their related materials.
- 10. Southeast New England Program Technical Assistance Program. During a press conference sponsored by Senator Reed, formal announcement of the 2025 SNEP grant and technical assistance awards was made. Barrington was selected to receive a technical assistance award to help with completing feasibility study including public engagement focused on the establishment of a stormwater utility in Town. Such a utility would help the Town address stormwater issues regarding requirements of US EPA but, importantly, would also help the Town to better plan for and carry out projects related to being more resilient to issues of climate change (e.g., sea level rise and storm surge, including related stormwater runoff issues).
- 11. **RI Housing Municipal Technical Assistance Program (MTAP)** The department learned that the Town was awarded a grant (<u>no</u> local match required) to hire Libra Planners to conduct a build-out analysis, public engagement process, and preparation of recommended strategies to incorporate into the Town's various regulations that are intended to incent additional affordable housing in Town.
- 12. **Collaboration with Town Energy Manager** Energy Advocates, LLC (Kathy Stanley) serving as the Town's Energy Manager worked with the department to initiate "preplanning" for energy needs of "government center" (i.e., Town Hall and the Library/Peck Center) with the possibility of establishing a micro-grid project in the area. The project intends to carry out three general tasks: 1) improve and/or add HVAC systems and controllers to create energy savings/efficiencies as necessary in the two buildings, 2) carry out weatherization projects as necessary, also, to create energy savings/efficiencies, and 3) to replace the standby generator for Town Hall while working to install a similar generator at the Library / Peck Center that currently

has NO backup power supply. The project likely will take 3-5 years at least and, again, the concept of a micro-grid implementation project is being woven into the project, including consideration of the EV chargers at Town Hall.

- 14. Town Manager's Barrington Transportation Advisory Committee (BTAC). Continued to serve as a member of the Town Manager's advisory committee on transportation-related matters.
