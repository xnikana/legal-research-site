**[DECLARATION OF CONDOMINIUM]{.underline}**

**[FOR THE COTTAGES ON FREEMONT CONDOMINIUM]{.underline}**

As of \_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_ \_\_, 2025, Cottages on Freemont, LLC, a Rhode
Island limited liability company with an office at 79 Peckham Lane,
Middletown, Rhode Island 02842 (the "Declarant"), being the owner in fee
simple of certain real estate located in the Town of Barrington, State
of Rhode Island, as further described in [Exhibit A]{.underline}
attached hereto and made a part hereof, consisting of real property and
all buildings and other improvements erected or to be erected thereon,
with all easements and rights appurtenant thereto (hereinafter the
"Property"), declares:

**ARTICLE 1**

**[Submission of Property]{.underline}**

The Declarant hereby submits the Property owned by it in the Town of
Barrington, County of Bristol, State of Rhode Island to the provisions
of the Rhode Island Condominium Act, 34-36.1 et seq., for the purposes
of (1) creating THE COTTAGES ON FREEMONT CONDOMINIUM, a condominium
initially consisting of twelve (12) land only units ("Land Units"), in
which the use of the Land Units will be limited to residential uses,
subject to the rights reserved by the Declarant, or its assignees, as
hereinafter set forth (the "Condominium"); (2) making the improvements,
if any permitted by this Declaration; and (3) managing the Condominium
and each of its Land Units as provided in this Declaration. The Property
will be administered in accordance with the provisions of the Act, this
Declaration and the Bylaws.

**ARTICLE 4**

**[Description of Land; Use Restrictions]{.underline}**

[Section 4.1 - Land Description]{.underline}. The entire Condominium is
located in Barrington, Rhode Island. A legal description of the Land
comprising the Condominium is found at [Exhibit A]{.underline}. The Land
is benefited by and subject to a variety of utility and drainage
easements and by such other encumbrances as shown on [Exhibit
A]{.underline}.

[Section 4.2 - Use Restrictions: General]{.underline}. Each Land Unit is
hereby restricted to residential uses. The Common Elements may be used
only for such ancillary uses as are required in connection with such
permitted uses.

In addition to the restrictions set forth above, no unlawful use shall
be made of the Condominium or any part thereof, and all valid laws,
orders, rules and regulations of all governmental agencies having
jurisdiction thereof (the "Legal Requirements") shall be complied with.
Compliance with any Legal Requirements shall be accomplished by and at
the sole cost and expense of the Unit Owner or Unit Owners with respect
to the Land Units, or the Board or Association with respect to the
Common Elements. Each Unit Owner shall give prompt notice to the Board
of any written notice it receives of the violation of any Legal
Requirements affecting its Land Unit or the Condominium. Notwithstanding
the foregoing provisions, any Unit Owner may, at its expense, defer
compliance with and contest, by appropriate proceedings prosecuted
diligently and in good faith, the validity or applicability of any Legal
Requirement affecting any portion of the Condominium such Unit Owner is
obligated to maintain and repair. The Board shall cooperate with such
Unit Owner in such proceeding, provided that:

(i) Such Unit Owner shall pay and defend, save harmless and indemnify
    the Board, Association and any other Unit Owner(s) against all
    liability, loss or damage any of them respectively shall suffer by
    reason of such contest and any noncompliance with such Legal
    Requirements, including reasonable attorneys' fees and other
    expenses reasonably incurred; and

(ii) Such Unit Owner shall periodically keep the Board advised as to the
     status of such proceedings.

Such Unit Owner need not comply with any Legal Requirements so long as
the Unit Owner shall be contesting the validity or applicability
thereof, provided that noncompliance shall not create a dangerous
condition or constitute a crime or an offense punishable by fine or
imprisonment and that no part of the Condominium shall be subject to
being condemned or vacated by reason of such contest.

The Board shall also have the right to contest any Legal Requirements
without being subject to the foregoing conditions as to contest and
shall also have the right to defer compliance with any Legal
Requirements, but only subject to the foregoing conditions as to
deferral of compliance. The cost and expense of any contest by the Board
shall be a Common Expense. The amount due the Board under the foregoing
obligation of a Unit Owner to defend and save harmless and indemnify the
Board shall, without prejudice to any other remedy of the Board, be
enforced by assessing the same to the Land Unit or Land Units of such
Unit Owner as a special Common Expense under the Bylaws.

The use of Land Units by all persons authorized to use the same shall be
at all times subject to the provisions contained in this Declaration,
the Act, the Bylaws and such rules and regulations as may be prescribed
and established to govern such use or which may hereafter be prescribed
and established by the Board as any of the same may be from time to time
amended, and any Unit Owner found by a Rhode Island District, Superior
or Supreme Court or any other Court of competent jurisdiction, to be in
violation of the provisions of this Declaration, the Act, Bylaws and
rules and regulations of the Board or the aforesaid law and regulations
shall be liable for the reasonable counsel fees incurred by the Board in
enforcing same.

[Section 4.3 -- Violation of Restrictions]{.underline}. If the Unit
Owner or tenant or other occupant of the Land Unit violates any portion
of this Article 4, the Unit Owner or tenant of a Land Unit (or portion
thereof) that is intended to benefit from such provision shall have such
rights and remedies against the violating Unit Owner or tenant or
occupant as are available at law or in equity, including without
limitations, injunctive relief so long as the beneficiary first provides
the Unit Owner deemed to have violated any portion of this provision
with written notice of the violation along with a reasonable amount of
time to cure such violation.

Notwithstanding the above, the Unit Owner of any Land Unit shall not be
deemed to have violated any portion of this provision if, upon the
receipt by such Unit Owner of notice from any beneficiary (which shall
include the Unit Owner of any Land Unit and the tenant of any Land Unit)
of this provision that any space in such other Land Unit is being used
in violation of this provision, the Unit Owner of such other Land Unit
shall forthwith, at its expense, institute such actions, suits or
proceedings as may be required to stay and enjoin any such violation,
obtaining if possible, an injunction [pendente lite]{.underline}, and
shall diligently prosecute such actions, suits or proceedings to a final
conclusion thereof and enforce the final judgment, decree or order which
may be rendered, passed, made or entered in such actions, suits or
proceedings in favor of the Unit Owner of such other Land Unit. If the
restrictions upon use of such other space are held by any court of
competent jurisdiction to be in violation of any law or statute
prohibiting restrictions upon competition or otherwise not enforceable,
then the Unit Owner or tenant or occupant of such other Land Unit shall
not be deemed to have violated the foregoing provisions of this
Declaration.

[Section 4.4 -- Compliance]{.underline}. Each Unit Owner shall at its
expense comply or cause compliance with all laws, ordinances, orders,
rules, regulations and requirements of all governmental authorities and
insurance underwriting boards or any other body exercising functions
similar to those of any of the foregoing with respect to its Land Unit
and whether foreseen or unforeseen, ordinary as well as extraordinary
and whether or not the same shall be presently contemplated or shall
involve any change of governmental policy or require extraordinary or
structural repairs, alterations, equipment or additions or any work of
any kind, including without limitation, the fixtures and equipment or
the sidewalks, streets and curbs. Any Unit Owner shall have the right,
without cost to any other Unit Owner, to contest any other Unit Owner,
to contest any obligation imposed upon that Unit Owner pursuant to this
Section 4.4 and to defer compliance during the tendency of such contest
so long as such contest does not subject: (i) any other Unit Owner or
any tenant of the Condominium to criminal liability, and (ii) any other
Unit Owner or any tenant of the Condominium to civil liability unless
there is provided by the Unit Owner undertaking such contest security,
reasonably satisfactory to the other Unit Owner or tenant who would be
subject to such civil liability adequate to protect such other Unit
Owner or tenant form any costs, liability or expenses, including
reasonably attorney's fees and costs, resulting from such civil
liability. Any Unit Owner who acts under this Section 4.4 shall
diligently prosecute any such contest to final determination by an
appropriate court or governmental body having jurisdiction thereover.
Any such Unit Owner shall indemnify and hold all other Unit Owners and
their tenants harmless of and from all costs, expenses, liabilities,
fines, penalties and claims of every kind and nature arising out of any
failure of the Unit Owner to comply with any of its obligations under
this Section 4.4 and shall promptly discharged and perform such
obligations as are imposed hereunder.

[Section 4.5 -- Environmental]{.underline}. Except in accordance with
all applicable laws and regulations thereunto appertaining, no Land Unit
or other portion of the Property shall be used for any operation of
which would result in the escape, disposal or release of ay amount of
biologically active, toxic or hazardous wastes, materials, or
substances, or any other substance that is prohibited, limited or
regulated by any governmental or quasi-governmental authority or that,
even if so regulated, could or does pose a hazard to health and safety
of the occupants of the Land Unit or surrounding property (collectively,
"Hazardous Materials") in violation of any applicable Environmental Laws

be used for the storage or use of Hazardous Materials in any manner, nor
shall any Unit Owner allow to be brought into the Land Units or onto the
Property any such Hazardous Materials. All Land Units and other portions
of the Property will, at all times, be kept and maintained so as to
comply with all federal, state, local and foreign laws, orders,
regulations, rules or ordinances or other governmental restrictions
relating to the environment or to emissions, discharges or releases of
pollutants, contaminants, petroleum or petroleum products, chemicals or
industrial, toxic or hazardous substances or wastes into the environment
including, without limitation, ambient air, surface water, ground water
or land, or otherwise relating to the manufacture, processing,
distribution, use, treatment, storage, disposal, transport or handling
of pollutants, contaminants, petroleum or petroleum products, chemicals
or industrial, toxic or hazardous substances or wastes or the cleanup or
other remediation thereof ("Environmental Laws") whether existing as of
the date hereof, previously enforced or subsequently enacted or issued.

Each Unit Owner (the "Indemnifying Owner") agrees to indemnify and hold
harmless the Unit Owners of the other Land Units, the tenants of the
other Land Units and the directors, managers, officers, employees,
members, and agents of such parties and any assignees, subtenants or
successors to such Unit Owners, tenants, their directors, managers,
members, officers, employers and agents from and against any and all
expenses, losses, claims, damages, penalties and liabilities (including
all out-of-pocket litigation costs and the reasonable fees and expenses
of counsel) for damages, directly or indirectly arising out of the
presence, use, generation, storage, release, or disposal of Hazardous
Materials in violation of Environmental Laws by the Indemnifying Owner,
its agents, contractors, tenants or licenses including, without
limitation, the cost of any required or necessary repair, cleanup,
remediation or detoxification and the preparation of any closure or
other required plans, to the full extent that such action is
attributable, directly or indirectly, to the presence, use, generation,
storage, release, or disposal of Hazardous Materials in violation of
Environmental Laws by Indemnifying Owner on, under or about the Land.

4.6 No signs (excluding any currently existing signs) shall be permitted
on or about the Land Units or Common Elements except as approved by the
Board, except that Declarant shall have the right to display signs on

approval of the Board.

4.7 No Owner of a Land Unit and no ground lessee of a Land Unit shall
subject its Land Unit or leasehold interest therein to a condominium
form of ownership pursuant to a declaration of condominium or to any

**ARTICLE 5**

**[Number of Land Units; Boundaries]{.underline}**

[Section 5.1- Number of Land Units]{.underline}. Declarant does hereby
establish twelve (12) Land Units (Unit 1, Unit 2, Unit 3, Unit 4, Unit
5, Unit 6, Unit 7, Unit 8, Unit 9, Unit 10, Unit 11 and Unit 12) and
does designate the Land Units for separate ownership as set forth in
Article 1 hereof. The maximum number of Land Units in the Condominium
shall be twelve (12) Land Units.

Each Land Unit shall be a land only unit consisting of the land and
existing Improvements as shown on the Survey as well as any future
Improvements to be constructed on each Land Unit in accordance with this
Declaration. Except with respect to any currently existing Building
located on any Land Unit, floor area square footage, lot coverage,
height, and set-back restrictions on each such Land Unit shall comply
with the restrictions, if any, set forth in the Town of Barrington
Zoning Ordinances.

[Section 5.2 -- Boundaries]{.underline}. The Boundaries of each Land
Unit created by the Declaration are shown on the Survey and the Plats as
numbered Land Units with their identifying number. The Plats initially
filed with this Declaration are attached as [Exhibit C]{.underline} to
this Declaration. Boundaries are shown on [Exhibit C]{.underline} and
are described as follows:

(a) *Upper Boundary*: The Upper Boundary of each Land Unit is the air
    space above the Lower Boundary, extended in all directions to an
    intersection with the Perimeter Boundaries of the Land Unit.

(b) *Lower Boundary*: Land Unit 1, Land Unit 2, Land Unit 3 and Land
    Unit 4 are land only units which include all the land within the
    Perimeter Boundaries of those units as hereinafter provided,
    extending within those planes to Lower Boundaries at the center of
    the earth, to the extent permitted by law.

(c) *Perimeter Boundaries*: The Perimeter Boundaries of each Land Unit
    are extended planes running between the Lower Boundary and the Upper
    Boundary of each Land Unit in the locations shown on the Survey. The
    planes of the Perimeter Boundaries intersect the Lower Boundary at
    an angle which would cause those planes, if infinitely extended
    below the Lower Boundary, to intersect the center of the earth.

(d) *Inclusions*: In those instances where a Land Unit comprises a
    volume of air space which does not in any instance intersect with
    the Land, such Land Unit includes all Improvements located within
    that airspace, except to the extent that particular Improvements are
    defined as Common Elements in this Declaration. To the extent any
    Improvement or portion of an Improvement located within the
    boundaries of a Land Unit is defined as a Common Element, that
    Improvement, or the portion of that Improvement designed to serve
    only that Land Unit, is a Limited Common Element allocated
    exclusively to the Land Unit within which it is located.

(e) *Exclusions*: Except when specifically included by other provisions
    of this Section, the following are excluded from each Land Unit: The
    spaces and Improvements lying outside of the boundaries described in
    Section 5.2 (a), (b) and (c), above; and all Common Utility Lines
    running through or under any Land Unit.

**ARTICLE 6**

**Common Elements; Limited Common Elements;**

**[Maintenance; Easement Rights]{.underline}**

[Section 6.1 -- Common Elements]{.underline}. The Common Elements
consist of the areas depicted as "Common Elements\" on the Survey and
shall include, but not be limited to, all property normally in common
use by the Unit Owners, including all areas of the Property which do not
fall within the Land Units, the parking areas on the Property, and all
areas and facilities designated as "Common Elements" in the Act.

The Common Elements shall be subject to the provisions of the Act and
Declaration and to rules and regulations promulgated pursuant thereto
with respect to (a) the use thereof, (b) the reservation of the
exclusive use and enjoyment of certain of such Common Elements (e.g.,
the ) to particular Unit Owners as Limited Common Elements, and (c) the
payments required therefore.

[Section 6.2 - Limited Common Elements]{.underline}. The areas, to the
extent any, depicted as \"Limited Common Elements\" on the Survey shall
be Limited Common Elements of the designated Land Unit or Land Units,
for the exclusive use and enjoyment of the specified Unit Owners and
their tenant(s), guests and invitees. In addition, the following
portions of the Condominium are Limited Common Elements assigned to the
specified Land Units for the exclusive use of the Unit Owners, as
follows:

\(a\) Any Separate Utility Line allocated exclusively to a Land Unit
served.

\(b\) A Common Utility Line allocated to certain Land Units being served
by same.

\(c\) The retaining walls, if any, guard rails and fences located on a
particular Land Unit are Limited Common Elements allocated exclusively
to the Land Unit served.

[Section 6.3 -- Maintenance; Expenses Allocated to Common Elements and
Limited Common Elements]{.underline}.

\(a\) Each Unit Owner shall be exclusively liable for and pay for the
maintenance, repair, landscaping, replacement and upkeep of its Land
Unit and all other improvements located on that Land Unit, including the
Limited Common Elements and for the Separate Utility Lines utilized by
each Land Unit wherever located and for the portion of a Common Utility
Line or storm and sanitary waste lines located on or under its Land
Units. Each Unit Owner agrees that any such maintenance, repair,
landscaping, replacement and upkeep shall be done so as to ensure its
Land Unit, Limited Common Elements, Separate Utility Lines and portions
of any Common Utility Line are maintained in good, clean and safe and
attractive condition.

\(b\) Nothing in this Declaration prevents two or more Unit Owners from
agreeing to cooperate in the maintenance of their Land Units.

\(c\) All maintenance and replacement of an repairs to any part of the
Common Elements and to certain of the Limited Common Elements shall be
made by or at the direction of the Board and shall be a Common Expense,
except to the extent that, in the reasonable determination of the Board,
the same are necessitated by the negligence, misuse or neglect of a Unit
Owner, in which event such expense shall be assessed to such Unit Owner.
Any Common Expenses associated with the maintenance, repair or
replacement of Common Elements shall be assessed to the Association
entirely against the Unit Owners in accordance with the Allocated
Interests.

\(d\) Any expenses associated with the maintenance, repair or
replacement of Limited Common Expenses shall be separately assessed in
their entirety against the Land Unit(s) to which that Limited Common
Element is allocated.

\(e\) In the event that the need for maintenance, repair or replacement
is caused through the willful or negligent act of a Unit Owner, its
tenant, assignees, invitees or licenses or the invitees or customers of
such tenants, assignees, invitees or licensees, the cost of such
maintenance, replacement or repairs shall be added to or become a part
of the assessment to which such Land Unit is subject. Each Unit Owner
shall repair, replace and clean all portions of the Common Elements, any
other Land Unit and any improvements located on the Common Elements or
other Land Units, to the extent that such repair, replacement, or
cleaning is necessitated by such Unit Owner's activities in the
construction of improvements upon its Unit. Each Owner shall conduct all
such construction activities in accordance with the terms of this
Declaration.

[Section 6.4 -- Easement Rights of the Association]{.underline}. In
addition to the rights which the Association has under the Act, the
Association and its representatives shall have easements through the
Common Elements as may be necessary for the purpose of discharging the
Association's obligations under the Declaration, and the Association is
granted an easement across each Land Unit as may be reasonably necessary
for purposes of maintaining, repairing and replacing the Common
Elements.

[Section 6.5 - Additional Easements and Restrictions]{.underline}. Each
of the following easements is hereby created, all of which shall be
nonexclusive easements, except as set forth below, and shall run with
the Property and the individual Land Units and shall inure to the
benefit of and be binding upon the Declarant, the Association, Unit
Owners, occupants, assignees, Lenders and mortgage holders, and any
other Person or entity having an interest in the Condominium and,
notwithstanding any of the other provisions of this Declaration, may not
be substantially amended or revoked in such a way as to unreasonably
interfere with their proper and intended uses and purposes.

\(a\) *Pedestrian and Vehicular Traffic*. Ingress, egress and, where
applicable, parking easements for pedestrian and bicycle traffic over
and upon the sidewalks and paths existing from time to time upon the
Common Elements, and ingress, egress and, where applicable, parking
easements for pedestrian and vehicular traffic over and upon the roads,
parking areas, and other paved areas as existing from time to time upon
the Common Elements and intended for such purposes, same being in favor
of the Unit Owners for their use and benefit and for the use and benefit
of their mortgagees, tenants, guests and invitees.

\(b\) *Service and Utility Easements*. Easements in favor of
governmental and quasi-governmental authorities, utility companies,
cable television companies, ambulance or emergency vehicle companies,
and mail carrier companies, over and across all roads existing from time
to time within the Condominium, and over, under, on and across the
Common Elements, as may be reasonably required to permit the foregoing,
and their agents and employees, to provide their respective authorized
services to and for the Condominium. Also, easements as maybe reasonably
required for the installation, maintenance, repair, and providing of
utility services, equipment and fixtures, in order to adequately serve
the Condominium or any Land Unit or Common Element, including, but not
limited to, electricity, telephones, sewer, water, lighting, irrigation,
drainage, television antenna and cable television facilities, and
electronic security. Easements through a Land Unit shall be only
according to the plans and specifications for the Building or as the
Building is actually constructed, or reconstructed, unless approved in
writing by the Unit Owner of the Land Unit. A Unit Owner shall do
nothing within or outside his Land Unit that interferes with or impairs
the utility services using these easements.

\(c\) *Support*. Every portion of a Building located upon a Land Unit
contributing to the support of a Building or other structural
improvements constructed and located upon an adjacent Land Unit shall be
burdened with an easement of support for the benefit of such other Land
Unit's Building or other structural improvements.

\(d\) *Perpetual Nonexclusive Easement In Common Elements*. The Common
Elements shall be, and the same are hereby declared to be, subject to a
perpetual non-exclusive easement in favor of all of the Unit Owners and
residents of the Condominium, and their guests and invitees, for all
proper and normal purposes and for the furnishing of services and
facilities for which the same are reasonably intended.

\(e\) *Air Space.* Each Land Unit shall have an exclusive easement for
the use of the air space occupied by the Land Unit as it exists at any
particular time and as the Land Unit may lawfully be altered.

\(f\) *Encroachments.* If any portion of the Common Elements encroaches
upon any Land Unit; if any Land Unit, Limited Common Element, or any
other improvement located upon a Land Unit encroaches upon any other
Land Unit or upon any portion of the Common Elements; or if any
encroachment shall hereafter occur as a result of (i) construction,
alteration or reconstruction of any Improvements; (ii) settling,
unintentional placement or shifting of any Improvements; (iii) any
addition, alteration or repair to the Common Elements or Limited Common
Elements made by or with the consent of the Association; (iv) any repair
or restoration of any Improvements (or any portion thereof) or any Land
Unit after damage by fire or other casualty or any taking by
condemnation or eminent domain proceedings of all or any portion of any
Land Unit or the Common Elements; or (v) any non-purposeful or
non-negligent act of a Unit Owner except as may be authorized by the
Board, then, in any such event, a valid easement shall exist for such
encroachment and for the maintenance of the same so long as the
Improvements shall stand.

\(g\) Easements for overhanging troughs or gutters, downspouts, and the
discharge therefrom of rainwater and the subsequent flow thereof over
the Land Units and the Condominium.

\(h\) *Declarant's Easement*. Declarant reserves an easement over, upon,
across and under the Common Elements and the Property as may be
reasonably required in connection with the development and construction
of Improvements within the Condominium and the Property, if any, and the
sale, promotion and leasing of the Land Units. Declarant also reserves
such an easement through the Common Elements as may be reasonably
necessary for the purpose of discharging Declarant\'s obligations or
exercising Development Rights or Special Declarant Rights, whether
arising under the Act or reserved in the Declaration.

\(i\) *Additional Easements*. The Association, on its behalf and on
behalf of all Unit Owners, shall have the right to grant and declare
additional easements, licenses and permits over, upon, under, and/or
across the Common Elements and the Property in favor of any person,
entity, public or quasi-public authority or utility company so long as
the additional easements do not substantially interfere with the rights
of any Unit Owners or unreasonably affect any Limited Common Elements.
The Association shall also have the right, upon consent of the
Association, to modify, relocate, abandon or terminate existing
easements, licenses and permits within or outside of the Condominium in
favor of the Association and/or the Unit Owners or in favor of any
person, entity, public or quasi-public authority, or utility company.
This section does not authorize the Association to modify, relocate,
abandon or terminate any easement, license or permit created in whole or
in part for the use or benefit of anyone other than the Unit Owners, or
crossing the property of anyone other than the Unit Owners, without
their consent or approval as otherwise required by law or by the
instrument creating the easement.

\(j\) *Easements and Restrictions of Record*. The creation of this
Condominium is subject to other restrictions, reservations and easements
of record.

\(k\) *Planning and Zoning*. This Declaration, the Association and the
Unit Owners shall be bound by and subject to all relevant decisions of
the Town of Barrington Planning Board and the Barrington Zoning Board.

\(l\) Any damage occurring to any easement area described herein as a
result of Unit Owner's exercise of such easements for construction
purposes, or otherwise, shall be remedied at the sole cost of such
Owner.

\(m\) All easements granted herein are appurtenant to and shall run with
the Property and the individual Land Units, and shall inure to the
benefit of and be binding upon the Declarant, the Association, Unit
Owners, occupants, assignees, lessees and mortgage holders, and any
other person or entity having an interest in the Condominium.

**ARTICLE 8**

**[Allocated Interests]{.underline}**

[Section 8.1 -- Allocation of Interests]{.underline}. The votes in the
affairs of the Association allocated to each Land Unit shall be
proportionate to the relative Gross Area of the Land Units, and rounded
to the nearest one-hundredths as shown on [Exhibit D]{.underline}.
Except as set forth herein, approval of any action of the Association
shall require majority consent of the votes. Any Person, including a
Declarant, may cast the votes allocated to all Land Units owned by that
Person.

The total undivided interest in all Land Units shall at all times equal
one hundred percent (100%). If the additional real estate is added, the
undivided interest for each Land Unit will be reallocated in accordance
with the Gross Area of the Land Units. After reallocation, the total
undivided interest of all Land Units will equal one hundred percent
(100%).

[Section 8.2 -- Limitation of Challenges]{.underline}. No action to
challenge the validity of an amendment adopted by the Association
pursuant to this Section may be brought more than one (1) year after the
amendment is recorded.

[Section 8.3 -- Recordation of Amendments]{.underline}. Every amendment
to the Declaration shall be recorded in the Town of Barrington Land
Evidence Records and is effective only on recordation. An amendment,
except an amendment pursuant to Article 9 of this Declaration, shall be
indexed in the grantee\'s index in the name of The Cottages on Freemont
Condominium and in the grantor\'s index in the name of the Association
as the Person executing the amendment.

[Section 8.4 -- Consent Required; Exceptions]{.underline}. Except to the
extent expressly permitted or required by other provisions of the Act
and/or this Declaration, no amendment to the Declaration may be adopted
or take effect, including but not limited to an amendment to create or
increase Special Declarant Rights or Development Rights, increase the
number of Land Units, change the boundaries of any Land Unit, the
Allocated interest of a Land Unit, or the uses to which any Land Unit is
restricted by this Declaration, in the absence of the majority consent
of the Unit Owners, the consent of all persons holding First Security
Interests on the Land Units affected and the consent of the Unit Owner
whose Land Unit is affected.

[Section 8.5 -- Execution of Amendments]{.underline}. Amendments to the
Declaration required by this Act to be recorded by the Association shall
be prepared, executed, recorded and certified on behalf of the
Association by the president or secretary of the Association, or their
designee.

[Section 8.6 -- Special Declarant Rights]{.underline}. Provisions in the
Declaration creating Special Declarant Rights and Development Rights may
not be amended without the consent of the Declarant, such consent to be
in the sole discretion of the Declarant.

The Bylaws may be amended only by majority vote of the members of the
Board.

**ARTICLE 11**

**[Assessment and Collection of Common Expenses; Self-Help
Lien]{.underline}**

[Section 11.1 -- Assessment; Lien]{.underline}.

\(a\) Assessments shall commence against a Land Unit when it has been
created by the recording of this Declaration and the Plats or any
amendment thereof.

\(b\) Declarant, for each Land Unit owned, and each Unit Owner by
acceptance of a deed for any Property within the Condominium, whether or
not it shall be so expressed in such deed, hereby covenants and agrees
to pay the Association (1) annual assessments or charges levied by the
Association to be used as provided in Section 11.1(e) below; (2) special
assessments for capital improvements levied by the Association, such
assessments to be established and collected as hereinafter provided; and
(3) additional assessments levied by the Association pursuant to this
Declaration or the Act. With respect to those assessments which pertain
exclusively to the use, maintenance, repair, replacement, insurance and
capital improvements of the Limited Common Elements, the liability of
the Unit Owner of the Land Unit to which a Limited Common Element is
allocated shall be one hundred (100%) on the grounds that the Unit Owner
has the sole right to use and enjoy its allocated Limited Common
Element. Should any assessments levied by the Association pertain to a
portion of the Condominium other than the Limited Common Elements, the
liability of each Unit Owner for such assessment shall be in accordance
with each Unit Owner's Allocated Interest, as set forth in [Exhibit
D]{.underline}, except that any Unit Owner which is assessed a special
assessment for damage to the Limited Common Element caused by such Unit
Owner shall pay one hundred percent (100%) of such special assessment,
all in accordance with this Article 11 and Section 15.2.

\(c\) Fees (including reasonable attorneys' fees), charges, late
charges, fines and interest thereon are also enforceable as assessments.
Each assessment shall be the personal obligation of the Person who was
the Unit Owner of such Land Unit at the time when the assessment became
due. The personal obligation for delinquent assessments shall not pass
to a Unit Owner's successors in title unless expressly assumed by such
successors.

\(d\) Under the Act, the Association has a statutory lien on a Land Unit
for any assessment levied against the Land Unit or fines imposed against
its Unit Owner from the time the assessment or fine becomes due. Fees,
charges, late charges, fines and interest charged pursuant to the Act
are enforceable under this Section.

The statutory lien of the Association for assessments under Section
34-36.1-3.16 shall be expressly limited to the sole purposes of (1)
enforcing payment from the Unit Owners of the general and special
assessments assessed by the Association as part of its regular budget
under Section 11.2 and (2) securing repayment to the Association for any
sums paid by the Association to a Unit Owner; and not for other fines,
penalties or assessments against the Land Unit. In the event the
interest of an Unit Owner is foreclosed or extinguished as a result of
the enforcement of the Association\'s lien, a purchaser at such
foreclosure or execution sale shall succeed to the interest of such Unit
Owner in all leases affecting such Land Unit and such leases shall
otherwise remain in full force and effect as leases between such
successive Unit Owner and the tenant thereunder.

\(e\) The Board shall establish annual assessments in accordance with
the Bylaws of the Association. The annual assessments levied by the
Association shall be used to obtain such insurance as the Association is
required to maintain or its reasonable discretion maintains, and for
doing such other things as are necessary or desirable, in the reasonable
discretion of the Association, to keep the Common Elements in a clean
and good order and provide for the health, welfare and safety of the
Unit Owners and occupants of the Land Units and the Common Elements.

The Board may also levy special assessments payable by the Unit Owner to
whom a Limited Common Element is allocated for the purpose of defraying,
in whole or part, the cost of any construction, reconstruction, repair,
replacement of a capital improvement upon the same, including fixtures
and personal property related thereto.

[Section 11.2 -- Budget Adoption and Ratification]{.underline}. The
budget for the costs to insure, maintain, repair or replace the Common
Elements as provided in this Declaration shall be included in the Budget
prepared by the Board and shall be adopted and ratified by the Board
pursuant to the terms and provisions of the Declaration. The Board may
also, from time to time after the adoption of the Budget, determine that
special assessments may be needed for the repair, maintenance and/or
upkeep of the Common Elements, in the same manner that regular
assessments are determined and allocated pursuant to this Declaration,
provided however, that in the event that any such special assessment is
caused by the act or failure to act of one or less than all of the Unit
Owners, such special assessments may be allocated solely against such
individual Unit Owner or Unit Owners.

[Section 11.3 -- Certificate of Payment of Common Expense
Assessments]{.underline}. The Association, on written request, shall
furnish to a Unit Owner a statement in recordable form setting forth the
amount of any unpaid assessments against that Unit Owner\'s Unit. The
statement shall be furnished within ten (10) business days after receipt
of the request and is binding on the Association, the Board and every
Unit Owner.

[Section 11.4 -- Payment of Common Expenses]{.underline}. All Common
Expenses assessed under Section 11.2 shall be due and payable in twelve
(12) equal monthly installments by each Unit Owner beginning thirty (30)
days after a statement for such expenses is submitted to the Unit Owner.

[Section 11.5 -- Maintenance of Common Elements]{.underline}. The
Association shall insure, maintain, repair and replace the Common
Elements as is appropriate for a first class condominium of this nature.
With respect to the cost, maintenance and landscaping of the common
surface areas and parking areas, the Unit Owners agree to bear the costs
as all other Common Elements.

**ARTICLE 14**

**[Insurance]{.underline}**

[Section 14.1 -- Insurance Coverage]{.underline}. The Association shall
obtain and maintain, to the extent reasonably available, the following
insurance coverage:

\(a\) Casualty or physical damage insurance with a deductible determined
by the Board of Directors and in an amount equal to the replacement cost
of the Condominium, including each Unit but excluding any personal
property owned by a Unit Owner or any improvements, alterations,
replacements or additions made by a Unit Owner a Unit, with an \"agreed
amount\" endorsement and a \"Condominium replacement cost\" endorsement
without deduction or allowance for depreciation (said amount and
coverage to be re-determined annually by the Board of Directors with the
assistance of the insurance agency or insurance company affording such
coverage), such coverage to afford protection against at least the
following:

> \(i\) loss or damage by fire or other hazards covered by the standard
> extended coverage endorsement together with coverage for Common
> Expenses with respect to Units during any period of repair or
> reconstruction;
>
> \(ii\) such other risks as shall customarily be covered with respect
> to projects similar in construction, location and use, including but
> not limited to, vandalism, malicious mischief, windstorm, water
> damage, machinery explosion or damage, and such other insurance as the
> Board of Directors may from time to time determine; and

\(b\) General liability insurance with a \"Severability of Interest\"
endorsement in such amounts and in such forms as may be considered
appropriate by the Board of Directors including, but not limited to,
water damage, legal liability, liability for property of others, and any
and all other liability incident to the ownership and/or use of the
Condominium or any portion thereof; and

\(c\) Workers' compensation insurance to the extent necessary to comply
with any applicable law; and

\(d\) Such other policies of insurance, including insurance for other
risks of a similar or dissimilar nature, as are or shall hereafter be
required by law or which may be considered appropriate by the Board of
Directors, including without limitation officer's and director's
liability insurance and fidelity insurance.

[Section 14.2 -- Insurance Limitations]{.underline}. Any insurance
obtained pursuant to the requirements hereof shall be subject to the
following provisions to the extent available:

\(a\) All policies shall be written with a company or companies licensed
to do business in the State of Rhode Island with a rating equal or
comparable to a rating of \"A\" or better in Best\'s Insurance Guide;

\(b\) Exclusive authority to negotiate losses under said policies shall
be vested in the Association or its authorized representative, provided,
however, that no loss under any insurance policy shall be compromised or
settled by the Association, without the prior written consent of the
mortgagee holding the first mortgage on the Unit for which such claim
has been made;

\(c\) All policies of casualty insurance shall provide that,
notwithstanding any provisions thereof which give the carrier the right
to elect to restore damage in lieu of making a cash settlement, such
option shall not be exercisable without the prior written approval of
the Board of Directors;

\(d\) All policies shall contain a waiver of subrogation by the insurer
as to any and all claims against the Condominium, the Board of
Directors, the Owners and/or their respective agents, employees or
invitees, and of any defenses based upon co-insurance or invalidity
arising from the acts of the insured;

\(e\) No act or omission by any Unit Owner, unless acting within the
scope of his or her authority on behalf of the Association, will void
any policy or be a condition to recovery under the policy; and

\(f\) All policies shall contain the standard mortgagee clause, except
that any loss or losses payable to named mortgagees shall be payable in
the manner hereinafter set forth in this Article.

[Section 14.3 -- Individual Insurance Policies]{.underline}. Each Owner
of a Unit (and the holder of any mortgage thereon) shall obtain
additional insurance (including a \"Condominium Unit Owner\'s
Endorsement" for improvements and betterments to the Unit made or
acquired at the expense of the Owner) after the initial construction of
the Unit at the Owner's expense. Such insurance shall be written by the
same carrier as that purchased by the Association pursuant to this
Article or shall provide that it shall be without contribution as
against the same. Such insurance shall contain the same waiver of
subrogation provision.

[Section 14.4 -- Endorsement, Etc]{.underline}. The Association, at the
request of any Owner or at the request of the mortgagee of any such
Unit, shall promptly obtain and forward to such Owner or mortgagee (a)
an endorsement to any of the policies aforementioned in this Article
showing the interest of such Owner or mortgagee as it may appear; and
(b) certificates of insurance relating to any of such policies; and (c)
proof of payment of premium for any such policy or policies.

[Section 14.5 -- Insurance Proceeds]{.underline}. All insurance policies
purchased by the Board of Directors shall be for the benefit of the
Association and the Owners and the mortgagees of the Units as their
respective interests may appear and shall provide that all proceeds
covering property losses shall be paid to the Association. Proceeds of
insurance policies received by the Association shall be distributed to
or for the benefit of the beneficial owners in the following manner:

\(a\) [Reconstruction or Repair]{.underline}. In the event the damage
for which the proceeds are paid is to be repaired or reconstructed, the
proceeds shall be paid to defray the cost thereof as herein elsewhere
provided. Any proceeds remaining after defraying such costs shall be
distributed to or credited to the Owners in accordance with the Act,
subject to the terms of mortgages held by the Mortgagees. This is a
covenant for the benefit of any mortgagee of a Unit and may be enforced
by such mortgagee.

\(b\) [When Damaged Area is not to be Restored]{.underline}. In the
event it is determined in the manner herein elsewhere provided that the
damage for which the proceeds are paid shall not be reconstructed or
repaired, then the remaining proceeds shall be paid to the Unit Owners
in proportion to their Percentage Interests and their respective
mortgagees (if any) as their interest may appear, and subject to the
terms of mortgages held by the Mortgagees. This is a covenant for the
benefit of any mortgagee of a Unit and may be enforced by such
mortgagee.

[Section 14.6 -- Repair or Reconstruction after Fire or Other
Casualty]{.underline}.

\(a\) In the event of damage to or destruction of the Condominium as a
result of fire or other casualty (except as provided in the Act), the
Association shall arrange for the prompt repair and restoration of the
damaged area, substantially in the same condition which existed prior to
the damage (including any damaged Units but not including, except to the
extent covered by the Association\'s insurance, any decorations or
coverings on any wall, ceiling or floor or any furniture, furnishings,
fixtures or equipment installed in the Units), and the Association shall
arrange for the disbursement of the proceeds of all insurance policies.
If the proceeds of insurance are insufficient to defray the estimated
costs of reconstruction and repair or if at any time during
reconstruction and repair or upon completion of reconstruction and
repair, the funds for the payment of the costs thereof are insufficient,
the Board of Directors may use Common Expense funds in the hands of the
Board of Directors or may increase the annual assessment to provide
funds for the payment of such costs. Said funds shall be paid to the
Association for disbursement as hereinafter set forth.

\(b\) Immediately after a casualty causing damage to property for which
the Association has the responsibility of restoration and repair, the
Association shall obtain reliable and detailed estimates of the cost to
replace the damaged property in condition as good as that before the
casualty. The Association may, but shall not be required to, retain the
services of a public adjuster.

\(c\) The proceeds of insurance collected on account of casualty and the
sums received from collection of assessments against Unit Owners on
account of such casualty, if any, shall constitute a construction fund
which shall be disbursed in payment of the costs of reconstruction and
repair upon order of the Board of Directors.

\(d\) In the event that the damage is not to be repaired, the provisions
of the Act, as the case may be, shall govern future actions.

**ARTICLE 15**

**[Damage or Destruction]{.underline}**

[Section 15.1 -- Damage to Common Elements]{.underline}. If the Common
Elements are damaged or destroyed, insurance proceeds attributable to
the damaged Common Elements shall be used by the Association to restore
the damaged area to a condition compatible with that which existed prior
to the damage or destruction. Any cost of such repair and restoration in
excess of insurance proceeds shall constitute a Common Expense. Any
insurance proceeds remaining after such repair or reconstruction shall
be retained by the Association for future maintenance expenses or, in
the discretion of the Board disbursed to the Unit Owners in the same
proportion as Common Expenses are assessed against such Unit Owners.

[Section 15.2 -- Damage to Limited Common Elements]{.underline}. Any
portion of the Limited Common Elements which is damaged or destroyed
shall be repaired or restored by the Unit Owner upon or under whose Land
Unit the damage or destruction occurred at its sole cost and expense
with all the diligence. In the event such damage or destruction is
caused by another Person, whether directly or indirectly, the Unit Owner
obligated to make such repair reserves the right to proceed against such
other Person for indemnity, contribution or damages. []{.mark}

[Section 15.3 -- Damage to Building]{.underline}. In the event any of
the Buildings on the Condominium are damaged or destroyed by fire other
casualty, the Unit Owner or Unit Owners of the Building located upon the
Land Unit or Land Units, shall, subject to governmental regulations and
insurance adjustment delays, immediately remove the debris resulting
from such event and provide a sightly barrier, and within a reasonable
time thereafter shall either (i) repair or restore the Building so
damaged to a complete unit, such repair or restoration to be performed
in accordance with all provisions of the Declaration and applicable
governmental regulations, or (ii) erect another Building in such
location, such construction to be performed in accordance with all
provisions of this Declaration and applicable governmental regulations,
or (iii) demolish the damaged portion and/or the balance of such
Building and restore the cleared area to either a hard surface condition
or a landscaped condition. Such Unit Owner shall have the option to
choose which of the foregoing alternatives to perform, but such Unit
Owner shall be obligated to perform one of such alternatives. Should a
Unit Owner elect not to rebuild the improvements constructed on such a
Land Unit, such Land Unit shall not be converted into Common Elements.

**ARTICLE 16**

**[Open Meetings]{.underline}**

[Section 16.1 -- Access]{.underline}. All meetings of the Board, at
which action is to be taken by vote at such meeting, shall be open to
the Unit Owners, except as hereafter provided.

[Section 16.2 -- Sessions]{.underline}. Meetings of the Board may be
held in executive session, without the requirement that they be open to
Unit Owners, in either of the following situations, only:

\(a\) No action is taken at the session requiring the affirmative vote
of the Board members; or

(b) The action taken at the session involves personnel, pending
    litigation, enforcement actions, or the action taken is derivative
    of authorized action previously approved by the Unit Owners.

**ARTICLE 17**

**[Board; Board Limitations]{.underline}**

[Section 17.1 -- Board]{.underline}. Except in those circumstances where
the Act or this Declaration (including those set forth in Section 17.2
below) or the Bylaws requires the vote of the Association, the rights
and powers of the Association shall be exercised by and through the
Board. The initial members of the Board shall be appointed by the
Declarant, and their successors shall be elected as provided in the
Bylaws.

[Section 17.2 -- Board Limitations]{.underline}. The Board may not act
on behalf of the Association to amend the Declaration, to terminate the
Condominium or to elect members of the Board or determine the
qualifications, powers and duties, or terms of office of Board members,
but the Board may fill vacancies in its membership for the unexpired
portion of any term.

**ARTICLE 18**

**[Real Estate Taxes]{.underline}**

Each Unit Owner shall pay or cause to be paid directly to the assessing
authority, as and when due and payable and before any fine or penalties
imposed thereon by reason of late payment, all real estate taxes levied
on its Land Unit. By acceptance of a deed to a Land Unit each Unit Owner
shall be deemed to have agreed to the terms and conditions of this
Article. The Declarant, the Association and all the Unit Owners shall
comply with the requirements of the Act regarding separate taxes and
taxation.

**ARTICLE 19**

**[Repairs]{.underline}**

Each Unit Owner shall make or cause to be made any and all repairs and
replacements required to be made to the Limited Common Elements located
on or under its Land Unit and to the Land Units.

**ARTICLE 20**

**[Alterations of Land Units]{.underline}**

Each Unit Owner may, from time to time, at its own cost and expense,
make such alterations, restorations, changes, replacements or
installations (hereinafter called "Alterations(s)"), in, of, or to any
Building located on a Land Unit or otherwise to a Land Unit as such Unit
Owner deems necessary or desirable, whether structural or
non-structural, exterior or interior, provided same does not materially
or adversely affect the other Land Units in the Condominium. Exterior
changes shall be architecturally harmonious with exteriors of other
Buildings in the Condominium. In making the Alterations, each Unit Owner
shall comply with all applicable laws, orders and regulations of
governmental authorities, and shall obtain, or cause to be obtained, all
permits and licenses, and other governmental approvals which may be
required in connection with the making of the Alterations. The
Association shall cooperate with the Unit Owners in the obtaining of any
such approvals and shall promptly execute any consents, applications
and/or documents reasonably required in furtherance of such purpose.

**ARTICLE 21**

**[Eminent Domain]{.underline}**

[Section 21.1 -- No Voluntary Consent on Taking Without
Consent]{.underline}. No Unit Owner of a Land Unit may voluntarily
convey any portion of such Land Unit to any public or quasi-public
authority in lieu of condemnation without the prior written consent of
the Unit Owners of the other Land Units and the holders of First
Security Interests in all of the Land Units.

[Section 21.2 -- Restoration]{.underline}.

\(a\) In the event any portion of the Condominium shall be condemned, or
conveyed under threat of condemnation, the award shall be paid to the
Unit Owner owning the tract or the improvements taken, and the other
Unit Owners hereby waive and releases any right to recover any value
attributable to the property interest so taken, except that (i) if the
taking includes improvements belonging to more than one (1) Unit Owner,
such as utility lines or sign structures, the portion of the award
allocable thereto shall be used to relocate, replace or restore such
jointly owned improvements to a useful condition, and (ii) if the taking
includes easement rights, the portion of the award allocable to each
such easement right shall be paid to the respective grantees thereof.

\(b\) Upon acquisition by eminent domain, (i) that Land Unit's Allocated
Interests are reduced in proportion to the reduction in the size of the
Land Unit, or any other basis specified in the Declaration; (ii) the
portion of the Allocated Interests divested from the partially acquired
Land Unit are automatically reallocated to that Land Unit and the
remaining Land Units in proportion to the respective allocated interests
of those Land Units before the taking, with the partially acquired Land
Unit participating in the reallocation on the basis of its reduced
allocated interests; (iii) if part of the Common Elements are acquired
by eminent domain the portion of the award attributable to the Common
Elements taken must be paid to the Association and (iv) any portion of
the award attributable to the acquisition of a Limited Common Element
must be equally divided among the owners of the Land Units to which that
Limited Common Element was allocated at the time of acquisition.

**[THE COTTAGES ON FREEMONT CONDOMINIUM ASSOCIATION]{.underline}**

These Bylaws have been adopted by the Persons constituting all of the
members of the first Board of THE COTTAGES ON FREEMONT CONDOMINIUM
ASSOCIATION (the \"Association\"), a Rhode Island unincorporated
association.

[ARTICLE I]{.underline}

[INTRODUCTORY PROVISIONS]{.underline}

Section 1.1 [Applicability]{.underline}. These Bylaws (\"Bylaws\") shall
relate solely to the property called THE COTTAGES ON FREEMONT
CONDOMINIUM, in the Town of Barrington, Rhode Island (the \"Property\"),
more fully described in the Declaration of Condominium for THE COTTAGES
ON FREEMONT CONDOMINIUM and the Plats attached thereto (collectively the
\"Declaration\") recorded herewith in the Land Evidence Records of the
Town of Barrington, as the same be amended from time to time.

Section 1.2 [Definitions]{.underline}. The capitalized terms used herein
without definition shall have the same definitions as such terms have in
the Declaration and the Rhode Island Condominium Act, as amended,
R.I.G.L. §34-36.1, et seq. (the \"Act\"). Unless otherwise provided in
the Act, in the event of consistencies in definitions between the Act
and the Declaration, the Declaration shall control.

Section 1.3 [Compliance]{.underline}. Pursuant to the provisions of the
Act, every Unit Owner shall comply with these Bylaws.

Section 1.4 [Office]{.underline}. The office of the Condominium, the
Association and the Board shall be located at the Property or at such
other place as may be designated from time to time by the Board.

Section 1.5 [Incorporation of Statutory Law]{.underline}. Except as
expressly provided herein, in the Declaration, or in the Act, the
Association shall be governed by the provision of any applicable statute
of the State of Rhode Island.

[ARTICLE 2]{.underline}

[THE ASSOCIATION]{.underline}

Section 2.1 [Membership]{.underline}. The Association is a Rhode Island
corporation, all of the members of which are Unit Owners of the
Property. The Declarant, being the initial owner of all Land Units,
initially shall constitute all of the members of the Association. A
Person shall automatically become a member of the Association at the
time it acquires legal title to a Land Unit, and shall continue to be a
member so long as it continues to hold title to such Land Unit. A Unit
Owner shall not be permitted to resign from membership in the
Association prior to the time when it transfers title to his Land Unit
to another. No membership may be transferred in any way except as
appurtenant to the transfer of title to the Land Unit to which that
membership pertains. Transfer of membership shall be automatic upon
transfer of title, but the Association may treat the prior Unit Owner as
a member for all purposes until satisfactory evidence of the recording
or the instrument transferring title shall be presented to the Secretary
of the Board. The date of recordation of an instrument of conveyance in
the Land Evidence Records of the Town of Barrington shall be
determinative of all disputes concerning the date of transfer of title
to any Land Unit or Land Units.

Section 2.2 [Meetings]{.underline}. Meetings of the Association shall be
conducted in the following:

(a) [Annual Meetings]{.underline}.

\(1\) Unit Owners shall hold Annual Meetings for the purpose stated in
[Section 2.2(a)(2)]{.underline} hereof (the "Annual Meetings"). The
Annual Meetings of Unit Owners shall be held on a date established by
the Board during the month of July of each year.

\(2\) The purpose of the Annual Meetings of the Association shall be to
elect the members of the Board unless such action is being taken
pursuant to the provisions of [Section 2.2(g)]{.underline} hereof or
[Section 3.5]{.underline} hereof, and to conduct such other business as
may be required or permitted by law, the Declaration or these Bylaws to
be done by a vote of Unit Owners. The Treasurer of the Board shall
present at each Annual Meeting a financial report for the Association's
immediately preceding fiscal year.

\(b\) [Special Meetings]{.underline}. The President shall call a special
meeting of the Association if so directed by resolution of the Board or
upon petition signed and presented to the Secretary by any Unit Owner
entitled to cast at least fifty percent (50%) of the aggregate Allocated
Interests in the Association. The notice of any special meeting shall
state the time, the place and purpose thereof. Such meetings shall be
held within forty-five (45) days after receipt by the President of said
resolution or petition; provided, however, if the purpose includes the
consideration of the rejection of a budget or capital expenditure
pursuant to [Section 5.8]{.underline} hereof, such meeting shall be held
within fifteen (15) days after receipt by the President of said
resolution or petition. No business shall be transacted at a special
meeting except as stated in the notice.

(c) [Notice]{.underline}. Notices to Unit Owners of meetings of the
    Association shall be delivered either by hand or prepaid mailing
    address of each Land Unit or to another mailing address designated
    in writing by the Unit Owner to the Board. If a notice sent to Unit
    Owners pursuant to the foregoing sentence includes an item on the
    proposed agenda which would require the approval of all holders of
    all mortgages pursuant to the Declaration, a copy of such notice
    will also be sent to the holders of all mortgages. Notwithstanding
    the foregoing sentence, copies of notices of impending meetings will
    be provided to the holders of mortgages strictly as a courtesy, and
    the failure of the Association or the Board to provide any Mortgagee
    with a copy of such notice shall not invalidate any actions taken by
    the Association or the Board or subject any members of the
    Association or the Board to any liability whatsoever. All such
    notices shall be delivered to all Unit Owners (and holders of
    mortgages, if applicable) not less than ten (10) nor more than
    sixty (60) days in advance of the date of the meeting to which the
    notices relates and shall state the date, time and place of the
    meeting and items on the agenda, including the general nature of any
    proposed amendment to the Declaration or Bylaws. The Secretary of
    the Board shall cause all such notices to be delivered as aforesaid.
    Notices sent by mail shall be deemed to have been delivered on the
    second day after the date of mailing, in the case of mailed notices;
    or the date of deposit in the Unit Owner's (or if applicable, in the
    Mortgagee's) mailbox, in the case of hand delivery. No subject may
    be voted upon at any Annual or Special Meeting of the Association
    unless the notice for such meeting stated that such subject would be
    discussed at such meeting. Notice of meeting may be waived by Unit
    Owners before or after meetings. A Unit Owner's attendance at a
    meeting shall constitute his waiver of notice of such meeting.

(d) [Quorum]{.underline}. Except as set forth below, the presence in
    person, by remote means, or by proxy of the Unit Owners of
    seventy-five percent (75%) or more of the aggregate Allocated
    Interests at the commencement of a meeting shall constitute a quorum
    at all meetings of the Association. When a quorum is once present,
    it cannot be broken by the subsequent withdrawal of a Unit Owner.

(e) [Voting]{.underline}. Each Land Unit shall be entitled to one
    weighted vote based on its Allocated Interests as set forth in the
    Declaration. Where the ownership of a Land Unit is in more than
    one (1) Person, the Person who shall be entitled to cast the vote of
    such Land Unit shall be the Person owning such Land Unit who is
    present. If more than one Person owning such Land Unit is present,
    then such vote shall be cast only in accordance with the majority in
    interest of the owners pursuant to Section 36.1-3.10 of the Act.
    There shall be deemed to be majority agreement if any one of the
    multiple owners casts the votes allocated to that Land Unit without
    protest being made promptly to the Person presiding over the meeting
    by any of the other owners of the Land Unit. Subject to the
    requirements of the Act, wherever the approval or disapproval of the
    Unit Owner is required by the Act, the Declaration or these Bylaws,
    such approval or disapproval shall be made only by the Person who
    would be entitled to cast the vote of such Land Unit at any meeting
    of the Association. The Unit Owners of more than fifty-one (51%) of
    the aggregate vote in person, by remote means, or by proxy at one
    time at a duly convened meeting at which a quorum is present is
    required to adopt decisions at any meeting of the Association. Any
    specified percentage of the Unit Owners means the Unit Owners owning
    such Allocated Interests in the aggregate. In all elections for
    Board members, each Unit Owner shall be entitled to cast for each
    vacancy to be filled at such election the number of votes allocated
    to the Land Unit or Land Units owned by such Unit Owner based on its
    Allocated Interests as provided in the Declaration. Those candidates
    for election receiving the greatest number of votes cast in such
    elections shall be elected. If the Declarant owns or holds title to
    one or more Land Units, the Declarant shall have the right at any
    meeting of the Association to cast the votes to which such Land Unit
    or Land Units are entitled. There shall be no cumulative or class
    voting or splitting of votes. No votes allocated to a Land Unit
    owned by the Association may be cast.

\(f\) [Proxies]{.underline}. A vote may be cast in person or by proxy.
If a Land Unit is owned by more than one Person, each owner of the Land
Unit may vote through a duly executed proxy. Such proxy may be granted
by any Unit Owner only in favor of another Unit Owner, the holder of a
mortgage on a Land Unit or the Declarant. Proxies shall be duly executed
in writing, shall be valid only for the particular meeting designated
herein and must be filed with the Secretary before the appointed time of
the meeting. Such proxy shall be deemed revoked only by actual receipt
by the Person presiding over the meeting of written notice of revocation
from the grantor of the proxy. No proxy shall be valid for a period in
excess of one year after the execution thereof. A proxy is void if it is
not dated or purports to be revocable without notice. A later dated
proxy will nullify and void a prior dated proxy.

\(g\) [Actions of Association without a Meeting]{.underline}. Any action
required or permitted to be taken by a vote of the Association may be
taken without a meeting by the written consent, stating the action so
taken, of at least that number of Unit Owners whose votes would have
otherwise been sufficient to take the action if a meeting had been held
at which all Unit Owners were present.

\(h\) [Conduct of Meetings]{.underline}. The President (or in his
absence, one of the Vice Presidents) shall preside over the meetings of
the Association, and the Secretary shall keep the minutes of the meeting
and a record in a Minute Book, all resolutions adopted at the meeting,
as well as a record of all transactions occurring at the meeting. The
President may appoint a Person to serve as a parliamentarian at any
meeting of the Association.

[ARTICLE 3]{.underline}

[BOARD]{.underline}

Section 3.1 [Composition]{.underline}. The affairs of the Association
shall be governed by the Board. The Board shall have three (3) members.
Each member of the Board must be a Unit Owner (or a representative
appointed by such Unit Owner). If a Land Unit is owned by a corporation,
any single officer of such corporation will qualify for candidacy and
election to the Board.

Section 3.2 [Election of Board]{.underline}

\(a\) Each Unit Owner shall have one (1) vote for electing a person to
fill a seat on the Board and shall be authorized to serve on the Board
or appoint a representative to the Board ("Board Membership
Requirements"). The election of the Board shall be held at the Annual
Meeting of the Association, subject to [Section 16.1]{.underline} of the
Declaration. The term of office of any Board member to be elected shall
be fixed at one year. The members of the Board shall hold office until
the earlier to occur of the election of their respective successors or
their death, adjudication of incompetence, removal or resignation.

\(b\) Subject to the Board Membership Requirements set forth in Section
3.2(a), persons qualified to be members of the Board may be nominated
for election only as follows:

\(1\) Each Unit Owner shall submit to the Secretary at least thirty days
before the meeting at which the election is to be held a nominating
petition signed by each respective Unit Owner, together with the
statement that the Person nominated is willing to serve on the Board and
a biographical sketch of the nominee. The Secretary shall mail or hand
deliver the submitted items to every Unit Owner together with the notice
of such meeting; and

\(2\) Nominations may be submitted from the floor at a meeting at which
the election is held for each vacancy on the Board for which no more
than one Person has been nominated by petition.

Section 3.3 [Meetings]{.underline}. Meetings of the Board shall be
conducted in accordance with the following:

\(a\) [Time and Location]{.underline}. The Board shall hold an annual
meeting within ten days following the Annual Meeting of the Association
for the purpose of electing officers, as more fully set forth in Article
4 hereof, and for any other purpose.

\(1\) In any event, the Board shall meet at least once each fiscal year
(in addition to the Annual Meeting of the Association), unless all
members of the Board shall waive such requirements as to a particular
meeting or meetings;

\(2\) The first such Board meeting shall be held promptly after the date
on which the Declaration is recorded;

\(3\) There shall be a meeting of the Board during the month of November
for the purpose of adopting the budget of the Association for the next
following fiscal year of the Association; and

\(4\) The President shall call any Board meeting requested by a majority
of the members of the Board for a date occurring not less than five nor
more than twenty days after receipt of such request. The President shall
designate the time and location of the Board meetings. No business shall
be transacted at Board meetings other than as specified in the notice
thereof.

\(b\) [Notice]{.underline}. Not less than forty-eight (48) hours prior
to the time of any Board meeting, a written notice stating the date,
time and place of such meeting shall be delivered, either by hand or by
mail or telegram, to each Board member at the address given to the Board
by such Board member for such purpose. Any Board member may waive notice
of a meeting or consent to any action of the Board without a meeting. An
Board member's attendance at a meeting shall constitute his waiver of
notice of such meeting.

\(c\) [Voting]{.underline}. The votes of the affairs of the Board
allocated to each Board Member shall be proportionate to the Allocated
Interest of the Unit Owner serving on the Board ("Allocated Board
Interests"). A majority of the aggregate vote shall bind the Board for
all purposes unless otherwise provided in the Declaration or these
Bylaws.

\(d\) [Quorum of the Board]{.underline}. At all meetings of the Board, a
majority of the aggregate Allocated Board Interests shall constitute a
quorum for the transaction of business, and the votes of the majority of
the aggregate vote shall constitute a decision of the Board, except
where approval by a greater vote is required by the Declaration, these
Bylaws or the Act. One or more members of the Board may participate in
and be counted for quorum purposes at any meetings by means of
conference telephone or similar communications equipment by means of
which all Persons participating in the meeting can hear each other.

\(e\) [Organization]{.underline}. Board meetings may be held under such
reasonable rules consistent with these Bylaws as the Board may
determine. The Board is hereby entitled to promulgate such rules. All
Unit Owners shall have the rights to attend and be heard, but not the
right to vote, at the Board meeting at which the fiscal year budget of
the Association shall be presented to the Board for adoption. The
Secretary of the Board shall give Unit Owners notice of such meeting,
accompanied by a copy of the proposed budget, in the manner provided in
[Section 2.2(c)]{.underline} hereof.

\(f\) [Conduct of Meetings]{.underline}. The President, or in the
President's absence or disqualification, the senior officer, of the
Board shall preside over all meetings of the Board, and the Secretary
shall keep a Minute Book of the Board meetings, recording therein all
resolutions adopted by the Board and a record of all transactions and
proceedings occurring at such meetings. The then current edition of
Robert's Rules of Order shall govern the conduct of the meeting of the
Board if and to the extent such rules are not in conflict with the
Declaration, these Bylaws or the Act.

\(g\) [Action Without a Meeting]{.underline}. Any action by the Board
required or permitted to be taken at any meeting may be taken without a
meeting if all of the members of the Board entitled to vote thereon
consent thereto in writing. Any such written consent shall be filed with
the minutes of the proceedings of the Board.

Section 3.4 [Resignation and Removal]{.underline}. Subject to the Board
Membership Requirements set forth in Section 3.2(a), at any regular or
special meeting of the Association duly called, any one or more of the
members of the Board may be removed with or without cause by Unit Owners
entitled to cast a majority of all votes in the Association and a
successor may then and there be elected to fill the vacancy thus
created. Any Unit Owner proposing removal of a Board Member shall give
notice thereof to the Secretary. Any member whose removal has been
proposed by a Unit Owner shall be given at least 10 days' notice by the
Secretary of the time, place and purpose of the meeting and shall be
given an opportunity to be heard at the meeting. A member of the Board
may resign at any time and shall be deemed to have resigned upon
transfer of title to his Land Unit.

Section 3.5 [Vacancies]{.underline}. Subject to the Board Membership
Requirements set forth in Section 3.2(a), any vacancy or vacancies on
the Board, whether caused by resignation, removal, death, adjudication
of incompetence, or an increase in size of the Board, shall be filled by
the Board with an interim appointee who shall serve until the next
Annual Meeting of the Association, at which time such vacancy may be
filled by the vote of more than fifty-one (51%) of the votes of the Unit
Owners; provided, however, that the Declarant shall have the right to
fill any vacancy created by the resignation, death, or adjudication of
incompetence of a member who had been appointed by the Declarant and had
not been elected by the Unit Owners. If the vacancy results from removal
by the Association, the election of a new member or members may be held
at the same meeting where such removal takes place, and notice of an
election for removal shall be considered notice of an election to fill
each vacancy so caused. The vote of more than fifty-one (51%) of the
Unit Owners present at such meeting in Person or by proxy shall cause
the postponement of the election to a later date, but if such vacancy is
not filled within sixty days after it occurs, the Board shall promptly
thereafter elect a replacement.

Section 3.6 [Compensation]{.underline}. No member of the Board shall
receive compensation for performing his duties solely as a member of the
Board unless such compensation is expressly authorized or approved by
unanimous vote of all Unit Owners, at any Annual or Special Meeting of
the Association.

Section 3.7 [Validity of Contracts with Interested Board
Members]{.underline}. No contract or other transaction between the
Association and one or more of its Board members or between the
Association and any corporation, firm or association in which one or
more of the Board members are directors or officers, or are financially
interested, shall be void or voidable because such Board member or
members are present at any meeting of the Board which authorized or
approved the contract or transaction or because his or their votes are
counted, if the circumstances specified in either of the following
subparagraphs exist:

\(a\) The fact that an Board member is also such a director or officer
or has such financial interest is disclosed or known to the Board and is
noted in the minutes thereof, and the Board authorizes, approves or
ratifies the contract or transaction in good faith by a vote sufficient
for the purpose without counting the vote or votes of such Board Member
or Member; or

\(b\) The contract or transaction is made in good faith and is not
unconscionable to the Association at the time is authorized, approved or
ratified.

Section 3.8 [Inclusion of Interested Board Members in a
Quorum]{.underline}. Any Board member holding such director or officer
position or having such financial interest in another corporation, firm
or association may be counted in determining the presence of a quorum at
a meeting of the Board or a committee thereof which authorizes, approves
or ratifies a contract or transaction of the type described in [Section
3.7]{.underline} hereof.

Section 3.9 [Powers of the Board]{.underline}.

\(a\) [Enumeration]{.underline}. The Board shall have all of the powers
and duties granted by the Act, the Declaration, these Bylaws and the
laws governing associations or both, subject only to approval by Unit
Owners when such is specifically required under the Act, the Declaration
or these Bylaws .

\(b\) [Limitation]{.underline}. Nothing in this Section or elsewhere in
these Bylaws shall be considered to grant to the Board or to the
officers of the Association any powers or duties which, by law, are
possessed by Unit Owners. Unless otherwise provided herein or in the
Declaration, the Board shall comply with the instructions of more than
fifty-one (51%) of the Unit Owners present, by remote means, in Person
or by proxy, as expressed in the resolution duly adopted at any annual
or special meeting of the Unit Owners.

\(c\) [Delegation of Powers; Managing Agent]{.underline}. The Board may
employ for the Condominium a managing agent at the compensation
established by the Board. The managing agent shall perform such duties
and services as the Board shall authorize, including, but not limited
to, all of the duties listed in the Act, the Declaration and these
Bylaws; provided, however, where a managing agent does not have the
power to act under the Act, the Declaration or these Bylaws, the
managing agent may act as an advisor or in an advisory capacity to the
Board. The Board may delegate to the managing agent all of the powers
granted to the Board by the Act and Declaration and these Bylaws
including but not limited to the following powers: (i) to adopt an
annual budget and any amendment thereto or to assess Common Expenses;
(ii) to adopt, repeal or amend rules and regulations; (iii) to designate
signatories on Association bank accounts; (iv) to borrow money on behalf
of the Association; (v) to acquire mortgages on Land Units; and (vi) to
assign Common Elements as Limited Common Elements. Any contract with the
managing agent must provide that it may be terminated with cause on no
more than thirty days' written notice and without cause on no more than
ninety days' written notice.

[ARTICLE 4]{.underline}

[OFFICERS]{.underline}

Section 4.1 [Election]{.underline}. At the first meeting of the Board,
and at every Annual Meeting of the Board thereafter, the Board members,
if a quorum is present, shall elect officers of the Association for the
following year, such officers to serve for a one-year term and until
their respective successors are elected. The officers to be elected are:
President, Secretary, Treasurer and such other officers as the Board
shall determine. Each officer may serve an unlimited number of terms as
long as such member or officer continues to be re-elected to the Board.
Any member may hold two offices simultaneously, except that the
President shall not hold any other office.

Section 4.2 [Duties]{.underline}. The duties of the officers shall be as
follows:

\(a\) [President]{.underline}. The President shall be a chief executive
officer of the Association and the chairperson of the Board. The
President shall be responsible for implementing the decisions of the
Board and in that capacity shall direct, supervise, coordinate and have
general control over the affairs of the Association and the Board,
subject to the limitations of the laws of the State of Rhode Island, the
Condominium documents and the actions of the Board. The President shall
have the power to sign checks and other documents on behalf of the
Association and the Board, or both, with or without the signatures of
any other officers as may be determined by the Board. The President
shall preside at all meetings of either body at which he is in
attendance and shall be a member of all committees. If the President is
absent from such meetings, the senior officer of the Association present
at such meeting shall preside, and in the absence of any officer, the
body holding the meeting shall elect a Person to preside. If the Board
so provides, the President also shall have any or all of the powers and
duties ordinarily attributable to the chief executive officer of a
corporation domiciled in Rhode Island.

\(b\) [Secretary]{.underline}. Unless otherwise determined by the Board,
the Secretary shall keep or cause to be kept all records (or copies
thereof if the original documents are not available to the Association)
of the Association and the Board and shall have the authority to affix
the seal of the Association to any documents requiring such seal. The
Secretary shall give or cause to be given all notices as required by
law, the Declaration or these Bylaws, shall take and keep or cause to be
taken and kept minutes of all meetings of the Association, the Board and
all committees, and shall take and keep or cause to be taken and kept in
the Association's office a record of the names and addresses of all Unit
Owners as well as copies of the Declaration, the Plats, these Bylaws and
the Rules and Regulations, all of which shall be available at the office
of the Association for inspection by Unit Owners or prospective Unit
Owners during normal business hours and for distribution to them at such
reasonable charges (if any) as may be set from time to time by the
Board. The Secretary shall keep or cause to be kept the register of
holders of mortgages. The Secretary shall also perform all duties and
have such other powers as are ordinarily attributable to the Secretary
of a corporation domiciled in Rhode Island.

\(c\) [Treasurer]{.underline}. Unless otherwise determined by the Board,
the Treasurer shall have the charge and custody of, and be responsible
for, all funds and securities of the Association, shall deposit or cause
to be deposited all such funds in such depositories as the Board may
direct, shall keep or cause to be kept correct and complete accounts and
records of all financial transactions of the Association and the Board
and shall submit or cause to be submitted to the Board and the
Association such reports thereof as the Act, the Declaration, the Board,
or these Bylaws may from time to time require. Such records shall
include, without limitation, chronological listings of all receipts and
expenditures on account of the Common Elements, Limited Common Elements
and each Land Unit, the amount of each assessment for Common Expenses
and expenses assessable to individual Land Units, if any, and the amount
paid and the amounts due on such assessments. Such records shall specify
and itemize the maintenance, repair and replacement expenses relating to
the Common Elements and the Limited Common Elements and any other
expenses incurred by the Association. The foregoing financial records
shall be kept at the Association's office and shall be available there
for inspection by Unit Owners or prospective Unit Owners during normal
business hours. The Treasurer shall, upon request, provide any Person
who shall have entered into a written agreement to purchase a Land Unit
with a written statement of the information required to be provided by
the Association pursuant to [Sections 36.1-3.16(h)]{.underline} and of
the Act. The Treasurer shall also perform such duties and have such
powers as are ordinarily attributable to the Treasurer of a corporation
domiciled in Rhode Island.

Section 4.3 [Compensation]{.underline}. The officers of the Board shall
serve without compensation for their services in such capacity unless
such compensation is expressly authorized or approved by unanimous vote
of the Unit Owners, at any Annual or Special Meeting of the Association.

Section 4.4 [Resignation and Removal]{.underline}. Any officer may
resign at any time by written notice to the Board, such resignation to
become effective at the next Board meeting. Any officer who ceases to be
a member of the Board for any reason also shall be deemed to have
resigned or been removed, ipso facto, from any Board office he may have
held. Any officer may be removed from his office at any time by a
majority vote of the Board whenever in the judgment of the Board members
the interests of the Association will be best served thereby, or by the
vote of the Association with or without cause, in the same manner as set
forth in the removal of Board members in Section 3.4 hereof.

Section 4.5 [Vacancies]{.underline}. Vacancies caused by resignation or
removal of officers or the creation of new offices may be filled by a
majority vote of the Board members if the vacancy resulted from action
of the Board, provided however that each Unit Owner shall have a
representation on the Board at all times. If, however, the vacancy
resulted from action by the Association, such vacancy shall be filled in
the same manner as set forth in Section 3.5 hereof for filling Board
vacancies.

[ARTICLE 5]{.underline}

[COMMON EXPENSES: BUDGETS]{.underline}

Section 5.1 [Fiscal Year]{.underline}. The fiscal year of the
Association shall be January 1 through December 31; provided, however,
that the first partial fiscal year shall begin upon the recordation of
the Declaration.

Section 5.2 [Preparation and Approval of Budget]{.underline}.

\(a\) [Adoption]{.underline}. On or before the first day of December of
each year the Board shall adopt an annual budget for the Association
containing an estimate of the total amount considered necessary to pay
the cost of insurance, maintenance, management, operation, repair and
replacement of the Common Elements and those parts of the Land Units as
to which it is the responsibility of the Board to maintain, repair and
replace, and the cost of wages, materials, insurance premiums, services,
supplies and other expenses that may be declared to be Common Expenses
by the Act, the Declaration, these Bylaws or a resolution of the
Association and which will be required during the ensuing fiscal year
for the administration, operation, maintenance and repair of the
Property and the rendering to the Unit Owners of all related services.
Such budget shall also include such reasonable amounts as the Board
considers necessary to provide working capital, a general operating
reserve and reserves for contingencies and replacements.

\(b\) [Available for Inspection]{.underline}. On or before the fifteenth
day of November, the Board shall make the budget available for
inspection at the Association office and shall send to each Unit Owner a
copy of the budget in a reasonably itemized form that sets forth the
amount of Common Expenses. Such budget shall constitute the basis for
determining each Unit Owner's assessments for Common Expenses of the
Association and shall automatically take effect at the beginning of the
fiscal year for which it is adopted, subject to [Section
5.8]{.underline} below.

\(c\) [Reasonable Efforts]{.underline}. The Board shall make reasonable
efforts to meet the deadlines set forth above, but compliance with such
deadlines shall not be a condition precedent to the effectiveness of any
budget.

Section 5.3 [Assessment and Payment of Common Expense]{.underline}.

\(a\) [Common Expenses]{.underline}. The Board shall calculate the
monthly assessments for Common Expenses against each Land Unit by
dividing (i) the total amount of the estimated funds required for the
operation of the Property set forth in the budget adopted by the Board
for the fiscal year in question, after deducting any income expected to
be received from sources other than common expense assessments by the
number of Land Units in the Condominium and (ii) dividing the result by
the number of calendar months in such fiscal year, and applying the
Allocated Interest applicable to each Land Unit. Such assessments,
payable in monthly installments, shall be due and payable in twelve (12)
equal monthly installments by each Unit Owner beginning thirty (30) days
after a statement for such expenses has been submitted, and shall be a
lien against the Unit Owner's Unit as provided in the Act and
Declaration; provided however, that such lien shall be subordinate such
lien to any Lender of a Land Unit. Within ninety days after the end of
each fiscal year, the Board shall prepare and deliver to each Unit Owner
and to each record holder of a first mortgage on a Land Unit who has
registered an address with the Secretary an itemized accounting of the
Common Expenses and funds received during such fiscal year less
expenditures actually incurred and sums paid into reserves. Any net
shortage with regard to Common Expenses, after application of such
reserves as the Board may determine, shall be assessed promptly against
the Unit Owners in equal shares and shall be payable as a Special
Assessment, in such manner as the Board may determine.

\(b\) [Reserves]{.underline}. The Board shall have the option to build
up and maintain reasonable reserves for working capital, operations,
contingencies and replacements. Extraordinary expenditures not
originally included in the annual budget which may become necessary
during the year may be charged first against such reserves. If the
reserves are deemed to be inadequate for any reason, including
nonpayment of any Unit Owner's assessments, the Board may at any time
levy further assessments for Common Expenses which shall be assessed
against the Unit Owners or in accordance with their Allocated Interests
and shall be payable as a Special Assessment, in such manner as the
Board may determine.

Section 5.4 [Further Assessments]{.underline}. The Board shall serve
notice on all Unit Owners of any further assessments pursuant to
[Section 5.3(a)]{.underline} or [5.3(b)]{.underline} or otherwise as
permitted or required by the Act, the Declaration and these bylaws by a
statement in writing giving the amount and reasons therefore, and such
further assessments, unless otherwise specified in the notice, shall
become effective with the next Monthly Assessment which is due more than
ten days after the delivery of such notice of further assessments. All
Unit Owners so assessed shall be obligated to pay the amount of such
Monthly Assessments. Such assessments shall be a lien as of the
effective date as set forth in the preceding [Sections
5.3(a)]{.underline} and [5.3(b)]{.underline} above, and shall be
subordinate.

Section 5.5 [Initial Budget]{.underline}. At or prior to the time
assessment of Common Expenses commences, the Board shall adopt the
budget, as described in this Article, for a period commencing on the
date the Board determines that assessments shall begin and ending on the
last day of the fiscal year during which such commencement date occurs.
Assessments shall be levied and become a lien against the Unit Owners
during such period as is provided in [Section 5.3]{.underline} above.
Until such time as the Board takes such action, the initial Budget
established by the Declarant in the condominium documents shall apply
and shall be the basis for all assessments.

Section 5.6 [Effect of Failure to Prepare or Adopt Budget]{.underline}.
The failure or delay of the Board to prepare or adopt a budget for any
fiscal year shall not constitute a waiver or release in any manner of a
Unit Owner's obligation to pay his allocable share of the Common
Expenses as herein provided whenever the same shall be determined and,
in the absence of any annual budget or adjusted budget, each Unit Owner
shall continue to pay each Monthly Assessment at the rate established
for the previous fiscal year until the new annual or adjusted budget
shall have been adopted.

Section 5.7 [Accounts; Audits]{.underline}. All sums collected by the
Board with respect to assessments against the Unit Owners or from any
other source may be commingled into a single fund. All book and records
of the Association shall be kept in accordance with good and accepted
accounting practices, and the same shall be audited at least once each
year by an independent accountant retained by the Board.

Section 5.8 [Rejection of Budget; Limitations on Expenditures and
Borrowing]{.underline}. Anything herein to the contrary notwithstanding,
the Association, by a vote of more than eighty percent of all votes in
the Association, may reject any budget , capital expenditure or
borrowing approved by the Board within thirty days after approval by the
Board.

Section 5.9 [Statement of Common Expenses]{.underline}. The Board shall
promptly provide any Unit Owner, contract purchaser or proposed
mortgagee so requesting the same in writing with a written statement of
all unpaid assessments for Common Expenses due from such Unit Owner. The
Board may impose a reasonable charge for the preparation of such
statement to cover the cost of its preparation, to the extent permitted
by the Act.

[ARTICLE 6]{.underline}

[REPAIR OR RECONSTRUCTION]{.underline}

Section 6.1 [Restoration of Property Out of Common Expense
Fund]{.underline}. Damage to or destruction of the Common Elements shall
be promptly repaired and restored by the Association in accordance with
the provisions of Article 15 of the Declaration and Section 36.1-3.13(b)
of the Act. The Board shall be responsible for accomplishing the full
repair or reconstruction which shall be paid out of the Common Expense
fund. The disbursements of funds for such repair or reconstruction
shall, at the option of the Board, be made only as the work progresses.
Unit Owners may apply the proceeds from their individual property
insurance policies, if any, to the share of such Common Expense as may
be assessed to them, subject to the Parking Area Common Expense
Calculation. The Board shall be responsible for restoring the Property
only to substantially the same condition as it was immediately prior to
the damage, and each Unit Owner shall personally assume the additional
expense of any improvements to his Land Unit to restore it beyond such
conditions. If any physical changes are made to any restored Common
Elements, which renders inaccurate the Plats which are then of record,
the Board shall record amended Plats showing such changes.

[ARTICLE 7]{.underline}

[SEPARATE REAL ESTATE TAXES]{.underline}

Section 7.1 [Assessments Against Individual Land Units]{.underline}. In
the event that, commencing with the taxable period during which occurs
the first conveyance of a Land Unit to a Person other than the
Declarant, real estate taxes are not separately assessed against each
Unit Owner, but rather are assessed against the Property as a whole,
then each Unit Owner (including the Declarant, as to the Units then
owned by it and as to any portion of the Property as to which the
Declarant has reserved Development Rights) shall pay his proportionate
share thereof in accordance with his respective Allocated Interest in
the Common Elements.

[ARTICLE 8]{.underline}

[AMENDMENTS]{.underline}

Section 8.1 [General Requirements; Consent of Declarant or Holders
of]{.underline} [Mortgages; Curative Amendments to Bylaws]{.underline}.
Except as otherwise provided in any one or more of these Bylaws, the
Declaration or the Act, these Bylaws may only be amended by the vote of
all of the Unit Owners; provided, however, that if such amendment shall
make any change which would have a material effect upon any rights,
privileges, powers and options of the Declarant, such amendment shall
require the joinder of the Declarant; and further provide that no
amendment seeking (i) to abandon, partition, subdivide, encumber, sell
or transfer any portion of the Common Elements, or (ii) to abandon or
terminate the condominium form of ownership of the Property except as
otherwise provided in the Declaration, shall be effective without the
prior written approval of all mortgagees of the Land Units.
Additionally, if any amendment is necessary in the judgment of the Board
to cure any ambiguity, or minor defect or to correct or supplement any
provision of these Bylaws that is defective, missing or inconsistent
with any other provision hereof, or with the Act or the Declaration,
then at any time and from time to time the Board, acting through the
President, may effect an appropriate corrective amendment without the
approval of the Unit Owners or the holders of any liens on all or any
part of the Property.

Section 8.2 [Amendments to the Declaration]{.underline}. The Declaration
may be amended pursuant to the provisions of the Act and the
Declaration. The President is empowered to prepare and execute any
amendments to the Declaration on behalf of the Association, and the
Secretary or any Assistant Secretary is empowered to attest and record
any such amendment on behalf of the Association.

[ARTICLE 9]{.underline}

[GENERAL PROVISIONS]{.underline}

Section 9.1 [Severability]{.underline}. The provisions of these Bylaws
shall be deemed independent and severable, and invalidity, partial
invalidity or unenforceability of any provision or portion hereof shall
not affect the validity or enforceability of any other provision or
portion thereof unless the deletion of such invalid or unenforceable
provision shall destroy the uniform plan for development and operation
of the condominium project which the Declaration (including the Plats)
and these Bylaws are intended to create.

Section 9.2 [Conflicts]{.underline}. The Act and the Declaration shall
control in the event of any conflict between the provisions thereof and
the provisions of these Bylaws. The Act, the Declaration and these
Bylaws shall control in the case of any conflict between the provisions
thereof and the provisions of the Rules and Regulations.

Section 9.3 [Notices]{.underline}. All notices or other communications
required or permitted under these Bylaws shall be in writing and shall
be deemed to have been given when personally delivered or on the second
business day after the day on which mailed by certified mail, return
receipt requested, postage prepaid (or otherwise as the Act may permit),
(i) if to the Unit Owner at the single address which the Unit Owner
shall designate in writing and file with the Secretary, or (ii) if to
the Association, the Board or to the managing agent, at the principal
office of the Association and to the managing agent, or at such other
address as shall be designated by notice in writing to the Unit Owners
pursuant to this Section. If a Land Unit is owned by more than one
Person, each such Person who so designates a single address in writing
to the Secretary shall be entitled to receive all notices hereunder.

Section 9.4 [Headings]{.underline}. The headings preceding the various
sections of these Bylaws are intended solely for the convenience of
readers of the Bylaws and in no way define, limit or describe the scope
of these Bylaws or the intent of any provision thereof.

Section 9.5 [Gender]{.underline}. The use of the masculine gender in
these Bylaws shall be deemed to include the feminine and neuter genders,
and the use of the singular shall be deemed to include the plural, and
vice versa, whenever the context so requires.
