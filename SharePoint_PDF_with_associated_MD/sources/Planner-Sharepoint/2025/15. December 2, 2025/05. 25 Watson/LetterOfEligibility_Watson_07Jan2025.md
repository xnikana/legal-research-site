## Letter of Eligibility: Cottages on Freemont, 25 Watson Avenue, Plat 7, Lot 4, Barrington

Dear Mr. Spinella:

We have received your application for a letter of eligibility to pursue a Comprehensive Permit in the Town of Barrington. You have represented that you propose an Eighteen (18) unit development that would be comprised of:

- a. 12 (12) two-bedroom, two-bathroom single-family homes, of which five (5) of the homes will be marketed and sold to households earning up to a maximum of 120% of the Area Median Income ("AMI").
- b. Six (6) vacant lots are to be sold for the development of market-rate homes.

The affordable units are currently priced for sale as 100% cash purchases for \$457,725.00 (four-hundred fifty-seven thousand seven hundred twenty-five dollars and 00/100). However, the final sales price will be based on the maximum 120% area median price for the end borrower, who may or may not pay cash.

Based on the current AMI, the Freddie Mac Primary Mortgage Market Survey 30-year fixed-rate mortgage average interest rate (6.91%) as of the date of this letter, anticipated taxes, insurance, mortgage insurance costs, the maximum sales price for households earning 120% of the AMI in Barrington is \$300,911 for a two-bedroom affordable condominium unit. The maximum permitted sales price may change in the future due to changes in any of the above-noted purchase price inputs.

In addition to requirements for affordability and monitoring, under R.I.G.L. 45-53, the construction or rehabilitation of qualifying low-and-moderate income housing units must be assisted by a state, federal or municipal subsidy program.

After a preliminary review of the plans and financial assumptions for the proposed sale of six (6) market-rate lots, seven (7) market-rate units, and five (5) affordable units to households earning 120% of the AMI, all to be located at 25 Watson Avenue, Barrington, Plat 7-Lot 4, RIHousing has determined that:

• This project appears to be eligible for a municipal subsidy under R.I.G.L. Title 45 Towns and Cities, Chapter 45-53 Low- and Moderate-Income Housing, Section 45-53-4 Procedure for approval of construction of low-or-moderate-income housing.

Cottages at Freemont December 9, 2024

- In conformance with R.I.G.L. 42-55-5.3, RIHousing performed an on-site inspection of the site and has reviewed pertinent information supplied by the applicant.
- The updated project proforma was reviewed and the project appears to be feasible based on projected overall development costs and potential sources.
- The applicant, FJS Associates, Ltd., has provided evidence of site control.

Based upon the review of the submitted information and supporting materials and based on the representations in your request, FJS Associates, Ltd. is eligible to pursue a Comprehensive Permit application in the town of Barrington to develop 25 Watson Avenue, Plat 7, Lot 4.

Modifications to the development proposal resulting from local review do not require a revised letter of eligibility.

Thank you for committing to providing affordable housing opportunities to low- and moderate-income Rhode Island families.
