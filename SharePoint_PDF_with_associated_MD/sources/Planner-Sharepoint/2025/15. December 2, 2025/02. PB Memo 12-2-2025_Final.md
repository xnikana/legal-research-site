#### **TRC Recommendation:**

**MOTION: Member Durham moved to recommend that the Planning Board approve the project for 25 Watson Avenue, subject to rethinking the turning radius access points for deliveries on parcel 6 driveway and installation of a USPS certified mailbox cluster on Freemont Avenue for parcel 6. Seconded by Member Bardell. Motion carried (4-0).**

#### **Staff Recommended Stipulations:**

- **1.** For Parcels 1 and 5, the plans need to be revised to include an area of no disturbance buffer, no less than 30' in width running along the northern property boundary adjacent lots AP 07, Lot 095 (Disanto) and AP 07, Lot 097 (Butera).
  - The building envelopes for these two parcels shall be shifted southerly and shown on a revised site plan to create additional distance between future buildings on these two parcels and lots AP 07, Lot 095 and AP 07, 097.
  - The draft deeds for Parcels 1 and 5 need to include language indicating the "no disturbance buffer" and the required location of the building envelope.
  - The above information shall be submitted as part of any Final Plan application submittal for approval by the Administrative Officer in collaboration with the Town Solicitor.
- **2.** Notwithstanding 1. above, there is some existing encroachment by the property owner of AP 07, Lot 095 (currently Distanto) onto proposed Parcel 5. To address this matter, the Town shall prepare a permanent easement for recording in the Town's Land Evidence Records, allowing the current encroachment to remain in its current condition but without any additional encroachment being allowed. Such easement language shall be drafted by the Town Solicitor in collaboration with the owner of AP 07, Lot 095, or their designee, and submitted as part of any Final Plan application submittal for approval by the Administrative Officer. Also, the draft deed for Parcel 5 shall include language acknowledging such permanent easement.

![](02. PB Memo 12-2-2025_Final_attachments/_page_1_Picture_11.jpeg)

**3.** Notation on the plans shall be included indicating that at least two street trees shall be planted and maintained by the owners of the single-family parcels proposed to be sold for private property construction (i.e., Parcels 1-5 and 7). The draft deed language for each of those parcels shall include language that the species selection for said trees shall be approved by the Administrative Officer in consultation with the Town Tree Warden. The deed language also shall indicate that each tree caliper shall be no less than 2.5", be nursery grown, be maintained by the property owner for a period no less than a two-year period from the date of planting according to the detailed specifications included on the Landscape Plan, and be replaced within that two-year period should any of the trees be dead or diseased as determined by the Town Tree Warden.

The revised plans and deed language shall be submitted as part of any Final Plan submittal for approval by the Administrative Officer. Also, the required street trees shall be planted on the applicable parcels prior to the issuance of any Certificate of Occupancy for each respective parcel.

- **4.** For Parcel 8 public open space parcel, the plans and detailed specifications shall be revised to include a driveway entrance off Watson Ave leading to a small parking area with space for no greater than 4 vehicles, including 1 handicapped spot appropriately designed, configured, and including MUTCD-acceptable signage. The revised plans and detailed specifications shall be submitted as part of any Final Plan submittal for approval by the Administrative Officer in consultation with the DPW Director.
- **5.** For Parcel 8 public open space parcel, on the Watson Ave side of the parcel, street trees shall be proposed on a revised site plan with detailed specifications for approval by the Administrative Officer in consultation with the Town Tree Warden. The trees shall be of a caliper no less than 2.5" and shall be planted before issuance of the last Certificate of Occupancy for the Cottage units on Parcel 6. The Town shall be responsible for replacing said trees shall any be dead or diseased, as determined by the Town Tree Warden, within two years' planting of the trees.
- **6.** For Parcel 8 public open space parcel, a draft deed needs to be prepared by the Town. Such draft deed shall include language indicating that the parcel is a public open space parcel and not just for the benefit of the residents of parcels 1-7. Also, the draft deed shall state that the use of the park shall be limited to passive recreational activity during the hours from dawn until dusk or as otherwise established by the Town Council but not to be earlier than 5:30 am or later than 9:00 pm on any given day of the week. Also, the Town shall install a sign at the entryway to the parcel at least indicating, "Parking for Park Use Only; No Beach Parking; Dogs Must be Leashed".
- **7.** For Parcel 6 Cottage units parcel, the Town needs to be more specific to explain how solid waste (e.g., trash, recycling, curbside compost) will be collected. It is preferable that curbside pickup be conducted near the Cottages' internal driveways' intersection with

However, any dispute between the property owners and any adjacent property owner to 25 Watson Ave or property owners after the sale of individual parcels occurs that may be a party to any such temporary construction easement(s) shall be a civil matter to be settled between the parties to the easement(s) and not by the Town (except for any Town-owned parcel that may be involved).

#### **Required Findings:**

- a. The proposed development is consistent with local needs as identified in the community's affordable housing plan, and it will offer additional LMI units that will count toward the Town and state's 10% affordable housing goal.
- b. With the granting of the waivers requested and with adherence to the conditions of any approval granted herein, the proposed development is in compliance with the standards and provisions of Barrington's Town Code and, further, that no undue adverse local impact will result by the relief granted nor will the conditions imposed with any approval granted herein outweigh the state and local need for low- and moderate-income housing.
- c. The low- and moderate-income housing units proposed are integrated into the parcel intended to house such units; are compatible in scale, meaning that: (1) The size of the low- and moderate-income units are not less than seventy-five percent (75%) of the size of the market rate units; (2) the affordable units are of similar architectural style to the market rate units within the project so that the exterior of the units look like an integrated neighborhood with similar rooflines, window patterns, materials and colors; and (3) the affordable units will be built and occupied in a proportional manner with the construction and occupancy of the market rate units on Parcel 6.
- d. With adherence to the conditions of any approval granted by the Planning Board herein, there will be no significant negative impacts on the health and safety of current or future residents of the community, in areas including, but not limited to, safe circulation of pedestrian and vehicular traffic, provision of emergency services, sewerage disposal, availability of potable water, adequate surface water run-off, and the preservation of natural, historical, or cultural features.
- e. The proposed development and lots as configured will have adequate and permanent physical access to a public street in accordance with the requirements of § 45-23-60(a)(5).
- f. With adherence to the conditions of any approval granted by the Planning Board herein, the proposed development will not result in the creation of individual lots with any physical constraints to development that building on those lots according to pertinent regulations and building standards would be impracticable, except for Parcel 8 which intentionally is being created only as permanent open space or permanently reserved for a public purpose on the approved, recorded plans.
