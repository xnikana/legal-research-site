**SUBJECT: Planning Board Review of School Construction Project**

As you know, our office has previously advised the Town that public school construction projects are not required to undergo review by the Planning Board, and the Town has not reviewed recent school construction projects such as the Middle School. This memorandum sets forth the reasoning underlying our revised opinion: that state law requires school construction projects, including the current project, to be reviewed by the Planning Board under the framework applicable to a major land development project.

Sec. 185-4 of the Zoning Ordinance, entitled 'Compliance Required,' provides an exemption from the Zoning Ordinance for Town-owned land and municipal uses. ("The Town of Barrington itself, both as to land owned by the Town and to governmental activity and use, shall be exempt from the provisions of this chapter.") Based on this provision of the Ordinance, and on the Town's past practice, we advised former Planning Director Teresa Crean last year that the school construction project did not require review by the Planning Board. We affirmed this opinion in February 2025.

However, based on recent caselaw and recent statutory changes, questions have been raised regarding whether the exemption from the Zoning Ordinance can be construed to exempt municipal land and activities from review under the Town's Land Development & Subdivision Regulations, as provided by the Rhode Island Development Review Act (the "Act"). The relevant provision of the Act, § 45- 23-32(19) defines a "land development project" as follows: "A project in which one or more lots, tracts, or parcels of land or a portion thereof are developed or redeveloped as a coordinated site for one or more uses, units, or structures, including but not limited to, planned development or cluster development for residential, commercial, institutional, recreational, open space, or mixed uses." School construction projects are encompassed within this definition, as they constitute institutional development projects. Notably, the Act defines project categories and associated requirements for review and approval that are separate from any review that may be required under a local zoning ordinance.

Further, the definition of a "major land development project," set forth in the Act at § 45-23-32(19)(ii) states in pertinent part that the definition encompasses "a land development project that exceeds the thresholds for a minor land development project as set forth in this section and local ordinance or regulation." Reviewing the definition of a "minor land development project," it is not apparent that a school construction project would fit within the enumerated categories of § 45-23-32(19)(i). Significantly, school construction projects in Bristol (Mt. Hope High School) and South Kingstown (South Kingstown High School) are currently undergoing review by their respective Planning Boards as major land development projects. Notwithstanding Barrington's recent history of allowing school construction projects to proceed without any requirement for Planning Board review, our review of this issue has caused us to revise our opinion and advise the Town that the school construction project requires review and approval as a major land development project.

The process by which the Planning Board reviews major land development projects is set forth in the Act at § 45-23-39. A pre-application meeting is required, followed by a public hearing at the next stage of review. The administrative officer may combine the master plan and preliminary plan stages of review, provided that the application requirements for both stages have been satisfied. The final plan stage is administrative. An advisory recommendation from the Technical Review Committee will also be required prior to the public hearing before the Planning Board. Because of the exemption in the Zoning Ordinance for governmental activity and use, no dimensional restrictions such as setback/lot coverage will apply to the school construction project. The Planning Board's review will focus only on the required findings for approval set forth in state law and mirrored in our local regulations.

To get the ball rolling on this process, the project team should first request a pre-application meeting so that Town and School Department staff can discuss any areas of concern. We are cognizant that this opinion will add an additional layer of regulation to this project, which has been and is being reviewed by the Building Committee, School Committee, and Department of Education, in addition to other reviewers. We are confident that the Planning Board and Town staff will work with the School Department administration and project team to ensure an orderly, thorough and expeditious review for the benefit of all stakeholders.

Please let us know if you have any questions.
