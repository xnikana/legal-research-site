## **M E M O R A N D U M**

**TO: Herbert A. Durfee, III, Director of Planning, Building, and Resilience**

**FROM: Amy H. Goins, Assistant Town Solicitor**

**DATE: May 28, 2025**

**SUBJECT: Proposed Synthetic Turf Field at Barrington High School – Planning Board** 

**Review Not Required** 

This memo follows our previous memo dated May 13, 2025, regarding Planning Board review of the school construction project. As you know, I shared my opinion that Planning Board review of the larger school construction project was required because the larger project qualifies as a major land development project under state law. This memo is provided to memorialize and confirm my opinion that the synthetic turf field at BHS does not require review by the Planning Board.

As we've discussed, a recent Superior Court decision involving a proposed synthetic turf field in Burrillville has prompted questions regarding potential implications for the BHS turf field. However, the Burrillville decision does not establish a statewide rule that all proposed synthetic turf fields require review by municipal planning boards. The Burrillville decision was based on the specifics of the Burrillville Zoning Ordinance, was rendered after an evidentiary hearing, and does not even represent a final decision in the case, which remains pending.

Turning to Barrington, the exemption for Town-owned land and municipal uses in our Zoning Ordinance, at Sec. 185-4, remains valid, meaning that a school construction project will require Planning Board review only if review is required under our Land Development & Subdivision Regulations pursuant to state law. The replacement of an existing natural grass field with a synthetic turf field does not, in my opinion, trigger any requirement for Planning Board review and approval. It is wholly different in scope and scale than the the larger school construction project, which involves alterations to several schools at different sites in Town. This larger project falls within the ambit of a "major land development project" under state law and therefore requires review and approval by the Planning Board. By contrast, the turf field project does not qualify as either a major or minor land development project. Further, development plan review is not required and even if it were, the exemption for municipal uses would prevail.
