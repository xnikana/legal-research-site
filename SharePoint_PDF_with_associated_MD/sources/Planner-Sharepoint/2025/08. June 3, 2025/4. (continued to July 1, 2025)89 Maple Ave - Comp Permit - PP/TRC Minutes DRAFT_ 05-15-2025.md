# **BARRINGTON TECHNICAL REVIEW COMMITTEE**

## **4 pm Thursday May 15, 2025**

Council Chamber, Barrington Town Hall

## MEETING MINUTES

#### **1. Call to Order**

The meeting was called to order by Chair Durfee at 4:00 PM.

### **2. Roll Call and Determination of Quorum**

*Present:* Chair/Administrative Officer, Herb Durfee III; Lawrence Bacher, Jeff Bardell, Jordan Durham

**3. Discuss and Act: Comprehensive Permit for 89 Maple Avenue, AP 23, Lot 58, Business District. Proposes converting the building from a mixed-use residential/commercial to a two-family residential dwelling. Additions to the building will be part of the conversion. The conversion will consist of a market rate unit on the first floor and an affordable unit on the second. The application seeks both dimensional and use waivers.** 

*Present: Attorney Paul Ryan, Son of applicant; Anthony Mancino*

Chair Durfee gave a brief description of the application and welcomed the applicant and attorney for the applicant to address the Committee.

Attorney Paul Ryan introduced himself as the applicant's representative, accompanied by Anthony Mancino, who is the son of the applicants and has worked in an architectural firm for 25 years. Mr. Ryan explained that both Al Mancino and his wife had recent surgeries, which affected their ability to attend.

Mr. Mancino provided an overview of the existing building, noting that the first floor was already partially residential and commercial, with a residential apartment in the rear. He expressed the desire to convert the first floor entirely to residential to accommodate his parents, emphasizing the property's long history—owned by the family for nearly a century. He commented on the limitations of the small parking lot, especially after sidewalk improvements reduced available parking and complicated delivery access for commercial use. He argued that a fully residential use was more appropriate and in better alignment with neighboring properties, citing vacancies among commercial properties along Maple Avenue.

Mr. Mancino confirmed that ramp access exists, and the proposed first-floor bedroom would be level with the grade to facilitate wheelchair access and explained that emergency egress would be straightforward via sliding doors.

Attorney Ryan addressed stormwater management considerations, noting that the property is in an area not requiring a formal stormwater permit, but suggested obtaining informal confirmation from DEM to ensure no additional stormwater runoff would occur.

Chair Durfee asked the Committee if they had any questions for Mr. Mancino.

Member Bacher raised concerns about the lack of grading information on the plans. Mr. Mancino responded by referencing the existing survey plan, which included grade lines, and clarified that no significant grading changes were planned due to the flat terrain. Member Bacher inquired about encroachment on green space; Mr. Mancino confirmed there would be none, only modifications to existing paving, with repairs as needed.

Member Bacher also questioned the drainage and stormwater runoff, emphasizing the importance of ensuring no negative impact on neighboring properties. Mr. Mancino explained that water currently drains toward the street storm drain and that the project would not alter this flow.

Member Bacher noted that the width of the sidewalk and ramp might not meet ADA standards, with a minimum width of 42 inches. Mr. Mancino acknowledged the concern and indicated they would adjust plans accordingly.

The Committee discussed trash storage, with Member Bacher advised screening from the street, such as a fence or trellis, to comply with regulations. Mr. Mancino agreed to relocate the receptacles to the rear of the building for better screening.

Member Bacher pointed out unresolved issues with elevation details, especially concerning the water table note and the grade change from front to back. Mr. Mancino assured the Committee that these details would be addressed and clarified in revised plans.

The Committee also discussed the existing side yard width, the proposed ramp, and the need for compliance with ADA guidelines.

Member Durham inquired about the eligibility letter from Rhode Island Housing, noting it was missing from the packet and raised questions about the designation of a monitoring agent for the affordable unit, emphasizing that income calculations should follow state and county guidelines, which are lower than Barrington's median income. Mr. Mancino confirmed that the second-floor unit would be deed-restricted for affordability and that he and his wife would qualify based on income. The Committee agreed that a letter of eligibility from RI Housing and confirmation of the monitoring agent should be submitted before the Planning Board's June meeting.

Chair Durfee reminded the applicants to clarify the waiver requests and ensure all permits, including State and Federal, are obtained. Chair Durfee also emphasized the importance of aligning the site plan, elevations, and specifications, especially regarding grading, walkways, and trash screening.

Chair Durfee explained the Board's conditions for a motion and asked for a member of the Board to make the motion.

**MOTION: Member Bardell, seconded by Member Durham, moved to recommend to the Planning Board the Preliminary Plan for the Comprehensive Permit for 89 Maple Avenue** 

#### **with the following considerations:**

- **1. Clarify the waivers sought and submit documentation accordingly.**
- **2. Obtain all applicable federal and state permits, including stormwater management if required, addressing the impact on neighboring properties.**
- **3. Revise elevations on sheet three to match site plans and address grading issues.**
- **4. Confirm and submit the letter of eligibility from Rhode Island Housing along with monitoring agent designation.**
- **5. Ensure that walkways and ramps meet ADA standards.**
- **6. Move trash receptacles to the rear of the building and provide screening in accordance with regulations.**

**The motion carried unanimously.**

### **4. Adjourn**

By a unanimous consensus of the Committee, the meeting adjourned at 4:35 PM.
