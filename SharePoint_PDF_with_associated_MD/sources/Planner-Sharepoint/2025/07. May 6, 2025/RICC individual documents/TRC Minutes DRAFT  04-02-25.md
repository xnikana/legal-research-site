#### **3. Discuss and Act: Citizens Bank – Backlit Sign Proposal**

*Present: Heather Dudko, National Sign Corp.*

Chair Durfee summarized the application and the request for a new backlit sign on the existing Citizen's Bank location at 965 County Road. Dudko explained that the letters themselves will be opaque with a backlight. **4. Discuss and Act: Unified Development Review – Revised Special Use Permit and Master Plan. Rhode Island Country Club, Inc., 150 Nayatt Road, AP 06, Lot 001 (Parcel for Proposed Development) – Demolition of existing storage cart barn, accessory sheds, equipment building, and concrete pads. Proposing a new cart barn, tennis court, multiuse building and relocated paddle ball court. Resurface existing paved parking area and add additional spaces southwest of the cart barn. Installation of stormwater best management practices, utilities, and associated landscaping**

*Present: Joe Malo, Narragansett Engineering* 

Chair Durfee welcomed Joe Malo of Narragansett Engineering, presenting for the Rhode Island County Club's application for Unified Development Review and asked him to summarize the project. Malo gave an overview of the project. Malo explained they aim to relocate the paddle ball court further north, remove worn-down sewer sheds and an old warehouse building, and replace the existing cart barn with a larger one in the same location. Additionally, a new tennis court would be

added between the relocated paddle ball court and the new golf cart storage facility, with existing configurations being improved for safety and efficiency. This included the addition of a landscape island, resurfacing efforts, and new ADA parking spaces. All existing services were set to be reused, except for the water service, which would be new. Coordination with Rhode Island Energy for gas and electrical services was in progress due to the increased demand from the cart barn for charging electric golf carts, which required a transformer that was indicated in their plans to be landscaped and screened appropriately. They communicated with the Fire Marshal via email, and since the initial conversation, they adjusted the turning radius into the access road leading to the cart barn, widening it to enhance fire access. The Fire Marshal confirmed that the existing hydrants on Nayatt Road are sufficient. Although written confirmation has not yet been received since resending the plans, these discussion points are addressed in the updated plan set.

Malo went through the conditions mentioned in the letter of completeness. One condition was related to oil-water separators concerning the golf carts and a proposed washdown area for them. Since the golf carts are electric and leased, any maintenance is conducted off-site by the leasing company. Conversations with DEM clarified that there would be no fuel, oils, or maintenance performed on-site, and no chemicals would be used. A five-foot-wide gravel area with an underdrain is planned to capture runoff from power washing. This runoff will be directed to a sediment forebay for additional treatment before being discharged into a bioretention area. Due to site grades and constraints, the first bioretention area would be discharged into a second bioretention area to the north before flowing off the property.

Malo confirmed that they did not trigger a CRMC wetland application, as they were outside the 200 foot buffer for CRMC wetlands; however, they were applying to CRMC for a stormwater permit. This will be included in an affidavit. The soil types were addressed in their stormwater report, and soil boundary lines were added to the plan set for visibility. The wetlands report also discussed the soils, and coordination is ongoing between their plans and the landscape plans regarding existing vegetation to be protected. There was some miscommunication, as they specified individual trees for protection, while the landscape plan only included a general note. They are coordinating to ensure clarity, particularly regarding the four evergreens near the south end of the property, that are outside the limits of disturbance but designated for protection. Additionally, there was concern about existing vegetation on the east side of the proposed cart barn, and they included a note to protect as many as possible, addressing the question of potential damage during construction.

Chair Durfee expressed the need for the landscape architect to be present for the Planning Board meeting.

Malo continued that they were also working with the DPW director to get all sign offs and proper permits for the project and made note that they were tying into preexisting sewer service. They could have increased flow, but they are working with DPW.

Malo explained that they are not altering the existing outdoor lighting in the parking lots and all cut sheets will be included to show they will meet "dark sky" guidelines. Any new site lighting will be building mounted and will be dark sky compliant.

Chair Durfee asked if any security lighting was going to be dark sky compliant. Malo said they were

working with the architects to find the answer to that question.

The Board discussed the dumpster pad with Malo. It was decided that instead of an elevated lip around the concrete pad, which could trap water and scum, crushed stone will be added around the edges for some ability to absorb liquids. Malo also mentioned that this was not a restaurant facility so the dumpsters would have minimal liquids dumped into them. Chair Durfee expressed that the county club should consider some fencing with a lock around the dumpster to prevent illegal dumping in their dumpster.

The Board briefly discussed Perm voids, a type of plastic substructure that allows roots to continue to grow and allows for additional sustainability of water. This was regarding the protection of the landscape within the parking lot islands.

The Board discussed the impervious structures. Malo noted that they will be reducing some of the existing impervious structures. The Board asked if they could move the cart barn building back from Middle Highway and Nayatt Road.

Member Bacher asked if all electrical is going to be underground, to which Malo confirmed it will. Member Bacher then asked about the drainage plan drawing and where the PDA drawings lead to. Malo explained where each of the PDA drawings empties to.

There was discussion about the material of the tennis courts, and it was confirmed that they are currently clay and will be clay again when they are reconstructed.

The Board moved to make a motion.

**MOTION: Member Wetherill, seconded by Member Durham, moved to recommend to the Planning Board the possibility that the cart barn be made less imposing to the corner of the property located at Middle Highway and Nayatt Road. Including, but not limiting reduction of building height, moving north, away from the intersection, maximizing landscaping, as well as pushing the building down a little bit, meaning subsurface, or to the north. Motion carries unanimously.**

### **5. Adjourn**

By a unanimous consensus of the Committee, the meeting adjourned at 4:47 PM.
