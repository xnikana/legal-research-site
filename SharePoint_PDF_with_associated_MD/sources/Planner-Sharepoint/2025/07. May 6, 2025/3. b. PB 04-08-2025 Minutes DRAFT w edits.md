### **4. Old Business:**

**a. Discussion and Action: Amendments to Chapter 185: Zoning Ordinance (recommendation to Town Council)**

Solicitor Goins provided an overview of the Zoning Ordinance amendments, emphasizing that they reflect changes to State Law enacted in the previous year. Key points discussed included:

- ➢ The amendments aim to ensure local ordinances align with State Law.
- ➢ The recent changes in the Enabling Act requiring municipalities to review their Development Plan Review categories along with what constitutes Minor Land Development Projects.
- ➢ The intention is to limit the Development Plan Review (DPR) categories primarily to changes in use when no extensive construction is involved. More complex projects, such as adaptive reuse, would be designated as Minor Land Development projects instead.

Member Bacher expressed his concerns regarding the proposal, clarifying that, based on his review, Land Development Projects only allow review of the standard features shown on a plat map (e.g., rights of way, landscaping), and he feared that design standards could effectively be lost if all categories were relocated under Land Development Review. Member Bacher also emphasized that maintaining the existing categories of review is critical, particularly for discussions on design standards that should be enforced based on the town's objectives.

Solicitor Goins responded to these concerns, stating that design standards remain part of the regulatory framework, even under Land Development Reviews (LDR), and they're essential for compliance with local ordinances. Solicitor Goins further explained that the change in State Law eliminates the ability to subject one project to both LDR and DPR simultaneously, thus shifting review processes for various projects.

Planner Durfee suggested that the ongoing discussion should inform future Comprehensive Planning and design guidelines and the overarching idea is to document any recommendations in the Comprehensive Plan to facilitate design review implementation.

Chair Phipps sought clarification on whether the proposed changes would eliminate existing review categories. It was confirmed that maintaining the categories under current ordinance provisions is essential for balancing community aesthetics with development.

Member Bacher reiterated his support for retaining existing categories requiring DPR before the Board votes on forwarding the proposal.

Chair Phipps opened the floor for motions regarding the proposed Zoning Ordinance amendments.

**MOTION: Member Bacher moved to approve the proposed Zoning amendments for ADUs and Design Plan Review and inclusionary zoning and substandard lots, with the provision that under DPR, we would retain the uses requiring DPR that are called out in the current section 185-151 and with the provision that we are also recommend deletion of the article that refers to Wellhead Protections. No second was made and the motion did not carry.**

Chair Phipps requested another motion be brought forward.

**MOTION: Member Kilmartin seconded by Member Simms, moved to forward the revisions to the Zoning Ordinance as recommended, with the recommendation to delete the Wellhead Protection District section from the Ordinance, and acknowledging that these recommendations are in accordance with the current Comprehensive Plan and bring us into compliance with State Law. Motion carried (5-1).**

# **b. Discussion and Action: Comprehensive Plan 2024-2025 Updates (Standing Agenda Item):**

Chair Phipps initiated the discussion regarding updates to the Comprehensive Plan, addressing previous concerns about the work conducted by Michael Baker. Key points included:

- ➢ Disagreement among the Planning Board on Michael Baker's approach, particularly regarding the confusion surrounding housing data.
- ➢ Notable inaccuracies, including references to a non-existent train station in Barrington, which arose from limited community input sessions.
- ➢ Lack of comprehensive community input; the original sessions only involved discussions with nine or fewer individuals.

Planner Durfee introduced Member Kilmartin, highlighting her efforts in revising the Comprehensive Plan and emphasized the necessity for subgroup insights to contribute to the discussion.

### **i. Heather Kilmartin Reports**

Member Kilmartin provided an update on the progress made:

- ➢ The small working groups had the opportunity to meet early in the month to gather input for the revision process, emphasizing that the Comprehensive Plan should reflect the aspirations and goals generated from within the town.
- ➢ Discussion focused on maintaining alignment in identifying and prioritizing the goals, policies, and actions that are key components of the plan.
- ➢ The small working groups reviewed the goals from the previous 2015 Comprehensive Plan to determine which were still applicable and which new goals need to be included. The emphasis was on keeping those that were in progress or incomplete and identifying gaps that required attention.
- ➢ Work has been initiated on three draft chapters, although they are roughly drawn out in their current state. Drafts include sections on Natural Resources, Historic and Cultural Resources, and Outdoor Recreation and Open Space.
- ➢ The need to organize each element into separate chapters that include necessary required elements such as existing conditions, identified needs, and prioritization was stressed.
- ➢ noted that there is a significant amount of available data, including an athletic fields analysis report, that can strengthen the plan.
- ➢ Highlighted inconsistencies in the previous drafts from Michael Baker, specifically in population projections and the coverage of relevant topics.
- ➢ There is an urgent need to reconcile discrepancies across chapters and ensure that the final compilation aligns with the required mapping and other essential elements.

Member Kilmartin expressed concern about the timeline, indicating that they are off track in terms of submitting draft sections to the state and emphasized the importance of a coherent presentation to facilitate effective feedback.

### **ii. Small working group Reports**

Chair Phipps mentioned that working groups paired up to work on goals and steps, noting that these can be shared in writing rather than discussed aloud. The Board transitioned to discuss the process and schedule for moving forward.

Member Costa inquired about the future role of Michael Baker International.

Planner Durfee explained that Michael Baker has been instructed to pause work pending a reassessment of their value to the project. Planner Durfee further explained that discussions are ongoing with the Town Manager about whether to retain their services or let Michael Baker go, with the intention of utilizing remaining funds for mapping if necessary. Currently, the firm is in what was described as "stasis," but that it was still important to maintain communication to ensure the Planning Board has the necessary support moving forward.

Member Rua brought up the need to revisit the Transportation Plan to ensure that recommendations align with the Complete Streets initiative. He suggested a review meeting for the Planning Board to ensure previous recommendations align with newer plans.

Member Kilmartin concluded that she would prioritize completing her review of the Complete Streets plan and incorporate its recommendations into the ongoing work, remaining open to community input and revisions later on.

### **iii. Process and Schedule Moving Forward**

The Board discussed with Planner Durfee about sending drafts to the State in a piecemeal approach, allowing for feedback on chapters as they become available.

Member Kilmartin expressed the desire to continue incorporating feedback from the Steering Committee.

Chair Phipps highlighted the need for Steering Committee members to provide their insights effectively moving forward. Acknowledgment of prior insufficient collaboration. Chair Phipps suggested a work session to facilitate collaboration between the Steering Committee and Planning Board to review the drafts collectively.

Member Kilmartin pointed out that the drafts are currently in need of significant refinement and that the Steering Committee's involvement should resume once the drafts are in better shape.

The Board proposed a collaborative approach moving forward, emphasizing the necessity for collective conversations across Boards and Commissions.

Mr. Paul requested to review the draft related to hazard planning, indicating the necessity for input from relevant committees to enhance the plan's effectiveness.
