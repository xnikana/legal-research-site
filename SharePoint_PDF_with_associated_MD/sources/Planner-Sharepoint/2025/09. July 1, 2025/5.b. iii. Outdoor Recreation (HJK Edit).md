## **OUTDOOR RECREATION**

## **Overview**

Barrington is an active community where residents of all ages participate in a wide variety of outdoor activities. The Town's recreational spaces offer many ways to unwind and enjoy the area's abundant natural resources. Residents walk, run, or bike along the East Bay Bike Path, which crosses through several neighborhoods throughout the town. Well-subscribed sports leagues make use of the athletic fields. Hiking trails crisscross parks and open space parcels. The town's lengthy shoreline and multiple public access points offer opportunities for walking, swimming, kayaking, and an array of other watersports. These resources are assets for people of all ages, contributing to a sense of community and providing opportunities for a healthy enjoyment of the outdoors.

Community input regarding Barrington's parks and recreation facilities was solicited throughout the public engagement process. Participants identified several priorities, chief among them expanding and enhancing the quality of trails for walking and biking, improving access to the shore, and developing playgrounds and recreational facilities for underserved segments of the community.

## *Townwide Trail Systems*

Trails are valuable community assets, providing opportunity for healthy recreation and enjoyment of Barrington's scenic resources. The town's trail system was the top-ranked priority identified by survey respondents during the comprehensive plan publicengagement process. Specifically, residents desired more and better maintained trail systems for walking, running, and cycling.

Barrington has a considerable trail inventory. These trails offer access to scenic shorelines, woodlands, and meadows, as well as various recreation areas throughout the town. Public awareness of lesser-known sites is lacking, however. Open space areas open for passive recreation—whether publicly or privately owned—should be clearly identified in new signage and maps, along with essential information for visitors, such as location of trail heads, parking availability, and amenities. Access would be improved by consistent trail infrastructure, including trail-head kiosks and trail markers.

Municipally-owned open space properties also suffer due to lack of management plans for regular trail improvement and maintenance. Many of the town's trails—including the most expansive trail network within Veterans Park—pass through water-prone areas. Such trails require regular inspection and improvement (bridges over wetlands, resurfacing of eroded trail treads, removal of trash and invasive vegetation, etc.) to ensure they remain in safe, accessible condition.

The need for additional athletic fields has been an ongoing issue since at least 1987, when the Park and Recreation Commission released a report on recreational needs. In addition to necessary field improvements, the 2023 needs analysis identified the following areas of opportunity:

- **Field Reservation System**. The current registration system for field use is inadequate and creates frustration among users. Fields are registered on a seasonal basis, and in-season leagues receive priority. Leagues often over-register for fields, on the assumption that weather or emergency maintenance could affect actual availability. As a result, fields are sometimes underutilized despite reservation. While leagues usually try to cooperate, lack of coordination and competition over available fields can sometimes cause frustration. An improved field reservation system would help alleviate conflict among leagues, clarify timing for maintenance by DPW, and allow for necessary resting of fields.
- **Field Maintenance**. Proper field maintenance stands as the cornerstone of optimal sports performance and safety. Given the lack of space for development of new fields, the Town must focus its efforts on improving existing athletic resources. A regular maintenance program including resting periods should be implemented to improve the existing natural turf surfaces, rendering these fields safer and more enjoyable for all users. Maintenance plans should be developed for each outdoor athletic complex, involving at least semi-annual inspection and report on maintenance progress and requirements.
- **Maintenance Funding**. Regular maintenance plans allow for more predictable and reasonable funding allocations as opposed to less frequent, larger requests. Currently, there are no line-items related to maintenance of athletic fields, which has contributed to the overall poor condition of facilities. The Department of Public Works provides what maintenance services it can from its own budget, however such expenditures tend to be reactionary (irrigation repairs, emergency repairs, etc.) The town should affirmatively budget for field maintenance and give DPW the resources needed to satisfy residents' needs.
- **Equity in Field Development**. The town does not have a balance of fields to equally support boys and girls sports, specifically softball. There are currently 11 Baseball/Little League fields with 5 of those fields being full-sized 90' base path fields, and 6 softball fields with 3 of those being full-sized 60' base path fields. The town should consider equity of access when planning and prioritizing athletic field projects.
