### **4.1 Discuss and Act: Amendments to Chapter 185: Zoning Ordinance (recommendation to Town Council)**

Technician Gillibrand provided an introduction. Assistant Solicitor Goins provided an overview and an explanation of each, also noting the use of templates from Rhode Island Housing:

- ➢ Inclusionary Zoning.
  - o Strikethroughs in the findings section of outdated language.
  - o Verbatim changes from the inclusionary zoning law amended this year, effective January 1, 2025. This will make the Town inclusionary zoning ordinance consistent with the State law.
  - o No decision points for the Town. There was discussion on the assumption of the Town wanting to keep inclusionary zoning on the books.
- ➢ Substandard Lots.
  - o Definition edits for "lot frontage."
  - o Additional language on calculating the "building bonus" on substandard lots, verbatim from State law. Intent is to make it consistent across the state, as there was ambiguity in the initial language.
- ➢ Accessory Dwelling Units (ADUs).
  - o Delete existing section of ordinance on "accessory apartments."
  - o Definition edits of accessory dwelling units and accessory apartments.

Planning Board November 5, 2024 Regular Business Meeting, 1

- o Categories of eligibility when ADUs permitted by right.
  - (1) For a family member with a disability.
  - (2) Lots with 20,000 square feet and is a residential lot.
  - (3) Within the existing primary or accessory structure and no footprint expansion.
- o Advice from the Town Solicitor's office on how to handle requests for ADUs, and whether to process as a special use permit or dimensional variance from lot area.
- o The State law on ADUs is already in effect.
- o Clarifying an detached accessory structure ADU versus an ADU that is attached or part of the primary structure.
- o Discussion on what the municipalities are to adopt as a bare minimum, and what they may and may not restrict regarding ADUs.
  - The Board discussed permitting ADUs by right so long as it meets the dimensional requirements. The Board asked about reducing the lot size by right, and the appetite of the Town Council to adopt such a policy as an affordable housing / housing production strategy.
  - Suggestions for conditions such as not building in the floodplain, and proposed ADU meets design standards in the Land Development and Subdivision Regulations.
  - Assistant Solicitor Goins referred to what the Town can and cannot do, and how ADUs cannot be subject to both special use and dimensional variance (i.e., one or the other). Suggestion from Staff to adopt the minimum requirements and get a feel from both the Town Council and General Assembly.
- o Reviewed maximum building size for an ADU.
- o Procedural requirements for ADUs.
- ➢ Development Plan Review (DPR).
  - o Definition edits and changes, including adaptive reuse and development plan review.
  - o Additional changes to the Land Development and Subdivision Regulations related to DPR.
  - o Amendments to adaptive reuse, and what projects require land development project review versus development plan review.
  - o Redlined most of the existing development plan review from the ordinance to instead embed into the Land Development and Subdivision Regulations.
  - o The Board discussed administrative review versus formal review. Assistant Solicitor Goins clarified that classification of formal and administrative review is existing law. Further discussion ensued regarding the decision points the Board had to make on DPR, and the relationship between DPR references in the ordinance versus regulations. The projects that will be formal versus administrative will be specified in the regulations. The Board discussed whether that should be defined prior to Town Council review and adoption of the ordinance. There was discussion about minor land development project versus development plan review, and a project can only be subject to one or the other.
  - o Discussion of categories for what projects are permitted under development plan review, and has been narrowed.

o There was discussion on if the Town Center qualifies under the Urban Growth Center from the State Guide Plan.

Assistant Solicitor Goins noted that the Board has the option to continue this item to review with the draft regulation amendments.

Member Bacher commented on the "unrelated persons" decision, and Assistant Solicitor Goins provided an update. Member Bacher also noted the outdated Article XXIV wetlands overlay article, and Assistant Solicitor Goins confirmed that this is an item that still needs to be addressed and updated.

**MOTION: Vice Chair Rua, seconded by Member Costa, moved to continue the agenda item to the December 3, 2024 meeting.**
