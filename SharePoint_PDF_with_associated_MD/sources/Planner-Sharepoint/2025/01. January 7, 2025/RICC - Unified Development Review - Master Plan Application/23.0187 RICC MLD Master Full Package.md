#### Background on Rhode Island Country Club (RICC):

Located at 150 Nayatt Rd in Barrington, Rhode Island, exists the 1911 Donald Ross designed golf course known as Rhode Island Country Club (RICC). The course has a diverse mix of parkland, wooded and seaside holes, with the final four holes located on Narragansett Bay which offers views of the bay and its islands. This golf course is consistently ranked as one of top classic golf course in the country. It has hosted four USGA championships and is home of the CVS Charity Classic.

Throughout the years, renovations have been done to improve the golf course and associated facilities such as, a new dining room, members bar, camp room, fitness room and two golf simulators. RICC also offers other amenities such as tennis, squash, and paddle ball courts as well as a heated pool to enjoy with family and friends.

#### Project Background:

The proposed project aims to facilitate the design of a new 1,187 sq ft ± multi-use building to accommodate the relocated paddle ball court, new tennis court, replacement of the existing cart barn with a new 6,277 sq ft ± cart barn. The parking area will also be reconfigured by demolishing the existing amenities and will provide an opportunity for beautification of the surrounding area and provide space and access for the abovementioned facilities. The proposed facilities will increase parking requirements, these requirements are remedied by redesigning the existing parking lot immediately adjacent the projects area of concern and providing additional spaces that meet zoning requirements.

A pre-application town meeting was conducted by video call on October 24, 2024. Zoning classification, parking requirements, and application procedures were discussed. The plan has been updated, based on the content of the pre-app meeting, as well as updated survey, soils and utility research. The property is zoned RE, which requires a special use permit for the current use of the "Golf Course." Due to the modification of an existing special use permit this project will be presented to Unified Development Review with Special Use. The prior special use permit was approved in 2015 to add a 116' x 43' building, move gasoline lines, add storage bins, complete paving, and add a loft to the existing building (BK 1430 PG 273).

#### Existing Conditions

The existing lot is owned by the Rhode Island Country Club which provides their 138.2 acre lot to private members on Nayatt Road in Barrington. The lot itself exists just south of Echo Lake (RI0007020L-07) nestled between S Lake Drive to the north, Nayatt Road to the south, Washington Road to the west, Middle Highway to the east and resides just north of Upper Narragansett Bay (RI0007024E-01A). The coverage of the lot is predominantly grasslands of low slopes with varying, sporadic vegetation. Unnamed water bodies (Golf Water Features) and Mussuchuck Creek (RI0007020R-01) run east to west through the mid portion of the property. Wetlands exist to the north of the project site (CRMC jurisdiction), near to CRMC jurisdictional areas. If required, an Assent will be applied for, when the projects' RIPDES permit is submitted. The projects area of concern is in the southeastern most area of the site, directly east of residential properties and north of Nayatt Road. The project area consists of the existing cart barn, paddle ball courts, tennis courts, parking area, tennis clubhouse, storage areas, and sheds. The main entrance for this project is east of the paddle ball courts. The existing lot has approximately 153,000 sq ft ± with various impervious covering about 2.5 % ± of the lot.

#### Proposed Site Plan

The proposed improvement of this project is to install a 65'x18' multi-use building, 122'x50' cart barn, add an additional a tennis court, and relocate the paddle ball court. Existing facilities including the cart barn, warehouse, paddle ball court, and multiple utility sheds will be demolished to accommodate these site improvements. The proposed multi-use building shall be a 1-story building with a crawl space for storage capability. The proposed cart barn shall be 1 story building with slab on grade, placed in the same location as the existing cart barn. The purpose of the project is not to add new uses, per se, but to relocate the paddle ball courts, and increase the size of the cart barn. The project does propose to install an additional tennis court.

The parking requirements are determined by gross floor area (GFA) with an approximate increase of 700 sq ft GFA. The current site configuration of the subject area and the parking area surrounding the club house provides a total of 158 parking spaces with 6 ADA spaces. The existing parking spaces surpasses the 130 spaces per GFA required. The parking table can be found within the civil plan set (C-200). The proposed conditions reconfigure the adjacent parking lot with landscape islands to accommodate 4 additional parking spaces, 1 of which to provide ADA access to the proposed multi-use building.

Parking is adequate for the proposed use, based existing and expected parking demand, it is neither insufficient and will not result in unauthorized or excessive parking and will not result in an excess of impervious surfaces for stormwater runoff.

The property has two existing entrances that will remain as is and traffic patterns around the existing parking lot will remain the same. The general entrance fronting the subject property will be remain intact continuing to serve the Club access to Nayatt Road.

#### Proposed Utilities

A sewer connection ties into the existing warehouse building via Nayatt Road to the south from the north side of the building traversing the site via the east side of the building. A sewer connection will be required to connect the multi-use building and the updated cart barn and is proposed to tie into the existing service. A water connection serves the existing cart barn off Nayatt Road near the intersection with Middle Highway. A new water line is proposed to connect to the multi-use building and cart barn. The existing irrigation system will need to be relocated around the proposed structures. Coordination with BCWA will be completed to determine the size of the new line and possible lateral locations.

According to RI Energy, there is an existing 2" gas service in use for the existing paddleball courts and an there is an existing service for the cart barn. A new gas service will be installed for the multi-use building, the relocated paddleball court, and updated cart barn.

The existing warehouse and cart barn are served overhead by electricity from an on-site utility pole. Proposed conditions will relocate this utility pole and connect to the proposed facilities with a new transformer and underground electric service. NEI will continue to coordinate with RI Energy through design and construction to ensure the site infrastructure meets their requirements.

#### Stormwater Requirements

Due to the existing impervious areas and the size of the lot, the project will have the same stormwater requirements as a new development to treat 100% of all new impervious surfaces and will follow the guidelines of the RI Stormwater manual. The stormwater management plan will include roof drains, catch basins, and drain manholes connected to surface infiltration practices or an underground infiltration and surge tank system. Five soil evaluations have recently been performed on site to aid in the stormwater design process. The next steps of this project will include applications with RIDEM, the project will be in compliance with these regulations.

#### Potential Impacts

Being well within the scenic areas stated within the Barrington Comprehensive Plan, much care was taken to ensure that the proposed work falls within the scope of "betterment of the existing facilities to better serve the private residents of Barrington". Potential impacts are minimized with this redevelopment site work due to all work occurring within the limits of the site. The limit of disturbance has been minimized to either reduce or eliminate impacts to off-site features including neighboring residents and public right-of-way. The proposed work is within the zoning use of the property under recreation and the change in land use is negligible thus maintaining or elevating the value of the property and surrounding area.

#### Conclusion

All of the proposed improvements to the project's area of concern are necessary for the functional use of the country club, its amenities, and the needs of the club members. The existing cart barn, storage building, and surrounding area are obsolete and in need of improvement to be consistent with the condition country clubs' amenities.

The improvements to the project's area of concern include additional parking, additional landscaping area, and improved stormwater facilities where no stormwater facilities exist other than catch basin drywells. The proposed landscaping will also improve the appearance of the property along Nayatt Rd.
