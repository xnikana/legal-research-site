#### **Procedural History**

On August 21, 2023, a written decision for an Administrative Subdivision Approval was recorded in the Barrington Land Evidence Records as approved by then Administrative Officer Teresa Crean. The approval granted an "Administrative Subdivision to shift the property line between [AP 23, Lot 3 and AP 23, Lot 323] to the West on Maple Avenue to make [AP 23, Lot 3] equal 13,187 square feet and [AP 23, Lot 323] equal 22,400 square feet for Gizzarelli Plat at Maple Avenue & Prince's Hill Avenue, AP 23, Lots 3 and 323 is approved..."

On or about December 18, 2024, the Applicant submitted their request for the 2-lot Administrative Subdivision application that is approved with condition as contained herein.

Based on conversation with Bruce H. Cox, Esq. (legal counsel for the applicant) on December 30, 2024, the current Administrative Officer was told an outbuilding/accessory structure was demolished on AP 23, Lot 3 sometime in 2024 in order to ensure the boundary line adjustment approved by the previous Administrative Officer via an Administrative Subdivision as cited above did not result in an encroachment of that outbuilding/accessory structure onto AP 23, Lot 323 (specifically proposed Lot 1) as reconfigured.

Based upon a review of the Building Department's permit database, the applicant did <u>not</u> apply for a Demolition permit before taking down and disposing of the outbuilding/accessory structure. Though the demolition already has occurred, as a condition of the approval granted herein, the applicant needs to submit a Demolition Permit with the applicable fee along with an affidavit that the demolished materials were disposed of properly including for any materials that contained asbestos, lead paint, and/or any other hazardous materials.

#### **Findings of Fact:**

With adherence to the conditions contained herein, the proposed subdivision is consistent with the Town's Comprehensive Plan as it pertains to enabling the development of two vacant lots (from one existing vacant lot). Said developable lots were enabled given the lot line adjustment approved via the Administrative Subdivision Approval recorded on August 21, 2024 (Book 1866, Page 105). In that Approval, the lot line between AP 23, Lot 3 and AP 23, Lot 323 was shifted resulting in parcel AP 23, Lot 323 being enlarged enough (22,400 SF) to be subdivided into two lots that each meet minimum lot size and road frontage requirements for the NB Zoning District – i.e., into the two lots proposed herein (Lot 1 equal to 10,385 SF with 90.66' of road frontage on Maple Avenue and Lot 2 equal to 12,015 SF with 90.00' of road frontage on Maple Avenue).

With adherence to the conditions contained herein, the proposed subdivision complies with the standards and provisions of the Town's Zoning Ordinance and Subdivision Regulations.

The proposed lots have access to a public street – i.e., Maple Avenue.

### **Conditions of Approval:**

- 1. Prior to the submission of any Building Permit application for development of proposed Lots 1 or 2, the applicant shall submit to the Planning, Building & Resiliency Department (PBR) for approval by the Administrative Officer a revised subdivision survey/plat plan to include:
  - a. A signature block for the Administrative Officer's signature and date. (E.g., "Approved by the Town of Barrington" with signature for "Herbert A. Durfee, III, Administrative Officer" and the date of signature/approval.)
  - b. A corrected Graphic Scale measure such that the scale truly reflects that 1'' = 20'. (The current graphic scale shows that  $1'' = ^224'$  though actual measurements on the survey plan accurately are based on a 1'' = 20' scale.)
- 2. Once Condition 1. is carried out and approved and signed by the Administrative Officer, the revised subdivision survey/plat can then be recorded in the Land Evidence Records along with the applicable fee paid at the time of recording.
- 3. Prior to the submission of any Building Permit application for development of proposed Lots 1 or 2, in regard to the already demolished outbuilding/accessory structure that was located at the time entirely on AP 23, Lot 3 as identified in the Procedural History contained herein, a Demolition Permit application with the applicable fee along with an affidavit that the demolished materials were disposed of properly including any materials that contained asbestos, lead paint, and/or any other hazardous material shall be submitted to the Building Official for approval.
- 4.
