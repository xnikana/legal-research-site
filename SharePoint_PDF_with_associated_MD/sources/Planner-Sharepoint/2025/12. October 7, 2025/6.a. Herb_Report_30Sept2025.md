# Planning, Building & Resiliency Director's Monthly Report

August 2025

- 1. National Electric Vehicle Infrastructure Phase 2A Program. An application was completed for grant funding that, if awarded, would help offset (75%) the cost of purchasing and installing two dual port, level 3, fast chargers at Town Hall (i.e., equal to four additional parking spaces). To date, RI Office of Energy Resources is amid reviewing applications, and the Town was notified that no additional information is necessary for the department's review.
- 2. Participation in the Town's quarterly check-in with the Town's Energy Manager a renewed effort between the Town Manager, Town Energy Manager, Town Planner, and other staff as applicable and warranted.
- 3. Conducted a host of work concerning the proposed school construction project:
  - a. Continued to participate on the School Building Steering Committee.
  - b. Attended/participated in a **Sen Whitehouse** sponsored workshop with the **Army Corps of Engineers**, especially related to the Barrington High School given its location entirely within a FEMA designated flood hazard area.
  - c. Trying to continue working collaboratively with the Corps to identify **grant funding for purposes of "dry-proofing"** affected, existing areas of the high school buildings/facilities.
  - d. Participated in several meetings with **RIEMA/FEMA**, Town staff, and School District Project Managers for the construction project concerning the high school as to what existing buildings/facilities must be "dry-proofed".
  - e. Received hard and soft copies of the **school construction plans** and shared the information with relevant department heads asking them to review the plans for the anticipated project review process, including in preparation for the forthcoming Building Permit application anticipated in early 2026.
- 4. Two-year Curbside Compost Pickup Pilot Project in tandem with the Barrington Farm School's on-going drop-off composting program. In tandem with the Eastern Rhode Island Conservation District (ERICD), the Town has been promoting the pilot project since the notice of grant award from 11<sup>th</sup> Hour Racing. Most of the effort has been conducted by the Town's Communications Officer through webpage, newsletters, social media, et al. The Communications Officer has been at the forefront in helping to create the information (e.g., flyers, sign-up materials, articles, written posts, etc.) that has and continues to be posted through the Town's various media outlets.
- 5. **Comprehensive Plan.** The Planning Board with assistance from staff continues to prepare elements for the plan's update. Preliminary comments from the State on

three of the submitted elements have been received while two additional elements are pending before the State. Another element is being prepared for submittal to the State based on the Planning Board's August meeting. Work is continuing the Land Use, Circulation (Transportation) and Utilities, and Natural Hazards/Climate Change elements.

- 6. Foote St / Maple Ave Tree Planning Project. Staff continue to collaborate with the Town's tree supplier/contractor (North-Eastern Tree Services), and staff learned that the tree planting period can extend into the first week or two of November, if necessary. Staff have been connecting with neighborhood residents to sign up for the trees and their planting. The first round of tree planting is scheduled during the Day of Caring when about 30 trees are scheduled for planting whereby North-Eastern Tree Service will deliver the trees and dig the holes. Thereafter, the Day's volunteers, including area Scouts, will plant the trees. The second round of tree planting is scheduled after the Day of Caring but before the 2<sup>nd</sup> week of November. If necessary, a third and final round of tree planting will occur during Spring 2026. Tags for planted trees are being created to safely attach to the trees to provide basic tree care instructions. Also, all tree recipients will receive a USDA tree stewardship guide and additional stewardship support from department staff. Also, importantly, department staff will create point information as a GIS datalayer to complement the Town's Tree Inventory (from last year's effort to create the inventory) both for presenting a tree map to the Rhode Island Infrastructure Bank (RIIB) and for tracking the progress of this and future community tree-planting initiatives. Later in the year, staff will report on the project's progress to the Conservation Commission that doubly serves as the Town's Tree Committee.
- 7. **FEMA Planning for Disaster Debris Management (MGT-460).** Attended and participated in this all day FEMA training workshop on behalf of the Town.
- 8. Rhode Island Country Club Unified Development Review. Based on the Planning Board's Preliminary Plan Approval, the department received the Final Plan application for administrative review and approval. [The Final Approval recorded written decision is in the Planning Board's October 2025 meeting packet.] Staff also worked with RICC to address several issues with the application, including ensuring the ultimate plan set that was recorded in the Land Evidence Records met all the conditions of the Planning Board.
- 9. Other Applications of Interest. The Town's 25 Watson Ave Comprehensive Permit Application is in the door with a Pre-Application review being conducted by the Planning Board on October 7. A full set of the School Construction Project (\$250MM bond issue) plan set has been given to Town staff for review and comment. Also, Belton Court Village Comprehensive Plan Preliminary Plan Application

- was submitted (Sept 26) and currently is under review for application completeness.
- 10. The Director was asked and worked in collaboration with other panelists to prepare a breakout presentation on the **Route 114 Resiliency Plan** during the **Association of Metropolitan Planning Organization (AMPO) at its annual national conference** in September.
- 11. Organized a meeting/discussion concerning a parcel of land on the "Great Tongue" in Town. It's an undevelopable parcel of land that will soon be inundated due to sea level rise. The primary stakeholder is the **Hooker Family Trust** that the surviving cousins want to be relieved of the property and the outstanding small amount of property taxes. Other stakeholders include RIDEM, Barrington Land Trust, and some Town departments including the Town Manager, Assessor, and Planning. Based on the discussion it was agreed that the Town Council should be asked to basically clear the title so that it's listed as an "unknown owner" (that essentially takes the property off the tax rolls ~75 annually) and abate the outstanding property taxes (~\$1,700+/-).
- 12. Participated with Town staff and stakeholders concerning a site visit and **tour by Senator Whitehouse of the Walker Farm** Recreation and Resiliency Improvement Project.
- 13. Working to schedule a tour of the **Nockum Hill (Lot 3A George St)** site by a select group of officials (e.g., Secretary of State, RI Historical Society, local Legislative delegation, Town Manager, and Town Council). Also, working with the Barrington Historical Preservation Society to arrange a larger, multi-jurisdictional historical society seminar/workshop about the historical and archaeological significance of Nockum Hill and the technical report and findings, to date.
- 14. Completed a formal request for proposals process to identify and contract with UMass-Amherst's Center for Resilient Metro-Regions to carry out the consulting component of the Town's **Comprehensive Signage Regulation update**. A contract was negotiated and executed, work was initiated to schedule the first public and stakeholder engagement session (Oct 15, 2025), and existing sign survey work by the Center is being carried out. The project is funded in part by the RI Commerce *Site Readiness* grant.

- 15. The latest Spencer Trust Monthly Report is attached. Additionally, the following Spencer Trust update is offered pertaining to the **Barrington Farm School and the Village Common's use of FY26 Spencer Trust funds**:
  - a. Barrington Farm School provided donation totals for 2024 and 2025. The Farm School has provided over \$11,000 of fresh produce so far in 2025, approximately \$6,500 of which has been provided since the start of the Spencer Trust fiscal year on July 1 (2.5 months). The organization is using Spencer Trust funds to enable them to increase the amount of fresh produce they can grow and donate to TAP-IN. So far, they have donated more than twice as much produce in 2025 as they did in 2024.
  - b. The Village Common of RI provided indicated they will use Spencer Trust funds to:
    - i. Provide supplies and printed materials for Barrington Village.
    - ii. Vet and train Barrington Village volunteers.
    - iii. Maintain insurance for Barrington Village volunteers.
    - iv. Provide bookkeeping for Barrington Village.
    - v. Maintain the data center and eVoice that handles all memberships, emails, requests, and matching for services for Barrington Village.
    - vi. Provide social and educational events for Barrington Village members and volunteers.

The Village Common of RI pays \$115,000 to support Barrington Village, and services are continuing to grow.

#### Resilience Planner Update: August 2025

August saw intensified community outreach in Barrington's Foote St. - Maple Ave. neighborhood for the Town's native street tree planting project. This area, comprising over 400 lots and 4 miles of streets, has now received at least one visit to every residence by this project's Community Engagement and Planting Coordinator, Bel Johnson, and town volunteers. Their mission is to gauge resident interest in having shade trees planted on their street-facing yards, either within the right-of-way or up to 20 feet set back. For interested property owners, the planting coordinator works with them to select suitable native tree species and optimal planting locations.

This Rhode Island Infrastructure Bank-funded project has targeted the planting of 120 shade trees. These trees are crucial for mitigating the neighborhood's identified heat island effect by gradually reducing ambient summer temperatures. Such efforts are additionally vital as Barrington's 2024 Strategic Tree Canopy Plan indicates a need to plant over 245 trees annually across public and private lands to expand the town's overall canopy. The Foote-Maple neighborhood was selected as an ideal launchpad for the Town's community planting program due to its significant heat island effect and its demographic as a relatively lower-income area.
