Re: Opposition to Proposed Variance - Barton Ave

Dear Members of the Zoning Board,

We are writing to express our strong opposition to the request for a variance to build a new home on an undersized lot on Barton Avenue. We (Daniel & Casey Henchman) have lived at 4 Barton Ave for 17 years, raising our four children here, and feel deeply invested in preserving the character and safety of our neighborhood.

When we built an addition to our house several years ago, we adhered to all zoning requirements, including lot size and building-to-land area ratios. The only variance we received was a minor extension (about 4 feet) to connect two existing porches. We accepted that constraint because we believe those standards exist for good reason — to maintain consistency, quality of life, and fairness across the neighborhood.

The property in question lies in the R-10 zoning district, for which the minimum lot size is 10,000 square feet. Under the current proposal, two undersized lots would be created—neither would meet the 10,000 sq ft requirement. Allowing this would undermine the integrity of the zoning rules and set a precedent that weakens protections for all residents.

Almost every home on Barton Ave and in the surrounding area was built in compliance with proper lot size and building-to-land ratios. A house squeezed onto a lot smaller than that norm would stand out in a way that does not harmonize with the street. The scale, spacing,

and feel of the neighborhood depend on respecting those boundaries.

Having adequate land also matters for everyday life. Our neighborhood lacks nearby playgrounds or open space for kids to play. The closest option is Police Cove, but it's only open during the summer and is limited to water play. Yard space is one of our few real options for children to play safely near home.

Traffic and safety are also serious concerns. Barton Ave is already quite busy, especially near the County Road intersection, given that part of the road is half residential and half commercial. Adding another driveway—and more cars turning in and out near the end of the road—would increase danger for motorists, pedestrians, and children. Vehicles backing into or out of tight driveways so close to the busy intersection would exacerbate these risks.

## In short:

- This proposal would create two nonconforming lots in an R-10 district, undermining the purpose of the zoning ordinance.
- It would clash with the existing lot sizes and character of the neighborhood.
- It would deprive future occupants (and neighbors) of essential outdoor space in a neighborhood with limited access to parks.
- It would worsen traffic safety and congestion on an already busy roadway.

For all these reasons, we respectfully ask the Board to deny the variance request. Upholding the zoning standards ensures that Barrington remains a safe, consistent, and livable community.

Thank you for your time and consideration.

Sincerely,
