December 23, 2024

Dear Municipal Officials,

The Rhode Island Division of Statewide Planning (RIDSP), in cooperation with Rhode Island Department of Transportation (RIDOT) and Rhode Island Public Transit Authority (RIPTA), have initiated the process of preparing a new State Transportation Improvement Program (STIP). The federally required STIP is a list of transportation projects that the State of Rhode Island intends to implement using United States Department of Transportation (USDOT) funds. All transportation projects that utilize federal transportation funds must be included in the STIP. The new STIP will cover Federal Fiscal Years (FFY) 2026-2035, with the first four years (FFY 2026-2029) providing detailed funding information to demonstrate federally required fiscal constraint. Abridged information will be provided for the out-years (FFY 2030-2036).

In December 2020, the State adopted the Long-Range Transportation Plan (LRTP), Moving Forward Rhode Island 2040, which establishes a 20-year horizon for investing in the State's transportation systems. The LRTP examines the State's most critical transportation needs and challenges, as well as provides a framework for achieving statewide goals and objectives. These goals for bridges, highways, transit, and active transportation renew the state's commitment to funding projects that maintain a state of good repair, improve safety, enhance connectivity, build community character, and improve economic development opportunities. All projects in the STIP must be consistent with, and help implement, the goals of the LRTP. The STIP, with its 10-year timeframe, represents the translation of recommendations from the LRTP into a shorter-term program of improvements.

The state has worked diligently on the projects captured in the 2022-2031 STIP since its approval in September 2021, but several projects will be continuing into this next STIP cycle, including projects that contribute to bringing our bridges and pavement into a state of good repair*,* supporting mode shift and climate resiliency, and safeguarding equity. There are additional considerations being factored into the programming for the next STIP, such as an assumed funding level past 2026 with the end of the Infrastructure Investment and Jobs Act (IIJA). Changes over the next few years may impact how we develop and fund projects in the future.

RIDSP, RIDOT, and RIPTA recognize that it vital to hear from our communities when programming the STIP, and want to inform municipal leaders that we plan to conduct a solicitation for projects for the 2026-2035 STIP. This solicitation period is slated to open in mid- February for approximately 3-4 weeks and will feature a new intake platform.

The Statewide Intake Framework for Transportation (SWIFT), is a geospatially-based project intake tool that will allow municipalities to apply for project consideration through a platform where project locations are drawn on a map and scored, in part, by an electronic system that uses data provided by applicants. This will help us to score projects more efficiently and link them to our existing mapping for seamless integration into our E-STIP application.

A guidance document with instructions on how to submit your projects will follow. We will be available for office hours and calls to help with any questions. In the meantime, **each municipality must provide us with the name and email address of the municipal representative designated to submit projects on behalf of the city or town.** This will let us create a unique login for you to submit your applications. It is likely that there will be a limit on projects per municipality, due to anticipated funding levels.

In addition to the upcoming solicitation, we want to take this opportunity to reassess planned projects in your municipality to see if local priorities have changed. Attached, you will find a list of the projects that are currently planned to carry over into the next STIP. Please review the projects in your municipality and let us know if these projects are still needed for your city or town, and which projects are the highest priority for your community.

RIDSP will notify you of additional opportunities for feedback, such as public hearings, meetings, and comment periods, and will remain available to assist you as we move toward a STIP approval deadline of September 2025. If you have questions or feedback in the meantime, please contact Mason Perrone, Assistant Chief, Division of Statewide Planning at [mason.perrone@doa.ri.gov.](mailto:mason.perrone@doa.ri.gov) We are excited to begin this next STIP process with the broad involvement of our communities.

Sincerely,

Meredith E. Brady, Associate Director
