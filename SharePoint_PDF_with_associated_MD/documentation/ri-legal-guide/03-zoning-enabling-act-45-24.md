## 3. The Rhode Island Zoning Enabling Act (§ 45-24)

### 3.1 Overview

R.I.G.L. Chapter 45-24 ("Zoning Ordinances") grants municipalities authority to enact and enforce zoning.

Chapter index: https://webserver.rilegislature.gov/Statutes/TITLE45/45-24/INDEX.htm

In comprehensive permit proceedings, § 45-24 is relevant in two respects: (1) local zoning provisions remain applicable to the extent not expressly superseded by the § 45-53 process; and (2) the § 45-24 variance and special use permit standards continue to apply to non-§ 45-53 applications.

### 3.2 Subdivision and Land Development Regulations (§ 45-23)

Land subdivision and development is separately governed by § 45-23:
https://webserver.rilegislature.gov/Statutes/TITLE45/45-23/INDEX.htm

This chapter governs procedural requirements for preliminary and final plan approval, required findings, and conditions of approval. It operates alongside § 45-24 in most comprehensive permit proceedings and must be consulted for road design standards, stormwater requirements, and utility certifications.

### 3.3 Comprehensive Planning Requirement (§ 45-22.2)

Every RI municipality must maintain a current comprehensive plan under § 45-22.2, submitted to the Division of Statewide Planning. Plans on file are accessible at:
https://planning.ri.gov/planning-areas/local-comprehensive-planning

A municipality's comprehensive plan is legally significant in both § 45-53 proceedings (as a "local ordinance" per *East Bay*) and standard zoning appeals. Research should always begin with the most current adopted plan. Courts have distinguished between adopted plans and draft plans that have not yet received final approval.
