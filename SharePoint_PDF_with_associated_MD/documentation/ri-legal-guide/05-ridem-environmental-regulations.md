## 5. RIDEM Environmental Regulations

### 5.1 Freshwater Wetlands — 250-RICR-150-15-3

Current freshwater wetlands rules (effective July 1, 2022): https://rules.sos.ri.gov/regulations/part/250-150-15-3
RIDEM wetlands permitting overview: https://dem.ri.gov/environmental-protection-bureau/water-resources/permitting/freshwater-wetlands

Any development disturbing freshwater wetland buffers (typically 25–200 feet depending on wetland type) requires a RIDEM wetlands permit before local approval can be finalized.

§ 45-53-4(d)(3)(i)(A) requires that all state permits be obtained prior to final plan approval. Where RIDEM permitting is pending or uncertain, this gives a planning board grounds to withhold final approval regardless of preliminary plan approval status.

The regulatory definition of "wetland" in RI is broad. The 2022 rules added "filled swamp" as a distinct category. A developer's own soil scientist findings documenting seasonally high water table at or above ground surface ("SHWT = 0") can itself trigger wetlands jurisdiction even where the developer has not formally sought a determination.

### 5.2 Stormwater Management, Design, and Installation Rules — 250-RICR-150-10-8

Stormwater design rules: https://rules.sos.ri.gov/regulations/part/250-150-10-8

Key provisions for site plan challenges:

**§ 8.21(B)(7)** — Infiltration basins require a minimum 3-foot separation between the bottom of the basin and the seasonally high water table (SHWT).
**§ 8.21(B)(8)** — Where separation is less than 4 feet, a groundwater mounding analysis is required.
**§ 8.21(E)(4)(b)** — Standard percolation tests are explicitly prohibited for sizing infiltration basins; licensed soil scientist data is required.
**§ 8.22** — Permeable pavement requires a minimum 3-foot separation between the bottom of the aggregate base and the SHWT.

Where a developer's soil data reveals a high water table (common in coastal New England sites with historic wetland fill), these separation requirements may be impossible to satisfy without significant redesign. Practitioners should obtain the developer's soil evaluation reports and compare documented SHWT depths against required separations.

### 5.3 Onsite Wastewater Treatment Systems — 250-RICR-150-10-6

Where a development proposes individual OWTS/septic rather than municipal sewer connection, the design must comply with 250-RICR-150-10-6:
https://rules.sos.ri.gov/regulations/part/250-150-10-6

### 5.4 RIPDES Construction General Permit (Stormwater)

Any land disturbance of one or more acres requires a RIPDES Construction General Permit (CGP) from RIDEM, authorizing stormwater discharges from the construction site. The CGP requires a Stormwater Pollution Prevention Plan (SWPPP) and post-construction BMP maintenance program.

Where a developer's post-construction stormwater design does not satisfy the 250-RICR-150-10-8 separation requirements, the RIPDES permit cannot issue — and final plan approval under § 45-53-4(d)(3)(i)(A) must await resolution.

RIDEM RIPDES stormwater permitting portal: https://dem.ri.gov/environmental-protection-bureau/water-resources/permitting/stormwater-permitting
