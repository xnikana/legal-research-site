## 9. Appeals and Judicial Review: The Superior Court Land Use Calendar

### 9.1 The Abolition of SHAB

The State Housing Appeals Board (SHAB) was abolished effective January 1, 2024, by 2023 RI legislation. All appeals from comprehensive permit decisions that would previously have gone to SHAB now proceed directly to the Superior Court Land Use Calendar. The governing statute is R.I.G.L. § 8-2-40, enacted by H6060:
https://webserver.rilegislature.gov/Statutes/TITLE8/8-2/8-2-40.htm

### 9.2 The Superior Court Land Use Calendar

Land Use Calendar protocol (December 15, 2023):
https://www.courts.ri.gov/Calendar%20ProtocolMisc/LAND%20USE%20CALENDAR.pdf

Superior Court calendar and protocols page:
https://www.courts.ri.gov/Courts/superiorcourt/Pages/calendar-protocols-misc.aspx

Key procedural features:
- All parties must complete briefing within 60 days of filing of the certified record
- The local board must submit the complete certified record within 30 days of service
- The Superior Court conducts review on the record (no de novo evidentiary hearing) except for constitutional claims
- Standard of review: "arbitrary, capricious, or unreasonable" — unchanged from the former SHAB standard

### 9.3 Building the Appellate Record

Because Superior Court review is on the record, the quality of the hearing record below is determinative. Practitioners representing objectors must ensure:

1. All expert testimony is presented in writing and formally submitted to the record
2. All APRA-obtained documents are formally submitted as public comment
3. All board findings (or their absence) are clearly documented in the minutes
4. All objections to procedural irregularities are made on the record at the time they occur

Under *Bernuth v. New Shoreham*, 770 A.2d 396 (R.I. 2001), the court will not search a silent record to supply missing findings. The absence of required findings is itself sufficient grounds for reversal.

### 9.4 Key RI Case Law

**Comprehensive Permit / § 45-53:**

- *East Bay Community Dev. Corp. v. Barrington*, 901 A.2d 1136 (R.I. 2006) — Comprehensive plan is a "local ordinance"; board must document the balancing test; available on Google Scholar and Justia
- *Bernuth v. New Shoreham*, 770 A.2d 396 (R.I. 2001) — Conclusory findings fatal on appeal; landmark procedural standard
- *Churchill & Banks Cos., LLC v. City of Providence*, 924 A.2d 796 (R.I. 2007) — Developer bears burden on each required finding
- *Alde v. Town of Warren*, PC-2024-02129 (R.I. Super. Nov. 25, 2025) — Most current post-SHAB case; conclusory "consistent with local needs" findings vacated; missing statutory materials renders proceeding void; emergency access is a legitimate local concern

**Standard of Review:**

- *Kaveny v. Town of Cumberland Zoning Bd. of Review*, 875 A.2d 1 (R.I. 2005) — "Arbitrary, capricious or unreasonable" is the governing standard
- Access via Justia: https://law.justia.com/cases/rhode-island/supreme-court/
