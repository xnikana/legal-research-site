## 7. The Access to Public Records Act (§ 38-2)

### 7.1 Overview

R.I.G.L. Chapter 38-2 is Rhode Island's public records law (APRA).
Chapter index: https://webserver.rilegislature.gov/Statutes/TITLE38/38-2/INDEX.htm

Every state and municipal agency, board, commission, department, and quasi-governmental authority is a "public body" subject to APRA. This includes fire departments, planning departments, engineering departments, water authorities, conservation commissions, and school committees.

### 7.2 What Is Covered

APRA covers all public records, defined as "any document, paper, letter, map, book, tape, photograph, film, sound recording, magnetic or other tape, electronic data processing record, computer stored data (including electronic mail messages), or other material regardless of physical form or characteristics made or received pursuant to law or ordinance or in connection with the transaction of official business by any agency." (§ 38-2-2(4))

Key categories of obtainable records in land use proceedings:
- All engineering reports commissioned by or submitted to the municipality
- All correspondence between the municipality (or its consultants) and the applicant (or the applicant's engineers)
- Fire department correspondence, inspection reports, and apparatus specification sheets
- Water authority meeting minutes, hydraulic analyses, and will-serve letters
- Planning board executive session summaries (where not privileged)
- Town planner and peer reviewer communications regarding the application

### 7.3 Exemptions

Section 38-2-2(4) enumerates 25+ exemptions. The most commonly invoked in land use proceedings are:

- **Attorney-client privilege** (§ 38-2-2(4)(j)): Municipal solicitor communications with the board. The privilege belongs to the municipality, not the attorney; a new administration may waive prior counsel's communications.
- **Preliminary deliberations** (§ 38-2-2(4)(d)): Factual working papers or inter-agency memoranda not incorporated into a final decision. Inapplicable to final reports, peer review memos, or documents relied upon in the decision.
- **Personnel records** (§ 38-2-2(4)(b)): Narrow; does not cover job performance records relevant to a public function.

### 7.4 Procedure and Timelines

- Requests must be made in writing to the designated records officer of the public body.
- Response required within 10 business days of receipt; extensions available for good cause with notice.
- Copy fee: $0.15 per page (or actual cost of electronic reproduction).
- A request for electronic delivery must be honored where the record exists in electronic form.
- Denial must state specific exemption(s) relied upon.

### 7.5 Appeal from Denial

A denial may be appealed to:

1. **Attorney General's Office** — the AG may investigate and compel disclosure. Filing is free; typical resolution in 60–90 days. AG decisions: https://riag.ri.gov/divisions/civil/open-records
2. **Superior Court** — de novo review; court may award attorney's fees upon finding the denial was unlawful.

### 7.6 Strategic Use in Land Use Proceedings

APRA is most effective when deployed before the hearing record closes. Records obtained via APRA can be submitted as public comment, introduced through expert witnesses, or used to impeach the developer's or municipality's technical submissions.

Where the planning record is thin on fire safety, water supply, or environmental review — because the municipality failed to obtain or circulate key documents — APRA can demonstrate that those documents exist and were withheld from the public record.
